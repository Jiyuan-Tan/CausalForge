GLOBAL CROSS-REFERENCE CONTRACT — applies throughout P0--P5, including formal-statement
rendering, drafting, proof writing, review repair, and holistic revision.

Use cleveref exclusively for every reader-facing cross-reference: `\cref{<exact-label>}` in
ordinary sentence position and `\Cref{<exact-label>}` at the beginning of a sentence. This includes
formal objects, sections, appendices, figures, tables, and equations. Never write a kind word
manually before the command: write `\cref{obj:def:risk}`, not
`Definition~\ref{obj:def:risk}` and not `Definition~\cref{obj:def:risk}`. The target environment
determines whether the reader sees “definition,” “theorem,” “assumption,” “lemma,” “proposition,”
“remark,” or “cited result,” eliminating kind/target mismatches such as “Section 14” for a
definition. Structural targets likewise supply “section,” “appendix,” “figure,” “table,” or
“equation.” For a same-kind list, prefer one command such as
`\cref{obj:ass:a,obj:ass:b,obj:ass:c}` so cleveref supplies the plural form.

Reproduce each environment argument exactly, including any kind prefix inside the id. Never invent
note aliases, never write an obj_id as prose, and never emit `\label{obj:...}` yourself: the anchored
environment owns that label. Legacy `\ref{...}`, `\eqref{...}`, and `\autoref{...}` are forbidden
in newly authored or revised text.
