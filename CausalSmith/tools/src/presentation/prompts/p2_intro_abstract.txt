Rules:
- REVISION BRIEF: if the brief above lists findings (not the "first draft" sentinel), address each one that bears on the abstract or introduction — most often an overclaim, a missing qualifier on the scope/conditions, or framing that exceeds what the verified theorems support. Honesty about scope wins over salesmanship. Never alter a claim to be stronger than a body theorem; never mention the review, a revision, or "as flagged" — the paper reads as a first draft.
- CONTRIBUTION FRAMING: the abstract and introduction lead with the exact result, construction, and scope the paper delivers. Express scope through affirmative conditions (population/fixed-design/fixed-overlap/etc.), not through sentences listing what the paper, theorem, estimator, or analysis does not do. Reserve genuine non-coverage for an explicitly titled limitations/future-work section later in the paper.
- Every substantive claim ("we show…", "we establish…") must name conditions at the same strength as the corresponding theorem in the body; if the theorem is about a specific model class, the claim names that class.
- The paper's formal statements are machine-verified in Lean 4; mention this in the introduction in AT MOST ONE factual sentence plus a pointer to the verification appendix — never in the abstract, never as a listed contribution, without marketing language. Do NOT inventory the formalization scope (which results are machine-checked, which inputs are only assumed) in the introduction body: that enumeration belongs in the verification appendix. An ordinary econometrics paper states its assumptions normally and does not discuss "formalized vs not" in its intro.
- Sentences connecting this result to the literature ("extends", "answers", "sharpens") must be defensible from the related-work brief alone.
- Cite only keys already cited in the body or listed in the brief.
- Output exactly: \begin{abstract}...\end{abstract} followed by \section{Introduction}... (LaTeX only).
- Use `table`/`tabular` for comparison tables. Never encode prose tables as `array` inside `\[...\]`; paragraph-column arrays are not supported by the website's KaTeX renderer.

REQUIRED OUTPUT FORMAT: your stdout must be the raw LaTeX described above and nothing else — no code fences, no JSON object or receipt, no commentary, no file writes.

CROSS-REFERENCES: every formal environment owns `\label{obj:<obj_id>}`, where `<obj_id>` is the EXACT identifier in its argument. Cite it only with `\cref{obj:<obj_id>}` (or `\Cref` at sentence start); cleveref supplies the kind and number. Never prefix a manual kind word, never use legacy `\ref{...}` for any target, and never substitute note-style P-/T-/A-/L- aliases. Obj_ids may appear only in environment arguments and cleveref commands; never emit `\label` yourself.
NEVER write \label{obj:...} yourself anywhere — the environment macro defines it automatically; an extra one is a LaTeX duplicate-label error and reads as statement drift.

VERIFICATION-CLAIM SCOPE (anti-overclaim, kept out of the body): the single intro verification sentence must not claim more than the crosswalk backs — never write an unqualified "all statements and proofs are machine-checked" when any anchored object lacks a Lean declaration; it states the formal status factually and points to the verification appendix for the precise scope. The ENUMERATION of what is machine-checked versus which inputs (identification primitives, classical inputs, substrate-gates) are assumed-but-not-discharged is presented in the VERIFICATION APPENDIX, not the introduction. This preserves honesty without foregrounding the formalization: the intro neither overclaims nor reads as a formalized-vs-not inventory.
