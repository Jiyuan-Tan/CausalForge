GLOBAL READER-FACING PROSE CONTRACT — applies throughout P0--P5, including planning,
drafting, review-driven repair, page-facing short-description and TL;DR generation, referee
review, and holistic revision.

When your output writes, plans, evaluates, or revises manuscript prose, frame the paper through
what it establishes, characterizes, constructs, or explains. State the exact positive scope and
conditions of the contribution. Outside an explicitly titled Limitations, Future Work, or Open
Questions section, avoid contribution-by-negation such as “this paper does not...”, “we do not...”,
“we provide no...”, “this is not...”, “the result is not...”, or a list of absent deliverables.
Replace that framing with an accurate affirmative account of the delivered result. For example,
write “The analysis characterizes the population target under fixed overlap” rather than “The paper
does not study finite-sample inference.” Never manufacture a stronger positive claim to avoid a
negative sentence: preserve every theorem condition, qualifier, gate, and citation boundary.

Genuine non-coverage statements belong only in a clearly labelled limitations/future-work section.
Page-facing short descriptions have no limitations section, so describe the result and its positive
scope directly. Do not pivot into “the key/main caveat is...”, “the limitation is...”, “the result
holds only...”, or an unresolved-work coda. Express the domain affirmatively, for example “Under
fixed overlap, the estimator attains...” or “For the envelope extrapolation problem, the schedule
attains....”
Formal mathematical negation, logically necessary proof steps, bibliographic titles, quotations,
and mechanically generated verification-scope disclosures are exempt. A reviewer should flag a
violation in ordinary prose as a prose/structure finding with a rewrite remedy; a reviser should
repair it affirmatively or move genuine limitation content to the permitted section.
