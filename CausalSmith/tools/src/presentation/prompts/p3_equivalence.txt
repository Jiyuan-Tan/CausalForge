Method:
1. Read the Lean file around the declaration with your tools before judging — hypotheses often live in surrounding structures, section variables, or definitions the snippet does not show. Chase the definitions the statement uses when hypothesis strength is at issue.
   Support is DECLARATION-LOCAL: do not credit the paper environment with a theorem proved by a sibling declaration merely because that theorem is nearby or concerns the same object. A computability or operation-count claim belongs only in an environment whose mapped Lean declaration (or explicitly listed component set) delivers it.
2. Back-translate the paper statement into Lean-shaped natural language (hypotheses list + conclusion), then compare.
3. PRECISION AUDIT — enumerate every LOAD-BEARING quantitative condition in the Lean decl: each inequality, envelope, rate exponent, coupling/joint condition, and constant scaling whose weakening would change the conclusion's truth, scope, or rate. For each, verify the paper carries it EXPLICITLY — either inlined (the actual inequality / envelope / exponent) OR discharged by a `\cref` to a frozen assumption/definition environment that ITSELF states the quantitative form. A `\cref` to such a substantive environment SATISFIES this step (the reader resolves the condition there). A condition the Lean carries quantitatively but the paper renders as a bare label with no substantive cleveref is a defect — see DILUTION below.

Verdict policy — the test is EQUIVALENCE up to formalization packaging:
- DRIFT (fail): the paper's conclusion is stronger or different (constants, rates, quantifiers, model class); the paper omits a load-bearing Lean hypothesis (claiming the result under weaker assumptions), EXCEPT for exact citation erasure below; the paper describes a different mathematical object than the Lean declaration.
- DRIFT (fail) — OVER-ASSUMPTION: the paper carries a load-bearing hypothesis or scope restriction that the Lean result does not require ANYWHERE (not in the snippet, the surrounding structures, section variables, or the chased definitions) — the printed statement is strictly narrower than the verified one, so reporting it as the verified statement misstates the correspondence. Load-bearing means its removal would widen the claim's scope or weaken its premises; incidental explicit regularity that does not change the conclusion's truth, scope, or rate for the objects considered is packaging, not over-assumption.
- FAITHFUL — CITATION ERASURE: an omitted Lean binder is faithful when and only when it asserts exactly one proposition listed above as a source-matched cited dependency. The paper states the result conventionally while a generated theorem-local footnote discloses that machine verification is conditional on that published conclusion. Do not demand that cited proposition be printed as an assumption. This exception never applies to modeling assumptions, calibration conditions, unresolved substrate gates, or an unlisted proposition.
- DRIFT (fail) — DILUTION: the paper names a load-bearing quantitative condition WITHOUT its quantitative form AND without a `\cref` to a frozen environment that states that form, so that a competent reader resolves it to a WEAKER or merely different condition than the Lean. Example: writing "overlap-decay with exponent γ" (which reads as a pointwise floor min(e,1-e) ≥ c·|τ|^γ) for a Lean condition that is actually a joint envelope P{overlap ≤ v, |τ| ≤ u} ≤ C·u^α·v^(1/γ). A hypothesis present in NAME but absent in SUBSTANCE is drift, not a faithful gloss. NOT dilution: a hypothesis discharged by `\cref{obj:ass:margin-window}` to a frozen environment that states "0 < u_0 < 2" — that is faithfully carried by reference, a packaging choice, not vagueness.
- DRIFT (fail) — NON-ASSERTORIC: the environment is a theorem/lemma backed by a PROVEN Lean declaration (a `theorem`/`lemma` that type-checks), but its body is phrased as an objective, open question, or proof recipe ("determine/find the exponent…", "the derivation is required to use…", "it remains to show…") instead of DECLARATIVELY asserting the proved claim. A theorem block must state what is true — the bound/identity/rate that holds, with its constants and quantifiers — not a task to perform or a method to follow; an objective- or recipe-phrased body for a proven result fails to deliver the Lean conclusion and is drift.
- FAITHFUL (pass) despite surface differences: the paper makes explicit INCIDENTAL regularity the Lean statement leaves implicit or carries elsewhere (in ambient structures, section variables, or at use sites — measurability, boundedness, codomain restrictions, λ>0-type domain conditions); the paper restricts a Lean-total definition to its meaningful parameter range (junk-value totality is packaging); packaging differences where one paper environment bundles content that Lean splits across several declarations/structures (or vice versa), provided the bundled content is itself Lean-backed; named constants vs existential constants of the same strength. (Precision exemption: only INCIDENTAL regularity may stay implicit — never a load-bearing inequality/envelope/rate per step 3.)
- DEFAULT TO DRIFT only when, after reading the Lean context, the paper still appears to claim more — or to substantively assume more — than the Lean. Do not report packaging differences as drift.
