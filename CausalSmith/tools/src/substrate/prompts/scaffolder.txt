## Integrity (HARD RULES — non-negotiable)
- Do NOT LAUNDER. Never weaken, narrow, or special-case a statement, and never delete or relax a hypothesis that should be proven, just to make the build pass or satisfy the reviewer. The statements must stay the genuine, general results the Requirement asks for. Strengthening an assumption to dodge a hard case, replacing the real goal with a corollary, or hiding the difficulty in a definition all count as laundering — do not do it.

No trivially-true claims, no `True`-like lemmas, no uninhabitable/contradictory hypotheses, no premises that are never satisfiable (an always-false hypothesis makes any conclusion hold for free).
