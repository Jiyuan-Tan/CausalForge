## Failure classifier (Stage 7)

A theorem dispatched to causalsmith failed to formalize. Classify the failure
into exactly one of five classes so the orchestrator can decide what to do
next (reroute Pass B with feedback, surface a substrate gap, halt for human
review, or leave it alone).

Failure-Note body (verbatim):
{{failure_note_body}}

Last lines of causalsmith stderr:
{{stderr_tail}}

Pre-formalization forecast emitted by Pass B (may be empty):
{{forecast_json}}

Classes (pick exactly one):

- `substrate_missing` — proof referenced a prerequisite (lemma, definition,
  algebraic structure) that is not present in Causalean/Mathlib. Stderr or note
  body typically shows `unknown identifier`, `failed to synthesize`, or
  cites a named result by hand.
- `proof_sketch_bug` — the proof_sketch has a logical or step-ordering
  flaw. Translation got far enough that the bug is locatable inside the
  sketch (wrong invocation, missing case split, incorrect coercion, etc.).
- `setup_incomplete` — the proof would have worked but the `setup` did not
  introduce some object/symbol the proof_sketch references. Typically
  surfaces as "unknown identifier" on a name that the sketch uses.
- `statement_wrong` — the theorem statement itself is malformed or
  contradicts the Insight summary; formalization fails because no valid
  Lean translation exists. The orchestrator HALTS on this class.
- `lean_translation_issue` — causalsmith's own translation step (Stage 2 /
  Stage 3 / Stage 4) failed for reasons unrelated to the study pipeline's choices
  (e.g., Lean syntax noise, causalsmith stage timeout, tactic-engine bug).
  No study-pipeline action.

Output: a single JSON object on stdout (no prose, no fenced block):

    {
      "class": "<one of: substrate_missing | proof_sketch_bug | setup_incomplete | statement_wrong | lean_translation_issue>",
      "rationale": "<one or two sentences citing the specific stderr line or sketch step that led to your decision>"
    }
