=== GLOBAL SETUP — Panel anchor ===

Panel and treatment:
- We work with a balanced panel indexed by units i=1,...,N and periods t=1,...,T.
- Treatment is binary unless otherwise stated: D_it in {0,1}.
- For boundary periods, define D_{i,t-k}=0 whenever t-k is outside {1,...,T}.

Potential outcomes:
- The outcome has finite memory p:
    Y_it = Y_it(H_it),  H_it = (D_it, D_{i,t-1}, ..., D_{i,t-p}).
- There is no anticipation: Y_it does not depend on future treatments.
- The all-zero history is denoted 0_p = (0,...,0).
- Define the response surface m_it(h) = E[Y_it(h)] and the history effect tau_it(h) = m_it(h) - m_it(0_p).

Untreated baseline:
- The untreated potential outcome satisfies the two-way fixed-effect condition
    E[Y_it(0_p) | D_i] = alpha_i + lambda_t.
- Therefore, after residualizing against unit and time fixed effects, the untreated component should not contribute to the treatment-history coefficient. If your proof needs a stronger or weaker condition, state it explicitly.

Regression algebra:
- **You are free to use ANY estimation / identification machinery that fits the panel question.** FWL is one common substrate; matrix-completion, factor models, spectral / nuclear-norm methods, GMM (Arellano-Bond, dynamic panel), M-estimator / DR-orthogonal moments (DML, AIPW), and other techniques are equally welcome — use whichever actually delivers the result. Do NOT force FWL when it obscures the mechanism. The notation block below is provided in case FWL is what you choose; it is not a mandate.
- When FWL is the right substrate:
    * Let F denote the nuisance design matrix, usually unit and time fixed effects.
    * Let M_F be the residual-maker with respect to F.
    * For regressors B, define the residualized projection coefficient by
        theta_hat = (B' M_F B)^{-1} B' M_F Y,
      when B' M_F B is invertible.
    * If the Gram matrix may be singular, state the rank condition or use the Moore-Penrose inverse and explain the estimable projection.
- When a different technique is used, state which technique justifies each step and cite the published source (e.g. Arkhangelsky et al. 2021 for synthetic DiD; Athey et al. 2021 for matrix completion; Chernozhukov et al. 2018 for DML).

Causal interpretation:
- Derive the coefficient in terms of potential-outcome contrasts or response-surface cell means.
- Do not stop at matrix notation. Whenever possible, rewrite the result as
    target effect + contamination terms.
- Do not claim causal identification unless the coefficient is shown to equal the stated target under explicit assumptions.
