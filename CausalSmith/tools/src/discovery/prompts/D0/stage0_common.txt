=== UNIVERSAL PROOF DISCIPLINE (applies to all four clusters) ===

Proof style:
- Write in theorem-proof style with definitions, assumptions, theorem statements, proof, remarks, and sanity checks.
- The proof should be in natural language but equation-by-equation, so each step is self-contained and traceable to a named assumption or algebraic identity. (Stage 1 will read this artifact to derive the formalization plan; Stage 0's job is mathematical soundness, not Lean readiness.)
- Explicitly state all support, rank, positivity, overlap, and boundary conditions.

Causal interpretation:
- Distinguish the causal target from the identifying / regression / bounding functional. Do not collapse them by definition.
- For point-identification (Panel / ExactID): rewrite the result as `target effect + contamination terms` whenever possible; do not claim identification unless the contamination vanishes under explicit assumptions.
- For partial identification: state the result as `Θ_I(P) ⊆ [θ_L(P), θ_U(P)]` with a sharpness verdict; if not sharp, flag it.
- For Stat: identification of the causal estimand θ is GIVEN (cite the identifying functional; do not re-derive it). The result is a statement about the *estimator's* behavior — a convergence rate, an efficiency bound, or a limit law — and must ADVANCE THE FRONTIER (a matching minimax lower bound is NOT required): a matched lower bound (proves tightness), a SHARPER upper bound than a named prior result, the FIRST nontrivial bound for a named method/estimand, attainment of the efficiency bound, a limit law, OR a nontrivial estimator (consistency + a rate or limit law, not necessarily sharp) for a NOVEL causal estimand the literature had not estimated. A matched converse is the strongest form but not the only creditable one. Distinguish the estimator from its target and from the limit object.
- For Experimentation: identification of the causal estimand θ is GIVEN by the design (do not re-derive it). The randomness is the assignment alone and the potential outcomes are fixed. The result is design-based — EITHER a design-based estimator's limit law + Wald-coverage, DERIVED from primitive design / dependency conditions and never assumed, OR an optimal / efficient design with an optimality certificate. Distinguish the estimand from the estimator / design and from the limit object.

Usability of the conclusion (UNIVERSAL):
The theorem must expose its conclusion in a form a downstream reader can act on. For Panel / ExactID / PartialID the bar is identification / structure level — sampling, plug-in, consistency, inference are out of scope. **The Stat cluster is the exception: there sampling / rate / efficiency / limit-law IS the contribution**, so for a Stat instance the conclusion must expose the estimator as an explicit map from data, a named target object (rate `n^{-β}` / efficiency bound / limit law), and a frontier-advance (a matching minimax lower bound, a sharper bound than named prior art, a first nontrivial bound, or attainment of the bound). **The Experimentation cluster is likewise an exception: there the design-based limit law + Wald-coverage (design given) OR the optimal design + its optimality certificate (design chosen) IS the contribution**, derived from primitive design / dependency conditions and never assumed. The specific requirement depends on theorem type:

- **Identifying-formula / bound theorems** (Panel coefficient decomposition, ExactID identification, PartialID bound). The named functional must be a *computable map* from observed-population inputs. The bar is "can a downstream reader evaluate this on a population P", not "is it expressed in any particular formalism".
    The gate is *evaluability*, not any specific formalism — the conclusion must be a map a downstream reader can evaluate on P. ANY form that exposes evaluability is acceptable; the list below is a non-exhaustive set of examples to remind you of options, not a fixed menu. Examples: (i) closed-form functional of P; (ii) closed-form CDF / quantile / density envelope or copula identity; (iii) extremal-coupling / optimal-transport representation; (iv) explicit support-function / capacity-functional / random-set kernel; (v) explicit algorithm with stated complexity (vertex enumeration, basis-rule, dynamic program, support-pattern case split); (vi) reduction to a named optimization with explicit dimension, decision variables, and constraint list (LP, QP, convex program, conic, semidefinite, infinite-dimensional with named structure); (vii) Tikhonov-regularized inverse / completeness-bridge formula with stated regularization or basis; (viii) formula in named operators each of which is evaluable from P (conditional expectations, quantiles, projections onto stated subspaces). Invent other forms freely if they expose evaluability.
    NOT acceptable: "θ equals inf/sup of an unspecified set", "θ is identified" without writing the formula, "the optimum exists" without saying where or by what, a contamination term named but not written.
    Cluster cues:
      Panel — contamination terms must be explicit projection/residual functionals; do not stop at "the bias from misspecified controls".
      ExactID — write the identifying formula (back-door / g-formula / Wald / front-door / mediation / proximal-bridge) explicitly; if it contains an inverse (Tikhonov, integral equation), state the algorithm or basis under which the inverse is computable.
      PartialID — bound endpoints must expose evaluability in *some* explicit form: closed form, CDF/quantile/copula envelope, extremal coupling, support-function / random-set kernel, vertex characterization, OR reduction to a named finite optimization with explicit dimension and constraints. Do NOT default to an LP framing when a closed-form envelope or copula identity captures the bound more directly. Bare inf/sup is layer-(1) stenography regardless of substrate. PREFER the most explicit form the substrate supports — a closed-form endpoint over an optimization framing of the same bound. Before settling on an optimization, ATTEMPT the reduction (dual / extreme points / first-order conditions): if it reduces, the closed form is the conclusion and the program becomes a proof step; if not, name the obstruction. Never force a spurious closed form.
      Stat — the estimator must be an explicit map from the sample (an M-estimator / plug-in / one-step / orthogonal-moment / sieve form, written out), and the target object must be a named rate `n^{-β}` (β tied to a named structural exponent — smoothness, overlap-decay, sparsity), an efficiency bound (the semiparametric variance / influence function), or a limit law. The result must ADVANCE THE FRONTIER, but a matching minimax lower bound is NOT required: a matched lower bound (named two-point / Fano / Assouad / Le-Cam — proves tightness), a SHARPER upper bound than a named prior result, the FIRST nontrivial bound for a named method/estimand, attainment of the efficiency bound, OR a nontrivial estimator (consistency + a rate or limit law, not necessarily sharp) for a NOVEL causal estimand the literature had not estimated each suffice. A lone rate that is none of these (not tight, not an improvement, not a first, not a novel-estimand estimator) is layer-(1) stenography for Stat; a sharper upper bound, a first-nontrivial bound, or a novel-estimand estimator IS a genuine contribution — do NOT refuse to solve such a kernel because it lacks a converse.
      Experimentation — for an inference result, write the estimator as an explicit map from the realized assignment and the limit law + Wald-coverage, DERIVED from primitive design / dependency conditions (never assumed); for an optimal-design result, write the feasible design class + criterion and exhibit the optimal design WITH an optimality certificate (KKT / matching variance lower bound / saddle). The design is finite-population (randomness = assignment, outcomes fixed). Do NOT reflexively default to the inference shape when the open content is which design to run.
    The usability content belongs in §7 (Estimand-defining functional) and §9 (Main theorems), not relegated to §11 proof or §13 counterexample.

- **Counterexample / negative-result theorems** ("method X fails", "Conjecture is refuted"). The named obstruction must be an explicit *witness*: either a concrete observable law on which the failure occurs (with the failure quantified — endpoint moves by how much, which constraint is violated), or a generic-class certificate (a non-degenerate region / proxy system / graph family on which the obstruction holds). The witness requirement is already covered by Dimension 2's "Negative-result novelty" rule; restate the witness in §13 or in the theorem itself, not only in prose.

- **Equivalence / characterization theorems** ("Θ_I under R equals Θ_I' under R'"; "assumption A is equivalent to graphical condition G"). Either at least one side of the equivalence must be a computable map (so the equivalence is informative for evaluation), or both sides must be named objects already known to be evaluable. An equivalence between two opaque optimizations is not a usable theorem.

- **Pure impossibility / generic-class theorems** ("no observable choice of κ tightens X"; "on a generic region of laws, Y holds"). The deliverable is the obstruction or genericity certificate — what witnesses non-existence (a quantifier-elimination step, a dimension count, a topological argument). No evaluation map is required, but the certificate must be explicit, not "by a standard argument".

If the theorem does not fit any of the above types, say which type it is and justify why the corresponding deliverable is the right standard.
