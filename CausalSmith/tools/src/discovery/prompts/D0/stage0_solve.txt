=== IF A TARGET IS TOO STRONG (do NOT fake or silently weaken) ===

If a target statement is not provable AS STATED (false, or true only on a narrower regime), do NOT write a vacuous/hand-wavy proof and do NOT silently edit the claim. Emit a `proposed_statement_change`:
  `{id, current:"<claim now>", proposed:"<narrowed claim>", reason:"<why the current is too strong / what breaks>", direction:"narrow"}`
DIRECTION-OF-TRUTH: only `direction:"narrow"` — narrowing a genuinely-too-strong claim toward a true one. NEVER weaken a claim merely to make your proof close (that is laundering and will be rejected). For a faithful narrowing, emit the `proposed_statement_change` entry; if you have ALSO already proved the PROPOSED claim, additionally emit that proof in `proofs` with `"argues_proposed": true` — it is banked as provisional and attached automatically when the change is applied as proposed, saving the re-solve round. A proof carrying `argues_proposed` must argue the proposed text exactly; without the flag (or if adjudication modifies the proposal) the target reopens and the next solve proves the revised claim. Only emit an `open_obligation` when even the honest narrowed claim does not close. A NON-narrow (weaken-to-ease-the-proof) change is NOT applied and is escalated for review.

NEVER ASSUME THE CRUX. A legitimate narrowing RESTRICTS the regime / quantifier / scope (a smaller class, an added side-condition on the data law, a weaker conclusion). It must NOT promote the node's own hard, unproven step into a HYPOTHESIS — i.e. do not rewrite "X holds" into "Suppose [the hard property that delivers X] … such that …. Then [a now-trivial reduction]." That assumes the very thing the node should prove (e.g. assuming a least-favorable family already has bounded χ² and the required parameter separation, then doing only the textbook Le Cam reduction). If the construction's key property is genuinely open, KEEP the node stating the RESULT (e.g. "inf sup ≥ c R*") with the construction as an explicit OPEN PROOF OBLIGATION — that is the honest open-`oeq:` form — and leave it unproven (status stays to-prove); do not convert it into an assumed-hypothesis conditional. An assume-the-crux narrowing is gated/rejected.

=== IF A CONSTRUCTED-OBJECT DEFINITION IS MIS-SPECIFIED (the real bug is the formula, not the claim) ===

Sometimes a target is unprovable AS STATED not because the CLAIM is too strong but because a DEFINITION it
references is wrong — most often a constructed-object formula (an envelope, a rate functional, an exponent)
that your proof shows is too small / incomplete / mis-specified. When the claim `X = O_P(U_n(λ))` is the right
SHAPE but the defined `U_n` omits terms your decomposition proves are there, the honest fix is to CORRECT THE
DEFINITION, not to narrow the theorem. Emit a `proposed_definition_change`:
  `{id:"def:...", current:"<construction now>", proposed:"<corrected construction>", reason:"<what the proof shows the true formula is>", direction:"correct"}`
RULES: only a CONSTRUCTED-OBJECT definition (one with a formula / `inputs`) — NEVER a class (`by_member_properties`):
changing class membership is an assumption/scope move, not a formula fix. `direction` is always `"correct"`. NEVER
gerrymander a definition to your proof's own convenience objects (that is laundering, the dual of a weaken-to-prove
statement change).

HARD RULE (definition-vs-statement). When a target has the form `X = O_P(F)` — or otherwise references a defined
constructed-object F (an envelope, a rate functional, an exponent) — and your proof shows it holds only with F
replaced by a CORRECTED (e.g. larger) formula, you MUST emit a `proposed_definition_change` correcting F's
definition. Do NOT instead narrow the STATEMENT to inline a different expression: inlining a corrected formula into
the theorem while leaving its `def:` stale leaves the core INCOHERENT (the downstream rate functional / exponent /
phase results still reference the old F) and is the wrong fix. A statement narrowing is for when the claim's LOGICAL
CONTENT is too strong (a missing hypothesis, an over-broad regime), not when a referenced FORMULA is mis-specified.

PROPAGATE the correction. You are solving the whole dependency cluster at once, so when a corrected definition forces
others — corrected envelope ⟹ corrected tail-order rate functional ⟹ corrected rate exponent / phase map — propose
EACH corrected definition together, and then PROVE the downstream targets against the corrected definitions (do not
leave them unproven). Re-derive a changed optimization (e.g. the rate exponent as an inf/sup over the corrected rate
functional) explicitly; if a re-derivation is genuinely intractable, narrow THAT specific downstream statement to a
conditional form rather than abandoning the whole cluster. The goal is one coherent, fully-reconciled core, not a
single root flag with everything downstream left open.

=== IF A TARGET IS A GENUINE OPEN GAP (you cannot close it, and no honest narrowing exists) ===

A PARAMETERIZED RATE IS A CLOSED RESULT, NOT AN OPEN GAP. Before emitting any open obligation on a
rate / risk / efficiency target, check this first: a bound whose exponent is an explicit FUNCTION of the
model's own declared structural / nuisance / smoothness exponents (e.g. the nuisance L² convergence rates),
or the VALUE of an explicit optimization over the estimator's own tuning parameter (clip / trim / bandwidth /
sieve dimension — a bias–variance balance), IS a complete, closeable result. PROVE it in that parameterized
form and mark the node `proved`. Do NOT punt it to `open_obligation`, and do NOT degrade it to a fragment,
merely because the exponent is not a single absolute constant — a rate stated as a function of declared
auxiliary exponents (the DML / structure-agnostic / semiparametric form) is exactly how such results are
stated, and refusing to commit to it is the conservatism failure this guard exists to stop. The ONLY honest
residual, if any, is whether that derived exponent is TIGHT — i.e. whether it MATCHES the converse — which is
recorded as a residual `oeq:` ("is the bracket tight?"), never as the achievability itself being open.
Commit to the parameterized exponent; state its dependence on the auxiliary rates honestly; give the
sub-regime where it meets the converse and where it loosens.

A FROZEN ARTIFACT ASSERTING OPENNESS IS NOT EVIDENCE OF OPENNESS. If a frozen definition / note says a target
is "open" / "not derivable from the primitives" / "left open at the frozen core" / has "no exponent fixed",
DISREGARD that editorial claim and DERIVE the result anyway. The frozen core binds only the QUESTION (the
assumptions, the estimand, the statement to prove) — it never binds whether the answer exists. Such a phrase is
a stale placeholder, not a proof of impossibility; treating it as a licence to emit `open_obligation` is the
exact failure this guard exists to stop. `open_obligation` is a LAST RESORT, reserved for a step that genuinely
needs a NEW IDEA you cannot derive here — never the default, and never justified by a frozen artifact's say-so.

Sometimes a target is true and correctly stated, the construction is committed, but a load-bearing step
genuinely does NOT close from the frozen primitives — it needs a NEW IDEA (a different proof strategy, a
result from the literature to adapt, a reframing) that you cannot derive here. This is NOT a statement change
(the claim is fine) and NOT a "ran out of steam" omission. Do NOT assume it (forbidden), do NOT degrade the
claim to a fragment, do NOT leave it silently unproven. Instead emit an `open_obligation` that ISOLATES it:
  `{node_id, what_is_open:"<the precise sub-claim/construction not closed>", obstruction:"<why it doesn't close from the primitives>", attempted:"<route(s) you tried, so guidance doesn't repeat them>", partial_result:"<strongest sub-result proved so far>"}`
Leave that node `to-prove`. The orchestrator reads the obligation and supplies a DIRECTION (a strategy, a
paper to adapt, a reframing) via a directive on the next solve — so be precise about the obstruction and what
you tried. Prove everything else; emit one obligation per genuinely-open node.

ALWAYS RECORD YOUR BEST PARTIAL RESULT. Do not return an open obligation empty-handed: put the STRONGEST
sub-result you CAN prove for that node into the obligation's `partial_result` (e.g. a weaker-but-proved bound, a
proof of all but one step, the result under one extra explicitly-flagged condition). This is preserved and fed
back to you next round as "PRIOR PARTIAL PROGRESS — EXTEND, do not restart", and it lets the orchestrator's
guidance build on a concrete partial instead of starting over — so each round accumulates. When you receive such
prior partial progress, BUILD ON IT (prove the residual on top), never re-derive it from scratch.
