=== GLOBAL SETUP — Experimentation / design-based causal inference anchor ===

What this cluster is (and is NOT):
- The contribution is a DESIGN-BASED result about a CAUSAL estimand under a known or chosen randomization. The cluster identity: the **design** (the assignment distribution) is a first-class object, the ONLY source of randomness is the assignment, and the potential outcomes are FIXED (finite-population / fixed-array), NOT draws from a superpopulation. If your randomness comes from sampling units from a superpopulation, you are in the WRONG cluster (route to Stat).
- The estimand must be CAUSAL: a design-based average / direct / indirect / total / overall treatment effect, an expected average treatment effect (EATE) under interference, or another finite-population contrast of fixed potential outcomes. A result about an arbitrary design functional with no causal effect is out of scope.
- Identification is GIVEN: under the design, the estimand is a fixed finite-population contrast of potential outcomes. The reviewable content is NOT a new identification or a new bound — it is the design-based theory.
- TWO native shapes (others admissible if they keep the cluster identity above — do NOT force a question into the wrong one):
    (1) **Design-based inference (design GIVEN).** Construct an explicit design-based estimator and derive a limit law + Wald-coverage statement for the causal estimand. The limit law + coverage IS the headline.
    (2) **Optimal / efficient design (design CHOSEN).** Treat the design as the decision variable and characterize the design that optimizes a stated criterion (minimax / Bayes variance, asymptotic efficiency, power, or regret) for the causal estimand, subject to feasibility constraints. The optimal design + its optimality certificate IS the headline.
    (3) **Adaptive / sequential experiment inference (design DATA-DEPENDENT).** The assignment probabilities update DURING the experiment from accruing data (bandit / response-adaptive / batched / sequential), so the sample is not i.i.d. and the ordinary design-based CLT fails. Construct a stabilized estimator (adaptive / variance-stabilizing weights, e.g. propensity-stabilized AIPW) and derive a MARTINGALE CLT under the adaptively-generated filtration + a Wald-coverage statement. The valid limit law under adaptivity IS the headline.

Target object + estimand structure:
- State the causal estimand θ as a finite-population contrast of fixed potential outcomes, and the randomization DESIGN (the assignment distribution: Bernoulli / complete / two-stage / cluster / saturation, or an abstract design specified by its exposure probabilities). State the exposure mapping and the interference / dependency structure precisely, each as a labeled assumption.
- DEFINE THE DESIGN AND ITS PRIMITIVE CONDITIONS PRECISELY AND MINIMALLY. The design is carved by conditions on the assignment distribution and the dependency structure of the exposures (positivity of exposure probabilities, a dependency-graph / bounded-degree / mixing condition, finite-population moment / boundedness conditions on the fixed potential outcomes, a Lindeberg / negligible-max-influence condition). These are the PRIMITIVE conditions the headline is discharged from.
- For an INFERENCE result (shape 1): define the estimator as an EXPLICIT map from the realized assignment and observed outcomes (Horvitz–Thompson / Hájek / difference-in-means / a stated weighted form — written out, not "there exists an estimator"). Define the target object as a CLT (the centered, scaled estimator converges in distribution to a Gaussian with a stated design variance) AND a Wald-coverage statement (the resulting confidence interval covers θ at the nominal asymptotic level). Estimator- and inference-side conditions (variance-estimator choice, studentization, clipping) are hypotheses of the coverage theorem, not part of the design.
- For an OPTIMAL-DESIGN result (shape 2): define the FEASIBLE DESIGN CLASS (the constraint set the design ranges over: budget / allocation simplex / exposure-positivity / stated interference structure) and the CRITERION to optimize (a design variance / asymptotic efficiency / power / regret, written explicitly as a functional of the design). Define the target object as the OPTIMAL design within the class TOGETHER with an optimality CERTIFICATE — a first-order / KKT condition, a matching lower bound on the achievable criterion, or a saddle-point — not a design merely asserted to be good.
- For an ADAPTIVE result (shape 3): state the ADAPTIVE ALLOCATION RULE (how the assignment probability at stage/batch t depends on the data observed through t−1) and the filtration it generates. Define the estimator as an EXPLICIT stabilized map (adaptive / variance-stabilizing weights, written out) and the target object as a martingale CLT (the stabilized, scaled estimator → Gaussian) plus a Wald-coverage statement. The stabilizing-weight / clipping condition and the increment moment / Lindeberg condition are the named primitives the martingale CLT is discharged from.

The result must satisfy the EXPERIMENTATION floor:
- THE HEADLINE IS DERIVED FROM PRIMITIVE DESIGN CONDITIONS, NEVER ASSUMED. This is the cluster's anti-laundering guard, instantiated per shape:
    * Shape 1 (inference): the CLT must be DERIVED from the primitive conditions on the design and the dependency structure (a dependency-graph / Stein, Lindeberg–Feller, or martingale CLT under interference) — it may NEVER be taken as a hypothesis ("assume the estimator is asymptotically normal"). Assuming the limit law is disqualifying.
    * Shape 2 (optimal design): optimality must be CERTIFIED (KKT / matching variance lower bound / saddle-point) — a candidate design declared optimal without a certificate is the optimal-design analog of the laundering guard and is disqualifying.
    * Shape 3 (adaptive): the limit law must be a MARTINGALE CLT derived under the adaptively-generated filtration with the stabilizing-weight / clipping and increment-moment conditions named as primitives — naively assuming asymptotic normality of an unstabilized adaptive estimator (ignoring that the adaptive sample is non-i.i.d.) is disqualifying.
- For shape 1, a WALD-COVERAGE statement that follows from the CLT. Oracle / known-design-variance coverage is acceptable (coverage follows directly from the CLT + the normal CDF); a consistent / conservative variance-estimator result is a SEPARATE follow-on theorem, NOT a precondition. If you DO use a plug-in variance, state and prove its consistency-or-conservativeness as a labeled hypothesis.
- The estimand stays CAUSAL and the design stays explicit throughout.

Proof technique:
- **Use any technique that fits the problem.** The catalogue below is a freely-ignorable suggestion menu:
    * CLT under dependence: Stein's method on a dependency graph, Lindeberg–Feller for triangular arrays, martingale CLT, m-dependence / mixing CLTs;
    * unbiasedness / variance: Horvitz–Thompson inclusion-probability algebra, finite-population variance / covariance identities, law of total variance for two-stage / compound designs, edge-sum variance bounds;
    * design optimization: Lagrangian / KKT for a variance- or power-objective, convex relaxation of the feasible design class, Neyman-allocation algebra, minimax / Bayes-risk saddle-point, matching variance lower bounds;
    * convergence in probability: finite-population Chebyshev, variance-bound + design-asymptotics;
    * coverage: normal-CDF / Slutsky composition of the CLT with a variance estimator.
- The only hard requirement: each step is traceable to a named assumption or a named theorem/identity, and the headline (limit law OR optimality) is actually DERIVED from the primitive design conditions (not assumed) — the frontier-advance the result claims is PROVED, not asserted.

Stress-test before committing a result (or declaring an open gap):
- Build the SMALLEST instance and check the claim EXACTLY; if a stress-test refutes the target AS STATED, narrow it honestly (per the solver's too-strong rule), never launder.
    * Inference shape: a design with N ≤ 4 units and an enumerable assignment distribution — evaluate the conjectured variance / coverage over EVERY assignment and report any assignment where the HT/Hájek estimand is biased or coverage falls below nominal; separately, push the interference degree / max unit-influence to the boundary of the CLT's primitive condition and check the limit is still Gaussian and the variance stabilizes.
    * Optimal-design shape: search the feasible design class for a design whose criterion (variance / power / regret) BEATS the claimed optimum, or push a constraint to the boundary where the optimality certificate (KKT / matching lower bound / saddle) fails — either refutes optimality on the stated class.
    * Adaptive shape: build a 2-batch rule (batch-2 assignment depending on batch 1) and check whether the UNSTABILIZED estimator's studentized statistic is actually standard normal — if the adaptivity induces bias / over-dispersion, any claim assuming a plain i.i.d. CLT is refuted and the valid limit must come from the martingale CLT.

In-repo substrate (informational only — do NOT cite Lean files in the .tex):
- Downstream Stage 1 / Stage 2 will formalize against `Causalean/Experimentation/`. The paper-agnostic `Experimentation/DesignBased/` layer already carries: the finite-population design calculus (`FiniteDesign` with E / Var / Cov / Pr / indicator), exposure mappings and consistency (`Exposure`, `PotentialOutcome`), Horvitz–Thompson estimators with unbiasedness (`HT/{Estimator,Unbiased,Variance}`), finite-population Chebyshev (`Chebyshev`), edge-sum variance bounds (`EdgeVarianceBound`), convergence-in-probability scaffolding (`InProb`), compound / product-design variance (`CompoundVariance`, `ProductVariance`), the normal CDF bridge (`GaussianCDF`), an independent-summands CLT (`IndepSummandsCLT`), and a from-scratch Stein dependency-graph CLT (`SteinMethod/`, with a local-dependence CLT discharged from primitive conditions). Paper instantiations (Aronow–Samii 2017, Hudgens–Halloran 2008, Sävje–Aronow–Hudgens 2021) sit in sibling folders. Anchor the proposal so its Lean realization can build on this substrate — the `SteinMethod` local-dependence CLT for an asymptotic-normality step, and the same `FiniteDesign` E/Var/Cov + `EdgeVarianceBound` / `CompoundVariance` calculus for the variance objectives an optimal-design result optimizes — rather than re-deriving over plain `ℝ`. Caveat for the adaptive shape (3): the substrate's CLTs are the i.i.d.-summands and dependency-graph (Stein) cases; a MARTINGALE CLT under an adaptive filtration is NOT yet in `DesignBased` and would need a port from Mathlib's martingale theory — flag this as a known substrate gap at F1 rather than assuming the martingale limit.
