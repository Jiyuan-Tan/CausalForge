=== HARD QUALITY BAR (every emitted open problem must satisfy ALL) ===

(G1) **Specific, not generic.** The `open_problem` field names a concrete unresolved question, not a meta-question. "What are the bounds on θ under restriction R?" passes; "How do we extend ATE to heterogeneous settings?" fails — replace or drop.

(G2) **At least one source_ref with grounding.** Every entry has ≥1 entry in `source_refs[]`. Each `source_refs[i]` must contain enough information for Stage -1.2 to find and read the source independently — for `kind: "paper"`, the bibkey + claim; for `kind: "prior_proposal"`, the file path + section + ≤120-char excerpt.

(G3) **Why it is unsolved is anchored.** The `why_unsolved` field must point to a concrete obstacle: a missing assumption class, a regime the literature has not entered, a phenomenon observed but not pinned to a theorem, two papers in tension whose reconciliation requires new work. "It's hard" / "more research needed" do NOT count and the entry is dropped.

(G4) **Resolution is statement-level.** `what_a_resolution_would_look_like` names the kind of mathematical object a resolution would be: an identification formula, a sharp bound, a counterexample with regime statement, a sufficient-condition theorem. "Some result on X" fails.

(G5) **Cluster hint is set.** `cluster_hint ∈ {panel, exactid, partialid, stat, experimentation, scm}`. This routes Stage -1.2's anchor-cluster choice. Use `exactid` when the kernel proves uniqueness or recoverability of a causal target from the observational law: either an effect/query identifying functional OR a structure/mechanism recovery map and its identifiable/nonidentifiable frontier under named restrictions. Keep a structure-identification kernel in `exactid` when its minimax error, sample complexity, or attaining detector is the estimation rung rather than a separate causal-effect estimand. Use `stat` when a resolution would instead be an **estimation-and-inference** statement about a *causal* estimand — a minimax convergence rate, a semiparametric efficiency bound, a limit law / CLT, a coverage-facing theorem, or a finite-sample concentration result — where the rate/efficiency/limit/coverage IS the contribution (NOT a new identification or bounds claim, which route to exactid / partialid with an estimator as the M8 rung). Use `experimentation` when a resolution would be a statement about the experimental **design** or design-based inference for a *causal* estimand under a **known or chosen randomization** with **fixed (finite-population) potential outcomes** — the randomness is the assignment, not a superpopulation draw. This covers a design-based limit law / coverage result, an optimal- or efficient-design result, and an exact / randomization-inference result; the distinguishing feature vs. `stat` is that the design is a first-class object and the randomness source is the assignment (superpopulation sampling ⇒ `stat`). The estimand the rate/efficiency/coverage/design is *about* must be causal (ATE / ATT / CATE / LATE / direct-indirect-total spillover / policy value / a partial-ID bound endpoint); a rate or design for an arbitrary statistical functional with no causal estimand is out of scope. If the open problem genuinely straddles clusters, pick the dominant one and record the secondary in `why_unsolved`.

If after both tracks complete you cannot assemble ≥3 open problems satisfying G1–G5, emit `status: "needs-pivot"` with a one-line note that the topic anchor is too generic. The orchestrator will then either pivot the topic or abort the run; you do NOT continue.

=== EXEMPLAR DESIGNATION (per open problem) ===

For each emitted open problem, designate the papers the downstream proposer (Stage -1.2) and paper-writer (Stage 0) will keep open on the desk while they draft — the way a human writing a paper keeps the closest-written and closest-method references at hand. You are NOT pre-digesting or summarizing these papers; you only NAME them and say WHY, with enough locator information (arXiv id or DOI) that a downstream stage can pull up the actual paper at the moment it writes the introduction or derives the result. Per repo convention, prefer an arXiv id (the lazy lookup fetches the .tex, which reads cleaner than an encrypted PDF).

Designate two roles (one paper MAY fill both):

- **writing_exemplar** (exactly one per open problem): the paper whose MOTIVATION ARC and PROBLEM FORMULATION the proposal will adopt — the setup narrative the proposer reproduces and then pivots into THIS entry's gap. **DEFAULT to the anchor itself** (the paper that posed the problem this entry extends): its motivation chain is usually the one you want. Choose a DIFFERENT same-area paper only when it is a strictly better-written template for the SAME arc. Record:
   - `why` — what about its framing to mirror;
   - `motivation_arc` — the paper's 2–4 step setup narrative as an explicit chain `A -> B -> C`, where the FINAL step lands on this entry's gap, so the proposer's intro flows straight into the open problem. Example (proximal partial-ID): "proximal inference removes unmeasured confounding -> point identification requires bridge completeness -> what is the identified set when completeness fails?".

- **method_exemplars** (one to three per open problem): the paper(s) whose TECHNIQUE — key lemma, proof strategy, estimator construction, or identification argument — a resolution would most directly adapt. These are usually NOT the writing_exemplar and MAY come from an adjacent literature: pick them because they solve a STRUCTURALLY ANALOGOUS problem whose machinery transfers (e.g. a strong-functionals / source-condition NPIV estimation paper for a proximal-bridge target, because both are continuous linear functionals of an ill-posed inverse problem). Tag each with a `role`:
   - `role: "identification"` — the ID / partial-ID / characterization argument the resolution adapts.
   - `role: "estimation_inference"` — an estimator construction + its limit theory (rate, asymptotic normality, valid CI, double robustness, sharpness of a partial-ID region) under a similar setting. **Designate at least one `estimation_inference` exemplar whenever the open problem admits an estimation/inference rung** — most ExactID and PartialID targets do. This is NOT speculative scenery: the Causalean library now carries a substantial estimation/inference substrate (GMM, U-statistics, bootstrap SE, empirical-process / Donsker, concentration, minimax lower bounds, sample-quantile Bahadur, structure-agnostic efficiency bounds), so a ported estimator + inference proof has a real home. Designating it here lets the proposer (Stage -1.2 §8 M8 rung, SC13) commit to a specific estimator AND limit law instead of deferring with "inference is standard". Scout for it deliberately — it usually lives in an estimation/inference literature the gap-scan did not surface as prior art.
   For each entry record:
   - `technique` — the concrete move, METHOD AND ITS PROOF, to carry over;
   - `why_closest` — the structural analogy that licenses the transfer, tied to `what_a_resolution_would_look_like`.

The writing_exemplar normally reuses a `source_refs[]` bibkey (defaulting to the anchor). A method_exemplar OFTEN does NOT — the technique you adapt frequently lives in an adjacent literature (NPIV estimation, source-condition double-robust inference, minimax IV) that the gap-scan never surfaced as prior art; name it anyway with its own bibkey + arXiv-id/DOI, even if it is not in `source_refs[]`. If you genuinely cannot name a credible writing or method exemplar for an open problem, set that field to `null` and give a one-line reason — do NOT invent one. These designations are advisory pointers, not new gaps; they never count toward the ≥3 open-problem minimum.
