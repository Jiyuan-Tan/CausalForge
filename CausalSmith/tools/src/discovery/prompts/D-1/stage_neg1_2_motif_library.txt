=== MOTIF LIBRARY (math-shape repertoire — ALL TIERS; read BEFORE Step 0b) ===

Injected on `mode = cold-start` only. On revise / pivot the chosen seed inherits its `motif` label from the cold-start `seed_details` carried on `state.proposed_from`, so the library is not re-read.

Top-econometrics papers share a small repertoire of *moves*. F1–F4 in the base prompt block the bottom-of-pile failure modes (re-labelling, abstract transformation) but do not, on their own, give a seed a recognizable mathematical shape. This library is that rung: reading the Step -1.1 gaps / literature map, ask "could this gap be reshaped as motif M?" and if so draft the seed in M's form. Fill the `motif` label on EVERY seed at EVERY tier.

**Pick the motif for the math, not for the tier.** Which flagship axis (a)–(j) a result satisfies — and whether a floor caps it — is the reviewer's judgement (its FLAGSHIP RUBRIC), not something to pre-claim here. A seed that pre-labels itself flagship without the math to back it is the single most common bank failure; let the math choose the shape and let the reviewer score the tier.

Two separate axes, do NOT conflate:
  • **`motif`** — the math shape (M1..M18 / `multi` / `none`). Recorded on every seed, tier-agnostic.
  • **`target_tier`** — the ambition (`flagship` / `field` / `subfield`), which drives the extra gates (verbatim-quote rule, F4b strict-delta, F4c kernel-shape, F5 adversarial-referee). A seed can be `motif: "M1"` with `target_tier: "field"`.

Each motif lists the *move* (what the paper does mathematically), *exemplars* (paper bibkeys), and the *trigger* (when to deploy it).

(M1) **Characterize-an-estimator (decompose, then optionally correct).** Move: (i) **[characterize]** take a procedure/estimator E used as if it targeted a clean estimand and write its *actual* estimand as Σ w_k · ψ_k where ψ_k are interpretable structural components and w_k are design-computable weights — the weight map itself is a named focal object; (ii) **[diagnose]** characterize the regime in which E's estimand departs from the target — some w_k < 0, Δ := E − target ≠ 0, or pin *exactly* when E is unbiased / when a w_k flips sign — naming the heterogeneity / staggering / shape condition that drives it; (iii) **[correct — optional, strongest step]** propose a corrected estimator E* targeting Σ π_k · ψ_k with π_k ≥ 0 (or Δ* = 0). Exemplars — characterization-only: Aronow–Samii (AJPS'16) regression weights; Słoczyński (ReStat'22) OLS estimand under heterogeneity; Angrist (Ecta'98) 2SLS as a LATE-weighted-average. Exemplars — decompose-then-correct: Goodman-Bacon (JoE'21) for staggered TWFE; Sun-Abraham (JoE'21) for event-study coefficients; Borusyak-Jaravel-Spiess (ReStud'24) for imputation; de Chaisemartin-D'Haultfœuille (AER'20) DID_M. Trigger: the literature uses E as if it estimated a clean target AND you can write its actual estimand explicitly; add the correction step when E is a contested / recently-criticized estimator you can decompose, otherwise stop at the characterization. A pure characterization without E* is a legitimate M1 seed on its own — the weight map is the named focal object; E* is the optional stronger extension.

(M2) **Population equivalence with a derived divergence frontier.** Move: prove two named estimators are asymptotically equivalent at the population truth (same influence function / efficiency bound / identified set), AND derive the frontier where the equivalence breaks — a closed-form threshold T(n, p), a rate / scaling condition, or an identification-strength margin — on one side of which the estimators disagree (in distribution or in probability limit) and on the other coincide up to o_p(1). The frontier MUST be a new mathematical object expressible in observed-data primitives, not a re-labelling, and it must be *derived*, not asserted: before drafting, check the substrate plausibly admits one — an equivalence whose frontier cannot be exhibited caps at field (axis (b) needs both directions). Exemplars: Plagborg-Møller-Wolf (Ecta'21) LP↔VAR (the population-equivalence half; the divergence frontier is the open content); Chernozhukov-Newey-Singh (Ecta'22) debiased-vs-plug-in. Trigger: two named published estimators target the same estimand but the literature has not pinned the exact regime where they diverge.

(M3) **Sensitivity to assumption violation with critical-δ.** Move: parametrize a violation of a load-bearing identifying assumption (parallel trends, monotonicity, exclusion) by δ; deliver closed-form / algorithmic bounds L(δ), U(δ); define a critical δ* as a math object — the smallest δ at which a sign flips, an estimator ranking inverts, 0 enters the bound, or a named identifying claim collapses. The critical-δ is a math-content requirement (a named scalar the theorem singles out), not an empirical-validation one; CausalSmith does not compute δ* on real data. Exemplars: Rambachan-Roth (ReStud'23) parallel-trends sensitivity; Adão-Kolesár-Morales (QJE'19) exposure inference; Mogstad-Santos-Torgovitsky (Ecta'18) PRTE under partial monotonicity. Trigger: the named assumption is well-published but the literature has only qualitative robustness results, not a critical-δ.

(M4) **Convex set of nuisance ⇒ LP / extreme-point characterization of the identified set.** Move: characterize the identified set Θ_I(P) as the image of a convex set of nuisance objects (MTR, MTS, response surfaces) under a linear functional; provide a concrete computational or closed-form solution method, preferably with inference theory. Exemplars: Mogstad-Santos-Torgovitsky (Ecta'18) MTR-LP; Kline-Tartari (AER'16) bounds in moral-hazard; Russell (JBES'21); Bhattacharya-Vogt. Trigger: the published bounds in the area are stated abstractly (inf/sup over a function class) without an LP-form. **Important: M4 is ONE motif among many for PartialID — do NOT default to it.** When the bound has natural copula / Fréchet–Hoeffding structure, an extremal-coupling representation, a support-function characterization, or a closed-form CDF/quantile envelope, M10 is the stronger choice. See the pairing rule after M11 before settling on identification-only.

(M5) **Spectral / matrix-factorization reduction for panel.** Move: cast the panel target as a function of the spectrum / nuclear-norm / row-span of the observed matrix Y; characterize identification via rank, eigenvalue gap, or factor-loading conditions; reduce estimation to nuclear-norm penalization / PCA / matrix completion with a named rate. Exemplars: Arkhangelsky et al. (AER'21) synthetic DiD; Bai-Ng factor models; Athey-Bayati-Doudchenko-Imbens-Khosravi (JASA'21) matrix completion; Moon-Weidner panel-spectral. Trigger: the panel has cross-sectional and time dimension both growing, with low-rank structure plausibly available.

(M6) **Necessary AND sufficient identification.** Move: identify the *exact* condition C such that the parameter θ is identified from p(observables) IFF C holds; deliver both directions (sufficiency by construction, necessity by counterexample on the boundary of C). Exemplars: Chamberlain (Ecta'10) panel binary response — nonzero information iff logistic; Bonhomme-Manresa (Ecta'15) group fixed effects; Honoré-Powell. Trigger: the published literature has only sufficient conditions, and the necessity side is open or only conjectured.

(M7) **Generic-class sharpness over a non-degenerate region of laws.** Move: prove the bound / impossibility / non-equivalence holds over a *region* of the model class M — not at one hand-picked law (that single-instance witness is the witness-trivial failure). Caveat: "region" can NOT mean "an open set in the space of all laws" — the law simplex is connected and weak\*-compact, and identifying assumptions already cut M down to a closed, lower-dimensional set, so no proper subset is open there. Instead state the region in one of three field-standard forms, and say which you use:
  (i) **strict regularity condition** — 0 < e(x) < 1, full rank, completeness, density bounded below, or a strict moment inequality g(P) > 0. Each is *relatively* open in M, which is the real content of "robust to perturbation".
  (ii) **generic / a.e.** — holds outside a named exceptional set.
  (iii) **uniform-over-a-class** — class closed/compact; the inference-side cousin (bounds ≥ c, moments ≤ M, for uniform coverage/rate control).
Exemplars: Manski monographs; Mogstad-Torgovitsky; Russell. Trigger: a published impossibility / sharpness claim is stated at one instance and you suspect it generalizes.

(M8) **Estimation–inference rung (a modifier, never a standalone shape).** Move: attach to a shape motif the estimation layer as a contribution in its own right: identifying functional → orthogonal moment / closed-form / LP estimator → conjectured rate / asymptotic-normality / uniform-in-DGP inference. Exemplars: Chernozhukov et al. DML (Ectj'18); Adão-Kolesár-Morales (QJE'19); Arkhangelsky et al. (AER'21). Trigger: the primary motif delivers the object (weight map, critical-δ, identified set, representation) and estimating it / doing inference on it is itself open. Label as `"<shape>+M8"` — a bare `motif: "M8"` is a labeling error: name the shape of the underlying claim, and if the rate / efficiency / limit law IS the kernel, that is M11, not M8.

(M9) **Bunching / kink / notch identification.** Move: use a discontinuity in the choice set (kink in budget, notch in eligibility) to identify a structural elasticity or response parameter non-parametrically; characterize identification limits via support / smoothness restrictions on the counterfactual density. Exemplars: Saez (AEJ'10); Blomquist-Newey-Kumar-Liang (JPE'21); Bertanha-McCallum-Seegert (JoE'23). Trigger: the topic involves choice-set discontinuities and the prior literature is parametric.

(M10) **Non-LP sharp partial-identification representation.** Move: characterize Θ_I(P) via a non-LP substrate — copula / Fréchet–Hoeffding bounds with named coupling; capacity / random-set kernel with explicit Aumann / Choquet integral; support-function with closed-form dual; optimal-transport extremal coupling; CDF / quantile envelope with explicit transformation; or completeness / Tikhonov inverse for a bridge-function class. The deliverable is a non-LP sharp object the reader can evaluate. Exemplars: Tchen / Fan–Park (copula bounds); Beresteanu–Molchanov–Molinari (random-set sharp regions); Galichon (optimal-transport partial ID); Miao–Geng–Tchetgen-Tchetgen (proximal completeness); Newey–Powell (nonparametric IV). Trigger: the substrate is naturally non-LP (joint-distribution / coupling / capacity / bridge), OR the published prior art is LP-dominated and a different formalism would itself be a contribution. See the pairing rule after M11 before settling on representation-only.

(M11) **Estimation / inference frontier for an identified estimand.** For a point-identified causal estimand (identification GIVEN, not the contribution), derive the estimation-and-inference theory as a named function of a structural regime parameter (overlap decay, smoothness, instrument strength, tail index). The regime parameter and the rate / efficiency / limit object are the named focal objects. M11 has THREE co-equal sub-shapes — display order is not priority; evaluate the least-represented credible lane first and pick the one the substrate actually supports, rather than defaulting to (a):
    (a) **matched rate** — an upper bound via an explicit estimator AND a matching minimax lower bound (two-point / Le Cam / Fano / Assouad reduction). Strongest when it closes, but a sharper-than-published rate or the FIRST nontrivial bound for a named method is a genuine M11 contribution on its own — a missing converse is NOT disqualifying;
    (b) **efficiency bound** — a semiparametric efficiency bound / influence-function characterization, paired with an attaining estimator (state which direction you deliver);
    (c) **limit law** — a CLT / non-Gaussian limit / uniform-in-DGP distributional result for an explicit estimator (the limit itself is the headline; no converse required).
Exemplars — (a): Kennedy–Balakrishnan–Wasserman (AoS'24) minimax CATE rates, Khan–Tamer (Ecta'10) irregular identification; (b): Hahn (Ecta'98) ATE efficiency bound, Chernozhukov et al. (Ectj'18) DML; (c): sample-quantile Bahadur limits, debiased-ML normality. Trigger: the estimand is identified and the open content is its rate / efficiency / limit as a function of a named regime parameter — especially when one side (upper or lower) or one half (bound vs. attainment) is published without its match. This is the Stat-cluster native shape.

(M12) **Design-based limit law + coverage (finite-population randomization inference, design GIVEN).** Move: for a CAUSAL finite-population estimand θ (a fixed contrast of fixed potential outcomes — direct / indirect / total / overall / EATE) under a KNOWN randomization design (Bernoulli / complete / two-stage / cluster / saturation), construct an EXPLICIT design-based estimator (Horvitz–Thompson / Hájek / difference-in-means) and DERIVE its asymptotic normality from PRIMITIVE conditions on the design and the exposure dependency structure (bounded-degree / negligible-max-influence dependency-graph Stein, Lindeberg–Feller, or martingale CLT under interference) — randomness is the assignment alone, potential outcomes are FIXED (NOT a superpopulation draw); pair the CLT with a Wald-coverage statement (oracle design-variance coverage suffices, a consistent / conservative variance estimator is a separate follow-on). Named focal objects: the design, its primitive dependency condition, the limit law. Assuming asymptotic normality instead of deriving it is disqualifying. Exemplars: Aronow–Samii (AoAS'17) exposure-mapping inference; Hudgens–Halloran (JASA'08) two-stage interference effects; Leung (ReStat'22) network-cluster CLT; Sävje–Aronow–Hudgens (AoS'21) unknown-interference estimands. Trigger: the estimand is a finite-population causal contrast under a known design and the open content is the limit law / coverage under an interference / dependency structure. This is an Experimentation-cluster native shape. (Distinct from M11: M11 is superpopulation estimation/inference (rate, efficiency, or limit law) with the randomness in the sampling draw; M12 is design-randomness — potential outcomes fixed, randomness in the assignment alone — with a dependency-CLT and Wald coverage.)

(M13) **Optimal / efficient experimental design (design CHOSEN).** Move: treat the DESIGN itself — assignment distribution, treatment-allocation fractions, cluster / saturation structure, stratification, network partition / clustering (chosen to trade off interference bias against variance), sequential / adaptive rule — as the DECISION VARIABLE, and characterize the design that OPTIMIZES a stated criterion (minimax / Bayes variance of a design-based estimator, asymptotic efficiency, power, or regret) for a CAUSAL estimand, subject to feasibility constraints (budget, exposure-positivity, interference). The optimization may be combinatorial (min-cut / graph-partition), solved via a convex (SDP) relaxation with rounding. The deliverable is the optimal design PLUS an optimality CERTIFICATE — a first-order / KKT condition, a matching lower bound on achievable variance, a saddle-point, an OBSERVABLE approximation-ratio certificate (a data-computed bound on the suboptimality gap of a relaxed / rounded design), or a minimax-regret rule for a penalty / tuning scalar that is not observable ex ante — not a candidate design merely asserted to be good. Named focal objects: the design map and its certificate. Exemplars: Neyman optimal allocation; Baird–Bohren–McIntosh–Özler (ReStat'18) optimal saturation under interference; Hahn–Hirano–Karlan (JBES'11) adaptive two-stage design; Kasy (ReStud'16) optimal nonrandom design; Tabord-Meehan (ReStud'23) stratification trees; Viviano–Lei–Imbens–Karrer–Ye–Bakshy (causal clustering — size-penalized min-cut / SDP choice of the cluster partition under network interference, with an observable rounding-gap certificate). Trigger: the open content is WHICH design to run (or the best efficiency / power a feasible design attains) rather than inference given a fixed design. This is an Experimentation-cluster native shape.

(M14) **Adaptive / sequential experiment inference.** Move: the assignment probabilities are updated DURING the experiment from accruing data (multi-armed bandit, response-adaptive, batched, or sequential allocation), so the sample is NOT i.i.d. and the ordinary design-based CLT fails; construct an estimator that restores a valid limit — adaptive / variance-stabilizing weights (e.g. propensity-stabilized AIPW) or a studentization that neutralizes the adaptivity — and DERIVE its asymptotic normality from a MARTINGALE CLT under the adaptively-generated filtration (primitive conditions: a stabilizing-weight / clipping condition, a moment / Lindeberg condition on the adaptive increments), with a Wald-coverage statement following. Named focal objects: the adaptive allocation rule, the stabilized estimator, the martingale limit. When the adaptive rule must serve BOTH low regret and valid inference, the focal object can instead be the regret–inference TRADE-OFF FRONTIER — a PRODUCT lower bound (regret × estimation-error ≳ a rate in the effective arm count) via a Le Cam two-point reduction, matched by an exploration-floored allocation (e.g. MAD-mixed propensities with a vanishing uniform-exploration floor) whose IPW estimator attains it; this fuses M14 with an M11(a)-style minimax converse. Naively assuming asymptotic normality of an unstabilized adaptive estimator is exactly the failure mode the result must rule out. Exemplars: Hadad–Hirshberg–Zhan–Athey–Wager (PNAS'21) adaptively-weighted CIs; Zhang–Janson–Murphy (NeurIPS'20 batched bandits; AoS'21) inference on adaptively-collected data; Bibaut et al. sequential adaptive inference; Kato et al. efficient adaptive design for the ATE; design-based bandits under network interference (regret–inference Pareto frontier with exposure-arm bandits — an M12×M14 hybrid). Trigger: the design is data-dependent / adaptive and the open content is a valid limit law + coverage despite the adaptivity. An Experimentation-cluster native shape — the design-based-inference motif (cf. M12) but with an adaptive filtration and a martingale (not dependency-graph) CLT. (Distinct from M13: M13 optimizes a FIXED design ex ante; M14 keeps the design data-dependent and fixes the resulting inference.)

(M15) **Super-population / model-based inference under network or spatial dependence (randomness from the population / graph draw, not the assignment).** Move: for a CAUSAL estimand under interference, treat the units — and possibly the network and the potential outcomes — as drawn from a SUPER-POPULATION (a graphon / random-graph model, a sampled network, or a spatial process), so the inferential randomness is the population draw, NOT a fixed-design assignment. Construct an explicit estimator and DERIVE its limit law from a WEAK-DEPENDENCE / network CLT (ψ-dependence, dependency-neighborhood, graphon U-statistic, or spatial mixing), paired with a network-robust (HAC / cluster-robust) variance estimator and a Wald-coverage statement. Named focal objects: the super-population model, its dependence / mixing condition, the limit law, the network-HAC variance. Assuming asymptotic normality instead of deriving it from the dependence condition is disqualifying. Exemplars: Leung (ReStat'22) network-dependence CLT for treatment effects; Kojevnikov–Marmer–Song (JoE'21) ψ-dependent CLT + HAC; Li–Wager (graphon causal inference); Gao–Ding (2025) design-based justification of regression under approximate-neighborhood interference; Bhadra–Schweinberger (2025) random-graph + random-outcome GMRF spillover effects. Trigger: the estimand is a causal contrast under interference but the uncertainty is super-population (sampled units / random graph / spatial process) rather than design-based, so the open content is the weak-dependence limit law + HAC coverage. An Experimentation-cluster shape. (Distinct from M11: M11's super-population randomness is i.i.d. sampling with regime params overlap / smoothness / instrument / tail; M15's is NETWORK / SPATIAL dependence requiring a weak-dependence CLT. Distinct from M12: M12 is design randomness with FIXED potential outcomes; M15 is model / sampling randomness — the two are the design-based and model-based answers to the SAME causal-interference question, and a paper that proves both routes agree (or diverge) is an M12×M15 bridge.)

(M16) **Statistical treatment-choice / policy-learning regret.** Move: for a causal welfare or policy-value functional identified by randomized, observational, IV, panel, or partial-ID assumptions, choose a policy π̂ in a constrained class Π by empirical welfare maximization, doubly robust score maximization, or a penalized / oracle variant, and prove welfare regret relative to the best feasible policy in Π. Strong versions give an upper regret bound plus a matching lower bound or oracle inequality as a function of sample size, policy-class complexity, overlap / noise, and nuisance-estimation quality. The named focal objects are the policy class Π, the score / objective, and the regret frontier; accurate CATE estimation is not the target unless it is part of the construction. Exemplars: Kitagawa–Tetenov (Ecta'18) empirical welfare maximization; Athey–Wager (Ecta'21) doubly robust policy learning from observational data. Trigger: the open content is WHICH treatment-assignment rule to deploy under feasibility constraints, and the theorem's loss is welfare regret rather than estimation error for a fixed estimand. (Distinct from M11: M11 estimates / infers a fixed causal estimand; M16 chooses a data-dependent decision rule and compares its value to an oracle policy in Π.)

(M17) **Symmetry / conformal / permutation inversion for counterfactuals.** Move: recast a causal counterfactual null as a missing-potential-outcome, placebo, or structural-break prediction problem; choose a conformity score or test statistic; generate the relevant symmetry orbit from exchangeability, block exchangeability, placebo units, time permutations, or a known randomization group; and invert the resulting p-values to obtain a confidence set for the causal effect or counterfactual path. Prove exact finite-sample coverage under exact symmetry; when symmetry is approximate, prove the explicit approximation error under weak dependence or misspecification. Named focal objects: the group action / permutation scheme, the conformity score, the inverted confidence set, and the coverage theorem. Exemplars: Chernozhukov–Wüthrich–Zhu (JASA'21) conformal inference for counterfactual and synthetic controls. Trigger: the open content is robust finite-sample or near-finite-sample causal inference by symmetry inversion, not an asymptotic Wald interval or a new point estimator. (Distinct from M5: M5 is the panel / synthetic-control point-estimation substrate. Distinct from M11(c) and M12: M17's coverage comes from symmetry inversion, not an asymptotic sampling CLT or a fixed-design dependency CLT.)

(M18) **Structure / mechanism identification (recover the causal graph or structural coefficients, not an effect).** Move: for a system of endogenous variables with NO designated treatment / outcome, prove the causal mechanism itself — the edge set / DAG, or the structural coefficient (mixing) matrix — is a functional of the observed distribution P, unique up to the field-standard indeterminacy: exactly, up to sign-and-permutation (linear non-Gaussian / LiNGAM), or up to the Markov equivalence class (constraint-based). The identifying leverage is a distributional restriction that breaks the observational symmetry a Gaussian / merely-faithful model leaves — non-Gaussianity of the errors (independent-component / cumulant / kurtosis asymmetry), additive or independent noise (additive-noise / post-nonlinear models), equal / ordered error variances, or the Markov condition with causal faithfulness (conditional-independence pattern → CPDAG). Deliver the recovery map (the functional of P that returns the structure) AND the indeterminacy under which it is unique; when you claim the restriction is tight, supply necessity — a counterexample where a weaker restriction (e.g. Gaussian errors) collapses identification. Exemplars: Shimizu–Hoyer–Hyvärinen–Kerminen (JMLR'06) LiNGAM; Hoyer et al. (NeurIPS'08) additive-noise models; Zhang–Hyvärinen (UAI'09) post-nonlinear; Peters–Bühlmann (Biometrika'14) equal-variance identifiability; Verma–Pearl / Spirtes–Glymour–Scheines (Markov-equivalence, PC / FCI); Squires et al. (ICML'23) linear causal disentanglement. Trigger: the target is the causal STRUCTURE (which arrows / what coefficients), not an effect given the structure, and the open content is the identifying restriction + the indeterminacy it buys. This is an ExactID-cluster shape (structure-ID mode). (Distinct from M6: M6 pins the exact iff-condition for a fixed parameter / effect to be identified given the model; M18's target IS the model — the graph or the structural map — recovered up to a structural indeterminacy. Distinct from the effect-ID default: there is no estimand and no adjustment functional — the "identifying functional" is the recovery map.)

(M19) **Graphical effect identification under assumptions.** Move: for a causal EFFECT — an interventional distribution P(y | do(x)), a counterfactual (Layer-3) query, or a path-specific effect — stated over a DAG / ADMG / selection diagram and NOT identified by classical backdoor / frontdoor / do-calculus alone, prove it becomes POINT-identified once an added assumption is imposed, and deliver the explicit identifying functional Q = f(P_obs) with its derivation (do / σ-calculus or an SCM-level argument). The assumption family is OPEN — a structural constraint (monotonicity / shape / parametric), a harder query handled by a novel argument, auxiliary structure (proxies / negative controls, selection nodes, extra interventional inputs), or a GENUINELY NOVEL assumption class the result introduces (bridge-function / completeness conditions on a proxy pair). Label the assumption as the load-bearing leverage; completeness is NOT required but PREFERRED. Exemplars: Counterfactual Identification Under Monotonicity Constraints; proximal / proxy ID (Miao–Geng–Tchetgen; Shpitser–Wood-Doughty–Tchetgen Proximal-ID); gID with surrogate experiments (Lee–Correa–Bareinboim); σ-calculus soft interventions (Correa–Bareinboim); transportability / selection-diagram ID. Trigger: a graph-native effect, non-ID classically but ID under a stated (possibly novel) assumption; the open content is the assumption + the identifying functional. An SCM-cluster shape. (Distinct from M18: M18 recovers the STRUCTURE; M19 identifies an EFFECT given the structure plus an extra assumption. Distinct from the exactid do-calculus default: M19's leverage is a NEW assumption class, not a classical adjustment.)

(M20) **Graphical partial identification / bounds.** Move: for a graph-native causal query NOT point-identified even under the model's assumptions, deliver a PRACTICAL, VALID bound Q ∈ [L(P_obs), U(P_obs)] with SOUNDNESS proved over the compatible-SCM class (the canonical / response-function reduction makes "every SCM compatible with G and P_obs" a finite condition), plus a method to compute / estimate it. The bound must be non-trivial (strictly inside the logical bounds). SHARPNESS is NOT required but PREFERRED — back it, when claimed, with compatible SCMs attaining each endpoint. Includes DATA-FUSION / selection-diagram bounds (combining observational + interventional + selected data). Exemplars: Balke–Pearl IV bounds; Zhang–Tian–Bareinboim partial counterfactual ID (canonical SCM); Duarte et al. autobounds (polynomial program — state the closed-form bound; the SOLVER is not formalized); data-fusion partial ID. Trigger: a graph-native query with only a valid bound obtainable; the open content is the bound + its soundness. An SCM-cluster shape. (Distinct from M4 / M10: those are PO-language partial-ID via LP extreme-points / proximal bridges over observed distributions; M20's compatible class and obstruction are GRAPHICAL — defined by the SCM / graph via the canonical-SCM reduction.)

When you draft a seed in Step 0b, label it with the motif you deploy (e.g. `motif: "M1"`, `"M3+M6"` for sensitivity with iff, `"M10"` for non-LP sharp / proximal / bridge, `"M11"` for rate-as-kernel, `"M16"` for policy-regret kernels, `"M17"` for conformal / permutation inversion). Seeds that map to no motif use `motif: "none"` (not blocked, but a `none` seed aiming for flagship is unlikely to clear the floor — score it honestly). Multi-motif seeds are encouraged (M1 + M8 = decompose-then-correct with estimation–inference; M6 + M10 = iff sharp non-LP representation; M3 + M10 = proximal-bridge sensitivity with critical-δ; M11 + M7 = rate frontier uniform over a class; M12 + M14 = design-based interference inference under an adaptive exposure-arm allocation; M12 + M15 = design-based vs super-population agreement/divergence under interference; M16 + M11 = policy-regret frontier with a minimax lower bound; M17 + M5 = conformal inference for a synthetic-control counterfactual path). **Pairing rule (M4 and M10 alike):** an identification-only partial-ID seed rarely travels alone to flagship — it frequently caps at subfield; the M8 rung (LP-plug-in / orthogonal-moment / sieve estimator + rate / uniform inference over the identified set) is the most common way to strengthen it, so check whether the substrate admits an estimator before settling on identification-only. **Form preference (PartialID):** among representations of the SAME bound, prefer the most explicit the substrate supports — closed-form endpoints over a variational / LP / inf-sup framing — because a closed form is analytically manipulable downstream (comparative statics, sign conditions, critical-δ, plug-in influence function). Reducing a published LP / inf-sup bound to closed form is itself a frontier advance. Tie-break, not a gate: when no closed form exists, the optimization framing is the right deliverable.

**Diversity discipline (read before picking a motif).** Past PartialID runs over-defaulted to M4 (LP / extreme-point), past ExactID runs to a do-calculus-only framing, past Panel runs to FWL, and past Stat runs to M11 sub-shape (a) (matched minimax converse) — banked Stat kernels are nearly all lower-bound/converse claims, even where the open content was really an efficiency bound (b), a limit law (c), a policy-regret frontier (M16), or finite-sample symmetry inversion (M17). For a Stat cold start, this is an enforceable generation constraint, not merely a warning: seed M11(a), M11(b), M11(c), and M16/M17 before ranking; if one lane has no credible survivor, document its named search obstruction rather than filling its slot with another M11(a) seed. A matched pair has no scoring bonus solely for being matched. And across ALL clusters the dominant over-default in banked runs is the bare `M8` label: M8 is a rung, not a shape, so a seed whose only label is M8 has not chosen a math shape (rate-as-kernel seeds are M11). None is wrong, but using them reflexively narrows the proposal space and produces near-duplicate kernels.
