# Substrate requirement: affine-polynomial-image-dimension

## Goal
Build a reusable characteristic-zero bridge from explicit polynomial-coordinate
rank/transcendence certificates to the exact affine Zariski dimension of the
closure of a polynomial image, in the finite-dimensional complex affine setting.
The study imports and builds on neutral modules under
`CausalSmith.Substrate.AffinePolynomialImageDimension`; it must not import the
LiNGAM paper namespace or its `Helpers/` path.

## Provides (API contract)
- `affineZariskiDimension_iff_coordinateRingKrullDim`: for finite `ι` and a
  nonempty affine-closed `Z : Set (ι → ℂ)`, identify the existing chain predicate
  `HasAffineZariskiDimension d Z` with Krull dimension `d` of
  `MvPolynomial ι ℂ ⧸ vanishingIdeal ℂ Z` (or an equivalent canonical statement
  that specializes without new run-specific assumptions).
- `polynomialImageClosure_coordinateRing`: identify the coordinate ring of the
  Zariski closure of the image of a finite polynomial map with the finitely
  generated coordinate subalgebra / `eval₂` image of its coordinate family.
- `polynomialImageClosure_dimension_of_trdeg`: compute exact affine Zariski
  dimension from an exact transcendence-degree certificate for that subalgebra.
- `polynomialImageClosure_dimension_of_jacobian`: a characteristic-zero lower
  bound from a nonzero Jacobian minor, combined with a supplied algebraic or
  fiber-kernel upper certificate to yield exact image-closure dimension.

## Statement / milestones
For finite coordinate types `ι` and `κ`, a polynomial family
`f : κ → MvPolynomial ι ℂ` defines `F : (ι → ℂ) → (κ → ℂ)`.  Its image closure
must have exact `HasAffineZariskiDimension d` whenever the coordinate subalgebra
generated by `f` has transcendence degree `d`.  Supply a usable route for proving
that certificate from an explicit nonzero `d × d` Jacobian minor in
characteristic zero and a matching upper bound (algebraic dependence, kernel, or
fiber-dimension certificate).  All statements must use finite ambients and must
connect to the repository's existing `HasAffineZariskiDimension` definition;
introducing an unrelated dimension notion without proving the bridge is not a
delivery.

## Standard reference
Hartshorne, *Algebraic Geometry*, Chapter I (affine varieties, coordinate rings,
dimension); Shafarevich, *Basic Algebraic Geometry 1*, affine morphisms and image
dimension; Matsumura, *Commutative Ring Theory*, Noether normalization and
dimension equals transcendence degree for finite-type domains; the
Jacobian criterion for algebraic independence in characteristic zero.

## Intended reuse
Immediate consumer:
`EID_LingamDirectionMinOrderV1_Research.exceptionalLocusCodimensionOne`, where
explicit confluent-Vandermonde Jacobian minors give ranks `D` and `D-1` and
compiled low-order weight kernels give the matching upper counts.  The bridge is
general Mathlib-adjacent substrate expected to recur for polynomial
parameterizations, algebraic statistical models, secant/incidence varieties, and
generic-identifiability dimension calculations.

## May assume / must derive
May assume existing Mathlib commutative-algebra theorems with their real names and
hypotheses.  May accept an explicit exact transcendence-degree or upper-dimension
certificate as an input to a high-level specialization lemma.  Must derive the
coordinate-ring/image-closure identification and the bridge to
`HasAffineZariskiDimension`; must not assume the desired image dimension, hide it
in a bespoke structure, or add a LiNGAM-specific premise.  Any classical theorem
absent from Mathlib must be built as the smallest general reusable lemma, with no
`sorry`, custom axioms, or unregistered gates.

## Non-goals (optional)
No LiNGAM cumulant formulas, band-support bookkeeping, confluent-Vandermonde
determinant computation, or model-specific fiber-kernel proof.  No arbitrary
schemes or infinite coordinate types beyond what the finite affine consumer
requires.  Do not replace exact dimension with only irreducibility, nonemptiness,
or a one-sided bound.

### Existing local substrate reuse map (mandatory)

The current research run originally built substantial general algebraic-
geometry helpers under
`CausalSmith/ExactID/EID_LingamDirectionMinOrderV1_Research/Helpers/`. They have
now been extracted, without changing their mathematical definitions, into the
neutral `CausalSmith.Substrate.AffinePolynomialImageDimension` module DAG:

- `ZariskiClosure.lean` and `Irreducibility.lean` define
  `affineZariskiClosure` and
  `IsIrreducibleAffineClosed`, proves closure laws,
  `irreducibleAffineClosed_iff_isPrime`, and the strict ideal/zero-locus
  antitonicity lemmas.
- `ChainDimension.lean` defines the exact chain predicate
  `HasAffineZariskiDimension`; `AffineSpaceDimension.lean` proves
  `affineSpace_hasAffineZariskiDimension` using `ringKrullDim`.
- `PolynomialMap.lean` defines `IsPolynomialMap` and proves polynomial
  substitution and preimage/image closure laws;
  `PolynomialRetractDimension.lean` proves retract irreducibility and
  `polynomialRetract_range_dimension`.
- `AffineSubspaceDimension.lean` handles polynomial affine-linear maps and exact
  affine-subspace dimension.
- `DenseImage.lean` proves dense polynomial-image closure and irreducibility
  lemmas. The general opening of `CommonAxisImageGeometry.lean` reexports these
  results, while its later declarations remain LiNGAM-specific consumers.
- `ExceptionalHeightReduction.lean` already converts the two exact dimensions
  `D-1` and `D` into the required height-one/no-intermediate certificate.

Do not create a parallel closure, irreducibility, or dimension language.  Reuse
the neutral declarations directly. The original four paper helper files are
thin compatibility reexports, so existing paper imports and qualified names
continue to work but new substrate code must not depend on them. The genuinely
missing layer is the coordinate-ring/transcendence/Jacobian theorem for general
polynomial-image closures and its compatibility theorem with the existing chain
predicate.

Final promotion to `Causalean` must copy or move only neutral substrate modules
and proofs. No promoted `Causalean` module may import or mention
`CausalSmith.ExactID.EID_LingamDirectionMinOrderV1`, its research module path, or
any paper compatibility wrapper.
