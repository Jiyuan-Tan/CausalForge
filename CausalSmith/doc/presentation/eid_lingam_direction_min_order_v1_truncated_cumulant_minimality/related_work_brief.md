This paper belongs to the causal-discovery line that asks when observational distributions contain enough asymmetry to orient causal structure. The general language is structural causal modeling and graphical identification, with `Pearl2009`, `SpirtesGlymourScheines2001`, `PetersJanzingScholkopf2017`, and `RichardsonSpirtes2002` providing the canonical DAG, SCM, and latent-variable graphical background. The specific bivariate direction problem is adjacent to the additive-noise and post-nonlinear literatures, where `HoyerJanzingMooijPetersScholkopf2009`, `ZhangHyvarinen2009`, and the benchmark study `MooijPetersJanzingZscheischlerScholkopf2016` establish that functional asymmetries can distinguish cause from effect under restrictions stronger than ordinary Markov equivalence.

The methodological core is linear non-Gaussian causal discovery. ICA identifiability and higher-order contrast theory enter through `Comon1994`, `HyvarinenOja2000`, `HyvarinenKarhunenOja2001`, `Cardoso1999`, and the Darmois--Skitovich characterization tradition represented by `KaganLinnikRao1973`. `ShimizuHyvarinenKanoHoyer2006` introduced LiNGAM as a causal-discovery use of ICA, and `ShimizuInazumiSogawaEtAl2011` gave a direct ordering method under causal sufficiency. Hidden confounding changes the problem sharply: `HoyerShimizuKerminenPalviainen2008` moved LiNGAM toward hidden variables, `TashiroShimizuHyvarinenWashio2014` and `MaedaShimizu2020` developed robust or repetitive ordering procedures, `SalehkaleybarGhassamiKiyavashZhang2020` characterized learnable causal information with latent variables, and `Genin2021StatisticalUndecidability` clarified that non-Gaussianity alone does not remove all statistical non-identifiability.

The directly related frontier uses cumulants and tensor structure to recover latent-variable LiNGAM information. `Brillinger1969` and `McCullagh1987` supply the cumulant/tensor-statistics background, while `KoldaBader2009`, `AnandkumarGeHsuKakadeTelgarsky2014`, `ComonMourrain1996`, `BernardiBrachatComonMourrain2013`, `Landsberg2012`, and `Sylvester1851` frame moment-tensor and binary-form decompositions. In causal discovery, `CaiHuangChenEtAl2023` and `ChenHuangCaiEtAl2024` use higher-order cumulants to identify latent-confounded structures in special configurations; `SchkodaRobevaDrton2024` gives recursive higher-cumulant rank conditions for unobserved confounding; `TramontanoKivvaSalehkaleybarDrtonKiyavash2024` and `TramontanoKivvaSalehkaleybarDrtonKiyavash2025` shift toward causal-effect identification from cumulants; and `XieHuangChenEtAl2023`, `MorinishiShimizu2025`, and `ChenGuPengEtAl2025` represent nearby GIN, differentiable, and federated variants.

The immediate comparator is `ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction`, which proves a causal-direction test under an arbitrary number of latent confounders by an order-`2m+3` higher-cumulant rank-deficiency construction. The proposed contribution is narrower and more algebraic: it does not claim a new coefficient-identification theorem, but argues that the arrow itself is generically identifiable from the truncated joint cumulant tensor at order `2m+2`, with the remaining failures exactly described by a codimension-`m` exceptional variety. The newest related work, `CaiGaoHara2025Direction` and `CaiHara2026SparsestLvLiNGAM`, confirms that higher-order cumulant methods remain an active frontier for latent LiNGAM, while `MestersZwiernik2022` and `Virolainen2024` indicate a parallel econometric/statistical strand using non-Gaussianity and higher-order structure for identification beyond classical Gaussian restrictions.
