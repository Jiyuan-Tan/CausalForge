# Revision routing plan (major_revision)

## rewind P2
- [major·prose·rewrite] (abstract, introduction, and discussion) The causal-discovery framing remains stronger than the delivered theorem scope. Sentences such as “the results above generically identify causal direction from truncated population cumulants in the stated axis-normalized latent-source model” can be read as law-level generic causal identification, whereas the verified results separate axis-normalized representation fibers of polynomial cumulant maps and explicitly do not provide a global law-space genericity theorem.
- [major·prose·rewrite] (discussion and global) Several passages still make the source assumptions sound like hypotheses of the four main theorems rather than part of the probabilistic interpretation and real-feasibility/law-class definitions. For example, the setup says the probabilistic assumptions are “the standard restrictions behind latent-variable LiNGAM identification,” but the verified main theorems are algebraic statements over parameter spaces with feasibility clauses only where stated.
- [minor·structure·rewrite] (main results) Theorems “Generic apolar arrow recovery” and “Generic Fiber Obstruction” substantially duplicate generic loci, real-incidence, support-recovery, and opposite-fiber-empty claims. This obscures the main theorem hierarchy.
- [minor·prose·rewrite] (main results and appendix: apolar constructions) The “Open neighborhoods meeting feasibility” clause is easy to overread. It gives Euclidean-open O with nonempty intersection with F and inclusion after complexification, not an explicit interior characterization of F and not a law-level open set.
- [minor·prose·rewrite] (setup and assumptions) The model terminology still invites confusion between “middle latent-source slots” and actual shared latent confounders. Although the text qualifies zero slopes, the motivation and related-work comparison repeatedly use latent-confounder language.
- [minor·prose·rewrite] (appendix: proofs of the main results) The deferred proofs contain implementation-facing phrases such as “retained-band witness,” “truncated-moment interior theorem,” “height-one certificates,” and named Lean-style lemmas without enough reader-level explanation or citations.
- [nit·prose·rewrite] (definitions and notation) There are presentation blemishes and notation inconsistencies, e.g. “\mathbb C^{,m+1+(m+2)(L-1)}” and doubled commas in coordinate ranges.

## escalate — out of causalsmith scope (bank/causalsmith)
- [major·prose·rewrite] (contribution and positioning) The paper does not yet make a compelling econometric case for why this representation-level algebraic result is important absent an estimator, a testing procedure, robustness analysis, or a law-space identification theorem. The manuscript says what it does not do, but it does not sufficiently explain who can use the result and for what inferential target.
- [major·other·implementation] (verification note and proofs of the main results) The reproducibility standard for the Lean claim is still not met by the manuscript text alone. The verification note says the statements and proofs are machine-checked, but a referee cannot inspect the archive, build command, dependency lockfile, declaration mapping, or certificate files from the submission as presented.
- [major·citation·citation_research] (introduction and positioning) The comparison with ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction and other cumulant LiNGAM work is still too high-level to substantiate novelty. The table lists order and scope but does not compare assumptions, whether m is known, whether confounders must be shared, whether conclusions are over laws or parameterized cumulant maps, and whether the result is real or complex generic.
