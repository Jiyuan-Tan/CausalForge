# Referee review

**Recommendation:** major_revision
**Overall score:** 5.8/10 — The verified mathematical core appears correct and carefully scoped, but the manuscript as written is too narrow, hard to interpret economically, and insufficiently positioned to merit publication in a leading econometrics journal without substantial revision.

The paper proves representation-level generic separation results for two fixed-slot, axis-normalized bivariate latent LiNGAM cumulant maps, including support recovery at order 2m+2, codimension-one opposite-arrow compatibility, and real generic separation at order 2m+1 for m>=3. The formal claims are unusually explicit and the prose mostly avoids overclaiming causal or statistical identification. The main weakness is that the econometric payoff remains underdeveloped: the paper establishes an algebraic population fact, but does not yet show why this object should matter enough for the journal's audience or how it relates precisely to existing cumulant-based latent LiNGAM results.

## Strengths
- The manuscript is commendably clear that the results are representation-level fiber-exclusion statements, not estimators, tests, selectors of m, or law-space causal-identification theorems.
- The verified theorem statements support the main mathematical claims in the abstract and introduction, including codimension-one compatibility and lower real information order for m>=3.
- The paper usefully separates support recovery, opposite-fiber exclusion, same-arrow nonuniqueness, and real feasible-region interpretation.
- The discussion of the exceptional locus is honest and prevents the generic result from being oversold.

## Findings
- **[major·prose] contribution and positioning** — The paper still does not make a compelling case that the result is important for econometrics rather than primarily algebraic statistics. The introduction says the target is useful when "an institutional model, factor design, or separate model-selection analysis fixes the number of common shock slots," but it gives no concrete econometric design, empirical model, or identification workflow where excluding the opposite fixed-slot cumulant fiber changes an economic conclusion.
  - *Fix:* Add a concrete motivating subsection with one fully specified econometric scenario: what fixes m, what the two axis-normalized conventions represent, what population object is observed, and exactly what conclusion changes when the opposite fiber is empty. If no such scenario can be given, reframe the paper for an algebraic-statistics or econometric-theory niche and lower the claimed econometric importance.
- **[major·citation] introduction and related work** — The novelty comparison remains too qualitative. The paper repeatedly contrasts order 2m+2 or 2m+1 with the order-2m+3 result of ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction, but the reader still cannot tell whether the order difference is due to a genuine information gain, the maintained axis normalization, the source-count convention, the definition of confounding, or the change from a test to a complex/real fiber-exclusion target.
  - *Fix:* Replace the current comparison with a precise assumption-by-assumption mapping: define the m in each paper, state whether each source slot is a confounder, specify the normalization, state the population object, and state what conclusion is and is not implied across the two frameworks. The table should include a row explaining why the orders are not directly comparable.
- **[major·prose] proofs of the main results** — The deferred proofs still read partly like implementation notes rather than mathematical exposition. Phrases such as "retained-coordinate parameter spaces," "truncated coordinate chart," "determinant polynomial," and "moment-interior nonvanishing statement" are used as if they were already familiar mathematical lemmas, but the paper does not state the exact external propositions being invoked in conventional theorem/lemma form.
  - *Fix:* Rewrite the proof appendix as a mathematical proof outline with named auxiliary lemmas: apolar kernel lemma, real cumulant feasibility/interior lemma, Jacobian dimension lemma, and lower-order contraction-rank lemma. State each lemma with hypotheses before using it, even if the formal verification contract contains the implementation-level detail.
- **[minor·structure] main results** — The same-arrow corollary is better separated than before, but Theorem generic-arrow-recovery-and-fiber-obstruction still partly duplicates the apolar theorem's support-recovery language. This makes the novel same-arrow nonuniqueness and fiber-dimension content harder to isolate.
  - *Fix:* Shorten Theorem generic-arrow-recovery-and-fiber-obstruction to only the additional consequences: same-arrow multiset preservation, direct-middle swap nonidentification, and fiber dimension. Move all genericity and opposite-fiber material to a reference back to Theorem generic-apolar-arrow-recovery.
- **[minor·prose] setup and related work** — The manuscript is mostly careful about "middle source slots," but the surrounding related-work discussion still leans on latent-confounder terminology in a way that may blur the formal model. For example, the introduction says the work is closest to the "latent LiNGAM literature" and compares to latent-confounder constructions, while the maintained middle slots need not have nonzero loadings on both coordinates.
  - *Fix:* Add one explicit paragraph in the related-work section saying that the paper's middle slots are not equivalent to latent confounders, and that comparisons to latent-confounder LiNGAM papers are by cumulant geometry rather than by identical graphical semantics.
- **[minor·prose] setup and assumptions** — The separation between complex algebraic maps and probabilistic feasible regions is much improved, but the paper still risks letting readers conflate the two because the probabilistic assumptions precede several algebraic definitions and are repeatedly described as "assumptions" for the model.
  - *Fix:* Add a short boxed or italicized convention after Section 2.1: the theorems are first about complex polynomial maps with free cumulant weights; source-law assumptions enter only through the real feasible-region clauses. Then cross-reference this convention at each theorem that has a real-incidence clause.
- **[nit·prose] definitions and notation** — There are small LaTeX and notation blemishes, including malformed parameter-space displays in the contract/source such as "\mathbb C^{,m+1+(m+2)(L-1)}" and double commas in coordinate subscripts. Even if these are not in the compiled version, they undermine polish in a notation-heavy paper.
  - *Fix:* Clean the LaTeX source for the parameter-space displays and coordinate-index lists; run a source-level check for stray commas after exponent braces and in subscript ranges.

## Questions for authors
- Can you give a concrete econometric example in which m and the axis normalization are externally fixed before seeing the cumulants, and in which opposite-fiber exclusion changes the substantive conclusion?
- Which part of the lower order relative to ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction is due to the narrower target rather than sharper use of cumulant information under the same statistical model?
- Are the external algebraic inputs used in the proof appendix intended to be accepted as standard theorems, or are any of them paper-specific computations that should be reproducible outside the Lean contract?
