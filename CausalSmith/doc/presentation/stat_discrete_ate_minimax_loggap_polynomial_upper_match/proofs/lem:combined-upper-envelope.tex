\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:combined-upper-envelope}]
Fix \(n,d\) satisfying the displayed hypotheses, and abbreviate
\[
a=\frac1n,\qquad
u=\frac{d^2}{n^2(\log n)^2},\qquad
v=\left(\frac12-\epsilon\right)^2,\qquad
K_\epsilon=\max\{C_\epsilon,4\}.
\]
First, the centered estimator satisfies
\[
\sup_{P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}}
E_{P^{\otimes n}}\!\left[(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}-\tau(P))^2\right]
\le a+4v.
\]
Indeed, if the class is empty this is immediate, while otherwise it follows pointwise from \cref{obj:lem:near-randomization-linear-upper} and then by taking the supremum.

By the assumed hybrid bound, the hybrid estimator has worst-case risk at most \(C_\epsilon(a+u)\). The selected estimator is the hybrid estimator exactly when \(C_\epsilon(a+u)\le a+4v\), and is the centered estimator otherwise. Therefore, in the two cases respectively,
\[
\sup_{\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}}
E\!\left[(\widehat\tau^{\mathrm{sel}}_{C_\epsilon,\epsilon}-\tau(P))^2\right]
\le
\min\{C_\epsilon(a+u),\,a+4v\}.
\]


The same selected estimator is a measurable candidate in \cref{obj:def:minimax-risk}, so the minimax risk is no larger than its worst-case risk; this is just the infimum comparison over measurable estimators.

It remains to compare the last minimum with the asserted envelope. Since \(C_\epsilon\le K_\epsilon\), \(4\le K_\epsilon\), and \(a,u,v\ge0\), if \(u\le v\), then
\[
\min\{C_\epsilon(a+u),a+4v\}
\le C_\epsilon(a+u)
\le K_\epsilon(a+u)
=
K_\epsilon\{a+\min(u,v)\}.
\]
If \(v\le u\), then
\[
\min\{C_\epsilon(a+u),a+4v\}
\le a+4v
\le K_\epsilon(a+v)
=
K_\epsilon\{a+\min(u,v)\}.
\]
Substituting back the definitions of \(a,u,v\) gives the claimed bound for \(\widehat\tau^{\mathrm{sel}}_{C_\epsilon,\epsilon}\).

\end{proof}
