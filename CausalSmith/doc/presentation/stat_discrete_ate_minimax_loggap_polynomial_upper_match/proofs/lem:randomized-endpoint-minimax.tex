\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:randomized-endpoint-minimax}]
The lower bound is exactly \cref{obj:lem:one-category-bernoulli-lower}.

For the upper bound, take the centered estimator \(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}\) as a measurable candidate in \cref{obj:def:minimax-risk}. The infimum over all measurable estimators is therefore no larger than its worst-case risk:
\[
\mathsf R_{n,d,1/2}
\le
\sup_{P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,1/2}}
E_{P^{\otimes n}}\!\left[(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}-\tau(P))^2\right].
\]

It remains to bound this worst-case risk. If \(\mathcal E_{n,d,1/2}\) is empty, the supremum bound is immediate. Otherwise, for each \(P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,1/2}\), \cref{obj:lem:near-randomization-linear-upper} gives
\[
E_{P^{\otimes n}}\!\left[(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}-\tau(P))^2\right]
\le
\frac1n+4\left(\frac12-\frac12\right)^2
=
\frac1n .
\]
Taking the supremum over the class yields \(\mathsf R_{n,d,1/2}\le1/n\), which together with the lower bound proves the two-sided bracket.

\end{proof}
