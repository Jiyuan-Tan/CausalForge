\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:one-category-bernoulli-lower}]
Fix \(n,d\) with \(n>0\) and \(d>0\), and put
\[
\delta=\frac{2/5}{\sqrt n}.
\]
Then \(\delta\ge0\), \(\delta^2=4/(25n)\), and \(\delta\le 2/5\), since \(\sqrt n\ge1\). Thus both binary means \(1/2\) and \(1/2+\delta\) are valid probabilities. Consider the two product laws \(P_0^{\otimes n}\) and \(P_1^{\otimes n}\) in which \(X\) is uniform on the \(d\)-point alphabet, \(A\mid X\sim\mathsf{Bernoulli}(1/2)\), the control outcome mean is \(1/2\), and the treated outcome mean is \(1/2\) under \(P_0\) and \(1/2+\delta\) under \(P_1\). Both observed laws satisfy exact overlap with \(\epsilon=1/2\), hence both product laws belong to \(\mathcal E_{n,d,1/2}\). Their ATE values are
\[
\tau(P_0)=0,\qquad \tau(P_1)=\delta .
\]




Moreover,
\[
n\frac{(1/2)\delta^2}{(1/2)(1-1/2)}
=\frac{8}{25}
\le \log 2,
\]
so the parametric total-variation comparison gives
\[
\operatorname{TV}(P_0^{\otimes n},P_1^{\otimes n})\le \frac12 .
\]

Now let \(\widehat\tau\) be any measurable estimator. Since the two ATE values are separated by \(\delta\), the two-point testing bound with radius \(\delta/2\) and total variation at most \(1/2\) gives
\[
\max_{j=0,1}
P_j^{\otimes n}\!\left(\left|\widehat\tau-\tau(P_j)\right|\ge\frac{\delta}{2}\right)
\ge \frac14 .
\]

For each \(j=0,1\),
\[
\left(\frac{\delta}{2}\right)^2
P_j^{\otimes n}\!\left(\left|\widehat\tau-\tau(P_j)\right|\ge\frac{\delta}{2}\right)
\le
E_{P_j^{\otimes n}}\!\left[(\widehat\tau-\tau(P_j))^2\right],
\]
by integrating the squared error over the event on which it is at least \((\delta/2)^2\). Hence
\[
\max_{j=0,1}
E_{P_j^{\otimes n}}\!\left[(\widehat\tau-\tau(P_j))^2\right]
\ge
\frac{\delta^2}{16}
=
\frac{1}{100n}.
\]

Since \(P_0^{\otimes n}\) and \(P_1^{\otimes n}\) are members of \(\mathcal E_{n,d,1/2}\), the worst-case risk of this arbitrary estimator is at least \(1/(100n)\). Taking the infimum over all measurable estimators in \cref{obj:def:minimax-risk} proves the claim.

\end{proof}
