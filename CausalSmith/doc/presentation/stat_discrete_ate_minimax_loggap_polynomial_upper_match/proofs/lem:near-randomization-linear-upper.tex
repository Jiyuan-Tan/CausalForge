\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:near-randomization-linear-upper}]
Let
\[
S(O)=2(2A-1)\left(Y-\frac12\right).
\]
The estimator is the sample average \(n^{-1}\sum_{i=1}^n S(O_i)\), and \(S(O)^2=1\) for every binary observation; hence \(\operatorname{Var}_P(S)\le1\).  Under the product law in \cref{obj:def:experiment-class}, independence gives
\[
\operatorname{Var}_{\mu_n}(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}})\le \frac1n .
\]

It remains to bound the bias.  For every positive-mass category,
\[
E[S(O)\mid X=k]p_k
=
p_k(\mu_{1k}-\mu_{0k})
+2(\pi_k-\tfrac12)p_k(\mu_{1k}+\mu_{0k}-1),
\]
and zero-mass categories contribute zero.  Summing over \(k\) gives
\[
E_P S(O)-\tau(P)
=
\sum_k2(\pi_k-\tfrac12)p_k(\mu_{1k}+\mu_{0k}-1).
\]
By overlap, \(|\pi_k-\tfrac12|\le\tfrac12-\epsilon\), while \(\mu_{1k},\mu_{0k}\in[0,1]\) implies \(|\mu_{1k}+\mu_{0k}-1|\le1\), and \(\sum_kp_k=1\).  Therefore
\[
|E_P S(O)-\tau(P)|\le 2\left(\frac12-\epsilon\right).
\]
The mean of \(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}\) is \(E_P S(O)\), and the usual identity
\[
E[(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}-\tau(P))^2]
=
\operatorname{Var}(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}})
+\{E\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}-\tau(P)\}^2
\]
then yields
\[
E_{\mu_n}\!\left[(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}-\tau(P))^2\right]
\le
\frac1n+4\left(\frac12-\epsilon\right)^2 .
\]
\end{proof}
