The paper belongs first to the potential-outcomes and semiparametric causal-inference tradition. Rubin1974 formalized treatment effects through potential outcomes, while RosenbaumRubin1983 made adjustment through the propensity score central for observational studies under ignorability. Hahn1998 and HiranoImbensRidder2003 established efficiency theory for average treatment effects under unconfoundedness, and RobinsRotnitzkyZhao1994, BangRobins2005, and VanDerLaanRubin2006 provide the inverse-probability, doubly robust, and targeted-learning estimator lineages that underlie modern ATE estimation. RobinsRitov1997 is especially relevant because it directly warns that high-dimensional adjustment can create regimes where ordinary likelihood or plug-in reasoning is misleading.

A second strand is high-dimensional causal inference with structure imposed on nuisance functions. BelloniChernozhukovHansen2014 and Belloni2017 develop sparse high-dimensional program-evaluation methods, Chernozhukov2018 formulates double/debiased machine learning for treatment and structural parameters, and Chernozhukov2022 automates debiasing for regression-based causal and structural functionals. DAmour2021 studies the overlap burden in high-dimensional covariate adjustment, which is conceptually close to the present paper's bounded-overlap discrete-cell model but addresses a different geometric implication of high dimension. JinSyrgkanis2025 and Balakrishnan2023 represent the current frontier on structure-agnostic optimality of doubly robust or first-order methods; the proposed result is different because it uses the exact unrestricted finite-alphabet structure rather than only black-box nuisance rates.

The statistical core is minimax estimation of nonsmooth functionals. Bickel1993, VanDerVaart1998, and Tsybakov2009 provide the semiparametric and minimax background, while Lepski1999 and CaiLow2011 are canonical antecedents for nonsmooth functional estimation via approximation ideas. Robins2008, Robins2009, Robins2017, LiuMukherjeeNeweyRobins2017, and Kennedy2024 connect minimax functional estimation to missing-data and causal estimands through higher-order influence functions and structured smoothness assumptions. The present result sits alongside that literature but replaces smoothness or projection-kernel structure with unrestricted high-dimensional discrete confounding and a light-cell polynomial/factorial-moment construction.

The closest mathematical technology comes from large-alphabet discrete-distribution functional estimation. Fisher1943, GoodToulmin1956, and EfronThisted1976 are the classical unseen-species roots; Paninski2003 identified entropy estimation with polynomial approximation; Valiant2011, Valiant2017, Orlitsky2016, WuYang2019, JiaoVenkatHanWeissman2015, WuYang2016, HanJiaoWeissman2015, and HanJiaoWeissman2020 establish the modern minimax and moment-matching toolkit for support, entropy, distribution, and divergence functionals. ZengBalakrishnanHanKennedy2024 is the direct causal predecessor: it studies ATE estimation with high-dimensional discrete covariates, proves risk bounds for standard regression/weighting/doubly robust estimators, and gives a minimax lower bound with a logarithmic gap. The proposed paper is positioned as closing that gap, by matching the published lower-bound scale with a computable hybrid upper bound and thereby identifying the sharp consistency and parametric-rate regimes for unrestricted discrete confounding.
