\section{Main results}

The setup in \cref{obj:def:minimax-risk} reduces the statistical question to a worst-case mean-squared-error problem over finite-alphabet overlap classes. The main theorem identifies the rate in the fixed-interior case \(0<\epsilon<1/2\), for large enough \(n\) and within the calibrated growth range \(d\le \rho_\epsilon n\log n\). Polynomial approximation yields the nonparametric term \(d^2/(n^2\log^2 n)\), the same logarithmic improvement that appears in large-alphabet nonsmooth functional estimation \citep{JiaoVenkatHanWeissman2015, WuYang2016, HanJiaoWeissman2020}. The lower-bound scale is imported from the fixed-sample construction of \citet{ZengBalakrishnanHanKennedy2024}; the matching computable hybrid upper bound is the new step that closes the rate.

\begin{theoremv}{thm:sharp-minimax-fixed-interior}[Fixed-Interior Minimax Rate]*
Fix \(0<\epsilon<1/2\). Then there exist constants \(a_\epsilon,\rho_\epsilon,C_\epsilon>0\) and an integer \(N_\epsilon<\infty\) such that the following hold.
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(Sharp sandwich.)} For every \(n,d\), every single-observation law \(P\), and every sample law \(P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}\) as in \cref{obj:def:experiment-class}, if \(d>0\), \(n\ge N_\epsilon\), and
\[
d\le \rho_\epsilon n\log n,
\]
then, with \(\mathsf R_{n,d,\epsilon}\) as in \cref{obj:def:minimax-risk} and \(\widehat\tau_n^{\mathrm{hyb}}\) as in \cref{obj:def:hybrid-estimator-handle},
\[
a_\epsilon\left\{\frac1n+\frac{d^2}{n^2(\log n)^2}\right\}
\le
\mathsf R_{n,d,\epsilon}
\le
\sup_{Q^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}}
E_Q\!\left[\left(\widehat\tau_n^{\mathrm{hyb}}-\tau(Q)\right)^2\right]
\le
C_\epsilon\left\{\frac1n+\frac{d^2}{n^2(\log n)^2}\right\}.
\]
\item \textbf{(Parametric window.)} For every \(K>0\), there exists \(C_K>0\) such that, for every \(n,d\), every single-observation law \(P\), and every sample law \(P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}\), if \(d>0\), \(n\ge N_\epsilon\),
\[
d\le K\sqrt n\,\log n
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
d\le \rho_\epsilon n\log n,
\]
then
\[
\frac{a_\epsilon}{n}
\le
\mathsf R_{n,d,\epsilon}
\le
\frac{C_K}{n}.
\]
\item \textbf{(Consistency threshold.)} For any sequences \(n_j,d_j\) with \(n_j\to\infty\), eventually \(d_j>0\), and eventually
\[
d_j\le \rho_\epsilon n_j\log n_j,
\]
one has
\[
\mathsf R_{n_j,d_j,\epsilon}\to0
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\frac{d_j}{n_j\log n_j}\to0.
\]
\end{itemize}
\end{theoremv}

\cref{obj:thm:sharp-minimax-fixed-interior} gives three consequences under the displayed fixed-interior range. First, throughout \(d\le \rho_\epsilon n\log n\), the minimax risk is of exact order
\[
n^{-1}+\frac{d^2}{n^2\log^2 n}.
\]
Second, the parametric rate remains attainable when \(d=O(\sqrt n\log n)\). Third, within the same displayed range, consistent estimation is possible exactly when \(d=o(n\log n)\). Thus the logarithmic factor changes the consistency boundary as well as the constants. The constants in the theorem may depend on the fixed overlap margin, while the estimator in \cref{obj:def:hybrid-estimator-handle} uses the same formula across \(\epsilon\).

The next theorem records the hybrid computability statement, the fixed-interior upper envelope, and the separate behavior near the randomized endpoint. The hybrid part uses the same estimator for all fixed-interior overlap classes. For each fixed \(0<\epsilon<1/2\), under \(n\ge N_\epsilon\) and \(d\le \rho_\epsilon n\log n\), the selected rule in \cref{obj:def:selected-estimator} gives an oracle-calibrated deterministic comparison between that fixed-interior guarantee and the centered linear estimator from \cref{obj:def:centered-estimator}. Polynomial approximation and adaptation ideas of this form are standard in nonsmooth functional estimation \citep{CaiLow2011, Lepski1999, JiaoVenkatHanWeissman2015}, and here they are applied to the four-cell ATE contribution in \cref{obj:def:cell-functional}.

\begin{theoremv}{thm:overlap-adaptive-universal-hybrid}[Universal Hybrid Envelope]
The following conclusions hold for the hybrid estimator \(\widehat\tau_n^{\mathrm{hyb}}\) of \cref{obj:def:hybrid-estimator-handle} and the centered estimator \(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}\) of \cref{obj:def:centered-estimator}, with minimax risk \(\mathsf R_{n,d,\epsilon}\) as in \cref{obj:def:minimax-risk}.
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(Computability.)} There is a constant \(K\in\mathbb N\), \(K>0\), such that for every \(n,d\in\mathbb N\) there is a real-arithmetic program which, on the hybrid count input, returns exactly \(\widehat\tau_n^{\mathrm{hyb}}\) for every sample \(O_1,\ldots,O_n\), and whose arithmetic/comparison operation count is at most \(K\,d\,M(n)^4\).

\item \textbf{(Fixed-overlap hybrid calibration and envelope.)} For every \(0<\epsilon<1/2\), there exist constants \(C_\epsilon>0\), \(\rho_\epsilon>0\), and \(N_\epsilon\in\mathbb N\) such that, for all \(n,d\in\mathbb N\) with \(n>0\), \(d>0\), \(n\ge N_\epsilon\), and
\[
d\le \rho_\epsilon n\log n,
\]
the hybrid estimator satisfies
\[
\sup_{P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}}
E_P\!\left[(\widehat\tau_n^{\mathrm{hyb}}-\tau(P))^2\right]
\le
C_\epsilon\left\{\frac1n+\frac{d^2}{n^2(\log n)^2}\right\}.
\]
For the deterministic selected estimator obtained from the rule that chooses \(\widehat\tau_n^{\mathrm{hyb}}\) when
\[
C_\epsilon\left\{\frac1n+\frac{d^2}{n^2(\log n)^2}\right\}
\le
\frac1n+4(1/2-\epsilon)^2
\]
and otherwise chooses \(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}\), one also has
\[
\mathsf R_{n,d,\epsilon}
\le
\sup_{P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}}
E_P\!\left[(\widehat\tau^{\mathrm{sel}}_{C_\epsilon,\epsilon}-\tau(P))^2\right]
\]
and
\[
\sup_{P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}}
E_P\!\left[(\widehat\tau^{\mathrm{sel}}_{C_\epsilon,\epsilon}-\tau(P))^2\right]
\le
\max\{C_\epsilon,4\}
\left\{
\frac1n+
\min\left[
\frac{d^2}{n^2(\log n)^2},(1/2-\epsilon)^2
\right]
\right\}.
\]

\item \textbf{(Centered estimator.)} For every \(0<\epsilon\le 1/2\) and every \(n,d\in\mathbb N\) with \(n>0\) and \(d>0\),
\[
\sup_{P^{\otimes n}\in\mathcal E_{n,d,\epsilon}}
E_P\!\left[(\widehat\tau_{\mathrm{ctr}}-\tau(P))^2\right]
\le
\frac1n+4(1/2-\epsilon)^2 .
\]

\item \textbf{(Endpoint bracket.)} For every \(n,d\in\mathbb N\) with \(n>0\) and \(d>0\),
\[
\frac{1}{100n}\le \mathsf R_{n,d,1/2}\le \frac1n .
\]
\end{itemize}
\end{theoremv}

\cref{obj:thm:overlap-adaptive-universal-hybrid} separates three issues that are often conflated. The hybrid estimator is computable from the finite set of split counts, with operation count polynomial in \(M(n)\) and linear in the alphabet size. For each fixed \(0<\epsilon<1/2\), the same hybrid formula attains the fixed-overlap upper rate in \cref{obj:thm:sharp-minimax-fixed-interior}. Near \(\epsilon=1/2\), the centered estimator has the sharper envelope \(n^{-1}+4(1/2-\epsilon)^2\), and at the endpoint \(\epsilon=1/2\) the minimax risk is bracketed at the parametric order \(n^{-1}\). The deterministic selected rule uses the displayed theoretical constants as an oracle comparison device.
