\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:oracle-dose-regression-lower-all-beta}]
By the strict-slack baseline assumption, choose the baseline covariate density \(p_0\), treatment density \(q_0\), and slack \(\eta_0>0\). Set \(B=M/2\). The bump Hölder gate gives an amplitude \(\lambda>0\), with \(\lambda\le M/4=B/2\), such that every signed bump
\[
a\mapsto \zeta\,\lambda h^\alpha \psi\!\left(\frac{a-t_0}{h}\right),
\qquad \zeta\in\{-1,1\},\quad 0<h\le 1,
\]
lies in the required treatment-direction Hölder ball. % lean: doseBump_holder_gate
Let
\[
K=\frac{16\lambda^2(M-\eta_0)}{B^2},
\]
and let \(c_K>0\) be the Le Cam constant for this KL budget. Define
\[
c_{\mathrm{or}}=4c_K\lambda^2>0.
\]
% lean: le_cam_two_point_mse
For all sufficiently large \(n\), put
\[
h=n^{-1/(2\alpha+1)}.
\]
Then \(0<h\le 1\), \(h\le \varepsilon_0\), and \(n h^{2\alpha+1}=1\). % lean: oracle_dose_regression_lower_all_beta
Construct two laws \(P_0\) and \(P_1\) with the same \(p_0\) and \(q_0\), and with conditional outcome channel on \(\{-B,B\}\) having means
\[
\mu_\zeta(a,x)
=
\zeta\,\lambda h^\alpha
\psi\!\left(\frac{a-t_0}{h}\right),
\qquad \zeta=-1,1.
\]
The amplitude bound \(\lambda\le B/2\), the bump bound \(|\psi|\le 1\), and \(h\le 1\) give \(|\mu_\zeta(a,x)|\le B/2\). Together with the slack baseline and the bump Hölder gate, this proves that both \(P_0\) and \(P_1\) belong to \(\mathcal P_{\alpha,\beta,s}(M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0)\). % lean: doseWitness_mem_class
For the KL budget, condition on \((A,X)=(a,x)\). The two conditional outcome laws are the two-point mean channels with means \(\mu_{-1}(a,x)\) and \(\mu_1(a,x)\), so \cref{obj:lem:bernoulli-mean-channel-kl} gives the fibrewise quadratic bound. Integrating over the common \((A,X)\)-law and using that \(\psi((a-t_0)/h)\) is supported in \((t_0-h,t_0+h)\), where \(q_0\le M-\eta_0\), yields the one-observation estimate
\[
\mathrm{KL}(P_0,P_1)
\le
\frac{16\lambda^2(M-\eta_0)}{B^2}\,h^{2\alpha+1}.
\]
% lean: doseWitness_kl_single_le
Product tensorization therefore gives
\[
\mathrm{KL}(P_0^{\otimes n},P_1^{\otimes n})
\le
n\,\frac{16\lambda^2(M-\eta_0)}{B^2}\,h^{2\alpha+1}
=
K.
\]
% lean: doseWitness_kl_nfold_le
The functional separation is
\[
\theta_{P_1}(t_0)-\theta_{P_0}(t_0)
=
2\lambda h^\alpha,
\]
because \(\psi(0)=1\) and \(\int p_0=1\). % lean: doseWitness_theta_sep
Applying the two-point minimax reduction to the two \(n\)-sample laws, and then restricting the supremum in the minimax risk to the two class members \(P_0,P_1\), gives
\[
R_n\!\left(\mathcal P_{\alpha,\beta,s}(M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0),t_0\right)
\ge
c_K(2\lambda h^\alpha)^2.
\]
% lean: minimaxRisk_two_point_lower
Finally,
\[
c_K(2\lambda h^\alpha)^2
=
4c_K\lambda^2 h^{2\alpha}
=
c_{\mathrm{or}}\,n^{-2\alpha/(2\alpha+1)}.
\]
Thus the asserted lower bound holds for all sufficiently large \(n\). % lean: oracle_dose_regression_lower_all_beta
\end{proof}
