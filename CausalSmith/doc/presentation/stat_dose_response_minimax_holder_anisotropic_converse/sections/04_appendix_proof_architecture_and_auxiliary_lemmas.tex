\section{Appendix: proof architecture and auxiliary lemmas}

This appendix records the auxiliary statements that underlie the lower-bound argument and the rate comparisons in the main text. The purpose is organizational: the body of the paper states the econometric conclusions for the same-class Hölder dose model, while the present section isolates the elementary information bound, the two-point decision-theoretic reduction, the algebra for \(\rho_n\), and the oracle lower-bound input.

The first ingredient is a bounded two-point outcome channel. It converts a difference in conditional means into a Kullback--Leibler bound for distributions supported on a common bounded set, which is the form needed for the Le Cam comparison below.

\begin{lemmav}{lem:bernoulli-mean-channel-kl}[Bernoulli mean-channel KL bound]
Fix \(B>0\). Let \(Q_u\) and \(Q_v\) be distributions on \(\{-B,B\}\) with means \(u\) and \(v\), respectively, so that
\[
Q_u(B)=\frac{1+u/B}{2},
\qquad
Q_v(B)=\frac{1+v/B}{2}.
\]
If \(|u|\le B/2\) and \(|v|\le B/2\), then
\[
\mathrm{KL}(Q_u,Q_v)
\le
\frac{2(u-v)^2}{B^2}.
\]
\end{lemmav}

\cref{obj:lem:bernoulli-mean-channel-kl} is the local calculation that keeps the outcome part of the experiment within the common envelope. The support scale \(B\) fixes the range of the two possible outcome values, and the restriction \(|u|,|v|\le B/2\) keeps the Bernoulli probabilities away from the endpoints.

The next statement is the decision-theoretic step. It is the standard two-point Le Cam reduction for squared-error risk: a bounded Kullback--Leibler distance between two experiments prevents an estimator from being uniformly close to both corresponding parameter values \citep{tsybakov2009}.

\begin{lemmav}{lem:le-cam-two-point-mse-source}[Le Cam Two-Point Bound]
Let \(Q_0\) and \(Q_1\) be two laws for the \(n\)-sample experiment, and let \(\theta_0\) and \(\theta_1\) be the corresponding values of a real parameter. Set
\[
\Delta = |\theta_1-\theta_0|.
\]
If
\[
\mathrm{KL}(Q_0,Q_1)\le K<\infty,
\]
then there exists a constant \(c_K>0\), depending only on \(K\), such that every estimator \(T\) satisfies
\[
\max_{i=0,1} E_{Q_i}\!\left[(T-\theta_i)^2\right]\ge c_K \Delta^2 .
\]
\end{lemmav}

In the lower-bound construction, \cref{obj:lem:le-cam-two-point-mse-source} is applied after the alternatives have been embedded in the model class. The relevant separation is the difference in the dose-response partial mean, while the KL bound is controlled through the bounded channel in \cref{obj:lem:bernoulli-mean-channel-kl}.

The following two algebraic lemmas concern only the published benchmark \(\rho_n\) from \cref{obj:def:published-hoif-rate}. They identify which term in the maximum dominates on either side of the threshold \(d=4s\).

\begin{lemmav}{lem:rho-oracle-regime-algebra}[Smooth-Covariate Rate Collapse]
Let $\rho_n$ be the published HOIF benchmark of \cref{obj:def:published-hoif-rate}, evaluated at $(n,\alpha,s,d)$. Suppose that
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(Positive treatment smoothness.)} $\alpha>0$.
\item \textbf{(Positive covariate smoothness.)} $s>0$.
\item \textbf{(Smooth-covariate regime.)} $d\le 4s$.
\item \textbf{(Sample size.)} $n\ge 1$.
\end{itemize}
Then
\[
\rho_n
=
n^{-2\alpha/(2\alpha+1)} .
\]
\end{lemmav}

\cref{obj:lem:rho-oracle-regime-algebra} is the algebra used in the smooth-covariate part of the main theorem. It says that, when \(d\le 4s\), the comparison benchmark reduces to the one-dimensional treatment-regression exponent, the classical pointwise nonparametric scale \citep{stone1982,tsybakov2009}.

\begin{lemmav}{lem:rho-deficient-regime-algebra}[Deficient Regime Algebra]
For any sample size $n$, treatment smoothness $\alpha$, covariate smoothness $s$, and covariate dimension $d$, suppose that
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(Positive treatment smoothness.)} $\alpha>0$;
\item \textbf{(Positive covariate smoothness.)} $s>0$;
\item \textbf{(Deficient-covariate regime.)} $4s<d$;
\item \textbf{(Nonzero sample size.)} $n\ge 1$.
\end{itemize}
Then the published HOIF benchmark $\rho_n$ from \cref{obj:def:published-hoif-rate} satisfies
\[
\rho_n
=
n^{-2/(1+d/(4s)+1/\alpha)},
\]
and the deficient-regime exponent is strictly smaller than the oracle exponent:
\[
\frac{2}{1+d/(4s)+1/\alpha}
<
\frac{2\alpha}{2\alpha+1}.
\]
\end{lemmav}

\cref{obj:lem:rho-deficient-regime-algebra} supplies the comparison used when \(4s<d\). In that regime the benchmark is governed by the low-covariate-smoothness term, and its exponent is strictly smaller than the treatment-regression exponent appearing in the lower bound.

The next auxiliary statement is the oracle lower floor that feeds the main minimax conclusions. Its role is to package the two-point construction under the same-class restrictions from \cref{obj:def:holder-dose-class} and the slack baseline condition in \cref{obj:ass:baseline-submodel-slack}.

\begin{lemmav}{lem:oracle-dose-regression-lower-all-beta}[All-Beta Oracle Floor]
Fix a covariate dimension $d\in\mathbb N$ and constants
$\alpha,\beta,s,M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0\in\mathbb R$. Suppose that:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(Positive smoothness.)} $\alpha>0$, $\beta>0$, and $s>0$.
\item \textbf{(Regime constants.)} $M>0$, $c_0>0$, $t_0\in(0,1)$, $\varepsilon_0\in(0,1/2)$, and the window of radius $\varepsilon_0$ around $t_0$ lies in the interior dose region.
\item \textbf{(Slack baseline.)} The strict-slack baseline condition of \cref{obj:ass:baseline-submodel-slack} holds for $(d,\beta,s,M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0)$.
\end{itemize}
Then there exists a constant $c_{\mathrm{or}}>0$ such that, for all sufficiently large $n\in\mathbb N$,
\[
R_n\!\left(\mathcal P_{\alpha,\beta,s}(M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0),t_0\right)
\ge
c_{\mathrm{or}}\, n^{-2\alpha/(2\alpha+1)} .
\]
Here $\mathcal P_{\alpha,\beta,s}(M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0)$ is the model class of \cref{obj:def:holder-dose-class}, and $R_n$ is the minimax risk of \cref{obj:def:minimax-risk}.
\end{lemmav}

The lower floor in \cref{obj:lem:oracle-dose-regression-lower-all-beta} is uniform over \(\beta>0\) because the hard submodel fixes the treatment density at a slack baseline and perturbs the treatment regression near \(t_0\). The constant \(c_{\mathrm{or}}\) may depend on the fixed model constants and remains uniform in the sample size.

Finally, the preceding pieces can be recorded in a single bracket statement. This statement is useful as a compact reference for the appendix architecture: it combines the same-class lower floor with the two algebraic reductions of \(\rho_n\).

\begin{lemmav}{lem:certified-beta-frontier-bracket}[Certified Beta Frontier Bracket]
Let \(d\in\mathbb N\), and let \(\alpha,\beta,s,M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0\in\mathbb R\). Assume:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(Regime constants.)} \(\alpha>0\), \(\beta>0\), \(s>0\), \(M>0\), \(c_0>0\), \(t_0\in(0,1)\), \(\varepsilon_0\in(0,1/2)\), and the window \([t_0-\varepsilon_0,t_0+\varepsilon_0]\) lies in \((0,1)\).
\item \textbf{(Strict-slack baseline.)} \cref{obj:ass:baseline-submodel-slack} holds for \(d,\beta,s,M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0\).
\end{itemize}
Then there exists a constant \(c>0\) such that, for all sufficiently large \(n\),
\[
c\,n^{-2\alpha/(2\alpha+1)}
\le
R_n\!\left(\mathcal P_{\alpha,\beta,s}(M,c_0,\varepsilon_0,t_0),t_0\right),
\]
where the minimax risk and the same-class Hölder dose-response model are those of \cref{obj:def:minimax-risk} and~\cref{obj:def:holder-dose-class}. Moreover, for every \(n\ge 1\), the benchmark \(\rho_n\) of \cref{obj:def:published-hoif-rate} satisfies
\[
d\le 4s
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\rho_n=n^{-2\alpha/(2\alpha+1)},
\]
and
\[
4s<d
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\rho_n=n^{-2/(1+d/(4s)+1/\alpha)}
\quad\text{and}\quad
\frac{2}{1+d/(4s)+1/\alpha}
<
\frac{2\alpha}{2\alpha+1}.
\]
\end{lemmav}

\cref{obj:lem:certified-beta-frontier-bracket} is the appendix counterpart of the comparison stated in the main results. It records the common lower floor and the benchmark algebra needed to interpret the smooth- and low-covariate-smoothness regimes.
