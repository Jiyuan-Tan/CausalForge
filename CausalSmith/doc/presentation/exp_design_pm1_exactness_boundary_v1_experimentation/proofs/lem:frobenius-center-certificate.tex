\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:frobenius-center-certificate}]
Let \(q=2(m-1)\).  Since \(q+2=2m\), the point \((1,1,1)\) has nonnegative coordinates and satisfies \(q\cdot1+1+1=2m\), hence belongs to \(T_m\). % lean: frobenius_center_certificate
For any \((x,y,z_{\mathrm{sp}})\in T_m\), the simplex equation gives
\[
qx^2+y^2+z_{\mathrm{sp}}^2
=
(q+2)+q(x-1)^2+(y-1)^2+(z_{\mathrm{sp}}-1)^2 .
\]
If the point is not \((1,1,1)\), the last three-square sum is strictly positive, since \(q>0\).  Taking square roots gives the stated strict Frobenius-center inequality. % lean: frobenius_center_certificate

Assume next that \((1,1,1)\) minimizes \(\phi_{r,\kappa}\) on \(T_m\).  Along the feasible tangent curve
\[
t\mapsto (1+t,\,1-qt,\,1)
\]
for all sufficiently small \(t\), the point \(t=0\) is a local minimum.  The Frobenius term has derivative \(0\) at \(t=0\), so the derivative of the full objective there is \(c_x-qc_y\); local minimality forces \(c_x-qc_y=0\), equivalently \(c_x/q=c_y\). % lean: frobeniusCenterTangentXY_deriv
Similarly, along
\[
t\mapsto (1,\,1+t,\,1-t),
\]
local minimality at \(t=0\) and the vanishing first derivative of the Frobenius term give derivative \(c_y-c_z\), hence \(c_y=c_z\). % lean: frobeniusCenterTangentYZ_deriv

Finally suppose \(\kappa>0\), \(c_x/q=c_y\), and \(c_y=c_z\).  Then \(c_x=q c_y\), and for every point of \(T_m\),
\[
c_x\cdot1+c_y\cdot1+c_z\cdot1
=
c_xx+c_yy+c_zz_{\mathrm{sp}},
\]
because both sides equal \(c_y(qx+y+z_{\mathrm{sp}})=c_y(q+2)\).  Thus the linear part of the objective is constant on \(T_m\).  For every point other than \((1,1,1)\), the first part gives a strictly larger Frobenius norm, and multiplication by \(\kappa>0\) preserves strictness.  Therefore \((1,1,1)\) is a strict minimizer. % lean: frobenius_center_certificate
\end{proof}
