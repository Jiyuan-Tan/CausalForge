\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:weighted-simplex-truncation}]
We first record the truncation dichotomy used in both robustness regimes.  Let \(t^{\mathrm{rel}}\in\Delta_M\) minimize \(\Phi\) over the full simplex.  If \(t^{\mathrm{rel}}\in K_d\), then every \(s\in K_d\) is also in \(\Delta_M\), so the full-simplex minimality immediately gives \(\Phi(t^{\mathrm{rel}})\le\Phi(s)\). % lean: trunc_from_minimizer
If instead \(t^{\mathrm{rel}}_y+t^{\mathrm{rel}}_z<d\), then \(d>0\).  The selector \(s^\star=\operatorname{truncSelector}(M,d,\alpha,\beta,\kappa)\) lies in \([0,d]\), so
\[
(M-d,s^\star,d-s^\star)\in K_d .
\] % lean: truncSelector_mem
For any \(s\in K_d\), convexity of the objective along the segment from \(s\) to \(t^{\mathrm{rel}}\) shows that \(s\) is dominated by some point on the boundary segment
\[
H_d=\{(M-d,\sigma,d-\sigma):0\le\sigma\le d\}.
\] % lean: truncSeg_reduction
On this segment the objective reduces to the one-dimensional convex function
\[
\alpha_x(M-d)+\alpha_y\sigma+\alpha_z(d-\sigma)
+\kappa\sqrt{\beta_x(M-d)^2+\sigma^2+(d-\sigma)^2},
\]
and the displayed selector is chosen exactly so that its tangent inequality holds on \([0,d]\).  Therefore the selected boundary point minimizes the objective over \(H_d\), and hence over all of \(K_d\). % lean: truncSeg_selector_le

When \(\kappa>0\), the assumed admissibility of \((S,\lambda)\) gives that the displayed active-set point \(t^{\mathrm{rel}}\) lies in \(\Delta_M\) and minimizes \(\Phi\) over the full simplex. % lean: activeSetPoint_isMinimizer
Applying the dichotomy just proved gives the two asserted alternatives: if \(t^{\mathrm{rel}}\in K_d\), it is already optimal on \(K_d\); if \(t^{\mathrm{rel}}_y+t^{\mathrm{rel}}_z<d\), the selected point \((M-d,s^\star,d-s^\star)\) is feasible and optimal on \(K_d\). % lean: weighted_simplex_truncation

When \(\kappa=0\), a point \(t^{\mathrm{rel}}\) of the exposed \(\alpha\)-minimizing face minimizes the linear objective over \(\Delta_M\). % lean: exposedMinFace_isMinimizer
The same dichotomy applies verbatim.  Thus, if the selected relaxed point lies in \(K_d\), it is optimal over \(K_d\); if its \(y+z\) mass is below \(d\), the constrained optimum lies on \(H_d\) at
\[
s^\star=\operatorname{truncSelector}(M,d,\alpha,\beta,0).
\]
For \(\kappa=0\), the selector guards reduce to the ordered endpoint rule: \(s^\star=0\) when \(\alpha_y-\alpha_z\ge0\), and \(s^\star=d\) when \(\alpha_y-\alpha_z<0\). % lean: truncSelector
\end{proof}
