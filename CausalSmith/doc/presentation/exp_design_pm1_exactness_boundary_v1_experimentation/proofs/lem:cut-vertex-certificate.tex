\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:cut-vertex-certificate}]
Write \(q=2(m-1)\) and \(M=2m\).  The point \((0,M,0)\) has nonnegative coordinates and satisfies \(q\cdot0+M+0=M\), so it belongs to \(T_m\). % lean: cut_vertex_certificate

Now take \((x,y,z_{\mathrm{sp}})\in T_m\), different from \((0,M,0)\).  Since \(q>0\), \(x,z_{\mathrm{sp}}\ge0\), and \(qx+y+z_{\mathrm{sp}}=M\), we have
\[
y\le \sqrt{qx^2+y^2+z_{\mathrm{sp}}^2},
\]
and hence \(\kappa y\le \kappa\sqrt{qx^2+y^2+z_{\mathrm{sp}}^2}\). % lean: cut_vertex_certificate
The coefficient assumptions imply
\[
c_x-qc_y-q\kappa>0,\qquad c_z-c_y-\kappa>0.
\]
If both \(x=0\) and \(z_{\mathrm{sp}}=0\), the simplex equation would force \(y=M\), contradicting the assumption that the point is not \((0,M,0)\).  Therefore at least one of \(x,z_{\mathrm{sp}}\) is positive, and so
\[
0<(c_x-qc_y-q\kappa)x+(c_z-c_y-\kappa)z_{\mathrm{sp}}.
\]
Using \(M=qx+y+z_{\mathrm{sp}}\), this is exactly
\[
c_yM+\kappa M
<
c_xx+c_yy+c_zz_{\mathrm{sp}}+\kappa y .
\]
Combining this strict inequality with the norm lower bound above gives the stated strict comparison of reduced objectives.  Hence no point of \(T_m\) other than \((0,2m,0)\) can attain the same value, so the cut vertex is the unique minimizer. % lean: cut_vertex_certificate
\end{proof}
