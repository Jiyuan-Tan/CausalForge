\section{Exactness at implementable designs}

The symmetry reduction in \cref{obj:prop:symmetry-reduction} leaves a precise question: when does the relaxed optimum over \(\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}}\) coincide with a covariance matrix generated by an actual block-exchangeable sign design? This section records two cases in which the answer is exact. The first is the cut covariance \leanref{sym:X_cut}{$X_{\mathrm{cut}}$}, which assigns opposite signs to the two communities up to the global sign symmetry. The second is the identity covariance \(I_n\), corresponding to independent fair signs. These two designs represent different econometric instincts: one deliberately separates the two homophilous communities, while the other spreads randomization independently across all units, as in classical randomized assignment and rerandomization benchmarks \citep{efron1971,morgan2012,harshaw2019}.

The cut design is optimal in a strict small-\(r\) region. There, the graph and balance terms favor separating the two blocks strongly enough to keep the relaxed optimum at the cut corner after including the pseudoinverse and robustness terms.

\begin{theoremv}{thm:cut-corner-exactness}[Cut-Corner Exactness]
Assume the two-block homophily condition in \cref{obj:ass:two-block-homophily}. Let \(r,\kappa\in\mathbb R\) satisfy
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(Nonnegative tradeoff.)} \(0\le r\).
\item \textbf{(Nonnegative robustness.)} \(0\le \kappa\).
\item \textbf{(Cut region.)}
\[
r< r_{\mathrm{cut}}(m,a,b,\kappa)
:= \max\!\left\{0,\,
\min\!\left(
2b(a+b)\left(1-\frac{\kappa}{a-b}\right),
2b(2m-2b-\kappa)
\right)\right\}.
\]
\end{itemize}
Then \(X_{\mathrm{cut}}\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}}\), and for every
\(X\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}}\) with \(X\ne X_{\mathrm{cut}}\),
\[
F_{r,\kappa}(X_{\mathrm{cut}})<F_{r,\kappa}(X),
\]
where \(F_{r,\kappa}\) is the objective in \cref{obj:def:design-objective}. Moreover,
\[
\Delta_m^{\pm}(r,\kappa)=0,
\]
with \(\Delta_m^{\pm}\) as in \cref{obj:def:implementability-gap}. Finally,
\(X_{\mathrm{cut}}\in\mathcal C_m^{\pm}\), and for every
\(X\in\mathcal C_m^{\pm}\) with \(X\ne X_{\mathrm{cut}}\),
\[
F_{r,\kappa}(X_{\mathrm{cut}})<F_{r,\kappa}(X),
\]
where \(\mathcal C_m^{\pm}\) is the class in \cref{obj:def:implementable-covariance-class}; the cut design satisfies
\[
P_{\mathrm{cut}}\in\mathcal P_m^{\mathrm{sym}}
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
X(P_{\mathrm{cut}})=X_{\mathrm{cut}},
\]
with \(\mathcal P_m^{\mathrm{sym}}\) as in \cref{obj:def:block-exchangeable-design-class}.
\end{theoremv}

\cref{obj:thm:cut-corner-exactness} is an exact attainability statement. The displayed strict inequality involving \(r_{\mathrm{cut}}(m,a,b,\kappa)\) gives a sufficient region in which the relaxed block-elliptope optimum is unique, implementable, and has zero implementability loss. The implementing law \leanref{sym:P_cut}{$P_{\mathrm{cut}}$} is the symmetric two-point law that puts mass on the cut assignment and its global sign reversal.

The second exactness result concerns the robust corner \(I_n\). Independent fair assignment is always implementable, but the theorem below shows that its relaxed optimality is much more restrictive: a finite positive robustness weight can make \(I_n\) optimal only on a particular affine relation among the graph scale and the pseudoinverse weight.

\begin{theoremv}{thm:robust-corner-exactness}[Robust Corner Exactness]
Let \(m\in\mathbb N\) and \(a,b,r\in\mathbb R\). Suppose that
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{(Two-block homophily.)} \cref{obj:ass:two-block-homophily} holds for \((m,a,b)\).
\item \textbf{(Nonnegative pseudoinverse weight.)} \(r\ge 0\).
\end{itemize}
For the objective \(F_{r,\kappa}\) of \cref{obj:def:design-objective}, the following statements hold.

First, there exists a finite nonnegative robustness weight \(\kappa\) such that \(I_n\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}}\) and
\[
F_{r,\kappa}(I_n)\le F_{r,\kappa}(X)
\qquad\text{for every }X\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}},
\]
if and only if
\[
a+3b=2m
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
r=2b(a+b).
\]

Second, on this affine-balanced locus, every positive robustness weight makes \(I_n\) the unique relaxed minimizer: if
\[
a+3b=2m
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
r=2b(a+b),
\]
then for every \(\kappa>0\),
\[
I_n\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}}
\]
and, for every \(X\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}}\) with \(X\ne I_n\),
\[
F_{r,\kappa}(I_n)<F_{r,\kappa}(X).
\]

Third, the iid design satisfies
\[
P_{\mathrm{iid}}\in\mathcal P_m^{\mathrm{sym}}
\]
for the block-exchangeable class of \cref{obj:def:block-exchangeable-design-class}, and
\[
X(P_{\mathrm{iid}})=I_n.
\]

Finally, off the affine-balanced locus, \(I_n\) is never a minimizer at any positive finite robustness weight, and relaxed minimizers converge entrywise to \(I_n\) as \(\kappa\to\infty\). Precisely, if
\[
\neg\bigl(a+3b=2m\ \text{and}\ r=2b(a+b)\bigr),
\]
then for every \(\kappa>0\) it is not the case that
\[
F_{r,\kappa}(I_n)\le F_{r,\kappa}(X)
\qquad\text{for every }X\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}}.
\]
Moreover, for any family \((X_\kappa)_{\kappa\in\mathbb R}\) such that, for every \(\kappa>0\),
\[
X_\kappa\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}}
\quad\text{and}\quad
F_{r,\kappa}(X_\kappa)\le F_{r,\kappa}(X)
\ \text{ for every }X\in\mathcal E_m^{\mathrm{blk}},
\]
each matrix entry converges to the corresponding entry of \(I_n\):
\[
(X_\kappa)_{ij}\longrightarrow (I_n)_{ij}
\qquad\text{as }\kappa\to\infty
\]
for every \(i,j\in\{1,\ldots,n\}\).
\end{theoremv}

\cref{obj:thm:robust-corner-exactness} separates implementability from optimality. The iid law \leanref{sym:P_iid}{$P_{\mathrm{iid}}$} belongs to the block-exchangeable design class and attains \(I_n\), with exact finite-robustness optimality precisely when \(a+3b=2m\) and \(r=2b(a+b)\). Elsewhere, increasing \(\kappa\) pulls relaxed minimizers toward \(I_n\) entrywise. Thus robustness makes iid assignment an exact solution on the affine-balanced locus and an asymptotic limiting target elsewhere.

Together, \cref{obj:thm:cut-corner-exactness} and~\cref{obj:thm:robust-corner-exactness} identify two implementable covariances whose roles are sharply different. The cut covariance is exactly optimal throughout the stated strict cut region, yielding zero implementability loss there. The iid covariance is implementable everywhere under the maintained homophily conditions, with exact relaxed optimality on the stated affine-balanced locus and limiting optimality elsewhere as robustness diverges. The next section turns to parameter regions with strictly positive implementability loss.
