\section{Appendix D. Verification Note}

This appendix records the scope of the formal layer underlying the statements in the paper.  The object of analysis is the observed-law offline policy-learning experiment introduced in \cref{obj:ass:iid}: one observation has law \(P\) on \(\Omega=\mathcal X\times\{0,1\}\times[-1,1]\), and the regret target is the contrast-weighted welfare loss in \cref{obj:def:welfare-regret}.  The potential-outcome notation used in \cref{obj:ass:bounded-outcome} fixes the outcome scale, but the lower-bound and upper-bound statements themselves are expressed through the observed law, the propensity score, the outcome regressions, the contrast, and the induced oracle policy.

The observed-law side of the analysis consists of the welfare identity in \cref{obj:thm:welfare-identity}, the disagreement notation in \cref{obj:def:disagreement}, the margin localization statement in \cref{obj:thm:margin-localization}, and the law class in \cref{obj:def:law-class}.  The exponents used in the lower bound are those in \cref{obj:def:exponents}; in particular, the denominator \(2+\alpha+\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}\) and the lower-bound exponent \(r_\star(\alpha,\gamma)\) are fixed there before the minimax statement is formed.

The lower-bound argument is represented by the two-point witness construction in \cref{obj:def:two-point-witness}, the overlap-envelope calibration in \cref{obj:prop:overlap-envelope}, witness membership in \cref{obj:lem:witness-membership}, the product-divergence control in \cref{obj:lem:two-point-divergence}, the regret separation in \cref{obj:lem:regret-separation}, and the chi-square testing reduction in \cref{obj:lem:le-cam-two-point-chisq}.  These statements support the minimax lower bound in \cref{obj:thm:minimax-lower}; the conditional upper analysis is represented by the separate clipped-AIPW chain below.

The feasible rule is represented separately.  The clipping schedule and feasible exponent are defined in \cref{obj:def:feasible-rate}; the clipped propensity and clipped AIPW score are defined in \cref{obj:def:clipped-propensity} and~\cref{obj:def:clipped-aipw-score}; and the cross-fitted empirical welfare rule is defined in \cref{obj:def:feasible-erm}.  The basic ERM inequality in \cref{obj:lem:feasible-erm-basic-inequality}, the clipped-score envelope in \cref{obj:lem:crude-clipped-score-envelope}, the clipped-bias identity in \cref{obj:lem:clip-bias}, the drift localization in \cref{obj:lem:localized-clipped-drift-bound}, and the master bound in \cref{obj:lem:crude-localized-master-bound} are the components used for the conditional upper bound in \cref{obj:oeq:feasible-upper}.

The upper-bound result has an explicit side-condition domain in addition to the observed-law class.  This domain includes the nuisance-rate assumptions in \cref{obj:ass:nuisance-rate} and~\cref{obj:ass:polynomial-nuisance-exponents}, the bounded cross-fitted nuisance condition in \cref{obj:ass:bounded-crossfit-nuisances}, the fixed balanced fold condition in \cref{obj:ass:fixed-crossfit-fold-count}, and the localized empirical-process and offset-envelope conditions in \cref{obj:ass:vc-localized-envelope} and~\cref{obj:ass:vc-localized-offset-envelope}.  The process reductions in \cref{obj:lem:localized-vc-self-bound}, \cref{obj:lem:localized-vc-process-bound}, \cref{obj:lem:crossfit-localized-process-reduction}, and~\cref{obj:lem:crossfit-localized-offset-control} are invoked within that conditional domain.

Accordingly, \cref{obj:oeq:feasible-upper} is a procedure-specific conditional achievability statement for the clipped cross-fitted AIPW ERM under \cref{obj:def:upper-risk}.  \Cref{obj:oeq:feasible-tight} records the open strict-gap question: whether another genuinely feasible estimator can attain the converse exponent \(r_\star(\alpha,\gamma)\), or whether the slower nuisance-limited exponent is intrinsic.

The theorem statements and proofs described above are machine-checked in Lean 4.  The machine-checked scope covers the observed-law objects, exponent definitions, witness construction, lower-bound skeleton, clipped-score identities, ERM inequality, and conditional upper-bound dependencies.  Identification primitives, nuisance-rate conditions, fixed-fold cross-fitting, localized VC envelope bounds, and offset-envelope controls enter as stated assumptions defining the conditional domain.
