\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:crude-clipped-score-envelope}]
Fix \(q\in(0,1/2]\) and one foldwise nuisance triple.  Write
\[
e_q=e_q(X;\widehat\eta^{(-k)}).
\]
By clipping, \(q\le e_q\le 1-q\).  Hence \(e_q^{-1}\le q^{-1}\) and \((1-e_q)^{-1}\le q^{-1}\).  The treatment indicators \(A\) and \(1-A\) have absolute value at most one.  By \cref{obj:ass:bounded-outcome}, and by the foldwise bounded nuisance ranges used for the cross-fitted outcome regressions, \(|Y|\le1\), \(|\widehat\mu_0(X)|\le1\), and \(|\widehat\mu_1(X)|\le1\) almost surely.  Therefore
\[
|\widehat\mu_1(X)-\widehat\mu_0(X)|\le2,\qquad
|Y-\widehat\mu_a(X)|\le2,\quad a=0,1 .
\]
% lean: crude_clipped_score_envelope
The two inverse-propensity terms are consequently bounded by
\[
\left|\frac{A}{e_q}\{Y-\widehat\mu_1(X)\}\right|\le \frac{2}{q},
\qquad
\left|\frac{1-A}{1-e_q}\{Y-\widehat\mu_0(X)\}\right|\le \frac{2}{q}.
\]
The triangle inequality gives
\[
|\Gamma_q(O;\widehat\eta^{(-k)})|
\le 2+\frac{2}{q}+\frac{2}{q}.
\]
Since \(q\le1/2\), the constant term is also bounded by \(2/q\), so the sharper envelope \(6/q\) holds almost surely; in particular it is bounded by \(36/q\).  Taking \(C=36\) gives the asserted absolute envelope. % lean: crude_clipped_score_envelope
Squaring the sharper bound yields
\[
\Gamma_q(O;\widehat\eta^{(-k)})^2\le \left(\frac{6}{q}\right)^2=\frac{36}{q^2}
\]
almost surely.  Thus, with the same constant \(C=36\), the pointwise squared envelope
\[
\Gamma_q(O;\widehat\eta^{(-k)})^2\le \frac{C}{q^2}
\]
holds almost surely. % lean: crude_clipped_score_envelope
\end{proof}
