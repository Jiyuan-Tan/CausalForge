\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:le-cam-two-point-chisq}]
Let \(A\) be the event that the test chooses \(+\).  Put
\(P=P_+^{\otimes n}\) and \(Q=P_-^{\otimes n}\).  Under the stated absolute-continuity and square-integrability assumptions, the Radon--Nikodym density satisfies
\[
\int \left(\frac{dP}{dQ}\right)^2\,dQ
=1+\chi^2(P\,\|\,Q)\le C_\chi+1 .
\]
Cauchy--Schwarz therefore gives
\[
P(A)\le \sqrt{(C_\chi+1)\,Q(A)} .
\]
Let \(e=P(A^c)+Q(A)\).  Since \(P(A^c)=1-P(A)\) and \(Q(A)\ge0\), the preceding display implies
\[
1-e=P(A)-Q(A)\le P(A)\le \sqrt{(C_\chi+1)Q(A)} .
\]
Suppose, to get a contradiction, that
\(e<1/(4(C_\chi+1))\).  Then \(Q(A)\le e\), hence
\(Q(A)<1/(4(C_\chi+1))\), and so
\(\sqrt{(C_\chi+1)Q(A)}<1/2\).  Also \(C_\chi+1\ge1\), so
\(e<1/4\), whence \(1/2<1-e\).  This contradicts
\(1-e\le \sqrt{(C_\chi+1)Q(A)}<1/2\).  Thus
\[
P_+^{\otimes n}(\psi\ne +)+P_-^{\otimes n}(\psi\ne -)
 =P(A^c)+Q(A)
 \ge \frac{1}{4(C_\chi+1)} .
\]

For the one-observation sufficient condition, suppose \(c_0\ge0\), \(n>0\), and
\(\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)\le c_0/n\), again with the stated absolute-continuity and square-integrability assumptions.  The product identity gives
\[
1+\chi^2(P_+^{\otimes n}\,\|\,P_-^{\otimes n})
=
\left(1+\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)\right)^n
\le
\exp\{n\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)\}
\le e^{c_0}.
\]
Hence the product chi-square divergence is at most \(e^{c_0}-1\).  Applying the preceding bound to the product experiment gives the uniform testing floor
\[
c(c_0)=\frac{1}{4e^{c_0}}>0 .
\]
% lean: Causalean.Stat.one_add_chiSqDiv_pi_iid_general % lean: le_cam_two_point_chisq
\end{proof}
