\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:prop:overlap-envelope}]
For any real \(b\), the tight-window value is first rewritten by the power laws
\[
(h^{b/\gamma})^\alpha (h^b)^{1/\gamma}
=h^{(b/\gamma)\alpha+b/\gamma}
=h^{(\alpha+1)b/\gamma},
\]
using \(0<h\) and \(\gamma>0\). % lean: hpow
Since \(0<h<1\), the map \(t\mapsto h^t\) is order-reversing, so
\[
h^{(\alpha+1)b/\gamma}\ge h^\alpha
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\frac{(\alpha+1)b}{\gamma}\le \alpha .
\]
As \(\alpha+1>0\) and \(\gamma>0\), this is equivalent to
\[
b\le \frac{\alpha\gamma}{\alpha+1}=\beta_{\alpha,\gamma},
\]
with \(\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}\) as in \cref{obj:def:exponents}. % lean: halg
Applying this with \(b=\beta\) gives both the displayed identity and the equivalence. % lean: overlap_envelope
If \(\beta=\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}\), the exponent calculation becomes
\[
\frac{(\alpha+1)}{\gamma}\frac{\alpha\gamma}{\alpha+1}=\alpha,
\]
and hence the envelope value is exactly \(h^\alpha\). % lean: hcalc
Finally, \(\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}\ge0\) follows from \(\alpha\ge0\) and \(\gamma>0\), and the same equivalence just proved holds for every \(\beta'\ge0\). % lean: overlap_envelope
Thus the largest, and therefore least informative, weak-arm exponent allowed by the overlap envelope is precisely \(\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}\). % lean: overlap_envelope
\end{proof}
