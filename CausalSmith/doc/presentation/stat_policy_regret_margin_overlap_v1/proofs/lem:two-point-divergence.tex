\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:two-point-divergence}]
Fix \(n\) large enough that \(0<h_n\le1/2\), \(0<q_n\le1/2\), and \(n\ge1\).  Let \(P_+=P_{n,+}\) and \(P_-=P_{n,-}\).  The two laws agree except on the treated active cell \(\{X\in B_n,A=1\}\).  Partition the observation space into the unchanged cell, the active cell with \(Y=1\), and the active cell with \(Y=-1\).  On these cells the likelihood ratios of \(P_+\) relative to \(P_-\) are respectively
\[
1,\qquad \frac{1+h_n}{1-h_n},\qquad \frac{1-h_n}{1+h_n}.
\]
The two charged cells under \(P_-\) have masses \(A(1-h_n)/2\) and \(A(1+h_n)/2\), where \(A=P_X(B_n)q_n\le c_Bh_n^\alpha q_n\). % lean: chiSqDiv_three_cell_bound_of_restrict % lean: twoPointWitness_one_draw_chiSq_bound

The finite-partition chi-square calculation, applied with the block-mass upper bound \(P_X(B_n)\le c_Bh_n^\alpha\), gives
\[
\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)
 \le 8\,(c_Bh_n^\alpha)\,q_n h_n^2 .
\]
The clipping level satisfies \(q_n\le h_n^{\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}}\), by a case split on \(\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}\): when \(\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}=0\), \(q_n\) is the fixed level \(1/4\le1=h_n^0\); when \(\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}>0\), \(q_n=h_n^{\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}}\).  Hence
\[
\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)
 \le 8c_B h_n^{2+\alpha+\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}} .
\]
This is the asserted one-observation bound.
% lean: twoPointWitness_one_draw_chiSq_bound

Now set \(D_{\alpha,\gamma}=2+\alpha+\beta_{\alpha,\gamma}\).  By \cref{obj:def:two-point-witness}, \(h_n=n^{-1/D_{\alpha,\gamma}}\), so \(h_n^{D_{\alpha,\gamma}}=n^{-1}\).  Hence
\[
n\,\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)\le 8c_B .
\]
For product laws, chi-square tensorizes as
\[
1+\chi^2(P_+^{\otimes n}\,\|\,P_-^{\otimes n})
 =
 \left(1+\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)\right)^n .
\]
Using \(1+x\le e^x\) and the construction constant \(8c_B<\log5\),
\[
\left(1+\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)\right)^n
 \le \exp\{n\chi^2(P_+\,\|\,P_-)\}
 \le e^{8c_B}
 \le 5 .
\]
Therefore \(\chi^2(P_+^{\otimes n}\,\|\,P_-^{\otimes n})\le4\), after increasing the generic constant if needed. % lean: Causalean.Stat.one_add_chiSqDiv_pi_iid_general % lean: two_point_divergence
\end{proof}
