\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:clipped-region-localization}]
Fix a policy \(\pi\in\Pi\), let \(D=D_\pi\), and fix \(u,q\) with
\(0<u\le u_0\), \(q>0\), and \(q\le c_o u^\gamma\).  Put
\[
T=D\cap\{x:p_P(x)\le q\}.
\]
We cover \(T\) by the three sets
\[
Z=\{x:\tau_P(x)=0,\ \pi(x)\ne\pi_P^\star(x)\},
\]
\[
S=\{x:p_P(x)\le q,\ 0<|\tau_P(x)|,\ |\tau_P(x)|\le u\},
\]
and
\[
B=D\cap\{x:u<|\tau_P(x)|\}.
\]
Indeed, if \(x\in T\) and \(\tau_P(x)=0\), then \(x\in Z\).  If
\(\tau_P(x)\ne0\) and \(|\tau_P(x)|\le u\), then \(x\in S\).  Otherwise
\(u<|\tau_P(x)|\), and since \(x\in D\), we have \(x\in B\). % lean: clipped_region_localization

The zero-effect condition gives \(P_X(Z)=0\).  In its first alternative the whole zero-contrast set is null, so its subset \(Z\) is null.  In its second alternative, applied to the present policy \(\pi\in\Pi\), it directly gives
\[
P_X\{x:\tau_P(x)=0,\ \pi(x)\ne\pi_P^\star(x)\}=0.
\]
% lean: clipped_region_localization

For the small-contrast weak-overlap piece, \cref{obj:ass:overlap-decay} applies because \(0<u\le u_0\), \(q>0\), and \(q\le c_o u^\gamma\).  Since \(\gamma>0\), this gives
\[
P_X(S)\le C_o u^\alpha q^{1/\gamma}
\le \max\{C_o,1\}u^\alpha q^{1/\gamma}.
\]
% lean: clipped_region_localization

For the large-contrast piece, the welfare regret identity controls disagreement on \(\{|\tau_P|>u\}\):
\[
P_X(B)\le \frac{R_P(\pi)}{u}.
\]
This follows because on \(B\) the integrand \(|\tau_P(x)|\mathbf 1_D(x)\) in the regret identity is at least \(u\). % lean: regret_disagreement_large_contrast_le

By monotonicity and subadditivity of measure, and using \(P_X(Z)=0\),
\[
P_X(T)\le P_X(S)+P_X(B)
\le \max\{C_o,1\}u^\alpha q^{1/\gamma}+\frac{R_P(\pi)}{u}.
\]
Since \(\max\{C_o,1\}>0\), relabeling this as the generic constant \(C\) proves the claim. % lean: clipped_region_localization
\end{proof}
