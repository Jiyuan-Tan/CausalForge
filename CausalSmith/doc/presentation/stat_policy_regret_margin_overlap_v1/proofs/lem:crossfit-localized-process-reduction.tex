\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:crossfit-localized-process-reduction}]
The localized VC envelope gives constants \(C_0>0\) and \(p\ge0\) such that every compatible centered process with envelope \(B\) and second moment bounded by \(B^2P_X(D_\pi)\) satisfies, for each sample size \(m>0\),
\[
E Z_m(r)\le
C_0 Bm^{-1/2}r^{\alpha/(2+2\alpha)}(\log m)^p .
\]
The fixed-fold assumption supplies \(K>0\).  Set \(C=C_0K\), so \(C>0\) and \(C_0\le C\). % lean: VCLocalizedEnvelope % lean: localized_vc_process_bound % lean: crossfit_localized_process_reduction

Fix \(n>0\), \(B\ge0\), \(r\ge0\), and a fold-indexed family of increment functions satisfying the stated compatibility, measurability, envelope, and second-moment hypotheses.  Applying the preceding bound at the full sample size \(m=n\) to each frozen foldwise increment family gives, for every fold \(k\),
\[
E\!\left[\sup_{\pi\in\Pi:\,R_P(\pi)\le r}
\left|(P_n-P)g_{k,\pi}\right|\right]
\le
C_0 B n^{-1/2}r^{\alpha/(2+2\alpha)}(\log n)^p
\le
C B n^{-1/2}r^{\alpha/(2+2\alpha)}(\log n)^p .
\]
This is the foldwise \(n\)-observation assertion; it is not the fold-size term used below in the pooled decomposition. % lean: Henv % lean: hC0_le_C

For the pooled supremum, let \(m_k\) be the size of fold \(k\) and \(w_k=m_k/n\).  Pointwise in the sample, the pooled localized supremum is bounded by the weighted sum of the fold-restricted localized suprema:
\[
Z_{\mathrm{cf}}(r)\le \sum_{k=1}^K w_k Z_k(r).
\]
The stated integrability hypotheses justify integrating this inequality and exchanging the finite sum.  The coordinate projection from the full product sample onto fold \(k\) identifies the expectation of \(Z_k(r)\) with the ordinary localized-supremum expectation for \(m_k\) i.i.d. observations from \(P\).  Hence, for every nonempty fold, applying the localized VC envelope at sample size \(m=m_k\) gives
\[
w_k E Z_k(r)
\le
w_k C_0 B m_k^{-1/2}r^{\alpha/(2+2\alpha)}(\log m_k)^p .
\]
If \(m_k=0\), then \(w_k=0\) and the fold contributes zero.  If \(m_k>0\), the inclusion \(I_k\subset\{1,\ldots,n\}\) gives \(m_k\le n\); therefore
\[
w_k m_k^{-1/2}\le n^{-1/2},
\qquad
(\log m_k)^p\le(\log n)^p .
\]
Thus each fold contributes at most
\[
C_0 B n^{-1/2}r^{\alpha/(2+2\alpha)}(\log n)^p,
\]
and summing over the fixed \(K\) folds yields
\[
E Z_{\mathrm{cf}}(r)
\le
C_0K B n^{-1/2}r^{\alpha/(2+2\alpha)}(\log n)^p
=
C B n^{-1/2}r^{\alpha/(2+2\alpha)}(\log n)^p .
\]
% lean: pooledLocalizedSup_pointwise_le_sum_fold % lean: measurePreserving_foldProjection % lean: integral_foldLocalizedSup_eq_expected % lean: fold_weight_mul_inv_sqrt_le % lean: log_nat_rpow_le % lean: crossfit_localized_process_reduction
\end{proof}
