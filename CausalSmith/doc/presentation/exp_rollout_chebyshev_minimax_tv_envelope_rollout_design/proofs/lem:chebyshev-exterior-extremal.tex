\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:chebyshev-exterior-extremal}]
Let \(v_i\), \(i=0,\ldots,\beta\), be the Chebyshev extremal nodes, so that \(v_i\in[-1,1]\) and \(T_\beta(v_i)=(-1)^i\). These nodes are distinct, and \(T_\beta\) has degree \(\beta\), so both \(T_\beta\) and any polynomial \(Q\) of degree at most \(\beta\) are recovered from their values at the nodes by Lagrange interpolation. % lean: hvs

First prove the one-sided claim: if \(\deg(Q)\le\beta\) and \(|Q(x)|\le1\) on \([-1,1]\), then
\[
Q(x_0)\le T_\beta(x_0).
\]
Interpolating \(Q\) and \(T_\beta\) at the nodes and evaluating at \(x_0\) gives
\[
Q(x_0)=\sum_{i=0}^{\beta}Q(v_i)L_i(x_0),
\qquad
T_\beta(x_0)=\sum_{i=0}^{\beta}T_\beta(v_i)L_i(x_0),
\]
where \(L_i\) is the \(i\)th Lagrange basis polynomial. % lean: hQeval
For \(x_0>1\), the signed exterior coefficients satisfy
\[
(-1)^iL_i(x_0)\ge0.
\]
Indeed, all numerator factors \(x_0-v_j\) are positive, and the sign of the denominator is obtained by splitting the indices below and above \(i\), using the monotone ordering of the Chebyshev nodes. % lean: chebyshev_exterior_lagrange_coeff_nonneg
Since \(v_i\in[-1,1]\), the bound on \(Q\) gives \(|Q(v_i)|\le1\), hence
\[
(-1)^iQ(v_i)\le1.
\] % lean: neg_one_pow_mul_le_of_abs_le_one
Multiplying by the nonnegative coefficient \((-1)^iL_i(x_0)\) yields
\[
Q(v_i)L_i(x_0)
\le
(-1)^iL_i(x_0)
=
T_\beta(v_i)L_i(x_0).
\]
Summing over \(i\) proves the one-sided claim. % lean: upper

Apply this claim first to \(Q=P\), obtaining \(P(x_0)\le T_\beta(x_0)\). Apply it again to \(Q=-P\); the degree bound and interval bound are unchanged, so \(-P(x_0)\le T_\beta(x_0)\). % lean: hneg_upper
The two inequalities are exactly
\[
-T_\beta(x_0)\le P(x_0)\le T_\beta(x_0),
\]
and hence
\[
|P(x_0)|\le T_\beta(x_0).
\] % lean: chebyshev_exterior_extremal
\end{proof}
