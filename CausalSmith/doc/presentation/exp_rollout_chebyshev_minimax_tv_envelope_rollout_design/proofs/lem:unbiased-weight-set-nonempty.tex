\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:unbiased-weight-set-nonempty}]
Because \(p\in S_{k,q}\), its nodes are distinct. Let \(L_j\) be the Lagrange basis polynomial associated with the node \(p_j\) among the full collection \(p_0,\ldots,p_k\), and define
\[
w_j=L_j(1)-L_j(0),
\qquad j=0,\ldots,k.
\] % lean: lagrange_endpoint_weights_unbiased
For each \(0\le \ell\le\beta\), the monomial \(P(t)=t^\ell\) has degree at most \(\beta\le k\). Hence Lagrange interpolation on the \(k+1\) distinct nodes gives
\[
P(t)=\sum_{j=0}^k P(p_j)L_j(t).
\]
Evaluating at \(t=1\) and \(t=0\), and subtracting, yields
\[
\sum_{j=0}^k w_j p_j^\ell
=
1^\ell-0^\ell.
\] % lean: lagrange_endpoint_weights_unbiased
For \(\ell=0\) this is \(1-1=0\), while for every \(1\le\ell\le\beta\) it is \(1-0=1\). These are exactly the moment equations defining \(W_\beta(p)\). Thus the vector \(w\) belongs to \(W_\beta(p)\), and the set is nonempty. % lean: unbiased_weight_set_nonempty
\end{proof}
