\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:prop:equal-spacing-benchmark}]
Let \(p_j=qj/\beta\). Since \(\beta\ge1\), \(q>0\), and \(q\le1\), each coordinate is nonnegative, and \(j\le\beta\) gives
\[
p_j=\frac{qj}{\beta}\le q\le1.
\]
Thus \(p_j\in[0,1]\) for every \(j=0,\ldots,\beta\). % lean: hrange

For the amplification bound, use the Lagrange endpoint weights on the equal grid,
\[
w_j=L_j(1)-L_j(0),
\]
where \(L_j\) is the \(j\)th Lagrange basis polynomial for the nodes \(p_0,\ldots,p_\beta\). The equal grid is injective because \(q>0\) and \(\beta>0\). % lean: equalSchedule_injective
By Lagrange interpolation, these weights reproduce \(r(1)-r(0)\) for every polynomial \(r\) of degree at most \(\beta\); applied to the monomials \(1,u,\ldots,u^\beta\), this is exactly \(w\in W_\beta(p)\) as in \cref{obj:def:unbiased-weight-set}. % lean: lagrange_endpoint_weights_unbiased
Therefore \cref{obj:def:amplification-criterion} gives
\[
A_\beta(p)\le \left(\sum_{j=0}^{\beta}|w_j|\right)^2.
\] % lean: hA_le

Set \(y=\beta/q\). Since \(q\le1\le\beta\), \(y\ge1\). % lean: hy_ge_one
For the value at \(1\), the product formula for \(L_j(1)\) gives
\[
|L_j(1)|
\le
\frac{y^\beta}{j!(\beta-j)!}.
\]
The denominator identity comes from splitting the product of distances from \(j\) to the other equal-grid indices into the indices below and above \(j\). % lean: equalSchedule_lagrange_basis_eval_one_le
Summing over \(j\) and using
\[
\sum_{j=0}^{\beta}\frac{1}{j!(\beta-j)!}\le2
\]
gives
\[
\sum_{j=0}^{\beta}|L_j(1)|\le 2y^\beta .
\] % lean: factorial_reciprocal_sum_le_two

At \(0\), the node \(p_0\) is exactly \(0\), so the Lagrange basis evaluates to \(L_0(0)=1\) and \(L_j(0)=0\) for \(j\ne0\). Hence
\[
\sum_{j=0}^{\beta}|L_j(0)|=1.
\] % lean: hsum_eval0
By the triangle inequality,
\[
\sum_{j=0}^{\beta}|w_j|
\le
\sum_{j=0}^{\beta}|L_j(1)|+\sum_{j=0}^{\beta}|L_j(0)|
\le
2y^\beta+1
\le
3y^\beta,
\]
where the last inequality uses \(y^\beta\ge1\). % lean: hsumw
Squaring and substituting \(y=\beta/q\) yields
\[
A_\beta\!\left(p^{\mathrm{eq}}(\beta,q)\right)
\le
(3y^\beta)^2
=
9\left(\frac{\beta}{q}\right)^{2\beta}.
\] % lean: equal_spacing_benchmark
\end{proof}
