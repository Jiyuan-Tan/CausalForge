\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:prop:rollout-polynomial-identity}]
The static consistency hypothesis is part of the rollout environment, but the endpoint calculation uses only the polynomial mean assumption. Since both endpoints belong to \([0,1]\), evaluating the degree-\(\beta\) expansion at \(u=1\) gives
\[
m_P(1)=\sum_{\ell=0}^{\beta}a_{P,\ell},
\]
because \(1^\ell=1\) for every \(\ell\). Evaluating the same expansion at \(u=0\) gives
\[
m_P(0)=a_{P,0},
\]
since every nonzero-degree term contains \(0^\ell\), while the \(\ell=0\) term is \(a_{P,0}\). Finally, the finite sum over \(\{0,\ldots,\beta\}\) splits into its constant term and the positive-degree terms:
\[
\sum_{\ell=0}^{\beta}a_{P,\ell}
=
a_{P,0}+\sum_{\ell=1}^{\beta}a_{P,\ell}.
\]
This finite-sum identity is obtained by induction on \(\beta\), adjoining the top endpoint at each step. Substituting the two endpoint evaluations into this split and cancelling the constant term yields
\[
m_P(1)-m_P(0)=\sum_{\ell=1}^{\beta}a_{P,\ell}.
\]
\end{proof}
