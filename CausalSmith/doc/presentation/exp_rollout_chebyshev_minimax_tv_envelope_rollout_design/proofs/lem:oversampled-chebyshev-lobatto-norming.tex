\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:oversampled-chebyshev-lobatto-norming}]
Fix \(c>1\), and set
\[
K(c)=\frac{1}{\cos(\pi/(2c))}.
\]
Since \(c>1\), the angle \(\pi/(2c)\) lies strictly between \(0\) and \(\pi/2\), so \(K(c)>0\). % lean: oversampled_chebyshev_lobatto_norming
Now let \(\beta\ge1\), \(k\ge c\beta\), and \(\deg(R)\le\beta\). Since \(c>1\) and \(\beta>0\), we have \(\beta<c\beta\le k\), hence \(\beta<k\). The assumed Ehlich--Zeller mesh inequality therefore gives, for every \(x\in[-1,1]\),
\[
|R(x)|
\le
\frac{1}{\cos(\pi\beta/(2k))}
\max_{0\le j\le k}
\left|R\!\left(-\cos\!\left(\frac{\pi j}{k}\right)\right)\right|.
\] % lean: EhlichZellerMesh
It remains only to bound the secant factor uniformly. From \(k\ge c\beta\) and \(k>0\),
\[
\frac{\beta}{k}\le \frac1c,
\qquad\text{hence}\qquad
0\le \frac{\pi\beta}{2k}\le \frac{\pi}{2c}.
\]
Also \(\pi/(2c)<\pi/2\). Since cosine is decreasing on \([0,\pi]\), this implies
\[
\cos(\pi/(2c))\le \cos(\pi\beta/(2k)),
\]
and positivity of \(\cos(\pi/(2c))\) gives
\[
\frac{1}{\cos(\pi\beta/(2k))}
\le
\frac{1}{\cos(\pi/(2c))}=K(c).
\] % lean: oversampled_chebyshev_lobatto_norming
The displayed maximum is nonnegative, being a maximum of absolute values, so multiplying the last inequality by it and combining with the Ehlich--Zeller bound proves the claim. % lean: oversampled_chebyshev_lobatto_norming
\end{proof}
