\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:pseudo-true-ppml-projection}]
First \(T>0\) and \(\mathcal C\neq\varnothing\).  Indeed, if either the time index set or the supported cohort set were empty, the nonzero pure-treatment direction would have zero weighted quadratic form, contradicting the collapsed rank condition.
Index the supported cell table by \(z=(g,t)\), put \(q_z=q_{gt}=\pi_g/T\), \(m_z=m_{gt}(\delta)\), and let the design map send \(\theta\) to the table \((r_{gt}'\theta)_{(g,t)}\).  The nested criterion \(L(\theta;\delta)\) is exactly the ordinary finite Poisson objective on this supported-cell table.
The weights \(q_z\) are strictly positive because \(\pi_g>0\) and \(T>0\), and the means \(m_z\) are strictly positive because they are products of positive baselines and exponentials.  The same rank condition makes the supported-cell design injective: if two parameter vectors give the same index in every supported cell, their difference has zero weighted squared index sum, so rank forces the difference to be zero.
The finite Poisson existence-and-uniqueness theorem therefore gives a unique maximizer \(x^\star\) of this supported-cell objective.  Since \(L\) is the same objective under the preceding identification, the selected \(\theta^\star(\delta)\) equals \(x^\star\), and \(\theta^\star(\delta)\) is the unique global maximizer of \(L(\cdot;\delta)\).
At the maximizer, the finite Poisson score vanishes in every parameter direction \(d\):
\[
\sum_{g\in\mathcal C}\sum_{t=1}^T
q_{gt}\,(r_{gt}'d)\{m_{gt}(\delta)-\mu^\star_{gt}(\delta)\}=0 .
\]

Taking \(d\) to be the unit vector in nuisance coordinate \(j\) gives \(r_{gt}'d=X_{gt,j}\), which yields
\[
\sum_{g\in\mathcal C}\sum_{t=1}^T
q_{gt}X_{gt,j}\{m_{gt}(\delta)-\mu^\star_{gt}(\delta)\}=0 .
\]

Taking instead the pure treatment-coordinate direction gives \(r_{gt}'d=D_{gt}\), and hence
\[
\sum_{g\in\mathcal C}\sum_{t=1}^T
q_{gt}D_{gt}\{m_{gt}(\delta)-\mu^\star_{gt}(\delta)\}=0 .
\]

\end{proof}
