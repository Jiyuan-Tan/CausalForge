The paper belongs to the randomization- or design-based causal-inference tradition, in which potential outcomes are fixed and assignment is the source of randomness. Its estimator and Wald-inference objectives therefore inherit the finite-population lineage from Neyman’s randomization analysis [@Neyman1923], Horvitz–Thompson weighting [@HorvitzThompson1952], and Hájek’s asymptotic sampling theory [@Hajek1964]. The interference problem is precisely a relaxation of the no-interference component of SUTVA [@Rubin1980; @Cox1958]. Formal potential-outcome estimands under interference were developed prominently by Hudgens and Halloran [@HudgensHalloran2008], while Liu and Hudgens [@LiuHudgens2014] supplied large-sample randomization inference in two-stage interference settings. These references motivate treating coverage and conservative variance estimation as first-class design-based requirements rather than as consequences of a superpopulation outcome model.

A second strand develops general network-experiment methods. Aronow and Samii [@AronowSamii2017GeneralInterference] formulate design, exposure mapping, and estimands jointly and give randomization-based estimation under general interference; Athey, Eckles, and Imbens [@AtheyEcklesImbens2018] provide exact randomization inference for network-interference hypotheses. The literature also delineates the limits of weakly structured inference: Basse and Airoldi [@BasseAiroldi2018] show that design-based estimation can fail to concentrate under arbitrary interference, whereas Sävje, Aronow, and Hudgens [@SavjeAronowHudgens2021] establish consistency results for an estimand that averages over unknown but limited interference. Leung [@Leung2019ApproxNeighborhoodInterference] weakens strict locality through approximate-neighborhood interference. These works support graph-growth and bounded-outcome conditions for the proposed non-identically Bernoulli Hájek CLT, but do not solve the graph-only minimax probability-design problem.

Experimental-design work under interference has largely focused on correlated or clustered assignments. Graph cluster randomization [@UganderKarrerBackstromKleinberg2013GraphClusterRandomization] and its randomized extension [@UganderYin2020RandomizedGCR] target exposure probabilities and variance reduction through clustering; Eckles, Karrer, and Ugander [@EcklesKarrerUgander2017] study bias reduction from correlated assignment. Viviano [@Viviano2026NetworkDesign] instead studies a two-wave, pilot-informed design for a general network setting. Eichhorn et al. [@EichhornKhanUganderYu2024LowOrderClusteredDesigns] combine clustered designs with low-order outcome models, while Brennan, Mirrokni, and Pouget-Abadie [@BrennanMirrokniPougetAbadie2022ClusterBipartite] derive a minimax-optimal clustering objective for bounded linear interference in one-sided bipartite experiments. The proposed contribution is distinct: it retains independent Bernoulli assignment but optimizes heterogeneous intervention-unit probabilities against a bounded-potential-outcome variance envelope, and supplies an observable relaxation-and-rounding certificate rather than a clustering rule or outcome-model-specific optimum.

The immediate bipartite frontier separates treatment-assignment and outcome-measurement units. Zigler and Papadogeorgou [@ZiglerPapadogeorgou2018] introduce the bipartite interference setting; Doudchenko et al. [@DoudchenkoEtAl2020BipartiteDesign] use generalized-propensity-score ideas for bipartite causal effects and confidence sets. Pouget-Abadie et al. [@PougetAbadie2019] optimize bipartite clustering for power, and Harshaw et al. [@HarshawSavjeEisenstatMirrokniPougetAbadie2021BipartiteERL] analyze an exposure-reweighted linear estimator and a cluster-based design under a linear exposure-response model. Chattopadhyay, Imai, and Zubizarreta [@ChattopadhyayImaiZubizarreta2023GeneralizedNetwork] give design-based estimation and conservative variance estimators for stochastic interventions, including bipartite networks. Most directly, Lu et al. [@LuShiFangZhangDing2025BipartiteDesign] establish a homogeneous-Bernoulli design-based framework, a CLT, and conservative variance estimation for bipartite experiments. The present paper extends that homogeneous assignment analysis to graph-adaptive heterogeneous Bernoulli probabilities, with minimax optimization and an explicitly nonvacuous approximation certificate under degree dispersion. Its variance-bound component is complementary to optimization-based conservative variance estimation [@HarshawMiddletonSavje2021OptimizedVariance] and to the general-purpose Neyman-jackknife approach [@ParkWager2026NeymanJackknife], while its target is design selection and not merely post-assignment uncertainty quantification.
