\begin{proof}[Proof of \cref{obj:lem:denominator-positivity}]
Put
\[
K=\epsilon^{-1}\max\{1,\epsilon^{-(\bar d-1)}\},
\qquad
C=2\max\{0,\bar D\}K.
\]
Then \(C\geq0\). % lean: CausalSmith.Experimentation.BipartiteMinimaxDesign.denominatorKernelBound_nonneg

Fix \(n\geq1\) and a feasible \(q\).  Since \(|O_n|=n\), the outcome population is nonempty.  Feasibility gives \(\epsilon\leq q_k\leq1-\epsilon\), so all treated and control exposure probabilities are positive.  For
\[
X_1=D_1(q,Z),
\]
the inverse-probability weighting gives \(\mathbb E_q[X_1]=n\), and the bounded-degree and bounded-overlap assumptions give
\[
\operatorname{Var}_q(X_1)\leq n\bar D K.
\]
% lean: CausalSmith.Experimentation.BipartiteMinimaxDesign.treatDenominator_mean
% lean: CausalSmith.Experimentation.BipartiteMinimaxDesign.treatDenominator_var_le
On \(\{X_1=0\}\), one has \(\lvert X_1-\mathbb E_q[X_1]\rvert=n\).  Hence Chebyshev's inequality yields
\[
\Pr_q(D_1(q,Z)=0)
 \leq \Pr_q\!\left(n\leq\lvert X_1-\mathbb E_q[X_1]\rvert\right)
 \leq \frac{\operatorname{Var}_q(X_1)}{n^2}
 \leq \frac{\bar D K}{n}.
\]
% lean: CausalSmith.Experimentation.BipartiteMinimaxDesign.treatDenominator_zero_prob_le

The identical argument for \(X_0=D_0(q,Z)\), using the control exposure probabilities, gives
\[
\Pr_q(D_0(q,Z)=0)\leq \frac{\bar D K}{n}.
\]
% lean: CausalSmith.Experimentation.BipartiteMinimaxDesign.ctrlDenominator_zero_prob_le
Therefore, by the union bound and \(\bar D>0\),
\[
\Pr_q\!\left(D_1(q,Z)=0\ \text{or}\ D_0(q,Z)=0\right)
 \leq \frac{2\bar D K}{n}
 =\frac{C}{n}.
\]
% lean: FiniteDesign.Pr_or_le
% lean: CausalSmith.Experimentation.BipartiteMinimaxDesign.denominator_positivity
\end{proof}
