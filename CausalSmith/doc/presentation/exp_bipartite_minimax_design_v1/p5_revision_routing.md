## rewind P2
- [major·prose] (Main results, variance representation and envelope optimization) The sentence “Theorem~\ref{obj:thm:convex-design} makes the allocation problem computationally tractable” exceeds what is delivered. Convexity, compactness, and KKT conditions establish existence and characterization, but neither the theorem nor the paper provides an algorithm, complexity guarantee, numerical stability analysis, or evidence that the dense outcome-pair objective can be solved at relevant graph sizes.
- [minor·prose] (Setup and assumptions, gradient-score definition; Main results, convex design interpretation) The manuscript says the score “gives the partial derivative” and that the optimizer reallocates treatment intensity “toward intervention units according to their overlap contribution.” The derivative interpretation requires the positive interior domain, and the KKT system does not imply a simple monotone allocation by intervention degree or even by a scalar overlap contribution because the objective is coupled and the treated/control derivatives change with the budget regime.
- [minor·prose] (Introduction, surrogate contribution; Discussion and extensions) “Balanced surrogate weights” and “broadly comparable surrogate weights” are less precise than the verified impossibility theorem’s one-sided conditional condition \(h_l>0\Rightarrow h_k\le C_{\mathrm{disp}}h_l\). Readers could infer a standard two-sided positive-weight balance condition that is not what is stated.

## your call — orchestrator decides
- [major·other] (abstract; Introduction; Discussion and extensions) The paper proposes a design rule while explicitly providing “no numerical evidence on computation time, exposure frequencies, realized variance, interval width, or coverage.” That candor is welcome, but it leaves the central applied question unanswered: whether minimizing this potentially loose envelope improves any decision-relevant quantity in graph classes likely to arise in bipartite experiments. The verified results alone only rank uniform upper bounds.
- [major·citation] (Introduction and positioning) The novelty case is plausible but not yet sufficiently sharp for a leading econometrics outlet. The literature table classifies alternatives at a high level, but the paper does not isolate whether its envelope is a genuinely new minimax object, a specialization of existing optimized conservative-variance criteria, or an algebraic extension of homogeneous Bernoulli formulas. In particular, the manuscript correctly proves an upper bound but does not establish that it is the tight or least favorable bound over the stated outcome class.
- [minor·citation] (Appendix, Dependency-graph normal approximation and convex-design proof) The exposition invokes a “dependency-graph Stein central limit theorem” and a finite-dimensional KKT theorem without identifying the precise external results or spelling out their relevant hypotheses. Machine verification establishes the displayed statements, but a journal reader still needs conventional scholarly provenance and a readable map from hypotheses to the imported results.
