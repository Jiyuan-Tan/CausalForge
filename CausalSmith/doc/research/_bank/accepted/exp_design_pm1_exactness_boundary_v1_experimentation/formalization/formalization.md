## Theorems (T)

### T-block: t1 — Assume ass:two-block-homophily
**Statement.** Assume ass:two-block-homophily. Set r_cut(m,a,b,kappa)=min{2b(a+b)(1-kappa/(a-b)), 2b(2m-2b-kappa)}. If 0<=r<r_cut(m,a,b,kappa), then X_cut is the unique minimizer of F_{r,kappa} over E_m^blk. Hence Delta_m^pm(r,kappa)=0, X_cut is the unique minimizer over C_m^pm, and P_cut attains that implementable optimum. (The range 0<=r<r_cut(m,a,b,kappa) is nonempty exactly when 0<=kappa<min{a-b,2(m-b)}, which in particular requires b<m; this is a non-vacuity remark, not a hypothesis.) At the boundary r=r_cut uniqueness can fail already when kappa=0, so the theorem uses the strict inequality r<r_cut.

### T-block: t2 — Assume ass:two-block-homophily
**Statement.** Assume ass:two-block-homophily. Finite-kappa iid exactness holds only on the affine-balanced locus. More precisely, I_n is a minimizer of F_{r,kappa} over E_m^blk for some finite kappa if and only if a+3b=2m and r=2b(a+b), equivalently r=4b(m-b). On this locus, for every kappa>0, I_n is the unique relaxed minimizer and the implementable optimum is attained by P_iid, with P_iid in P_m^sym by def:block-exchangeable-design-class and X(P_iid)=I_n. Off this locus, I_n is not a minimizer for any finite kappa, although minimizers converge to I_n as kappa tends to infinity.

The oddness restriction is necessary: if m is even, the whole block elliptope slice is implementable and the claimed positive gap is false.

**Statement.** Assume ass:two-block-homophily, with r and kappa in their definitional domains. Then rho_star(m,a,b,r,kappa)=Delta_m^pm(r,kappa). Let q=2(m-1), T_m={(x,y,z): x,y,z>=0 and qx+y+z=2m}, phi_{r,kappa}(x,y,z)=c_x x+c_y y+c_z z+kappa*sqrt(qx^2+y^2+z^2), c_x=q((a+b)+r/(a+b)), c_y=2b+r/(2b), c_z=2m, alpha=(c_x/q,c_y,c_z), beta=(1/q,1,1), and d_m=0 for even m and d_m=2/m for odd m. For kappa>0 the relaxed minimizer X_rel(r,kappa) is unique and is computed by lem:weighted-simplex-active-set: among the seven nonempty supports S subset {x,y,z}, the admissible support has multiplier lambda satisfying sum_{i in S} (lambda-alpha_i)^2/beta_i=kappa^2, lambda>max_{i in S} alpha_i, and alpha_j>=lambda for j notin S, with coordinates t_i=2m*((lambda-alpha_i)/beta_i)/sum_{h in S}((lambda-alpha_h)/beta_h) on S and zero off S after the change of variables t_x=qx, t_y=y, t_z=z. For kappa=0 the relaxed argmin set is the exposed face on the alpha-minimizing coordinates. Consequently rho_star is given by this finite active-set case split (the seven relaxed active-set cases together with the truncation-segment endpoint/interior cases), and rho_star=0 if and only if the relaxed argmin set intersects {y+z>=d_m}; when kappa>0 this is equivalent to y(X_rel(r,kappa))+z(X_rel(r,kappa))>=d_m.
