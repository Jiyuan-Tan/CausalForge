\section*{eid_lingam_direction_min_order_v1 (truncated_cumulant_minimality)}

\paragraph{TL;DR.} The core has two theorem-level generic orientation results. thm:generic-apolar-arrow-recovery proves order K=2m+2 full-opposite-fiber separation on a Zariski-open real-feasible locus, strengthening the direct ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction comparator from its order-2m+3 rank criterion and differing from SchkodaRobevaDrton2024's multivariate finite-root setting. Its support-annihilator functional factors Q_D from simultaneous divided-power cumulant blocks and reads the vertical-versus-horizontal fixed axis, independently of the definition-level selector identity on M^{sep}_{m,K}. The proof of oeq:minimal-real-information-order additionally establishes K^star(m)<=2m+1 for m>=3, so the all-m equality is refuted and the open frontier is confined to small m. The original single-G_m-orbit strengthening is refuted because same-arrow fibers have omitted direct-direction swaps and, for m>=2, low-order weight kernels.

\section{Project justification}

\paragraph{Gap.} ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction provides an order-2m+3 sufficient rank criterion, but not full-fiber separation from the complete order-2m+2 truncation; SchkodaRobevaDrton2024 provides multivariate finite-root analysis, not the fixed-pair axis-conditioned order frontier. Neither supplies the present direction-preserving treatment of within-arrow source swaps or an explicit real feasibility analysis of the exceptional locus.

\paragraph{Niche.} The fixed bivariate problem has simultaneous binary-form geometry across cumulant orders that is lost when one studies a single rank matrix, and it separates source-label ambiguity from structural arrow ambiguity.

\paragraph{Fill.} The project proves full-fiber generic recovery at order 2m+2 with a support-annihilator functional, a theorem-level one-order improvement over the cited order-2m+3 rank criterion, while separating this result from the tautological feasible-fiber selector on M^{sep}_{m,K}. It also establishes the positive regime K^star(m)<=2m+1 for m>=3, records exact exceptional-locus results, and does not treat a parameter count or failed rank witness as a minimality certificate.

\section{Related work}

HoyerShimizuKerminenPalviainen2008 is foundational lvLiNGAM work: through an overcomplete-ICA population framework it shows how non-Gaussianity can support causal-effect estimation with unknown hidden variables, but it does not calibrate a finite joint-cumulant truncation or compare complete opposite-arrow fibers. SalehkaleybarGhassamiKiyavashZhang2020 studies population linear non-Gaussian models with latent variables under faithfulness, recovers causal-order information, characterizes compatible causal effects, and gives extra graphical conditions for uniqueness; it does not give an arbitrary-latent bivariate finite-order orientation frontier. These distinguish the present theorem-level question—exact full-fiber direction recovery from T_K on the stated separated bivariate domain—from earlier population-identification regimes. ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction is the direct cumulant-order comparator: Theorems 4.4-4.7 use a k=m+2 cumulant matrix whose entries reach order 2m+3; Open-ended Question 1 asks whether the full truncation through order 2m+2 strictly lowers that sufficient order, while Open-ended Question 3 tests the required lower-order failure for a minimality conclusion. ChenHuangCaiEtAl2024 is the one-latent cumulant-asymmetry prototype and informs the m=1 fourth-order specialization, but is not an arbitrary-m order-frontier comparator. CaiHuangChenEtAl2023 develops recursive recovery under a One-Latent-Component condition rather than fixed-pair order calibration. SchkodaRobevaDrton2024 supplies the closest higher-cumulant rank and fiber-analysis ancestor, but studies multivariate discovery and compatible effect sets rather than the bivariate full-fiber overlap. TramontanoKivvaSalehkaleybarDrtonKiyavash2025, Section 3 and Theorem 3.1, uses Schkoda et al.'s finite-root recovery for a specified two-observed-variable graph and adds a proxy or instrument to identify a causal-effect root; it does not compare unrestricted full opposite-arrow fibers or determine the cumulant-order frontier. CaiGaoHara2025Direction permits dependent latent-variable DAGs for full-DAG recovery and therefore informs, but does not resolve, the independent-source bivariate frontier. CaiHara2026SparsestLvLiNGAM addresses finite-sample sparsest-DAG recovery rather than the least population cumulant truncation for one arrow. Genin2021StatisticalUndecidability establishes that asymptotic orientation can fail to be unrestrictedly statistically decidable under latent confounding; it neither identifies the real compatibility locus nor proves that this project's separation restriction is necessary.

\section{Assumptions}

\begin{assumption}[ass:independent-sources]\label{ass:independent-sources}
S_0 perp S_1 perp ... perp S_{m+1}
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (independent-component source assumption in LvLiNGAM, \cite{ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:finite-cumulants]\label{ass:finite-cumulants}
for every 0<=j<=m+1, E|S_j|^K<infty
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (finite-order cumulant existence, \cite{ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:source-nongaussianity]\label{ass:source-nongaussianity}
for every 0<=j<=m+1, S_j is not Gaussian
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (non-Gaussian disturbance assumption in LiNGAM, \cite{ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:forward-axis-model]\label{ass:forward-axis-model}
(X,Y)^T=sum_{j=0}^{m+1}u_j S_j
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (bivariate linear acyclic model with latent confounders, \cite{ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:reverse-axis-model]\label{ass:reverse-axis-model}
(X,Y)^T=sum_{j=0}^{m+1}v_j S_j
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (reversed bivariate linear acyclic parameterization, \cite{ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:forward-noncollinearity]\label{ass:forward-noncollinearity}
|{gamma,rho_i:1<=i<=m}|=m+1
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (generic distinct loading directions, \cite{SchkodaRobevaDrton2024}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:reverse-noncollinearity]\label{ass:reverse-noncollinearity}
|{delta,sigma_i:1<=i<=m}|=m+1
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (generic distinct loading directions, \cite{SchkodaRobevaDrton2024}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:forward-nonzero-edge]\label{ass:forward-nonzero-edge}
gamma!=0
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (nonzero direct-edge genericity restriction, \cite{ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:reverse-nonzero-edge]\label{ass:reverse-nonzero-edge}
delta!=0
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (nonzero direct-edge genericity restriction, \cite{ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{lemma}[lem:admissible-swaps-preserve-direction]\label{lem:admissible-swaps-preserve-direction}
For each pi in G_m, Phi^right_{m,L}(pi.theta)=Phi^right_{m,L}(theta) and Phi^left_{m,L}(pi.eta)=Phi^left_{m,L}(eta). If a forward structural model M has real-feasible parameter theta, relabelling its latent sources by pi gives a forward structural model with the same observational law P_M and D(M)=X->Y; the analogous reverse statement has D(M)=Y->X. Hence quotienting a same-arrow fiber by G_m identifies only source labels and never identifies opposite arrows.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Proof. Fix pi in G_m. In the forward map, the contribution of source j at coordinate (r,a) is c_{jr}(u_{j1})^(r-a)(u_{j2})^a. The action pi merely permutes, for j=1,...,m, the pairs consisting of the loading u_j=(1,rho_j) and all of its weights (c_{jr}:2<=r<=L), while it fixes the pairs indexed by 0 and m+1. Therefore reindexing the finite sum gives [Phi^right_{m,L}(pi.theta)]_{r,a}=sum_j c_{pi(j),r}(u_{pi(j),1})^(r-a)(u_{pi(j),2})^a=sum_j c_{jr}(u_{j1})^(r-a)(u_{j2})^a=[Phi^right_{m,L}(theta)]_{r,a}. The reverse identity is identical with (sigma,d,v) in place of (rho,c,u). Now let (S_0,...,S_{m+1}) be a real-feasible forward representation. Put S'_i=S_{pi(i)} for 1<=i<=m and leave S'_0=S_0 and S'_{m+1}=S_{m+1}. Relabel the corresponding rho's in the inverse way. Independence, centering, non-Gaussianity, finiteness of the retained moments, and every source cumulant list are preserved under this bijective relabeling. After reindexing, sum_j u'_j S'_j=sum_j u_j S_j=(X,Y)^T, so the observational random vector, and hence P_M, is unchanged. The fixed indices 0 and m+1 retain their structural roles, gamma is unchanged and nonzero, and the directed structural equation is still the forward one; consequently D(M)=X->Y. The reverse argument gives D(M)=Y->X. Thus the G_m action is only a latent-source label symmetry within a fixed arrow. No element of this action converts a forward axis pattern into a reverse axis pattern, so quotienting by G_m cannot itself equate opposite directions. QED.
\end{proof}
\par\smallskip\noindent \textit{Justification.} The quotient used in the decomposition comparison is meaningful only if its allowed source relabelings do not erase the structural causal-direction target. \textit{Gap.} The source-label indeterminacy is implicit in higher-cumulant decompositions used by ChenPengHuangEtAl2025Direction and SchkodaRobevaDrton2024; neither states the bivariate direction-preservation property required for a quotient-fiber orientation claim. \textit{Consumer.} The lemma licenses the separation question to treat G_m-orbits as one within-arrow decomposition without laundering an opposite-arrow representation.

\begin{theorem}[thm:generic-apolar-arrow-recovery]\label{thm:generic-apolar-arrow-recovery}
Put n=m+2 and K=2n-2. There are Zariski-open dense sets U^right_m subset Theta^{right,circ}_{m,K} and U^left_m subset Theta^{left,circ}_{m,K}, each meeting its real feasible region in a nonempty relatively Euclidean-open set, such that R^left_{m,K}(Phi^right_{m,K}(theta)) is empty for every theta in U^right_m and R^right_{m,K}(Phi^left_{m,K}(eta)) is empty for every eta in U^left_m. More explicitly, from t=T_K(P) in either set one forms the divided-power blocks f_r(x,y)=sum_{a=0}^r binom(r,a)t_{r,a}x^(r-a)y^a; the common kernel of q |-> (q(partial)f_{n+k})_{0<=k<=n-2} is the line generated by the squarefree degree-n support annihilator Q_D=product_{ell in D}ell^perp. Factoring Q_D recovers the unordered loading-direction set D, and its vertical-versus-horizontal fixed axis decides the arrow against every full opposite-arrow fiber.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Proof. Write a degree-r cumulant block as f_r(x,y)=sum_{a=0}^r binom(r,a)t_{r,a}x^(r-a)y^a. Under either map it is sum_{j=1}^n c_{jr}ell_j^r on n distinct projective directions. For a degree-n differential symbol q, direct differentiation gives q(partial)f_{n+k}=((n+k)!/k!)sum_j c_{j,n+k}q(ell_j)ell_j^k. Evaluation E_D:q |->(q(ell_j))_j is onto by a Vandermonde minor, so its kernel is the line <Q_D>, where Q_D=product_j ell_j^perp. The stacked contractions factor through B_D(e)=(sum_j c_{j,n+k}e_jell_j^k)_{k=0}^{n-2}. At K=2n-2, B_D has generic rank n: partition the n indices into blocks J_k of size at most k+1, specialize the order-(n+k) weights to be nonzero exactly on J_k, and use the Vandermonde independence of (ell_j^k:j in J_k); an n-by-n minor is therefore a nonzero polynomial and remains nonzero on a Zariski-open locus with every retained weight nonzero. Hence the common contraction kernel is exactly <Q_D>, so Q_D and then the unordered D are recovered from t. Any full n-source opposite-arrow representation supplies its own product annihilator in that same kernel, hence has the same D. On the forward open locus all finite slopes are nonzero: D contains the vertical fixed axis and omits the horizontal fixed axis, whereas every reverse representation contains the horizontal axis. Thus the full reverse fiber is empty; coordinate reversal gives the other arrow. The rank and non-axis conditions are Zariski-open and dense. Finally, distinct nonzero real slopes together with small bounded polynomial perturbations of a positive centered compactly supported density give a relatively Euclidean-open set of independent non-Gaussian real source-cumulant lists. The nonzero rank minor cannot vanish on that open set, so both Zariski-open loci meet the stated feasible regions as claimed. QED.
\end{proof}

The theorem is therefore unconditional.

\begin{openendedquestion}[oeq:generic-separation]\label{oeq:generic-separation}
For every m>=1, does there exist a Zariski-open dense subset of each Theta^{right,circ}_{m,K} and Theta^{left,circ}_{m,K} whose images have no full opposite-arrow fiber, while each same-arrow fiber is a single G_m-orbit, and do these subsets meet F^right_{m,K} and F^left_{m,K} in nonempty relatively Euclidean-open sets? A positive answer gives generic real direction recovery from T_K modulo admissible source swaps.
\end{openendedquestion}
\begin{proof}
Negative resolution of the question as stated, together with the corrected direction-recovery result. Put n=m+2 and K=2n-2. First observe a symmetry omitted from G_m. For a forward parameter, interchange the complete source pairs (gamma,(c_{0r})_r) and (rho_i,(c_{ir})_r), leaving all other pairs fixed. Both directions are of the form (1,s), so commutativity of the defining sum shows that every coordinate of Phi^right is unchanged. This interchange is not in G_m, because G_m fixes index 0. On the nonempty Zariski-open locus rho_i!=0, it maps Theta^{right,circ}_{m,K} to itself. Distinctness of the slopes implies that the original and interchanged points cannot lie in one G_m-orbit. Hence every proposed dense open set meets a dense open locus on which a full same-arrow fiber contains at least two G_m-orbits. The reverse map has the analogous omitted symmetry between its m+1 non-horizontal directions, including the direct direction (delta,1). Already for m=1, G_1 is trivial and swapping gamma with rho_1 gives two generic points of the same forward fiber. Therefore the requested conjunction, in particular its single-G_m-orbit clause, is false for every m. There is a second obstruction when m>=2. For fixed distinct projective directions, the degree-r weight synthesis map A_r:C^n->Sym^r(C^2), (z_j)_j |-> sum_j z_j ell_j^r, has rank min(n,r+1): after choosing an affine chart its columns form a Vandermonde matrix, with the fixed-axis column supplying the omitted endpoint, and every maximal minor for distinct directions is nonzero. Thus dim ker A_r=n-r-1 for 2<=r<=n-2. Varying only (c_{jr})_j along this kernel fixes the entire stacked tensor; outside finitely many values of the variation parameter all cumulants remain nonzero. The unavoidable generic fiber dimension is therefore h=sum_{r=2}^{n-2}(n-r-1)=(n-3)(n-2)/2=m(m-1)/2. A finite G_m-orbit can never equal such a fiber. This also corrects the raw parameter count: the generic image dimension is p_K-h, and its ambient codimension is q_K-p_K+h=m+h=m(m+1)/2, not m. We next prove that the arrow-only part of the question is nevertheless true and give its computable recovery map. Encode the order-r coordinates as the divided-power binary form f_r(x,y)=sum_{a=0}^r binom(r,a)t_{r,a}x^(r-a)y^a. Under either cumulant map, f_r=sum_{j=1}^n c_{jr} ell_j^r, where ell_j is the linear form corresponding to the j-th loading direction. For a degree-n homogeneous differential symbol q and k>=0, direct differentiation gives q(partial)f_{n+k}=((n+k)!/k!) sum_j c_{j,n+k}q(ell_j)ell_j^k. For a set D of n distinct projective directions, the evaluation map E_D:Sym^n(C^2)^*->C^n, q |-> (q(ell_j))_j is onto: in an affine chart an n by n minor is Vandermonde. Its one-dimensional kernel is spanned by the squarefree annihilator Q_D=product_j ell_j^perp. Stack the displayed contractions for k=0,...,s-1, where L=n+s-1. After evaluation, the stack is B_D(e)=(sum_j c_{j,n+k}e_j ell_j^k)_{k=0}^{s-1}. Its generic rank is min(n,T_s), T_s=s(s+1)/2. Indeed, when T_s>=n, partition the n indices into sets J_k with |J_k|<=k+1, set c_{j,n+k}=0 off J_k, and choose the weights on J_k nonzero. The powers (ell_j^k:j in J_k) are independent by their Vandermonde determinant, so B_D(e)=0 forces e_j=0 successively on every J_k. Thus one n by n minor is a nonzero polynomial; perturbing the zero weights proves the same rank on a Zariski-open set where all weights are nonzero. The analogous selection of only T_s coordinates proves rank T_s when T_s<n. At K=2n-2 one has s=n-1 and T_s=n(n-1)/2>=n. Consequently the common kernel of all contractions q(partial)f_{n+k}, 0<=k<=n-2, is exactly ker E_D=<Q_D>. Factoring this uniquely recovered binary form is the simultaneous-binary-form recovery map: it returns the unordered set D of loading directions. Any full n-source alternative representation has its own degree-n product annihilator Q_{D'}, and the same differentiation identity puts Q_{D'} in the recovered one-dimensional kernel. Hence Q_{D'} is proportional to Q_D and, since feasible loading directions are distinct, D'=D as unordered projective sets. On the forward Zariski-open set where every finite slope is nonzero, D contains the vertical axis fixed by the forward map but not the horizontal axis; every reverse representation must contain the horizontal axis. Thus no reverse full fiber exists. Coordinate reversal proves the converse. These rank and non-axis conditions define Zariski-open dense subsets. They meet each real feasible region in nonempty relatively Euclidean-open sets: choose distinct nonzero real slopes; for source cumulants, start with a strictly positive compactly supported density on a symmetric interval and choose bounded polynomial perturbations h_2,...,h_K orthogonal to 1 and x whose moment matrix against x^2,...,x^K is nonsingular. Densities f_epsilon=f_0(1+sum epsilon_r h_r) stay positive and centered for small epsilon, and their moments, hence cumulants by the triangular moment-cumulant change of coordinates, fill an open set in R^(K-1). They are non-Gaussian because they are nondegenerate and compactly supported. Taking independent copies yields an open subset of F. A nonzero real maximal-minor polynomial cannot vanish on this open set, so the apolar rank-open locus meets it. In summary, generic real arrow recovery at order K is valid by factoring Q_D, but the frozen claim that the complete same-arrow parameter fiber is one G_m-orbit is false. The exact generic same-arrow fiber instead has m+1 label components modulo G_m and, for m>=2, each has dimension h from the low-degree weight kernels. QED.
\end{proof}

Consequently the exact full-fiber incidence system is

       =d_{0r}1{a=0}+sum_{i=1}^m d_{ir}sigma_i^(r-a)+d_{m+1,r}delta^(r-a)                 (A_{r,a})

for 2<=r<=2m+2 and 0<=a<=r. Let Delta_right and Delta_left be the two nonvanishing products in def:generic-parameter-loci. 2. Dimension of either arrow-image variety. Fix pairwise distinct finite forward slopes s_0,...,s_{n-2}. At order r the weight-synthesis columns are V_r(s_0),...,V_r(s_{n-2}),e_r. For 2<=r<=n-2 their rank is r+1, since the finite columns contain a square Vandermonde minor. At r=n-1 their rank is n: use the finite-column Vandermonde minor in rows 0,...,n-2 and then the last-coordinate pivot e_r. Their rank remains n for every r>=n. Hence, for fixed slopes, the independent block dimensions sum to

sum_{r=2}^{n-1}(r+1)+sum_{r=n}^{d}n=n(n+1)/2-3+n(n-1).

The n-1 finite slopes add n-1 dimensions. This is an actual rank calculation, not only a parameter count. In the top block r=d, the derivatives with respect to its n weights and the n-1 finite slopes are, up to the nonzero weight factors,

V_d(s_0),V'_d(s_0),...,V_d(s_{n-2}),V'_d(s_{n-2}),e_d.

In rows 0,...,d-1 the first 2n-2 columns form a square confluent Vandermonde matrix. It is nonsingular: a vector in its left kernel would give a polynomial of degree at most 2n-3 having a double zero at each of the n-1 distinct s_j, hence at least 2n-2 zeros counted with multiplicity, and therefore the zero polynomial. The e_d column is zero in these rows and one in row d. Thus the top-block Jacobian rank is 2n-1. Weight columns from lower orders have disjoint coordinate support; adjoining bases of their block images and projecting first to the top block shows that all these columns remain independent. Therefore the total Jacobian rank is

D:=n(n+1)/2-3+n(n-1)+(n-1)=(3n^2+n)/2-4.                 (B)

Conversely, for every 2<=r<=n-2 the order-r weight map has kernel dimension n-r-1, independently across orders. Every parameter fiber therefore has dimension at least

h=sum_{r=2}^{n-2}(n-r-1)=(n-2)(n-3)/2.

Since the parameter-space dimension is p_K=nd-1, the image dimension is at most p_K-h=D. The displayed nonzero Jacobian minor gives the reverse inequality (equivalently, its selected output coordinates are locally independent), so dim C^right_{m,K}=D. Interchanging the two coordinate axes gives dim C^left_{m,K}=D. Notice that q_K-D=(n-1)(n-2)/2: the nominal count q_K-p_K=n-2 misses exactly the h low-order weight-kernel dimensions.

d_{m+1,r}=c_{0r}gamma^r.                                                        (C)

The ranges beginning at i=2 are empty for m=1. Because the forward finite slopes are distinct, gamma and rho_i for i>=2 are nonzero. Reciprocation preserves distinctness; delta is nonzero and distinct from every sigma_i; and every d_{jr} is nonzero. Thus the constructed reverse point is generic. For every r,

d_{0r}x^r+d_{1r}y^r+sum_{i=2}^m d_{ir}(sigma_i x+y)^r+d_{m+1,r}(delta x+y)^r
=c_{1r}x^r+c_{m+1,r}y^r+sum_{i=2}^m c_{ir}(x+rho_i y)^r+c_{0r}(x+gamma y)^r,

which is the forward form when rho_1=0. Hence this divisor maps into E_m, and the twin agrees at every cumulant order, not merely through K.

The image of this divisor has dimension D-1. The upper bound follows because the same h independent low-order weight kernels remain on a parameter divisor of dimension p_K-1. For the lower bound, delete V'_d(0) from the top-block Jacobian in Step 2. In rows 0,...,2n-4 the remaining 2n-3 finite columns form a confluent Vandermonde system with multiplicity one at 0 and multiplicity two at each other finite slope; the same zero-multiplicity argument proves nonsingularity. Adjoining e_d and row d gives top-block rank 2n-2, and adjoining the lower block images gives total rank D-1. Consequently dim barE_m>=D-1.

4. Properness of the exceptional closure. Each arrow-image variety is irreducible. Indeed, its parameter space is affine space and hence irreducible: if affine space were V(I) union V(J), then V(IJ) would be all of affine space, so IJ=(0) in a polynomial domain, forcing I=(0) or J=(0). A polynomial image of an irreducible space is irreducible in the subspace topology, because the inverse images of a decomposition into two closed subsets would decompose the domain; its closure is therefore irreducible.

We next prove that the two arrow-image varieties are distinct without assuming uniqueness of a single even-degree Waring decomposition. Encode a block t_r by the divided-power binary form

f_r(x,y)=sum_{a=0}^r binom(r,a)t_{r,a}x^(r-a)y^a.

A representation on projective directions ell_1,...,ell_n is f_r=sum_j c_{jr}ell_j^r. If q is a homogeneous differential symbol of degree n, direct differentiation gives, for k>=0,

q(partial_x,partial_y)f_{n+k}=((n+k)!/k!)sum_j c_{j,n+k}q(ell_j)ell_j^k.          (D)

For n distinct directions D={ell_j}, evaluation q |->(q(ell_j))_j from the n+1 dimensional space of degree-n symbols onto C^n is surjective by a Vandermonde minor. Its kernel is one-dimensional and is spanned by the squarefree support annihilator Q_D=product_j ell_j^perp. Stack (D) for k=0,...,n-2. On evaluation vectors this yields

B_D(e)=(sum_j c_{j,n+k}e_j ell_j^k)_{k=0}^{n-2}.

The map B_D is injective for a nonempty Zariski-open set of high-order weights. To exhibit one full-rank specialization, partition the n source indices into sets J_k with |J_k|<=k+1, set c_{j,n+k}=0 off J_k, and take it nonzero on J_k. The powers {ell_j^k:j in J_k} are independent by a Vandermonde minor, so B_D(e)=0 forces e_j=0 successively for every j. Thus some n-by-n minor is a nonzero polynomial in the weights. A small algebraic perturbation makes all weights nonzero while preserving that minor. On this open set the common kernel of the contractions (D) is exactly <Q_D>.

Degenerate opposite fibers cannot evade this certificate. For the top form f_d, d=2n-2, the middle catalecticant is, up to invertible binomial diagonal factors,

Cat_{n-1}(f_d)=V diag(c_{1d},...,c_{nd})V^T,

where V is the n-by-n Vandermonde evaluation matrix. At distinct directions with nonzero top weights this matrix has rank n. A sum of h powers has catalecticant rank at most h, since each power contributes a rank-one matrix. Hence any alternative representation using at most n listed terms must use exactly n nonzero terms on n distinct directions. Its support annihilator Q_{D'} belongs to the common contraction kernel, so Q_{D'} is proportional to Q_D and D'=D.

Choose a forward support D containing the vertical axis, with all finite slopes nonzero, and choose its weights in the preceding nonempty rank-open set. Let H_x be the n-dimensional space of degree-n differential symbols divisible by partial_y, the factor annihilating the horizontal direction x. Every reverse parameter list contains the horizontal direction. Therefore the product of the perpendicular factors to its n listed directions is a nonzero member of H_x and, term by term in (D), lies in the common contraction kernel. This remains true if directions coincide or weights vanish. Hence every n-by-n minor of the stacked contraction map restricted to H_x vanishes polynomially on the whole reverse image and therefore on C^left_{m,K}. For the chosen forward point the full common kernel is <Q_D>. Since its support omits the horizontal direction, Q_D is not divisible by partial_y, so <Q_D> intersects H_x only at zero. The restricted contraction map is injective there, and one of the same n-by-n minors is nonzero. Thus C^right_{m,K} is not contained in C^left_{m,K}; coordinate reversal also gives the reverse noncontainment.

The two arrow varieties are therefore distinct irreducible varieties of the same dimension D. Their intersection is a proper closed subset of each and has dimension at most D-1: any chain of irreducible closed subsets inside the intersection extends by adjoining the ambient irreducible arrow variety. Since E_m is contained in that intersection, dim barE_m<=D-1. Combined with Step 3,

codim_{C^right_{m,K}}(barE_m)=codim_{C^left_{m,K}}(barE_m)=1.                 (E)

This proves that the requested codimension m is impossible for every m>=2.

5. Exact parameter-level characterization. If theta is in Theta^{right,circ}_{m,K}, its right fiber over Phi^right_{m,K}(theta) is automatically nonempty. Unfolding def:generic-full-fiber-compatibility therefore gives

theta in H^right_m iff R^left_{m,K}(Phi^right_{m,K}(theta)) is nonempty.

The axis-reversed argument gives

eta in H^left_m iff R^right_{m,K}(Phi^left_{m,K}(eta)) is nonempty.

Thus H^right_m and H^left_m are exactly the respective generic parameter points retaining a full opposite-arrow representation.

\begin{theorem}[thm:exact-real-exceptional-atlas]\label{thm:exact-real-exceptional-atlas}
