Fix \(K,m,u\), and let \(\gamma\in\{1,\ldots,K-1\}\). Set \(r=K-\gamma\), so \(1\le r\le K-1\). By Definition \(\mathrm{def:dimension-functions}\), increasing \(\gamma\) to \(\gamma+1\) adds exactly the summand indexed by \(j=r\) to \(h_{K,m}\). Therefore
\[
h_{K,m}(\gamma+1)-h_{K,m}(\gamma)=\binom Kr(m-1)^r.
\]
The same definition gives
\[
\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)=h_{K,m}(\gamma)-(2u-1)m^r
\]
and
\[
\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)=h_{K,m}(\gamma+1)-(2u-1)m^{r-1}.
\]
Subtracting and using the displayed increment yields
\[
\begin{aligned}
\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)-\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)
&=\binom Kr(m-1)^r+(2u-1)(m^r-m^{r-1})\\
&=\binom Kr(m-1)^r+(2u-1)(m-1)m^{r-1}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Here \(\binom Kr>0\), \(m-1>0\), \(m^{r-1}>0\), and \(2u-1>0\), because \(1\le r\le K-1\), \(m\ge2\), and \(u\ge1\). Thus the difference is strictly positive, proving
\[
\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)>\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma).
\]
Now suppose \(g=\gamma_{\mathrm{dim}}(K,m,u)\) is finite. Definition \(\mathrm{def:dimension-frontier}\) gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(g)\ge0\), while minimality of \(g\) gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(s)<0\) for every \(s<g\). Strict monotonicity gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(s)>\Delta_{K,m,u}(g)\ge0\) for every \(s>g\). Consequently,
\[
\{s\in\{1,\ldots,K\}:\Delta_{K,m,u}(s)\ge0\}=\{g,g+1,\ldots,K\},
\]
so \(g\) is the unique boundary between negative and nonnegative dimensional slack.

Finally, fix \(s<g\), \(\alpha\in\mathcal A_s\), and \(P\in\mathcal P_{s,\alpha}\) satisfying Assumption \(\mathrm{ass:full-cell-support}\). The boundary characterization gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(s)<0\). Applying Theorem \(\mathrm{thm:universal-rank-barrier}\) with \(\gamma=s\) yields
\[
\operatorname{rank}M_s(P,\alpha)<Q_{K,m,u}(s),
\]
and hence the fortified pairing map is noninjective at every such law. This is precisely the asserted universal rank barrier for every selected valid-subset component below \(g\).
