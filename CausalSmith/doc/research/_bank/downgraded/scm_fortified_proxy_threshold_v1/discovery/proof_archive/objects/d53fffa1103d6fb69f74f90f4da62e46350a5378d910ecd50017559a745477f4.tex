Fix \(K,m,u\), and let \(\gamma\in\{1,\ldots,K-1\}\). Put \(r=K-\gamma\), so \(1\le r\le K-1\). From Definition \(\mathrm{def:dimension-functions}\), increasing \(\gamma\) to \(\gamma+1\) adds precisely the interaction term of order \(r\) to \(h_{K,m}\). Hence
\[h_{K,m}(\gamma+1)-h_{K,m}(\gamma)=\binom Kr(m-1)^r.\]
The same definition gives
\[\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)=h_{K,m}(\gamma)-(2u-1)m^r\]
and
\[\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)=h_{K,m}(\gamma+1)-(2u-1)m^{r-1}.\]
Subtracting the first identity from the second and using the preceding increment of \(h_{K,m}\) yields
\[\begin{aligned}
\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)-\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)
&=\binom Kr(m-1)^r+(2u-1)(m^r-m^{r-1})\\
&=\binom Kr(m-1)^r+(2u-1)(m-1)m^{r-1}.
\end{aligned}\]
Because \(1\le r\le K-1\), \(m\ge2\), and \(u\ge1\), both summands in the final expression are strictly positive. Therefore
\[\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)>\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)\]
for every \(\gamma\in\{1,\ldots,K-1\}\).

Now suppose that \(g=\gamma_{\mathrm{dim}}(K,m,u)\) is finite. By Definition \(\mathrm{def:dimension-frontier}\), \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(g)\ge0\), while minimality of \(g\) implies \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(s)<0\) for every \(s\in\{1,\ldots,g-1\}\). Strict monotonicity implies \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(s)>\Delta_{K,m,u}(g)\ge0\) for every \(s\in\{g+1,\ldots,K\}\). Consequently,
\[\{s\in\{1,\ldots,K\}:\Delta_{K,m,u}(s)\ge0\}=\{g,g+1,\ldots,K\},\]
so \(g\) is the unique boundary at which the slack changes from negative to nonnegative.

Finally, take any \(s<g\), any \(\alpha\in\mathcal A_s\), and any law \(P\in\mathcal P_{s,\alpha}\) satisfying Assumption \(\mathrm{ass:full-cell-support}\). The boundary characterization gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(s)<0\). Theorem \(\mathrm{thm:universal-rank-barrier}\), applied with \(\gamma=s\), therefore gives
\[\operatorname{rank}M_s(P,\alpha)<Q_{K,m,u}(s),\]
and hence the asserted universal rank barrier at every such law in every selected valid-subset component of cardinality below \(g\).
