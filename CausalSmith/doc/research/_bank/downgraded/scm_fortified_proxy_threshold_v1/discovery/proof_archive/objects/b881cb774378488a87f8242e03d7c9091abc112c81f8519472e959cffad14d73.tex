Fix \(K,m,u\), take \(\gamma\in\{1,\ldots,K-1\}\), and set \(r=K-\gamma\), so \(r\ge 1\). By Definition \(\mathrm{def:dimension-functions}\), increasing \(\gamma\) by one adds exactly the summand with interaction order \(r\), and therefore
\[h_{K,m}(\gamma+1)-h_{K,m}(\gamma)=\binom{K}{r}(m-1)^r.\]
The same definition gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)=h_{K,m}(\gamma)-(2u-1)m^r\) and \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)=h_{K,m}(\gamma+1)-(2u-1)m^{r-1}\). Subtraction yields
\[\begin{aligned}\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)-\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)&=\binom{K}{r}(m-1)^r+(2u-1)(m^r-m^{r-1})\\&=\binom{K}{r}(m-1)^r+(2u-1)(m-1)m^{r-1}>0.\end{aligned}\]
Indeed, \(K\ge2\), \(1\le r\le K-1\), \(m\ge2\), and \(u\ge1\), so both displayed summands are nonnegative and in fact each is positive. Thus \(\gamma\mapsto\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)\) is strictly increasing on \(\{1,\ldots,K\}\).

Suppose that \(\gamma_{\mathrm{dim}}(K,m,u)\) is finite and write \(g=\gamma_{\mathrm{dim}}(K,m,u)\). By Definition \(\mathrm{def:dimension-frontier}\), \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(g)\ge0\), whereas minimality gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(r)<0\) for every integer \(r<g\) in \(\{1,\ldots,K\}\). Strict monotonicity further gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(r)>\Delta_{K,m,u}(g)\ge0\) for every \(r>g\). Hence \(g\) is the unique boundary between negative and nonnegative slack. Finally, fix any cardinality \(r<g\), any \(\alpha\in\mathcal A_r\), and any \(P\in\mathcal P_{r,\alpha}\) satisfying Assumption \(\mathrm{ass:full-cell-support}\). The preceding argument gives \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(r)<0\), so Theorem \(\mathrm{thm:universal-rank-barrier}\) applies and yields the asserted universal rank barrier at \(P\).
