Fix \(K,m,u,\gamma,\alpha\) as in the statement, and put \(r=K-\gamma\), \(B=Z_\alpha\), \(C=C_\alpha\), \(n=m^\gamma\), and \(c=m^r\). Thus \(B\) has \(n\) cells, \(C\) has \(c\) cells, and \(nc=m^K\). Relabeling proxy coordinates if necessary does not change any of the spaces or dimensions, so the argument applies to every fixed \(\alpha\in\mathcal A_\gamma\).

We first construct the law of \((U,A,Z)\). Set \(P(A=a,B=b,C=x)=1/(2nc)\) at every cell. Under the weighted-array bijection in Theorem \(\mathrm{thm:weighted-dimension}\), the orthogonal complement of the image of \(\mathcal H_\gamma(P)\), with respect to the ordinary Euclidean pairing of arrays, is
\[
\langle\mathbf 1_A\rangle\otimes\mathcal L_r,\qquad\mathcal L_r=\bigoplus_{\substack{S\subseteq[K]\\ |S|\le r}}\left(\bigotimes_{i\in S}E_i^0\right)\otimes\left(\bigotimes_{i\notin S}\langle\mathbf 1_i\rangle\right).
\]
Indeed, Theorem \(\mathrm{thm:weighted-dimension}\) places every treatment-contrast tensor and every treatment-constant proxy interaction of order greater than \(r\) in the image, leaving exactly the displayed treatment-constant interactions of order at most \(r\). Define
\[
\mathcal N=\left\{\rho\in\mathcal L_r:\sum_b\rho(b,x)=0\text{ for every cell }x\text{ of }C\right\}.
\]
The subspace of \(\mathcal L_r\) consisting of arbitrary functions of \(C\) has dimension \(c\): every interaction supported only on the \(r\) complement coordinates has order at most \(r\). Averaging over \(B\) maps \(\mathcal L_r\) onto that subspace, with kernel \(\mathcal N\). Since the ambient \((A,Z)\)-array space has dimension \(2nc\), Theorem \(\mathrm{thm:weighted-dimension}\) and Definition \(\mathrm{def:dimension-functions}\) give
\[
\dim\mathcal L_r=2nc-h_{K,m}(\gamma),\qquad \dim\mathcal N=2nc-h_{K,m}(\gamma)-c=2nc-D_{K,m}(\gamma).
\]
In particular, \(\mathcal N\) is a subspace of the direct sum of \(c\) copies of the \((n-1)\)-dimensional zero-sum space
\[
V_x=\left\{v\in\mathbb R^n:\sum_bv_b=0\right\}.
\]
Permuting the \(m\) levels separately in each of the \(r\) coordinates of \(C\) preserves interaction order, preserves \(\mathcal N\), and acts transitively on the \(c\) cells of \(C\).

The inclusion of all functions of \(C\) in \(\mathcal L_r\) also yields \(\dim\mathcal L_r\ge c\), whence
\[
D_{K,m}(\gamma)=2nc-\dim\mathcal L_r+c\le2nc.
\]
Because the assumed slack inequality is \(D_{K,m}(\gamma)\ge2uc\), it follows that \(u\le n\). Put
\[
e=\max\{0,2u-n-1\},\qquad d=n-1.
\]
Then \(0\le e\le d\). If \(e=0\), the evident inclusion \(\mathcal N\subseteq\bigoplus_xV_x\) gives \(\dim\mathcal N\le cd=c(d-e)\). If \(e>0\), the slack inequality gives
\[
\dim\mathcal N=2nc-D_{K,m}(\gamma)\le2c(n-u)=c(n-1-e)=c(d-e).
\]
Lemma \(\mathrm{lem:transitive-block-avoidance}\) therefore supplies subspaces \(E_x\subseteq V_x\), each of dimension \(e\), such that
\[
\mathcal N\cap\bigoplus_xE_x=\{0\}.
\]

For each cell \(x\) of \(C\), let
\[
J_x=\langle\mathbf 1_B\rangle\oplus E_x,\qquad j=\dim J_x=e+1=\max\{1,2u-n\}.
\]
There exist \(u\)-dimensional subspaces \(L_{0x},L_{1x}\subseteq\mathbb R^n\) containing \(\mathbf 1_B\) and satisfying \(L_{0x}\cap L_{1x}=J_x\). To see this, work in \(\mathbb R^n/J_x\) and choose two disjoint subspaces of dimension \(u-j\). This is possible because
\[
2(u-j)\le n-j;
\]
when \(2u\le n+1\), this is \(2(u-1)\le n-1\), and when \(2u>n+1\), equality holds because \(j=2u-n\). Lift the two quotient subspaces and adjoin \(J_x\). By Lemma \(\mathrm{lem:positive-stochastic-subspace-realization}\), for every \(a\in\{0,1\}\) and \(x\) there is a strictly positive row-stochastic \(n\)-by-\(u\) matrix \(\Pi_{a,x}\) of column rank \(u\) whose column space is \(L_{ax}\). Define
\[
P(U=v\mid A=a,B=b,C=x)=\Pi_{a,x}(b,v).
\]
Every cell of \((U,A,Z)\) now has strictly positive mass, so Assumption \(\mathrm{ass:full-cell-support}\) holds.

We prove that the pairing map is injective. Let \(g\in\mathcal G_\alpha(P)\) lie in \(\ker\mathsf T_{P,\alpha}\), and write
\[
R_g(a,b,x)=\mathbb E\{g(U,A,C)\mid A=a,B=b,C=x\}=\sum_v\Pi_{a,x}(b,v)g(v,a,x).
\]
For \(s\in\mathcal H_\gamma(P)\), the weighted-array identity gives
\[
0=\mathbb E\{s(A,Z)g(U,A,C)\}=\sum_{a,b,x}q_s(a,b,x)R_g(a,b,x).
\]
Thus \(R_g\) lies in the orthogonal complement displayed above. There is consequently a \(\rho\in\mathcal L_r\) such that \(R_g(a,b,x)=\rho(b,x)\), independently of \(a\). Pairing also vanishes against every \(s=f(C)\in L_P^2(C)\). By iterated expectation and the uniform law of \((A,B,C)\),
\[
0=\mathbb E\{f(C)g(U,A,C)\}=\frac1c\sum_x f(x)\left\{\frac1n\sum_b\rho(b,x)\right\}
\]
for every \(f\). Hence \(\rho\in\mathcal N\). On the other hand, for every \(a,x\), the slice \(R_g(a,\cdot,x)\) belongs to \(\operatorname{col}(\Pi_{a,x})=L_{ax}\). Since the two treatment-arm slices are both \(\rho(\cdot,x)\),
\[
\rho(\cdot,x)\in L_{0x}\cap L_{1x}=\langle\mathbf1_B\rangle\oplus E_x.
\]
Its zero sum over \(b\) forces \(\rho(\cdot,x)\in E_x\). Therefore \(\rho\in\mathcal N\cap\bigoplus_xE_x=\{0\}\), so \(R_g=0\). Each \(\Pi_{a,x}\) has column rank \(u\), and hence \(g(\cdot,a,x)=0\) for every \(a,x\). This proves \(g=0\), so \(\mathsf T_{P,\alpha}\) is injective. Its demand space has dimension \(Q_{K,m,u}(\gamma)\) by Theorem \(\mathrm{thm:weighted-dimension}\); equivalently,
\[
\operatorname{rank}M_\gamma(P,\alpha)=Q_{K,m,u}(\gamma).
\]

It remains to complete the law and prove genericity. Take \(Y(0)\), \(Y(1)\), and \(W\) to be mutually independent nondegenerate Bernoulli variables, independent of \((U,A,Z)\), and set \(Y=Y(A)\). Consistency holds by construction. Conditional on \((U,C)\), each potential outcome is independent of \(A\); conditional on \((A,U,C)\), the observed outcome is independent of \(Z_\alpha\); and conditional on \((U,C)\), \(W\) is independent of \((A,Z_\alpha)\). Thus all member properties in Definition \(\mathrm{def:selected-component}\) hold, and the constructed full-data law belongs to \(\mathcal P_{\gamma,\alpha}\).

Finally fix cell-indicator bases of the demand space and of \(L_P^2(C)\), and fix a basis \((q_j)\) of the weighted-array image of \(\mathcal H_\gamma(P)\) given by Theorem \(\mathrm{thm:weighted-dimension}\). On the positive table parameter space use \(s_j=q_j/p_{AZ}\) for the corresponding basis of \(\mathcal H_\gamma(P)\). Entries of the resulting pairing matrix are rational functions of the conditional-table coordinates, and their denominators are products of strictly positive cell probabilities. After those denominators are cleared, every \(Q_{K,m,u}(\gamma)\)-minor has a polynomial numerator. At the law just constructed at least one such minor is nonzero. Therefore the common zero set of all maximal-minor numerators is a proper real algebraic subset of the compatible factor-table parameter space in Definition \(\mathrm{def:compatible-minor-handle}\). The selected-validity completion does not change these minors, which depend only on the law of \((U,A,Z)\), and the independent completion above shows that the nonzero minor occurs inside the compatible parameter space. The complement is nonempty and Zariski open; a proper real algebraic subset has empty Euclidean interior and Lebesgue measure zero in each free factor-table chart. This proves the genericity assertion.

As an endpoint check, if \(\gamma=K\), then \(c=1\), \(r=0\), and \(\mathcal N=\{0\}\). The argument says precisely that the two matrices \(\Pi_a=[P(U=v\mid A=a,Z=z)]_{z,v}\) must each have column rank \(u\), which is attainable exactly when \(u\le m^K\); this is equivalent to \(D_{K,m}(K)\ge Q_{K,m,u}(K)\).
