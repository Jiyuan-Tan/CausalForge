Fix \(K,m,u\), let \(\gamma\in\{1,\ldots,K-1\}\), and put \(r=K-\gamma\), so \(r\ge1\). From the definition of \(h_{K,m}\), increasing \(\gamma\) by one adds precisely the interaction order \(r\), whence
\[h_{K,m}(\gamma+1)-h_{K,m}(\gamma)=\binom Kr(m-1)^r.\]
Since \(\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)=h_{K,m}(\gamma)-(2u-1)m^r\), it follows that
\[\begin{aligned}\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma+1)-\Delta_{K,m,u}(\gamma)&=\binom Kr(m-1)^r+(2u-1)\bigl(m^r-m^{r-1}\bigr)\\&=\binom Kr(m-1)^r+(2u-1)(m-1)m^{r-1}>0.\end{aligned}\]
Strict positivity uses \(m\ge2\), \(u\ge1\), and \(r\ge1\). Thus the slack is strictly increasing over \([K]\). If \(\gamma_{\mathrm{dim}}(K,m,u)\) is finite, its definition and strict monotonicity imply that the slack is negative at every smaller cardinality and nonnegative at that cardinality and every larger one. Hence it is the unique boundary between the two regimes. For every smaller cardinality, every choice of the selected subset and every law in its selected component satisfying full-cell support has negative slack, so the universal-rank-barrier theorem applies.
