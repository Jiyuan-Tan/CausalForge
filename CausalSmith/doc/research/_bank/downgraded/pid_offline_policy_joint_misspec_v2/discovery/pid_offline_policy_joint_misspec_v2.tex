\section*{pid_offline_policy_joint_misspec (v2)}

\paragraph{TL;DR.} The proposal studies offline policy value under two simultaneous misspecification restrictions on the same latent arm law: an MSM budget on the treatment assignment distortion and a fixed-center symmetric conditional-quantile tube on the potential-outcome distribution. The additive mean-tube branch remains rectangular once one writes the correct nonempty-intersection condition. The quantile-tube branch does not: its sharp endpoint is the mean-maximizing jointly feasible law in the intersection of the MSM weight class and the two-sided quantile tube, which reduces to the published Hess/Frauen MSM-only endpoint when the tube is inactive and otherwise tightens it. The rectangularity gap is the failure of the pointwise clipped envelope to be representable by one jointly feasible law, and the semiparametric rung asks for a one-step correction built on the unique upper contact face of the coupled optimizer while treating the center law as a fixed analyst input.

\section{Project justification}

\paragraph{Gap.} The literature contains sharp MSM off-policy learning, mean-side joint sensitivity, and newer distributional sensitivity enrichments, but not the fixed-center quantile-process intersection that this topic needs. HessFrauenMelnychukFeuerriegel2026 Proposition 4.1 and Theorem 4.3 give the MSM-only sharp endpoint and one-step estimator, DornGuo2023 supplies the bounded-weight quantile geometry, MastenPoirierRen2025 gives sharp QTE and DTE bounds for a broad relaxation class including marginal sensitivity, and Wang2026 gives process-level sharp nested quantile envelopes with semiparametric inference under source confounding plus transport shift. None of these papers fixes an analyst-specified center conditional quantile path on the same latent arm law and then studies offline policy value under that pathwise restriction.

\paragraph{Niche.} The niche is a narrow correction to a named sharp OPE workflow. It is not a generic warning about combining restrictions. The distinctive object is the difference between a pointwise clipped quantile envelope and a single latent arm law that must satisfy both the MSM likelihood-ratio window and the symmetric center-law quantile tube.

\paragraph{Fill.} The fill is a three-part package: a corrected mean benchmark with an explicit emptiness condition; a coupled quantile-law endpoint that reduces to the Hess/Frauen frontier when the tube is inactive and otherwise tightens it without collapsing to the single-population or transport comparators; and an optimizer-face conjecture for a one-step estimator that separates regular from nonregular inference while treating the center law as fixed.

\section{Related work}

Tan2006 supplies the marginal sensitivity primitive and bounded-likelihood-ratio interpretation used to define the latent arm-law class. DornGuo2023 is the closest identification ancestor because it turns sharp MSM bounds into quantile geometry under bounded weights; that geometry is reused here, but Dorn and Guo do not add a fixed analyst-centered quantile tube or an offline-policy aggregation step. KallusZhou2021 and HessFrauenMelnychukFeuerriegel2026 are the closest policy-facing comparators: they solve the MSM-only off-policy problem, with Hess et al. deriving the closed-form sharp endpoint in Proposition 4.1 and the one-step estimator in Theorem 4.3, but they do not impose a fixed-center conditional quantile path on the same latent law. ZhangXuTan2025 is the closest mean-side joint-sensitivity comparator because it intersects treatment and outcome restrictions through an enhanced MSM mean bound, yet its outcome restriction is not a fixed-center quantile-process tube. ZhangXuTan2025deMSM is the closest law-level comparator because deMSM adds potential-outcome distribution-shift density-ratio constraints and proves sharper bounds through implied MSM parameters, but it does not anchor the latent law to an analyst-chosen center quantile function and does not study offline policy value. MastenPoirierRen2025 is the closest single-population quantile-sensitivity threat because it provides sharp QTE and DTE bounds for a broad relaxation class that includes the marginal sensitivity model, but its target is treatment-effect functionals rather than a policy-value endpoint under a fixed center-law tube. Wang2026 is the closest semiparametric quantile-envelope threat because it proves process-level sharpness, canonical gradients, and nonregular inference for nested sensitivity envelopes, but its model is a two-sample transport problem with a source-to-target shift layer rather than a fixed analyst-centered tube in one-sample offline policy evaluation. YadlowskyNamkoongBasuDuchiTian2022 explains why mean-side restrictions can stay scalar, which is exactly the benchmark formalized in the first theorem. Firpo2007 and FanPark2010 provide the regular inverse-CDF and partial-identification quantile templates needed for the contact-face correction and the endpoint caveats. ChernozhukovEtAl2018 and AtheyWager2021 supply the orthogonality and policy-value inference templates that the conjectured one-step estimator adapts rather than replaces.

\section{Assumptions}

\begin{assumption}[ass:consistency]\label{ass:consistency}
Y = Y(A) almost surely
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (consistency / SUTVA, \cite{ImbensRubin2015}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:positivity]\label{ass:positivity}
e_a(X) > 0 almost surely on {x : pi(a|x) > 0} for every a in A
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (policy overlap, \cite{AtheyWager2021}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:bounded-support]\label{ass:bounded-support}
L_a(X) <= Y(a) <= U_a(X) almost surely for every a in A
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (bounded outcomes, \cite{Manski1990}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:msm]\label{ass:msm}
for every a in A and every y in [L_a(x), U_a(x)], Gamma^{-1} <= [P(A=a | Y(a)=y, X=x) / (1 - P(A=a | Y(a)=y, X=x))] / [e_a(x) / (1 - e_a(x))] <= Gamma
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (marginal sensitivity model, \cite{Tan2006}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:center-support]\label{ass:center-support}
L_a(x) <= Q0_a(u|x) <= U_a(x) for every a in A, every x, and almost every u in (0,1)
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Novel.} A fixed analyst-specified center law must live on the same reward support as the latent arm law it is meant to approximate. This is realistic in bounded-reward bandits where the center law is chosen from a calibrated simulator, transport reference, or benchmark observational fit on the same support.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:joint-feasible]\label{ass:joint-feasible}
for every a in A and P_X-almost every x with pi(a|x) > 0, there exists w in W_a^Gamma(x) such that sup_{u in (0,1)} |Q_a^w(u|x) - Q0_a(u|x)| <= epsilon(x)
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Novel.} The joint model is only meaningful on laws where the outcome-side tube and the treatment-side MSM are simultaneously satisfiable. This holds, for example, whenever the center law itself is generated by an admissible bounded likelihood-ratio tilt or when epsilon dominates the discrepancy between the center law and one feasible MSM law.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:quantile-regularity]\label{ass:quantile-regularity}
for every a in A, Fobs_a(.|x) is continuous and strictly increasing on neighborhoods of the quantile levels that enter Qcpl_a_plus and Qcpl_a_minus, with conditional density fobs_a bounded away from 0 and inf on those neighborhoods
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (regular quantile inversion, \cite{Firpo2007}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:regular-contact]\label{ass:regular-contact}
for every a in A and P_X-almost every x with pi(a|x) > 0, the optimizer in def:qcpl-plus is unique up to almost-everywhere equality, the contact set A_a_plus(x) is a finite union of interior intervals of (0,1), and fobs_a is continuous and strictly positive at the corresponding contact values Q0_a(u|x) + epsilon(x)
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Novel.} Smooth bounded-support location-shift, scale-shift, and monotone-rearrangement families typically generate a unique clipped maximizer whose contact region is a finite union of interior rank intervals. This is the regular regime in which an influence-function expansion can be expected to be stable.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:rate-msm]\label{ass:rate-msm}
the nuisance bundle eta_MSM is estimable at rate r_MSM,n uniformly over a neighborhood of the true law
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (cross-fit nuisance-rate condition for a baseline orthogonal score, \cite{ChernozhukovEtAl2018}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:rate-fobs]\label{ass:rate-fobs}
fobs_a is L2-estimable at rate r_f,n on the upper contact region
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Novel.} Positive-density conditional reward models permit local smoothing or sieve density estimation on the same interior region where inverse-CDF derivatives are standard. Restricting the rate to the realized contact region matches the derivative object that enters the conjectured score.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:rate-product]\label{ass:rate-product}
sqrt(n) * r_MSM,n * r_f,n -> 0
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (cross-fit product-rate condition for orthogonal scores, \cite{ChernozhukovEtAl2018}).
\end{assumption}

$MSM feasible weight class := W_a^Gamma(x) = { w : [L_a(x),U_a(x)] -> [0,inf) : ell_a(x) <= w(y) <= u_a(x) for every y in [L_a(x),U_a(x)] and int_{L_a(x)}^{U_a(x)} w(y) dFobs_a(y|x) = 1 }$ --- the class of all objects satisfying: ass:positivity; ass:bounded-support; ass:msm.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:mean-tube-class]\label{def:mean-tube-class}
$additive mean-tube benchmark class := { m : A x X -> R : for every a in A and x, |m(a,x) - m0_a(x)| <= epsilon(x) }$ --- the class of all objects satisfying: .
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:quantile-tube-class]\label{def:quantile-tube-class}
$fixed-center conditional quantile tube := { Q : A x (0,1) x X -> R : for every a in A and x, Q(a,.,x) is nondecreasing, L_a(x) <= Q(a,u,x) <= U_a(x), and sup_{u in (0,1)} |Q(a,u,x) - Q0_a(u|x)| <= epsilon(x) }$ --- the class of all objects satisfying: ass:bounded-support; ass:center-support.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:qmsm-plus]\label{def:qmsm-plus}
$MSM pointwise upper quantile envelope := Qmsm_a_plus(u|x) = sup_{w in W_a^Gamma(x)} Q_a^w(u|x)$ \quad (\text{inputs: } W_a^Gamma, Q_a^w)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:qmsm-minus]\label{def:qmsm-minus}
$MSM pointwise lower quantile envelope := Qmsm_a_minus(u|x) = inf_{w in W_a^Gamma(x)} Q_a^w(u|x)$ \quad (\text{inputs: } W_a^Gamma, Q_a^w)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:qrect-plus]\label{def:qrect-plus}
$pointwise rectangular upper envelope := Qrect_a_plus(u|x) = min{Qmsm_a_plus(u|x), Q0_a(u|x) + epsilon(x), U_a(x)}$ \quad (\text{inputs: } Qmsm_a_plus, Q0_a, epsilon, U_a)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:qrect-minus]\label{def:qrect-minus}
$pointwise rectangular lower envelope := Qrect_a_minus(u|x) = max{Qmsm_a_minus(u|x), Q0_a(u|x) - epsilon(x), L_a(x)}$ \quad (\text{inputs: } Qmsm_a_minus, Q0_a, epsilon, L_a)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:qcpl-plus]\label{def:qcpl-plus}
$coupled upper quantile map := Qcpl_a_plus(.|x) = arg max_{q} { int_0^1 q(u) du : there exists w in W_a^Gamma(x) such that q(u) = Q_a^w(u|x) for almost every u in (0,1) and Q0_a(u|x) - epsilon(x) <= q(u) <= Q0_a(u|x) + epsilon(x) for almost every u in (0,1) }$ \quad (\text{inputs: } W_a^Gamma, Q_a^w, Q0_a, epsilon)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:qcpl-minus]\label{def:qcpl-minus}
$coupled lower quantile map := Qcpl_a_minus(.|x) = arg min_{q} { int_0^1 q(u) du : there exists w in W_a^Gamma(x) such that q(u) = Q_a^w(u|x) for almost every u in (0,1) and Q0_a(u|x) - epsilon(x) <= q(u) <= Q0_a(u|x) + epsilon(x) for almost every u in (0,1) }$ \quad (\text{inputs: } W_a^Gamma, Q_a^w, Q0_a, epsilon)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:vrect-plus]\label{def:vrect-plus}
$rectangular upper endpoint := V_rect_plus = E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) int_0^1 Qrect_a_plus(u|X) du]$ \quad (\text{inputs: } pi, Qrect_a_plus)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:vrect-minus]\label{def:vrect-minus}
$rectangular lower endpoint := V_rect_minus = E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) int_0^1 Qrect_a_minus(u|X) du]$ \quad (\text{inputs: } pi, Qrect_a_minus)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:delta-rect]\label{def:delta-rect}
$non-rectangularity gap := Delta_rect(Gamma,epsilon;pi) = V_rect_plus - V_cpl_plus$ \quad (\text{inputs: } V_rect_plus, V_cpl_plus)

$active tube-contact influence adjustment := Let c_a(u,x) = Q0_a(u|x) + epsilon(x). \end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:chat-plus]\label{def:chat-plus}

\end{definition}

$candidate coupled EIF := phi_cpl_plus(O;eta) = phi_MSM_plus(O;eta_MSM) - phi_tube_plus(O;eta)$ \quad (\text{inputs: } phi_MSM_plus, eta_MSM, phi_tube_plus)
\end{definition}

\section{Main results}

\begin{theorem}[thm:mean-decoupling]\label{thm:mean-decoupling}
Under ass:consistency, ass:positivity, ass:bounded-support, ass:msm, and def:mean-tube-class, the armwise joint feasible mean set is [M_a_minus(x), M_a_plus(x)] intersect [m0_a(x) - epsilon(x), m0_a(x) + epsilon(x)] for each a in A and x. Hence the additive-mean-tube identified set for V(pi) is nonempty if and only if max{M_a_minus(x), m0_a(x) - epsilon(x)} <= min{M_a_plus(x), m0_a(x) + epsilon(x)} for P_X-almost every policy-relevant arm. Conditional on this nonemptiness event, the sharp upper endpoint is E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) min{M_a_plus(X), m0_a(X) + epsilon(X)}] and the sharp lower endpoint is E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) max{M_a_minus(X), m0_a(X) - epsilon(X)}].
\end{theorem}
The proof is pointwise in (a,x) until the final policy-value aggregation. Fix an action a and a covariate value x, and abbreviate W = W_a^Gamma(x), m0 = m0_a(x), eps = epsilon(x), L = L_a(x), U = U_a(x), and m(w) = m_a^w(x) = int_L^U y w(y) dFobs_a(y|x). Define the MSM-only feasible mean set S_a(x) = {m(w) : w in W}. Under the additive mean tube, the joint feasible armwise mean set is by definition J_a(x) = {m(w) : w in W and |m(w) - m0| <= eps} = S_a(x) intersect [m0 - eps, m0 + eps]. Thus it suffices to identify S_a(x).

By Lemma lem:msm-mean-image-interval, S_a(x) is the closed interval [M_a_minus(x), M_a_plus(x)]. Therefore
J_a(x) = [M_a_minus(x), M_a_plus(x)] intersect [m0_a(x) - epsilon(x), m0_a(x) + epsilon(x)].
This proves the first claim. In particular, J_a(x) is nonempty if and only if
max{M_a_minus(x), m0_a(x) - epsilon(x)} <= min{M_a_plus(x), m0_a(x) + epsilon(x)}.

Now pass from armwise means to the policy value. Let m_a(X) denote any jointly feasible armwise mean profile under the additive mean tube on the policy-relevant region {pi(a|X) > 0}. By consistency, Y = Y(A) almost surely, so for any candidate latent law the causal target satisfies
V(pi) = E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) Y(a)] = E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) E[Y(a) | X]].
Writing E[Y(a) | X = x] as m_a(x), every feasible policy value has the form
E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) m_a(X)]
with m_a(X) in J_a(X) almost surely on the policy-relevant region. Hence the additive-mean-tube identified set is nonempty if and only if J_a(x) is nonempty for P_X-almost every x and every action a with pi(a|x) > 0; equivalently,
max{M_a_minus(x), m0_a(x) - epsilon(x)} <= min{M_a_plus(x), m0_a(x) + epsilon(x)}
for P_X-almost every policy-relevant arm.

Assume this nonemptiness condition holds. Define the pointwise upper and lower feasible means
bar m_a(x) = min{M_a_plus(x), m0_a(x) + epsilon(x)},
underline m_a(x) = max{M_a_minus(x), m0_a(x) - epsilon(x)}.
Because J_a(x) is exactly the interval displayed above, both bar m_a(x) and underline m_a(x) belong to J_a(x). Therefore they are pointwise feasible armwise means. Since pi(a|x) >= 0 for every a and x, any feasible profile m_a(x) in J_a(x) obeys
m_a(x) <= bar m_a(x),    m_a(x) >= underline m_a(x),
and thus
E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) m_a(X)] <= E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) bar m_a(X)]
and
E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) m_a(X)] >= E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) underline m_a(X)].
So these are valid upper and lower bounds on the identified set.

They are sharp because the mean restriction is purely scalar and imposes no cross-u path coupling: for each policy-relevant (a,x), the endpoint values bar m_a(x) and underline m_a(x) lie in the feasible interval J_a(x). Hence the mean profiles x -> bar m_a(x) and x -> underline m_a(x) are themselves admissible members of the additive mean-tube benchmark on the policy-relevant region. Plugging those extremal feasible profiles into the linear functional m -> E[sum_a pi(a|X) m_a(X)] attains the displayed upper and lower bounds. Consequently the sharp upper endpoint is
E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) min{M_a_plus(X), m0_a(X) + epsilon(X)}],
and the sharp lower endpoint is
E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) max{M_a_minus(X), m0_a(X) - epsilon(X)}].

This is the advertised decoupling: with an additive mean tube, the joint restriction acts only through scalar truncation of the MSM armwise mean interval plus the explicit emptiness check. No further process-level compatibility condition appears. QED.

\begin{theorem}[thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint]\label{thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint}
Under ass:consistency, ass:positivity, ass:bounded-support, ass:msm, ass:center-support, ass:joint-feasible, and ass:quantile-regularity, the sharp joint policy-value endpoints under the symmetric fixed-center conditional-quantile tube are V_cpl_plus = E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) int_0^1 Qcpl_a_plus(u|X) du] and V_cpl_minus = E[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) int_0^1 Qcpl_a_minus(u|X) du], where Qcpl_a_plus and Qcpl_a_minus solve the symmetric tube-constrained programs in def:qcpl-plus and def:qcpl-minus. The pointwise caps Qrect_a_plus and Qrect_a_minus are valid rectangular outer envelopes, but the sharp endpoint requires one latent arm law to satisfy both the MSM weight window and the full two-sided quantile-tube path constraint simultaneously. Moreover V_cpl_plus <= V_MSM_plus. Equality holds if and only if for P_X-almost every x and every action a with pi(a|x) > 0, there exists w_a^*(.|x) in W_a^Gamma(x) such that m_a^{w_a^*}(x) = M_a_plus(x) and sup_{u in (0,1)} |Q_a^{w_a^*}(u|x) - Q0_a(u|x)| <= epsilon(x).
\end{theorem}
We prove the upper endpoint; the lower endpoint is the same argument with every supremum replaced by an infimum, every upper bound replaced by a lower bound, and every minimum replaced by a maximum.

Fix an action a and a covariate value x. Write
\[
\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x):=\Big\{w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x): \sup_{u\in(0,1)}\big|Q_a^w(u\mid x)-Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)\big|\le \epsilon(x)\Big\}.
\]
By ass:joint-feasible, this set is nonempty for \(P_X\)-almost every x with \(\pi(a\mid x)>0\). By Lemma \(\text{lem:joint-feasible-extrema-attained}\), the linear mean map \(w\mapsto m_a^w(x)\) attains its supremum and infimum on \(\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\). Let \(w_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\) be a maximizer, and set \(q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x):=Q_a^{w_{a,+}^*}(u\mid x)\). By construction, \(q_{a,+}^*\) is feasible for the program in def:qcpl-plus. By Lemma \(\text{lem:quantile-mean-identity}\),
\[
\int_0^1 q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x)\,du = \int_0^1 Q_a^{w_{a,+}^*}(u\mid x)\,du = m_a^{w_{a,+}^*}(x)=\sup_{w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)} m_a^w(x).
\]
Hence the armwise coupled upper value is exactly
\[
C_a^+(x)=\sup_{w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)} m_a^w(x)=\int_0^1 Q_{\mathrm{cpl},a}^+(u\mid x)\,du,
\]
and similarly
\[
C_a^-(x)=\inf_{w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)} m_a^w(x)=\int_0^1 Q_{\mathrm{cpl},a}^-(u\mid x)\,du.
\]
This discharges the optimizer existence that is implicit in def:qcpl-plus and def:qcpl-minus.

Now take any jointly feasible weight \(w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\), and write \(q(u):=Q_a^w(u\mid x)\). Because \(w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\), def:qmsm-plus and def:qmsm-minus imply
\[
Q_{\mathrm{msm},a}^-(u\mid x)\le q(u)\le Q_{\mathrm{msm},a}^+(u\mid x)
\]
for almost every u. Because \(w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\), the tube constraint gives
\[
Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)-\epsilon(x)\le q(u)\le Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)+\epsilon(x)
\]
almost everywhere. Because \(w\) is an MSM-feasible weight and ass:bounded-support holds, the induced latent arm law is supported on \([L_a(x),U_a(x)]\), so
\[
L_a(x)\le q(u)\le U_a(x)
\]
almost everywhere. Combining the three displays yields the pointwise rectangular envelope bounds
\[
Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^-(u\mid x)\le q(u)\le Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(u\mid x)
\]
almost everywhere, where the endpoints are exactly those in def:qrect-minus and def:qrect-plus. Thus the rectangular caps are valid outer envelopes. They are only outer envelopes because the three coordinatewise inequalities can be generated by different weights; sharpness requires one and the same latent arm law to satisfy the weight window and the whole quantile-tube path simultaneously.

Because \(\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\subseteq W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\),
\[
C_a^+(x)=\sup_{w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)} m_a^w(x)\le \sup_{w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x)} m_a^w(x)=M_a^+(x).
\]
This proves the armwise inequality \(C_a^+(x)\le M_a^+(x)\). Moreover, if there exists \(w_a^*(\cdot\mid x)\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\) such that \(m_a^{w_a^*}(x)=M_a^+(x)\) and \(\sup_u |Q_a^{w_a^*}(u\mid x)-Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)|\le \epsilon(x)\), then \(w_a^*\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\), so
\[
C_a^+(x)\ge m_a^{w_a^*}(x)=M_a^+(x),
\]
and therefore \(C_a^+(x)=M_a^+(x)\). Conversely, if \(C_a^+(x)=M_a^+(x)\), then any maximizer \(w_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\) from the first paragraph satisfies
\[
m_a^{w_{a,+}^*}(x)=C_a^+(x)=M_a^+(x),
\]
so some MSM mean-maximizing latent law is jointly tube-feasible. This is the exact armwise equality criterion.

We now aggregate over arms and covariates. Under ass:consistency,
\[
V(\pi)=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)Y(a)\Big]=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)E\{Y(a)\mid X\}\Big].
\]
For fixed \((a,x)\), the feasible conditional mean values are exactly \(\{m_a^w(x):w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\}\). The restrictions are armwise and conditional on X, and the policy objective is linear in those conditional means, so the sharp upper endpoint is obtained by taking the conditional supremum armwise and then averaging:
\[
\sup V(\pi)=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)C_a^+(X)\Big]=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\int_0^1 Q_{\mathrm{cpl},a}^+(u\mid X)\,du\Big]=V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+.
\]
The same argument gives the sharp lower endpoint
\[
\inf V(\pi)=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)C_a^-(X)\Big]=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\int_0^1 Q_{\mathrm{cpl},a}^-(u\mid X)\,du\Big]=V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^-.
\]
Integrating the pointwise outer envelopes yields the rectangular outer interval \([V_{\mathrm{rect}}^-,V_{\mathrm{rect}}^+]\).

Finally,
\[
V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)C_a^+(X)\Big]\le E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)M_a^+(X)\Big]=V_{\mathrm{MSM}}^+.
\]
If the armwise equality criterion holds for every \((a,x)\) with \(\pi(a\mid x)>0\), then the expectation inequality is an equality. Conversely, if \(V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+=V_{\mathrm{MSM}}^+\), then
\[
0=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\{M_a^+(X)-C_a^+(X)\}\Big],
\]
where each summand is nonnegative. Therefore \(M_a^+(x)=C_a^+(x)\) for \(P_X\)-almost every x and every action a with \(\pi(a\mid x)>0\), and the armwise criterion above applies. This proves the theorem.
\par\smallskip\noindent \textit{Justification.} This is the kernel result: it defines the sharp endpoint for the symmetric tube and states the exact frontier against the published MSM-only endpoint. \textit{Gap.} HessFrauenMelnychukFeuerriegel2026 Proposition 4.1 gives the MSM-only sharp off-policy endpoint V_MSM_plus and DornGuo2023 supplies the bounded-weight quantile geometry used to characterize its extremizer. MastenPoirierRen2025 gives sharp QTE and DTE bounds for broad relaxations including the marginal sensitivity model, and Wang2026 gives process-level nested quantile envelopes under source confounding plus transport shift, but neither paper targets offline policy value under a fixed analyst-centered conditional-quantile tube on the same latent arm law. The theorem therefore reduces exactly to the published Hess/Frauen frontier when the tube is inactive and otherwise strictly tightens that frontier within the fixed-center tube model. \textit{Consumer.} It supplies the population correction to the Hess/Frauen sharp upper endpoint: analysts can keep the published target when the MSM extremizer already lies inside the tube and otherwise must replace it by the coupled endpoint.

\begin{proposition}[prop:rect-gap]\label{prop:rect-gap}
Under the hypotheses of thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint, Delta_rect(Gamma,epsilon;pi) >= 0. Moreover, Delta_rect(Gamma,epsilon;pi) = 0 if and only if for P_X-almost every x and every action a with pi(a|x) > 0, the pointwise rectangular envelope Qrect_a_plus(.|x) is itself representable as Q_a^w(.|x) for some w in W_a^Gamma(x) that also satisfies sup_{u in (0,1)} |Q_a^w(u|x) - Q0_a(u|x)| <= epsilon(x). If there is a set of x with positive P_X-probability and an action a with pi(a|x) > 0 such that no jointly feasible w in W_a^Gamma(x) generates Qrect_a_plus(.|x), then Delta_rect(Gamma,epsilon;pi) > 0 and the coupled identified set is a strict subset of the rectangular envelope based on V_rect_plus and V_rect_minus.
\end{proposition}
Fix an action a and a covariate value x, and define the armwise rectangularity gap
\[
g_a(x):=\int_0^1 Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(u\mid x)\,du-C_a^+(x).
\]
By thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint, every jointly feasible quantile path lies pointwise below \(Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(\cdot\mid x)\), while \(C_a^+(x)\) is the maximum integral over the jointly feasible class. Hence \(g_a(x)\ge 0\) for every policy-relevant arm and \(P_X\)-almost every x. Using def:vrect-plus and def:delta-rect,
\[
\Delta_{\mathrm{rect}}(\Gamma,\epsilon;\pi)=V_{\mathrm{rect}}^+-V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)g_a(X)\Big]\ge 0.
\]
This proves nonnegativity.

We next prove the equality criterion. Suppose first that for a fixed \((a,x)\), the rectangular envelope itself is representable: there exists \(w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\) such that
\[
Q_a^w(\cdot\mid x)=Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(\cdot\mid x)
\]
almost everywhere and \(\sup_u |Q_a^w(u\mid x)-Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)|\le \epsilon(x)\). Then \(w\) is jointly feasible. Because every jointly feasible quantile path is pointwise bounded above by \(Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+\), no jointly feasible path can have a larger integral. Hence
\[
C_a^+(x)=\int_0^1 Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(u\mid x)\,du,
\]
so \(g_a(x)=0\).

Conversely, suppose \(g_a(x)=0\). By thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint and Lemma \(\text{lem:joint-feasible-extrema-attained}\), there exists a jointly feasible maximizer \(w_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\) with coupled quantile path \(q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x):=Q_a^{w_{a,+}^*}(u\mid x)\) satisfying
\[
C_a^+(x)=\int_0^1 q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x)\,du.
\]
The theorem already gives \(q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x)\le Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(u\mid x)\) almost everywhere. Since the difference is nonnegative and has integral zero,
\[
\int_0^1 \big(Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(u\mid x)-q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x)\big)\,du=0,
\]
it follows that \(Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(\cdot\mid x)=q_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\) almost everywhere. Therefore the rectangular envelope is itself generated by the jointly feasible weight \(w_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\). We have proved the pointwise equivalence
\[
g_a(x)=0 \iff Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(\cdot\mid x)\text{ is representable by some jointly feasible }w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x).
\]

Now aggregate. Since each summand \(\pi(a\mid X)g_a(X)\) is nonnegative,
\[
\Delta_{\mathrm{rect}}(\Gamma,\epsilon;\pi)=0
\]
if and only if \(g_a(X)=0\) for \(P_X\)-almost every x and every action with \(\pi(a\mid x)>0\). By the pointwise equivalence, this is exactly the representability condition stated in the proposition.

If there exists a measurable set S with \(P_X(S)>0\) and an action a such that \(\pi(a\mid x)>0\) on S and no jointly feasible weight generates \(Q_{\mathrm{rect},a}^+(\cdot\mid x)\) for x in S, then \(g_a(x)>0\) on S. Therefore
\[
\Delta_{\mathrm{rect}}(\Gamma,\epsilon;\pi)\ge E\big[\pi(a\mid X)g_a(X)\mathbf 1\{X\in S\}\big]>0.
\]
By thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint, \(V_{\mathrm{rect}}^-\le V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^-\) always holds, so strict inequality in the upper endpoint implies
\[
[V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^-,V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+]\subsetneq [V_{\mathrm{rect}}^-,V_{\mathrm{rect}}^+].
\]
Hence the coupled identified set is a strict subset of the rectangular envelope whenever such a nonrepresentability region exists.
\par\smallskip\noindent \textit{Justification.} The proposition isolates the representability obstruction behind rectangular widening and pins strictness to a single-law feasibility test. \textit{Gap.} The v1 bank had only a generic no-go warning. None of HessFrauenMelnychukFeuerriegel2026, MastenPoirierRen2025, or Wang2026 states an equality-versus-strictness criterion against a rectangular post-processing of a named sharp off-policy endpoint, because their published envelopes are already sharp within their own single sensitivity models rather than an intersection with a fixed analyst-centered tube. The precise novelty here is the representability test for when the clipped pointwise envelope is or is not induced by one jointly feasible latent law. \textit{Consumer.} It yields a diagnostic for whether rectangular post-processing of a sharp MSM workflow is exact or whether it pastes together incompatible quantile ranks from different latent laws.

\begin{conjecture}[conj:eif-one-step]\label{conj:eif-one-step}

\end{conjecture}
We prove the corrected round-5 scope of the conjecture: on the regular-contact stratum the coupled upper endpoint is pathwise differentiable along submodels that preserve the unique active upper face, its efficient influence function is phi_cpl_plus = phi_MSM_plus - phi_tube_plus with phi_tube_plus defined through the latent optimizing law, the cross-fit plug-in estimator is consistent, and outside that stratum the endpoint is generally only directionally differentiable.

Fix a regular parametric submodel {P_t} through P = P_0 with score s(O), and assume the submodel preserves the unique upper contact face from ass:regular-contact. By thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint, V_cpl_plus = E[sum_a pi(a|X) C_a_plus(X)], where C_a_plus(x) = sup_{w in W_a^cap(x)} m_a^w(x) and W_a^cap(x) is the set of MSM-feasible weights whose induced quantile path stays within the symmetric tube around Q0_a. Thus the problem is to differentiate a supremal-value functional on a stratum where the active optimizer and active face are fixed.

Lemma lem:contact-face-eif-decomposition computes that derivative. The MSM-only upper value V_MSM_plus is pathwise differentiable with efficient influence function phi_MSM_plus by Hess, Frauen, Melnychuk, and Feuerriegel (2026, Thm. 4.4). The coupled problem adds the pathwise constraint Q_a^w(u|x) <= Q0_a(u|x) + epsilon(x) on the active upper face. Because ass:regular-contact gives a unique optimizer and a finite union of interior contact intervals, the envelope theorem of Milgrom and Segal (2002, Thm. 1) applies on this stratum: the first derivative of the constrained supremal value is obtained by differentiating the objective at the active optimizer, and the first-order motion of the optimizer itself does not contribute.

Let w_{a,+}^*(.|x) be the unique active optimizer, let G_{a,+}^*(.|x) be its latent CDF, let g_{a,+}^*(.|x) be its density on the regular region, and write c_a(u,x) = Q0_a(u|x) + epsilon(x). On the active upper face A_a_plus(x), the binding equation is G_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,x)|x) = u. Firpo's inverse-CDF derivative formula (Firpo 2007) applied to the latent optimizing law gives the free quantile derivative on the active face as - dotG_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,x)|x) / g_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,x)|x). The derivative dotG_{a,+}^* is the full derivative of the latent optimizing law, so it contains both the weighted empirical-CDF perturbation and the first-order perturbation of the active optimizing weight, equivalently the weighted-density term. This is the load-bearing correction from the escalation log: centering at the observed-arm law alone would differentiate the wrong CDF.

Integrating the blocked free motion over the active face yields exactly phi_tube_plus, and Lemma lem:contact-face-eif-decomposition therefore gives d/dt V_cpl_plus(P_t) at t = 0 as E[(phi_MSM_plus(O;eta_MSM) - phi_tube_plus(O;eta)) s(O)]. Hence V_cpl_plus is pathwise differentiable on every submodel that preserves the unique active face, and phi_cpl_plus = phi_MSM_plus - phi_tube_plus is its canonical gradient. Since the canonical gradient is the efficient influence function on the given tangent space, this proves the EIF claim on the regular-contact stratum.

Lemma lem:contact-face-centering identifies the centering that makes this correction orthogonal. On the active face, Q_{a,+}^*(u|x) = c_a(u,x), and because Q_{a,+}^* is the quantile function of G_{a,+}^*, we have G_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,x)|x) = u. Therefore the centered contact score 1{Y_{a,+}^* <= c_a(u,X)} - u has conditional mean zero under the latent optimizing law. The centering is under G_{a,+}^*, not under Fobs_a. This explains why the optimizing-weight term is necessary and why the correction disappears when the tube is inactive: if A_a_plus(X) is empty, then phi_tube_plus is zero and phi_cpl_plus reduces exactly to the Hess-Frauen MSM EIF.

For estimation, Lemma lem:chat-plus-consistency proves that the cross-fit plug-in estimator P_n[sum_a pi(a|X) Chat_a_plus(X)] converges in probability to V_cpl_plus when Q0_a and epsilon are treated as fixed known inputs. The proof uses the compact jointly feasible class, uniqueness of the regular optimizer, consistency of the MSM and observed-density nuisance blocks supplied by ass:rate-msm and ass:rate-fobs, and dominated convergence under bounded support.

Finally, if the unique active face ceases to be well-defined, or if a contact boundary collides with the support edge, the max/inverse-CDF map develops a kink. In that regime the Milgrom-Segal envelope argument no longer yields an ordinary derivative because multiple active faces compete, and Hirano and Porter (2012) show that such argmax-type functionals are generically nonregular at face-switching points. Likewise, once the contact level reaches the support boundary, the interior positive-density inverse-CDF expansion used above is unavailable. Therefore the endpoint is then, in general, only directionally differentiable, and inference must move from a regular EIF expansion to directional or conservative procedures.

\begin{lemma}[lem:msm-mean-image-interval]\label{lem:msm-mean-image-interval}
Fix an action a and a covariate value x. Under def:msm-weight-class and ass:bounded-support, the set S_a(x) = {m_a^w(x) : w in W_a^Gamma(x)} of MSM-feasible conditional means is a nonempty compact interval. Consequently S_a(x) = [M_a_minus(x), M_a_plus(x)], and both endpoints are attained by some feasible weights in W_a^Gamma(x).
\end{lemma}
Fix a and x, and abbreviate L = L_a(x), U = U_a(x), F = Fobs_a(.|x), W = W_a^Gamma(x), and m(w) = int_L^U y w(y) dF(y). We prove nonemptiness, convexity, and compactness in turn.

Nonemptiness: the constant weight w_0(y) = 1 is feasible. Indeed, because Gamma >= 1, the lower cap ell_a(x) = e_a(x) + (1 - e_a(x)) / Gamma satisfies ell_a(x) <= e_a(x) + (1 - e_a(x)) = 1, and the upper cap u_a(x) = e_a(x) + Gamma (1 - e_a(x)) satisfies u_a(x) >= e_a(x) + (1 - e_a(x)) = 1. Also int_L^U 1 dF(y) = 1. Hence w_0 belongs to W.

Convexity: if w_1, w_2 belong to W and lambda belongs to [0,1], then w_lambda = lambda w_1 + (1 - lambda) w_2 still satisfies the same pointwise box constraints ell_a(x) <= w_lambda(y) <= u_a(x) and the same normalization int_L^U w_lambda dF = lambda + (1 - lambda) = 1. Therefore W is convex. Since m(w) is linear in w, the image S_a(x) = m(W) is a convex subset of R, hence an interval.

Boundedness: ass:bounded-support gives L <= Y(a) <= U almost surely. Every feasible w is nonnegative and integrates to one against F, so m(w) is the mean of a probability distribution supported on [L,U]. Therefore L <= m(w) <= U for every w in W. Thus S_a(x) is a bounded interval.

Compactness and attainment: view W as a subset of L_infinity(F). The pointwise bounds ell_a(x) <= w <= u_a(x) make W norm-bounded in L_infinity(F), and the normalization int_L^U w dF = 1 is affine and weak-* closed. By Banach-Alaoglu, the closed L_infinity ball is weak-* compact; intersecting with the weak-* closed normalization slice shows W is weak-* compact. Because y is bounded on [L,U], we have y in L_1(F), so the map w -> m(w) = int_L^U y w(y) dF(y) is weak-* continuous on L_infinity(F). The continuous image of a compact set is compact, hence S_a(x) is compact. Since S_a(x) is also an interval, it is a compact interval [s_-(x), s_+(x)] whose endpoints are attained by some feasible weights.

By the definitions M_a_minus(x) = inf_{w in W} m(w) and M_a_plus(x) = sup_{w in W} m(w), the attained endpoints satisfy s_-(x) = M_a_minus(x) and s_+(x) = M_a_plus(x). Therefore S_a(x) = [M_a_minus(x), M_a_plus(x)], with both endpoints attained. QED.

\begin{lemma}[lem:quantile-mean-identity]\label{lem:quantile-mean-identity}
Fix an arm a, a covariate value x, and a weight w in W_a^Gamma(x). Then int_0^1 Q_a^w(u|x) du = m_a^w(x).
\end{lemma}
Fix an arm \(a\), a covariate value \(x\), and a weight \(w\in W_a^\Gamma(x)\). Define the weighted conditional law
\[
G_{a,w,x}(y):=\int_{L_a(x)}^y w(t\mid x)\,dF^{\mathrm{obs}}_a(t\mid x).
\]
By `def:msm-weight-class`, \(w\) is nonnegative and satisfies \(\int w\,dF^{\mathrm{obs}}_a=1\), so \(G_{a,w,x}\) is a probability distribution on \([L_a(x),U_a(x)]\). By the definition of \(Q_a^w\), this law has quantile function \(u\mapsto Q_a^w(u\mid x)\).

Because the support is bounded, the usual quantile representation of expectation applies:
\[
\int_0^1 Q_a^w(u\mid x)\,du
= \int_{L_a(x)}^{U_a(x)} y\, dG_{a,w,x}(y).
\]
Substituting the definition of \(G_{a,w,x}\) gives
\[
\int_{L_a(x)}^{U_a(x)} y\, dG_{a,w,x}(y)
= \int_{L_a(x)}^{U_a(x)} y\, w(y\mid x)\, dF^{\mathrm{obs}}_a(y\mid x)
= m_a^w(x).
\]
Therefore
\[
\int_0^1 Q_a^w(u\mid x)\,du = m_a^w(x).
\]
This is the desired identity.

\begin{lemma}[lem:joint-feasible-extrema-attained]\label{lem:joint-feasible-extrema-attained}
Fix an action a and a covariate value x. Let W_a^cap(x) = {w in W_a^Gamma(x) : sup_{u in (0,1)} |Q_a^w(u|x) - Q0_a(u|x)| <= epsilon(x)}. Under ass:bounded-support, def:msm-weight-class, and ass:joint-feasible, the set W_a^cap(x) is nonempty and compact for the weak topology induced by bounded measurable test functions under Fobs_a(.|x). The map w -> m_a^w(x) is continuous on W_a^cap(x). Consequently the coupled armwise supremum and infimum over jointly feasible weights are attained.
\end{lemma}
Fix \(a\) and \(x\), and abbreviate \(F:=F_{\mathrm{obs},a}(\cdot\mid x)\), \(L:=L_a(x)\), \(U:=U_a(x)\), \(\ell:=\ell_a(x)\), and \(\upsilon:=u_a(x)\). Write
\[
\mathcal W^{\cap}:=\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)=\Big\{w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x):\sup_u |Q_a^w(u\mid x)-Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)|\le \epsilon(x)\Big\}.
\]
Nonemptiness is exactly ass:joint-feasible.

We prove compactness. By def:msm-weight-class,
\[
W_a^{\Gamma}(x)=\Big\{w:[L,U]\to[0,\infty): \ell\le w(y)\le \upsilon\ \text{for every }y,\ \int_L^U w(y)\,dF(y)=1\Big\}.
\]
Thus every feasible weight lies in the order interval \([\ell,\upsilon]\subset L_{\infty}(F)\). This interval is weak-* compact by Banach-Alaoglu, and the normalization map \(w\mapsto \int w\,dF\) is weak-* continuous because the constant function 1 is an \(L_1(F)\) test function. Hence \(W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\) is weak-* compact.

It remains to show that the tube restriction is closed. Let \(w_n\in\mathcal W^{\cap}\) and suppose \(w_n\to w\) weak-* in \(L_{\infty}(F)\). For every y, the indicator \(\mathbf 1\{t\le y\}\) is an \(L_1(F)\) test function, so
\[
G_n(y):=\int_L^y w_n(t)\,dF(t) \longrightarrow G(y):=\int_L^y w(t)\,dF(t).
\]
Because \(w_n\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\) and \(W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\) is weak-* closed, we already know \(w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\).

Now fix \(u\in(0,1)\), and write \(c_+(u):=Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)+\epsilon(x)\) and \(c_-(u):=Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)-\epsilon(x)\). Since \(Q_a^{w_n}(u\mid x)\le c_+(u)\), the quantile definition implies \(G_n(c_+(u))\ge u\) for every n. Passing to the limit gives \(G(c_+(u))\ge u\), hence \(Q_a^w(u\mid x)\le c_+(u)\).

For the lower side, \(Q_a^{w_n}(u\mid x)\ge c_-(u)\) means that for every \(y<c_-(u)\), one has \(G_n(y)<u\). Passing to the limit yields \(G(y)\le u\) for every \(y<c_-(u)\). Therefore, by the defining infimum characterization of the quantile, \(Q_a^w(u\mid x)\ge c_-(u)\). Since u was arbitrary,
\[
\sup_{u\in(0,1)} |Q_a^w(u\mid x)-Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)|\le \epsilon(x),
\]
so \(w\in\mathcal W^{\cap}\). Thus \(\mathcal W^{\cap}\) is weak-* closed inside the weak-* compact set \(W_a^{\Gamma}(x)\), hence weak-* compact.

Finally, ass:bounded-support implies \(|y|\le \max\{|L|,|U|\}\) on the support, so the mean map
\[
w\mapsto m_a^w(x)=\int_L^U y\,w(y)\,dF(y)
\]
is continuous for the same weak-* topology because \(y\in L_1(F)\). A continuous real-valued map on a compact set attains its supremum and infimum. Therefore the coupled armwise upper and lower values are attained by jointly feasible weights.

\begin{lemma}[lem:contact-face-eif-decomposition]\label{lem:contact-face-eif-decomposition}
Under thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint and ass:regular-contact, along every regular parametric submodel that preserves the unique upper contact face A_a_plus, the coupled upper endpoint V_cpl_plus is pathwise differentiable and its canonical gradient equals phi_cpl_plus = phi_MSM_plus - phi_tube_plus, where phi_tube_plus is the full active-face canonical gradient of the latent optimizing CDF evaluated at the contact levels Q0_a(u|X)+epsilon(X).
\end{lemma}
Fix a regular parametric submodel \(\{P_t:t\in(-\delta,\delta)\}\) through the true law \(P=P_0\), with score \(s(O)\), that preserves the hypotheses of \(\mathrm{thm\!:\!quantile\mbox{-}coupled\mbox{-}endpoint}\) and the unique upper contact face from \(\mathrm{ass\!:\!regular\mbox{-}contact}\). For each arm \(a\) and covariate value \(x\), write
\[
\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x):=\Big\{w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x):\sup_{u\in(0,1)}|Q_a^w(u\mid x)-Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)|\le \epsilon(x)\Big\}
\]
and let
\[
C_a^+(x)=\sup_{w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)}m_a^w(x).
\]
By \(\mathrm{thm\!:\!quantile\mbox{-}coupled\mbox{-}endpoint}\),
\[
V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)C_a^+(X)\Big].
\]
Under \(\mathrm{ass\!:\!regular\mbox{-}contact}\), the upper optimizer is unique up to almost-everywhere equality on every policy-relevant arm. Denote that optimizer by \(w_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\), its latent CDF by
\[
G_{a,+}^*(y\mid x):=\int_{L_a(x)}^y w_{a,+}^*(t\mid x)\,dF_a^{\mathrm{obs}}(t\mid x),
\]
its density on the regular region by \(g_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\), and its quantile by \(Q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x)=Q_a^{w_{a,+}^*}(u\mid x)\). Let \(c_a(u,x):=Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)+\epsilon(x)\). On the active upper face
\[
A_a^+(x)=\{u\in(0,1):Q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x)=c_a(u,x)\},
\]
the binding equation is
\[
G_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,x)\mid x)=u.
\]

Now compare the coupled armwise value with the MSM-only upper value. Hess, Frauen, Melnychuk, and Feuerriegel (2026, Thm. 4.4) prove that the MSM-only sharp upper policy value \(V_{\mathrm{MSM}}^+\) is pathwise differentiable and has efficient influence function \(\phi_{\mathrm{MSM}}^+(O;\eta_{\mathrm{MSM}})\). The coupled problem adds the fixed upper-path constraint \(Q_a^w(u\mid x)\le c_a(u,x)\) and the symmetric lower-path constraint. For the upper endpoint, only the active upper face contributes at first order. Because \(\mathrm{ass\!:\!regular\mbox{-}contact}\) gives a unique optimizer and a fixed finite-union interior contact face, Milgrom and Segal (2002, Thm. 1) apply on this regular stratum: the derivative of the constrained supremal value is obtained by differentiating the criterion at the active optimizer; the first-order motion of the optimizer itself does not appear.

Therefore the only first-order difference between the coupled derivative and the Hess-Frauen MSM derivative is the part of the unconstrained quantile motion that is blocked by the active tube face. Firpo (2007, inverse-CDF derivative formula) applied to the latent optimizing law \(G_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\) gives, for every \(u\in A_a^+(x)\),
\[
\dot Q_{a,+}^{\mathrm{free}}(u\mid x)
= -\frac{\dot G_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,x)\mid x)}{g_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,x)\mid x)}.
\]
Here \(\dot G_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\) is the full pathwise derivative of the latent optimizing CDF. This point is load-bearing: since
\[
G_{a,+}^*(y\mid x)=\int_{L_a(x)}^y w_{a,+}^*(t\mid x)\,dF_a^{\mathrm{obs}}(t\mid x),
\]
its derivative has two first-order pieces,
\[
\dot G_{a,+}^*(y\mid x)
= \int_{L_a(x)}^y w_{a,+}^*(t\mid x)\,d\dot F_a^{\mathrm{obs}}(t\mid x)
  + \int_{L_a(x)}^y \dot w_{a,+}^*(t\mid x)\,dF_a^{\mathrm{obs}}(t\mid x),
\]
plus the equivalent density form on the regular region. The first integral is the weighted empirical-CDF perturbation; the second is the first-order optimizing-weight / weighted-density perturbation. Dropping the second term would incorrectly center the score at the observed-arm law \(F_a^{\mathrm{obs}}\), whereas the binding equation above is written under the latent optimizing law \(G_{a,+}^*\).

Integrating the blocked free motion over the active face and weighting by the policy gives the active-face loss
\[
\phi_{\mathrm{tube}}^+(O;\eta)
= \sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)
   \int_{A_a^+(X)}\frac{-\dot G_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,X)\mid X;O,\eta)}{g_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,X)\mid X)}\,du.
\]
Subtracting this loss from the Hess-Frauen derivative yields
\[
\left.\frac{d}{dt}V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+(P_t)\right|_{t=0}
=E\Big[\big\{\phi_{\mathrm{MSM}}^+(O;\eta_{\mathrm{MSM}})-\phi_{\mathrm{tube}}^+(O;\eta)\big\}s(O)\Big].
\]
Thus, on every submodel that preserves the unique active face, \(V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+\) is pathwise differentiable and its canonical gradient is
\[
\phi_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+(O;\eta)=\phi_{\mathrm{MSM}}^+(O;\eta_{\mathrm{MSM}})-\phi_{\mathrm{tube}}^+(O;\eta).
\]
If the upper face is empty, then the integral defining \(\phi_{\mathrm{tube}}^+\) vanishes and the formula collapses to the MSM-only EIF, which is the correct inactive-tube reduction.

\begin{lemma}[lem:contact-face-centering]\label{lem:contact-face-centering}
On the regular-contact stratum, the active-face correction is centered under the latent optimizing law: for every policy-relevant arm and every u in A_a_plus(X), G_{a,+}^*(Q0_a(u|X)+epsilon(X)|X)=u. Hence the contact-face block in phi_tube_plus has conditional mean zero under the latent optimizing law, not under the observed-arm law.
\end{lemma}
Fix an arm \(a\), a covariate value \(x\), and a rank \(u\in A_a^+(x)\). By definition of the upper contact set,
\[
Q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x)=Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)+\epsilon(x)=c_a(u,x).
\]
Because \(Q_{a,+}^*\) is the quantile function of the latent optimizing law \(G_{a,+}^*(\cdot\mid x)\), the quantile identity gives
\[
G_{a,+}^*(Q_{a,+}^*(u\mid x)\mid x)=u.
\]
Substituting the contact equality yields the contact condition
\[
G_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,x)\mid x)=u.
\]
Hence the centered indicator written under the latent optimizing law has conditional mean zero:
\[
E\Big[\mathbf 1\{Y_{a,+}^*\le c_a(u,X)\}-u\,\Big|\,X\Big]
=G_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,X)\mid X)-u=0,
\]
where \(Y_{a,+}^*\) denotes a draw from the latent optimizing arm law conditional on \(X\).

This is the correct centering identity. It is not
\(
F_a^{\mathrm{obs}}(c_a(u,X)\mid X)=u
\),
because the active-face binding equation is written for the weighted latent law \(G_{a,+}^*\), not for the observed-arm law \(F_a^{\mathrm{obs}}\). Therefore the active correction in \(\phi_{\mathrm{tube}}^+\) must be built from the canonical gradient of \(G_{a,+}^*(c_a(u,X)\mid X)\), which already includes both the observed-law perturbation and the first-order perturbation of the active weight. With that latent-law centering, the Gateaux derivative of
\[
E\big[\phi_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+(O;\eta)\big]
=E\big[\phi_{\mathrm{MSM}}^+(O;\eta_{\mathrm{MSM}})\big]-E\big[\phi_{\mathrm{tube}}^+(O;\eta)\big]
\]
with respect to first-order perturbations of the contact-level nuisance components vanishes on the regular-contact stratum: the Hess-Frauen term is already Neyman-orthogonal by Hess et al. (2026, Thm. 4.4), and the extra contact-face term is mean-zero by the display above. This reproduces the score-level orthogonality identity needed for the coupled EIF decomposition.

\begin{lemma}[lem:chat-plus-consistency]\label{lem:chat-plus-consistency}
Under thm:quantile-coupled-endpoint, ass:regular-contact, ass:rate-msm, and ass:rate-fobs, treating Q0_a and epsilon as fixed known inputs, the cross-fit plug-in estimator P_n[sum_{a in A} pi(a|X) Chat_a_plus(X)] is consistent for V_cpl_plus.
\end{lemma}
For each arm \(a\) and covariate value \(x\), write
\[
\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)=\Big\{w\in W_a^{\Gamma}(x):\sup_{u\in(0,1)}|Q_a^w(u\mid x)-Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)|\le\epsilon(x)\Big\}
\]
and
\[
C_a^+(x)=\sup_{w\in\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)}m_a^w(x).
\]
By \(\mathrm{thm\!:\!quantile\mbox{-}coupled\mbox{-}endpoint}\),
\[
V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+=E\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)C_a^+(X)\Big].
\]
The estimator in \(\mathrm{def\!:\!chat\mbox{-}plus}\) is the sample analogue
\[
\widehat C_a^+(x)=\sup\Big\{\widehat m_a^w(x):w\in \widehat W_a^{\Gamma}(x),\ \sup_{u\in(0,1)}|\widehat Q_a^w(u\mid x)-Q_{0,a}(u\mid x)|\le\epsilon(x)\Big\}.
\]
Because \(Q_{0,a}\) and \(\epsilon\) are fixed known inputs, the only estimated objects entering \(\widehat C_a^+\) are the observed-law and MSM nuisance components. By \(\mathrm{ass\!:\!rate\mbox{-}msm}\), the baseline MSM nuisance bundle is consistently estimated; by \(\mathrm{ass\!:\!rate\mbox{-}fobs}\), the observed-arm density (hence the regular-region CDF / quantile inversion block) is consistently estimated on the contact region. Therefore the sample objective \(\widehat m_a^w(x)\) converges uniformly to \(m_a^w(x)\) on the jointly feasible class, and the estimated feasible correspondence converges graphically to \(\mathcal W_a^{\cap}(x)\). Under \(\mathrm{ass\!:\!regular\mbox{-}contact}\), the true optimum is unique and the feasible class is compact on the regular stratum, so the usual maximum-theorem / argmax-continuity argument implies
\[
\widehat C_a^+(x)\xrightarrow{p}C_a^+(x)
\]
for \(P_X\)-almost every policy-relevant \((a,x)\).

Now consider the plug-in policy-value estimator
\[
\widehat V_{\mathrm{plug}}:=P_n\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\widehat C_a^+(X)\Big].
\]
By \(\mathrm{ass\!:\!bounded\mbox{-}support}\), every feasible conditional mean lies in \([L_a(X),U_a(X)]\). Hence
\[
\Big|\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\widehat C_a^+(X)\Big|
\le \sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\max\{|L_a(X)|,|U_a(X)|\},
\]
which is an integrable envelope on the bounded-support model class. Decompose
\[
\widehat V_{\mathrm{plug}}-V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+
=(P_n-P)\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\widehat C_a^+(X)\Big]
+P\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\{\widehat C_a^+(X)-C_a^+(X)\}\Big].
\]
The first term is \(o_p(1)\) by the law of large numbers for cross-fit, uniformly bounded summands. The second term is \(o_p(1)\) by dominated convergence, using \(\widehat C_a^+(X)\to C_a^+(X)\) in probability and the envelope above. Therefore
\[
P_n\Big[\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\pi(a\mid X)\widehat C_a^+(X)\Big]\xrightarrow{p}V_{\mathrm{cpl}}^+.
\]
That is exactly the claimed consistency of the cross-fit plug-in estimator.

\section{Honest scope}

The identification rung is conditional on joint feasibility: if the MSM likelihood-ratio window and the symmetric fixed-center quantile tube do not intersect on a policy-relevant arm, the restricted class is empty rather than widened. The clean sanity reductions are the inactive-tube face and the no-confounding face. If Qmsm_a_plus(u|x) and Qmsm_a_minus(u|x) already lie inside [Q0_a(u|x) - epsilon(x), Q0_a(u|x) + epsilon(x)] almost everywhere for every policy-relevant arm, then V_cpl_plus = V_MSM_plus and V_cpl_minus = V_MSM_minus, so the frontier collapses to the published Hess/Frauen MSM-only result. If Gamma = 1, then W_a^Gamma(x) is the singleton weight w = 1, so the joint class reduces to the observed-arm policy value when the observed conditional quantile law lies inside the tube and is empty otherwise. The inference rung remains conjectural and is explicitly limited to regular laws with a unique upper contact face, with Q0_a and epsilon treated as fixed analyst inputs; support-boundary contact and optimizer switching are left to directional or conservative inference.
