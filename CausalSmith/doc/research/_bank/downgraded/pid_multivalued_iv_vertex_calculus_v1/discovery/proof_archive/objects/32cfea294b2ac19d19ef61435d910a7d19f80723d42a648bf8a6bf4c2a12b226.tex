For a fixed active pair and fixed \(h\), put
\[m_{ab,z}=\min_{d\in R_{ab,z}(\mu)}h_{(\eta(d),d,z)}.\tag{1}\]
The auxiliary constraints for this pair are \(t_{ab,z}\le m_{ab,z}\) and \(\sum_zt_{ab,z}\ge0\). Under the affine change of variables \(k_{ab,z}=m_{ab,z}-t_{ab,z}\), they become
\[k_{ab,z}\ge0,\qquad \sum_{z=1}^lk_{ab,z}\le\sum_{z=1}^lm_{ab,z}=F_{ab}(h).\tag{2}\]
Feasibility of \(h\in\mathcal K_\mu\) gives \(F_{ab}(h)\ge0\). If it is zero, (2) is the singleton \(k=0\). If it is positive, (2) is an \(l\)-simplex with vertices
\[0,\ F_{ab}(h)e_1,\ldots,F_{ab}(h)e_l,\tag{3}\]
so it has \(l+1\) vertices. Auxiliary variables from different active pairs occur in disjoint copies of (2); hence the complete fiber is their Cartesian product and has \((l+1)^q\) vertices.

Let \(t\) be any such fiber vertex and suppose \((h,t)\) is the midpoint of two feasible extended points \((h^-,t^-)\) and \((h^+,t^+)\). Projection gives \(h=(h^-+h^+)/2\). Since \(h\) is a vertex of the projected polytope \(\mathcal K_\mu\), one has \(h^-=h^+=h\). The two auxiliary points then lie in the fixed fiber, and extremality of \(t\) in that fiber gives \(t^-=t^+=t\). Thus every fiber vertex is a vertex of the extension, proving the claim.
