Fix \(n\ge2\) and \(l\ge3\), and write \(N_n=5\cdot4^{n-1}-2^{n+2}+4\). Lemma~\(\text{lem:essential-pair-vertex-lower-bound}\) gives the first certificate
\[|\mathcal V_{n,l}|\ge \binom{l}{2}(N_n-2).\tag{1}\]
Lemma~\(\text{lem:bbak-upper-family-vertices-and-count}\) gives
\[\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}\subseteq\mathcal V_{n,l},\qquad |\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|=l(l-1)^{n-1}.\tag{2}\]
Lemma~\(\text{lem:universal-two-arm-omitted-vertex}\) supplies an explicit vertex \(\omega^{(n,l)}\in\mathcal V_{n,l}\setminus\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}\). The sets in (2) are finite, so their disjoint decomposition yields
\[|\mathcal V_{n,l}|=|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|+|\mathcal V_{n,l}\setminus\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|\ge l(l-1)^{n-1}+1.\tag{3}\]
Combining (1) and (3) proves the stronger simultaneous bound
\[|\mathcal V_{n,l}|\ge\max\left\{\binom{l}{2}(N_n-2),\ l(l-1)^{n-1}+1\right\}.\tag{4}\]
In particular, (3) proves \(|\mathcal V_{n,l}|>|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|\) for every \(n\ge2\) and \(l\ge3\), so the literal arm-indexed family is strictly incomplete. This conclusion does not require either direction of the comparison between \(2(l-1)^{n-2}\) and \(N_n-2\); hence it applies both to the complementary pairs satisfying \(2(l-1)^{n-2}\ge N_n-2\) and to the pairs covered by the earlier sufficient condition.
