By Lemma~\(\text{lem:essential-pair-vertex-lower-bound}\), for every \(n\ge 2\) and \(l\ge 3\),
\[
|\mathcal V_{n,l}|\ge \binom{l}{2}(N_n-2).
\]
The literal-set cardinality in \(\text{def:bbak-family}\) is
\[
|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|=l(l-1)^{n-1}.
\]
Suppose now that \(2(l-1)^{n-2}<N_n-2\). Since \(l(l-1)/2>0\), multiplying this strict inequality by \(l(l-1)/2\) yields
\[
\binom{l}{2}(N_n-2)>\frac{l(l-1)}{2}\,2(l-1)^{n-2}=l(l-1)^{n-1}.
\]
Combining the preceding three displays gives
\[
|\mathcal V_{n,l}|>|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|.
\]
Thus the literal arm-indexed family cannot exhaust \(\mathcal V_{n,l}\) whenever the displayed criterion holds.

It remains to check the listed regimes. If \(n=2\), then \(N_2-2=6\), while \(2(l-1)^{n-2}=2\); hence the criterion holds for every \(l\ge3\). Next suppose that \(3\le l\le5\), and put \(t=2^{n-2}\ge1\). Then
\[
2(l-1)^{n-2}\le2\cdot4^{n-2}=2t^2,
\]
whereas
\[
N_n-2=5\cdot4^{n-1}-2^{n+2}+2=20t^2-16t+2.
\]
The difference between the latter expression and \(2t^2\) is
\[
18t^2-16t+2=2(9t^2-8t+1)=2\bigl(t(9t-8)+1\bigr)>0,
\]
because \(t\ge1\). Therefore the criterion holds for all \(n\ge2\) and \(3\le l\le5\). Finally, direct substitution gives
\[
N_3-2=50,\qquad N_4-2=258,\qquad N_5-2=1154,\qquad N_6-2=4866.
\]
For \(n=3,4,5,6\), respectively, the criterion is
\[
2(l-1)<50,\qquad 2(l-1)^2<258,\qquad 2(l-1)^3<1154,\qquad 2(l-1)^4<4866.
\]
For integers \(l\ge3\), these inequalities hold through \(l=25,12,9,8\), respectively: the last admissible powers are \(24<25\), \(11^2=121<129\), \(8^3=512<577\), and \(7^4=2401<2433\), while the next integer powers fail the corresponding strict inequalities. This proves every advertised sufficient regime.

The calculation above identifies precisely what the pair-essential lower bound certifies by itself. For every pair outside these sufficient regimes, Theorem~\(\text{thm:universal-bbak-family-deficit}\) supplies the independent bound
\[
|\mathcal V_{n,l}|\ge |\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|+1.
\]
Consequently the failure of the pair-essential comparison in a complementary regime leaves no complementary cardinality gap: strict incompleteness still holds there by the universal deficit theorem.
