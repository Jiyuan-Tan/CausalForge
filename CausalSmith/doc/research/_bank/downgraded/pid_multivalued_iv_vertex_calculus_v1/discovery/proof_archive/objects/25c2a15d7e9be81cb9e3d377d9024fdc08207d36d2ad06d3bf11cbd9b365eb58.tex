The explicit global parent map supplies a known root, polynomial-time parent evaluation, strict descent, acyclicity, and reachability from every vertex. A supplied rational normalized tangent ray, its extremality, finite exact endpoint, vertex status, and parent relation are all checkable in polynomial bit time using at most \(n^2(l+1)\) compressed span generators. Every simple vertex has unconditional polynomial-total-time exact child generation. The proved \(\textup{lem:np-oracle-polynomial-delay-upper-bound}\) further shows that all normalized rays have polynomial-size canonical encodings and that an \(\mathsf{NP}\)-oracle prefix iterator yields exact-once local child generation with polynomial delay and exhaustion. Coupling that iterator to the parent tree via \(\textup{lem:parity-euler-delay-transfer}\) gives exact-once global polynomial delay and polynomial-time termination. In particular, the requested deterministic enumerator follows conditionally if \(\mathsf P=\mathsf{NP}\). The sole unconditional residual is removal of the oracle for nonsimple inverse-child generation.
