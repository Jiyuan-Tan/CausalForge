Unconditionally, the parent \(P\) has a known root, is computable in polynomial bit time, strictly descends, and reaches the root from every vertex. Given a rational candidate ray, extremality, a finite exact endpoint, and the condition \(P(\nu)=\mu\) are all decidable in polynomial bit time without expanding \(2^l n^2\) constraints. Every simple vertex admits exact polynomial-total-time child generation. For arbitrary vertices, all normalized rays have polynomial-length canonical encodings, and \(\mathsf{NP}\)-oracle prefix search lists every child exactly once with polynomial delay and polynomial-time exhaustion; the parity-Euler lemma then gives an exact-once global polynomial-delay enumeration with polynomial-time final termination. Hence the requested enumerator exists relative to \(\mathsf{NP}\), and in particular exists deterministically if \(\mathsf P=\mathsf{NP}\). The only unresolved part is unconditional oracle removal for nonsimple vertices.
