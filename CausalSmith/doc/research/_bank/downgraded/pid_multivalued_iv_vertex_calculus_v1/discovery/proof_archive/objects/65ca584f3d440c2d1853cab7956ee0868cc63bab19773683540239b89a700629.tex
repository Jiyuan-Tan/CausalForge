For \(\tau=(\delta,\eta)\), the definitions of \(A_{n,l}\) and \(P_\sigma\) give
\[(A_{n,l}^{\mathsf T}P_\sigma\lambda)_{(\delta,\eta)}=\sum_{z=1}^l\lambda_{(\eta(\delta(z)),\delta(z),\sigma^{-1}(z))}.\]
After the change of index \(j=\sigma^{-1}(z)\), the right-hand side is
\[\sum_{j=1}^l\lambda_{(\eta(\delta(\sigma(j))),\delta(\sigma(j)),j)}=(A_{n,l}^{\mathsf T}\lambda)_{(\delta\circ\sigma,\eta)}.\]
The map \(\delta\mapsto\delta\circ\sigma\) is a bijection of \(\mathcal D^{\mathcal Z_l}\), while \((c_{n,l})_{(\delta,\eta)}=\eta(1)-\eta(0)\) does not depend on \(\delta\). Hence \(P_\sigma\) maps \(\mathcal H_{n,l}\) bijectively onto itself, with inverse \(P_{\sigma^{-1}}\). The same display with zero on the left shows that \(P_\sigma K_{n,l}=K_{n,l}\), so \(P_\sigma\) induces a linear automorphism of the quotient \(\mathcal H_{n,l}/K_{n,l}\). By the gauge-transversal lemma, \(\Gamma\) is the affine bijection from this quotient to \(\mathcal H^0_{n,l}\). Therefore \(T_\sigma=\Gamma\circ P_\sigma\) is the corresponding automorphism in gauge coordinates. An affine bijection preserves nontrivial midpoint decompositions in both directions, and consequently maps vertices bijectively to vertices.

The map \(P_\sigma\) merely relabels the arm blocks. The correction made by \(\Gamma\) belongs to \(K_{n,l}\), and the gauge-transversal lemma says that it adds one constant to every coefficient in a given arm. Adding such a constant neither turns a nonconstant block into a constant block nor changes a difference of two coefficients within that block. Thus essentiality and all within-arm contrasts are gauge invariant and are permuted according to the arm relabeling.
