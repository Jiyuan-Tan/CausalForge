Fix \(n\ge2\) and \(l\ge3\). Lemma~\(\text{lem:bbak-upper-family-vertices-and-count}\) gives
\[\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}\subseteq\mathcal V_{n,l},\qquad |\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|=l(l-1)^{n-1}.\]
Lemma~\(\text{lem:universal-two-arm-omitted-vertex}\) constructs an explicit vector \(\omega^{(n,l)}\in\mathcal V_{n,l}\setminus\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}\). Since both sets are finite,
\[|\mathcal V_{n,l}|=|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|+|\mathcal V_{n,l}\setminus\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|\ge |\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|+1=l(l-1)^{n-1}+1.\]
This argument uses no inequality relating \(l\) and \(N_n\). It therefore applies in particular to every pair in the complementary regime \(2(l-1)^{n-2}\ge N_n-2\), and it also subsumes the strict-deficit conclusion in the previously certified sufficient regimes.
