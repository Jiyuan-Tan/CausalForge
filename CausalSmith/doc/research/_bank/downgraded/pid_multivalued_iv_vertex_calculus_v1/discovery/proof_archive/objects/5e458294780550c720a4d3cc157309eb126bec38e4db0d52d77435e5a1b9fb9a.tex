At \(\lambda^\star\), the global-parent formula gives
\[u^\star_{a,1}=n-1,\quad v^\star_{b,1}=b,\qquad u^\star_{a,2}=-a,\quad v^\star_{b,2}=0,\tag{1}\]
and both treatment coordinates are zero in every arm \(z\ge3\). For each outcome pair \((a,b)\), the separable minimum is \(b-a\), so the pair is active. In arm \(1\), the two choices in (1) tie exactly when \(b=n-1\). In arm \(2\), they tie exactly when \(a=0\). Every arm \(z\ge3\) always ties. Therefore the number of tight treatment words for this pair is
\[2^{l-2+\mathbf1\{a=0\}+\mathbf1\{b=n-1\}}.\tag{2}\]
Summing (2) over \(a,b\in\mathcal Y_n\) factorizes:
\[|\operatorname{Sig}(\lambda^\star)|=2^{l-2}\left(\sum_{a=0}^{n-1}2^{\mathbf1\{a=0\}}\right)\left(\sum_{b=0}^{n-1}2^{\mathbf1\{b=n-1\}}\right)=2^{l-2}(n+1)^2.\tag{3}\]
Each explicit type requires at least one output symbol and in fact an \(l\)-bit treatment word. Thus materializing the list in (3) before producing a child or certifying exhaustion takes exponentially many bit operations in \(l\), contradicting a polynomial preprocessing or postprocessing guarantee for that route.
