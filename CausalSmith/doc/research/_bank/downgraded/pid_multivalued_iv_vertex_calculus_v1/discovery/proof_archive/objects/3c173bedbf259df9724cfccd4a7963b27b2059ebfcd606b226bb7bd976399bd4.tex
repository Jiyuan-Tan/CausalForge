Set \(X=\mathcal V_{2,3}\) and \(F=\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,3}\). By the definition of the literal comparator family, for \(l\ge 3\) its distinct literal coefficient vectors have cardinality \(l(l-1)^{n-1}\). Substitution of \((n,l)=(2,3)\) therefore gives
\[|F|=3(3-1)^{2-1}=6.\]
By the certified omitted-vertex theorem, these six vectors are vertices, so \(F\subseteq X\). The same theorem constructs the three gauge-fixed cyclic arm permutations of \(\lambda^\dagger\), proves that they are pairwise distinct elements of \(X\), and proves through their gauge-invariant contrast vectors that none belongs to \(F\). Consequently,
\[|X\setminus F|\ge 3.\]
The sets \(X\) and \(F\) are finite. Applying the finite-set deficit-counting lemma to \(F\subseteq X\), \(|F|=6\), and \(|X\setminus F|\ge3\) yields
\[|X|\ge9>6=|F|\qquad\text{and}\qquad F\subsetneq X.\]
Restoring the definitions of \(X\) and \(F\) gives
\[|\mathcal V_{2,3}\setminus\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,3}|\ge3,\qquad |\mathcal V_{2,3}|\ge9>6=|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,3}|,\qquad \mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,3}\subsetneq\mathcal V_{2,3},\]
which proves every assertion.
