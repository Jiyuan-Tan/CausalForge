By Lemma~\(\text{lem:essential-pair-vertex-lower-bound}\),
\[
|\mathcal V_{n,l}|\ge \binom{l}{2}(N_n-2).
\]
The literal-set cardinality in \(\text{def:bbak-family}\) is
\[
|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|=l(l-1)^{n-1}\qquad(l\ge3).
\]
Suppose that \(2(l-1)^{n-2}<N_n-2\). Since \(l(l-1)>0\), multiplication by \(l(l-1)/2\) gives
\[
\binom{l}{2}(N_n-2)>\frac{l(l-1)}{2}\,2(l-1)^{n-2}=l(l-1)^{n-1}.
\]
Combining the last three displays proves
\[
|\mathcal V_{n,l}|>|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}|.
\]
In particular, \(\mathcal V_{n,l}\setminus\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{n,l}\neq\varnothing\), so the literal family cannot exhaust the vertices.

It remains to verify the advertised subregimes. If \(n=2\), then \(N_2-2=6\), whereas \(2(l-1)^{n-2}=2\), so the criterion holds for every \(l\ge3\). Next suppose \(3\le l\le5\). Put \(t=2^{n-2}\ge1\). Then
\[
2(l-1)^{n-2}\le2\cdot4^{n-2}=2t^2,
\]
while
\[
N_n-2=20t^2-16t+2.
\]
Their difference is
\[
18t^2-16t+2=2(9t^2-8t+1)>0,
\]
because it equals \(4\) at \(t=1\) and has positive derivative \(36t-16\) for \(t\ge1\). Thus the criterion holds for every \(n\ge2\) and \(3\le l\le5\). Finally, direct substitution gives \(N_3-2=50\), \(N_4-2=258\), \(N_5-2=1154\), and \(N_6-2=4866\). Hence the criterion becomes, respectively,
\[
2(l-1)<50,\qquad 2(l-1)^2<258,\qquad 2(l-1)^3<1154,\qquad 2(l-1)^4<4866.
\]
For integer \(l\ge3\), these inequalities hold exactly up to \(l=25,12,9,8\), respectively. This proves every asserted regime. The proof uses the displayed inequality only as a sufficient certificate and therefore makes no claim in its complement.
