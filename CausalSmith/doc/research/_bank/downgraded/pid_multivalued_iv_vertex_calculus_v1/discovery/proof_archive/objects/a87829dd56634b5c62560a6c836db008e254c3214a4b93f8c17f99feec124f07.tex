For \(n=2\), the binary-instrument count appearing in the essential-pair lower-bound lemma is
\[N_2=5\cdot4^{2-1}-2^{2+2}+4=20-16+4=8.\]
Applying that lemma with \(l=3\) gives at least
\[\binom{3}{2}(N_2-2)=3(8-2)=18\]
distinct vertices having exactly two essential arm blocks. Choose any set \(E\) of eighteen such vertices.

Let
\[A=\{T_\sigma\lambda^\dagger:\sigma\in S_3\}.\]
The full-orbit theorem proves that \(|A|=6\) and that every member of \(A\) has all three arm blocks essential. Hence \(E\cap A=\varnothing\).

The canonical-one-exchange lemma supplies the vertex \(\mu\) with blocks
\[(2,1,2,1),\qquad(0,-1,-1,0),\qquad(0,0,-1,0).\]
All three blocks are nonconstant. Direct substitution in the definition of \(I\) gives
\[I(\mu)=(-1,1,1).\]
For each \(j\in\{1,2,3\}\), choose an arm permutation \(\sigma_j\) that sends the unique arm carrying the value \(-1\) in this contrast vector to arm \(j\), and put \(\mu_j=T_{\sigma_j}\mu\). The arm-permutation lemma gives \(\mu_j\in\mathcal V_{2,3}\), preserves the three essential blocks, and yields the three distinct coordinate permutations of \((-1,1,1)\) as \(I(\mu_j)\). Therefore the set \(M=\{\mu_1,\mu_2,\mu_3\}\) has cardinality three and satisfies \(E\cap M=\varnothing\).

Every member of \(A\) has an \(I\)-vector whose coordinate multiset is \(\{-1,1,2\}\), whereas every member of \(M\) has coordinate multiset \(\{-1,1,1\}\). Equality of two vertices would imply equality of their \(I\)-vectors, so \(A\cap M=\varnothing\). Thus \(E\), \(A\), and \(M\) are pairwise disjoint subsets of \(\mathcal V_{2,3}\), and
\[|\mathcal V_{2,3}|\ge |E|+|A|+|M|=18+6+3=27.\]

The full-orbit theorem also proves that \(\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,3}\) consists of six distinct vertices. Since this family is a subset of the finite set \(\mathcal V_{2,3}\), finite additivity gives
\[|\mathcal V_{2,3}\setminus\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,3}|=|\mathcal V_{2,3}|-|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,3}|\ge27-6=21.\]
The complement is nonempty, so the inclusion is strict.
