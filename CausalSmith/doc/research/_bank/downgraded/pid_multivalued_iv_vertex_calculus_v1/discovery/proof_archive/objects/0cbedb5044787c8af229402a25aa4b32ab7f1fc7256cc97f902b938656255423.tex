The essential-pair lower-bound lemma, evaluated at \(n=2\), uses \(N_2=8\) and therefore gives at least
\[
\binom l2(N_2-2)=6\binom l2
\]
distinct vertices of \(\mathcal V_{2,l}\) having exactly two essential arm blocks. Their essential-arm supports distinguish the collections belonging to different unordered pairs.

By the certified twenty-seven-vertex theorem, there is a set \(W\subseteq\mathcal V_{2,3}\) of nine distinct vertices having exactly three essential arm blocks: take the six-element orbit of \(\lambda^\dagger\) and the disjoint three-element permutation collection generated by \(\mu\). Fix \(l\ge3\). The iterated zero-arm extension in the zero-arm vertex-lifting lemma gives an injective map
\[
\iota_{3\to l}:\mathcal V_{2,3}\longrightarrow\mathcal V_{2,l}
\]
which copies the first three arm blocks and puts constant zero blocks on arms \(4,\ldots,l\). For every unordered triple \(J\subseteq\mathcal Z_l\), choose an arm permutation \(\sigma_J\) which sends \(\{1,2,3\}\) onto \(J\), and define
\[
\Psi_J=T_{\sigma_J}\circ\iota_{3\to l}.
\]
The arm-permutation quotient-automorphism lemma says that \(T_{\sigma_J}\) is a bijection of \(\mathcal V_{2,l}\), permutes essential-arm supports, and preserves essentiality. Hence \(\Psi_J\) is injective on \(W\), and every vertex in \(\Psi_J(W)\) has essential-arm support exactly \(J\). Thus \(|\Psi_J(W)|=9\). Images corresponding to distinct triples are disjoint because their essential-arm supports differ. We have therefore constructed at least \(9\binom l3\) vertices with exactly three essential arms. These vertices are also disjoint from the \(6\binom l2\) pair-essential vertices. It follows that
\[
|\mathcal V_{2,l}|\ge6\binom l2+9\binom l3
=3l(l-1)+\frac32l(l-1)(l-2)
=\frac32l^2(l-1).
\]

It remains to compare this collection with the literal arm-indexed family. At \(n=2\), write an admissible index as \(s=(s_0,s_1)\). Nonconstancy means \(s_0\ne s_1\), and the definition also gives \(s_0,s_1\ne a\). Directly from the displayed formula defining \(\bar w(a,s)\),
\[
\bar w(a,s)_{(0,0,a)}=1,\qquad \bar w(a,s)_{(1,0,a)}=0,
\]
and
\[
\bar w(a,s)_{(0,1,s_0)}=-1,\qquad \bar w(a,s)_{(1,1,s_0)}=0.
\]
All blocks other than those indexed by \(a\) and \(s_0\) are constant zero blocks; the possible entry indexed by \(s_1\) has coefficient zero because \(n-1-y=0\) at \(y=1\). Thus exactly the blocks \(a\) and \(s_0\) are essential in \(\bar w(a,s)\). By the gauge-transversal lemma, applying \(\Gamma\) adds only an armwise constant and hence does not change which blocks are essential. Therefore every member of \(\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,l}\) has exactly two essential arms.

The upper-family vertex-and-count lemma gives
\[
\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,l}\subseteq\mathcal V_{2,l},\qquad |\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,l}|=l(l-1).
\]
Finite cardinality and the preceding lower bound now imply
\[
\begin{aligned}
|\mathcal V_{2,l}\setminus\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,l}|
&=|\mathcal V_{2,l}|-|\mathcal F^{\mathrm{BBAK}}_{2,l}|\\
&\ge\frac32l^2(l-1)-l(l-1)\\
&=\frac{l(l-1)(3l-2)}2.
\end{aligned}
\]
Substituting \(l=3\) yields \(27\) and \(21\), respectively.
