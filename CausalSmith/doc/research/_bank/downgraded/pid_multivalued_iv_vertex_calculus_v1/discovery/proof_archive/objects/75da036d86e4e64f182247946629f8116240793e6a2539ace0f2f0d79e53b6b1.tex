The exact positive frontier is complete up to deterministic nonsimple inverse-child generation. There is a known root and a polynomial-time global parent map whose chains reach it. A supplied rational vertex, normalized tangent ray, finite exact line-search endpoint, and inverse-child relation can all be verified in polynomial bit time using at most \(n^2(l+1)\) compressed span generators. At a simple vertex with \(|\operatorname{Sig}(\mu)|=r\), all children are generated in polynomial total time from the rays \(R_\mu^{-1}e_i\). For arbitrary vertices, an \(\mathsf{NP}\) oracle gives a polynomial-delay child iterator with polynomial exhaustion through prefix self-reduction, and the parity Euler traversal then gives exact-once global polynomial delay and polynomial final termination. Full active-list expansion is impossible within the requested preprocessing bound because the root signature has size \((n+1)^2 2^{l-2}\), while naive extended-formulation enumeration can repeat a projected ray \((l+1)^q\) times. Thus the remaining question is precisely whether the nonsimple, parent-filtered prefix-existence problem has a deterministic polynomial-time solution exploiting the coupled product-box geometry; tightness or hardness of this residual remains unproved.
