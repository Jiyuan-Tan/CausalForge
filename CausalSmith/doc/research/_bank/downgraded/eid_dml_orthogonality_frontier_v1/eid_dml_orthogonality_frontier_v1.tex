\begin{abstract}
This note fixes the shared setup for an ExactID heterogeneous-treatment-effect
DML frontier.  The identifying functional is the observable AIPW map
\(\Psi_P(\ell)=\E_P[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_0)]\), where
\(\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\) uses the conditional outcome regressions
\((g_{1,0},g_{0,0})\) and propensity \(p_0\).  The target causal estimand is the
weighted potential-outcome report
\(\theta_0(\ell)=\E[\ell(X)\{Y(1)-Y(0)\}]\) for
\(\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n\).  The per-conjecture stages will verify whether the
two-channel frontier
\(\mathfrak{F}_n(P)=\max\{\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P),
(\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P))\mathcal{E}_n\}\) is the exact boundary for
uniform first-order Gaussian HTE-DML inference, and whether its Holder-rate
specialization is the stated exponent pair.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
First-order Neyman-orthogonal DML is a standard tool for estimating treatment
effects with flexible nuisance learners.  For a single average treatment effect,
the familiar product-rate message is clear: the nuisance estimation errors must
be small enough that the second-order remainder is asymptotically negligible.
For heterogeneous-treatment-effect reports indexed by a growing class
\(\mathcal{L}_n\), a second channel matters.  The bounded population drift is a
bilinear product of outcome-regression and propensity errors, while the nuisance
empirical process can be amplified by the entropy of the report class.

The exact-identification part of the problem is classical.  Under consistency,
conditional unconfoundedness, and overlap, the weighted CATE report
\(\E[\ell(X)\{Y(1)-Y(0)\}]\) is identified by an AIPW signal.  What remains is
not another adjustment formula, but a sharp inference frontier for the
cross-fitted plug-in process over Holder nuisance classes and entropy-indexed
report weights.

This note sets up the common notation, assumptions, and computable identifying
functional for two later conjecture-specific derivations.  If Conjecture 1 is
confirmed, the scalar \(\mathfrak{F}_n(P)\) separates the region where
first-order orthogonality supports uniform Gaussian inference from the region
where either deterministic drift or nuisance empirical-process leakage remains
visible at first order.  If Conjecture 2 is confirmed, this abstract frontier
reduces in the canonical Holder case to two transparent exponent inequalities.

\paragraph{Motivating conjecture(s).}
\begin{quote}
\phantomsection\label{conj:frontier}
\textbf{Conjecture 1.}
Under Assumptions \ref{ass:unconfounded}--\ref{ass:curvature}, first-order
orthogonal DML is uniformly asymptotically linear and Gaussian for the
\(\mathcal{L}_n\)-indexed HTE report process if and only if
\[
\sup_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\mathfrak{F}_n(P)
=\sup_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\max\{\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P),
\{\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P)\}\mathcal{E}_n\}
\longrightarrow0 .
\]
The necessity half is interpreted in the minimax/adversarial first-order
experiment of Assumption \ref{ass:curvature}: if
\(\limsup_n\sup_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\mathfrak{F}_n(P)>0\) and either the active
curvature constant \(c_R\) or the empirical leakage constant \(c_{\mathrm{ep}}\)
is bounded below, then there exist an active sequence \(P_n\), an open subset
of the locked Holder nuisance class, and cross-fit nuisance estimates attaining
the active tangent directions for which
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}
\left|\sqrt n\{\hat\theta_n(\ell)-\theta_0(\ell)\}-\mathbb{G}_n(\ell)\right|
\]
has a liminf bounded away from zero in probability.  Thus the usual
oracle-centered first-order Gaussian studentization is not uniformly valid in
the minimax first-order experiment.  If \(\mathfrak{D}_n\) is the active
channel the failure is a deterministic translation by \(R_P\); if
\(\mathfrak{S}_n\) is the active channel the failure is non-negligible nuisance
empirical-process leakage.  Faster oracle learners outside the active error
class are not claimed to fail.

\phantomsection\label{conj:holder}
\textbf{Conjecture 2.}
In the canonical Holder-rate specialization of Assumption \ref{ass:holder} with
\(\mathcal{E}_n\asymp n^\zeta\), the frontier in Conjecture
\ref{conj:frontier} is equivalent, up to logarithmic factors, to
\[
\frac{\alpha_g}{2\alpha_g+d}+\frac{\alpha_p}{2\alpha_p+d}
>\frac12,\qquad
\min\left\{\frac{\alpha_g}{2\alpha_g+d},\frac{\alpha_p}{2\alpha_p+d}\right\}
>\zeta.
\]
Below this boundary, first-order DML cannot be uniformly Gaussian over the
locked Holder/entropy class; above it, first-order DML is uniformly Gaussian
provided the oracle Gaussian approximation in Assumption \ref{ass:crossfit}
holds.
\end{quote}

\section{Related work and positioning}
\cite{ChernozhukovEtAl2018DML} is the canonical DML reference: it introduces
orthogonal scores and cross-fitting with sufficient product-rate conditions,
but it does not state a necessary-and-sufficient Holder/entropy breakdown
frontier.  \cite{SemenovaChernozhukov2021DMLCATE} develops orthogonal signals
for CATE and structural-function summaries with simultaneous inference under
high-level nuisance conditions; the present setup targets the boundary at
which that type of report process ceases to be first-order Gaussian.
\cite{FosterSyrgkanis2023OSL} gives entropy-aware orthogonal statistical
learning risk bounds, which motivate the empirical-process channel but do not
by themselves imply Gaussian inference for DML reports.
\cite{ChernozhukovChetverikovKato2014Gaussian} supplies the Gaussian
approximation technology for suprema of empirical processes, used here only as
an oracle-process route.  \cite{KennedyBalakrishnanRobinsWasserman2024MinimaxCATE}
derives minimax CATE smoothness elbows using higher-order ideas, while
\cite{ArmstrongKolesar2021FiniteSampleATE} and
\cite{MackeySyrgkanisZadik2018OML} are closest limitations comparators for
root-\(n\) inference and first-order orthogonality.  The local proposal record
for this qid reports no banked ExactID theorem for a Holder/entropy DML
frontier; the closest failed predecessor targeted a different
studentization-report equivalence rather than the two-channel object
\(\mathfrak{F}_n(P)\).

\section{Formal setup}
Let \(W=(Y,D,X)\sim P\), with binary treatment \(D\in\{0,1\}\), covariates
 \(X\in[0,1]^d\), and bounded outcome \(|Y|\le M\).  The causal graph is
 \(X\to D\to Y\) and \(X\to Y\); latent variables are allowed only insofar as
 they do not violate the conditional exchangeability assumption below.
 Potential outcomes are \(Y(1)\) and \(Y(0)\), and consistency identifies the
 observed outcome with \(DY(1)+(1-D)Y(0)\).

Write
\[
g_{d,0}(x)=\E[Y\mid D=d,X=x],\qquad
p_0(x)=\Pr(D=1\mid X=x),\qquad
\tau_0(x)=g_{1,0}(x)-g_{0,0}(x).
\]
The report class \(\mathcal{L}_n\) is deterministic, uniformly bounded, and
measurable.  For \(\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n\), the target report is
\(\theta_0(\ell)=\E[\ell(X)\tau_0(X)]\).  The AIPW signal is
\[
\Gamma(W;\eta_0)=g_{1,0}(X)-g_{0,0}(X)
+\frac{D}{p_0(X)}\{Y-g_{1,0}(X)\}
-\frac{1-D}{1-p_0(X)}\{Y-g_{0,0}(X)\},
\]
where \(\eta_0=(g_{1,0},g_{0,0},p_0)\).  Cross-fitted nuisance estimates are
written \(\hat\eta_{-k(i)}=(\hat g_{1,-k(i)},\hat g_{0,-k(i)},\hat p_{-k(i)})\)
for the fold excluding observation \(i\), and
\[
\hat\theta_n(\ell)=\mathbb{P}_n[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\hat\eta_{-k(i)})].
\]

The nuisance radii are deterministic envelopes
\[
\delta_{g,n}(P)=\|\hat g_1-g_{1,0}\|_{2,P}+\|\hat g_0-g_{0,0}\|_{2,P},\qquad
\delta_{p,n}(P)=\|\hat p-p_0\|_{2,P}.
\]
The report-class entropy integral is
\[
\mathcal{E}_n
=1+\int_0^1
\sqrt{\log N(\varepsilon\|\mathcal{L}_n\|_{2,P},\mathcal{L}_n,L^2(P))}
\,d\varepsilon,
\]
uniformly over \(P\in\mathcal{P}_n\).  The two frontier channels are
\[
\mathfrak{D}_n(P)=\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P),\qquad
\mathfrak{S}_n(P)=\{\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P)\}\mathcal{E}_n,
\]
and \(\mathfrak{F}_n(P)=\max\{\mathfrak{D}_n(P),\mathfrak{S}_n(P)\}\).

\section{Assumptions}
\begin{assumption}[Unconfounded exact-ID model]\label{ass:unconfounded}
For every \(P\in\mathcal{P}_n\), \(Y=DY(1)+(1-D)Y(0)\),
\((Y(1),Y(0))\perp D\mid X\), \(X\in[0,1]^d\), \(|Y|\le M\), and
\(\underline e\le p_0(X)\le1-\underline e\) almost surely for a fixed
\(\underline e>0\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Holder nuisance and learner radii]\label{ass:holder}
The outcome regressions \(g_{1,0},g_{0,0}\) belong to a Holder ball
\(\mathcal{H}(\alpha_g,L_g,d)\), the propensity \(p_0\) belongs to
\(\mathcal{H}(\alpha_p,L_p,d)\), and the cross-fitted learners have uniform
\(L^2(P)\) envelopes \(\delta_{g,n}(P)\) and \(\delta_{p,n}(P)\).  In the
canonical Holder-rate specialization,
\(\delta_{g,n}\asymp n^{-\alpha_g/(2\alpha_g+d)}\) and
\(\delta_{p,n}\asymp n^{-\alpha_p/(2\alpha_p+d)}\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Report-class entropy]\label{ass:entropy}
The deterministic HTE report class \(\mathcal{L}_n\) is uniformly bounded by
\(L_\ell\), centered when required by the covariance normalization, and has
finite entropy integral \(\mathcal{E}_n\).  The centered oracle-process
convention used in Theorem \ref{thm:score-remainder} is
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}
\sqrt n\,|\theta_0(\ell)(\mathbb{P}_n-P)\ell(X)|=o_P(1),
\]
or equivalently the oracle summand may be read as
\(\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_0)-\theta_0(\ell)\) under an unnormalized report
convention.  Polynomial entropy classes with
\(\log N(\varepsilon,\mathcal{L}_n,L^2(P))\lesssim a_n\varepsilon^{-v_\ell}\)
are allowed; \(\mathcal{E}_n\) records the resulting growth.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Cross-fitting and empirical-process regularity]\label{ass:crossfit}
The sample is split into a fixed number of folds, nuisance estimates used for
observation \(i\) are trained off fold \(k(i)\), and the oracle process
\[
\mathbb{G}_n(\ell)=
\sqrt n\,(\mathbb{P}_n-P)[\ell(X)\{\Gamma(W;\eta_0)-\theta_0(\ell)\}]
\]
admits a tight Gaussian multiplier approximation uniformly on
\(\mathcal{L}_n\) whenever \(\mathfrak{F}_n(P)\to0\).  The nuisance
empirical-process remainder
\[
\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)=
(\mathbb{P}_n-P)[
\ell(X)\{\Gamma(W;\hat\eta_{-k(i)})-\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\}]
\]
obeys the sharp maximal scale
\[
\sqrt n\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}
|\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)|=O_P(\mathfrak{S}_n(P)),
\]
and is therefore \(o_P(1)\) on the upper-bound side when
\(\sup_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\mathfrak{S}_n(P)\to0\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Estimated-denominator residual]\label{ass:denominator}
The estimated propensity obeys the same fixed overlap bound as \(p_0\) with
probability approaching one, and the denominator-substitution residual is
negligible on the upper-bound side:
\[
\sqrt n\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}
\left|Q_P(\ell;\hat g-g_0,\hat p-p_0,\hat p)\right|=o_P(1).
\]
A sufficient primitive envelope is
\(\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P)^2=o(1)\), together with bounded
report weights and fixed overlap.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Active remainder curvature]\label{ass:curvature}
There is a nondegenerate active Holder tangent cone
\(\mathcal{T}_{g,n}\times\mathcal{T}_{p,n}\) and a constant \(c_R(P)>0\) such
that
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n,\; \|u\|_{2,P}\le\delta_{g,n},\; \|v\|_{2,P}\le\delta_{p,n}}
|R_P(\ell;u,v)|
\ge c_R(P)\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P).
\]
For the empirical-process channel, there is a constant
\(c_{\mathrm{ep}}(P)>0\) such that along active report-class sequences the
learner-error class contains cross-fit nuisance estimates with errors
\(u_n\in\mathcal{T}_{g,n}\) and \(v_n\in\mathcal{T}_{p,n}\) whose \(L^2(P_n)\)
norms are comparable to \(\delta_{g,n}(P_n)\) and \(\delta_{p,n}(P_n)\) and for
which
\[
\sqrt n\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}
|\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)|
\ge c_{\mathrm{ep}}(P)\{\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P)\}\mathcal{E}_n
\]
with probability bounded away from zero.  The lower-bound statement assumes
\(\inf_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\max\{c_R(P),c_{\mathrm{ep}}(P)\}\ge c_0>0\) on the
active channel under consideration.
\end{assumption}

\section{Estimand-defining functional}
For every \(P\) satisfying Assumption \ref{ass:unconfounded}, define the
observable nuisance functions
\[
g_{d,P}(x)=\E_P[Y\mid D=d,X=x],\qquad p_P(x)=P(D=1\mid X=x),
\]
and set \(\eta_P=(g_{1,P},g_{0,P},p_P)\).  The computable identifying
functional is the map
\[
\Psi_P(\ell)
=\E_P[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_P)]
\]
with
\[
\Gamma(W;\eta_P)=g_{1,P}(X)-g_{0,P}(X)
+\frac{D}{p_P(X)}\{Y-g_{1,P}(X)\}
-\frac{1-D}{1-p_P(X)}\{Y-g_{0,P}(X)\}.
\]
Equivalently, because the residual terms have conditional mean zero,
\[
\Psi_P(\ell)=
\int \ell(x)\{g_{1,P}(x)-g_{0,P}(x)\}\,dP_X(x).
\]
This is evaluable from the observed population law by computing the two
conditional outcome means, the propensity, and the \(P_X\)-integral over the
known report weight \(\ell\).  The associated plug-in drift maps, used by the
frontier theorem, are
\[
\widetilde R_P(\ell;u,v,\bar p)=
\E_P\!\left[
\ell(X)v(X)
\left\{\frac{u_1(X)}{\bar p(X)}+\frac{u_0(X)}{1-\bar p(X)}\right\}
\right],
\]
\[
R_P(\ell;u,v)=
\E_P\!\left[
\ell(X)v(X)
\left\{\frac{u_1(X)}{p_0(X)}+\frac{u_0(X)}{1-p_0(X)}\right\}
\right],
\qquad
Q_P(\ell;u,v,\bar p)=\widetilde R_P(\ell;u,v,\bar p)-R_P(\ell;u,v).
\]
The map \(R_P\) is a bounded bilinear functional under fixed overlap and
bounded report weights:
\[
|R_P(\ell;u,v)|
\le L_\ell \underline e^{-1}
\{\|u_1\|_{2,P}+\|u_0\|_{2,P}\}\|v\|_{2,P}.
\]

\section{Target causal estimand}
The causal target is the weighted heterogeneous-treatment-effect report
\[
\theta_0(\ell)=\E[\ell(X)\{Y(1)-Y(0)\}],\qquad \ell\in\mathcal{L}_n.
\]
For \(\ell\equiv1\), this is the average treatment effect
\(\E[Y(1)-Y(0)]\).  For subgroup or contrast weights, it is the corresponding
linear CATE report, not a sampling estimator and not the Gaussian process used
for inference.  Under Assumption \ref{ass:unconfounded}, the target equals the
observable functional \(\Psi_P(\ell)\); the later theorem sections decide when
the cross-fitted first-order DML estimator of \(\Psi_P(\ell)\) has a uniform
oracle-centered Gaussian approximation over \(\mathcal{L}_n\).

\section{Main theorems}
\begin{theorem}[Conjecture 1 frontier]\label{thm:frontier}
Equivalently, under Assumptions \ref{ass:unconfounded}--\ref{ass:curvature}, first-order orthogonal DML is uniformly asymptotically linear and Gaussian for the \(\mathcal{L}_n\)-indexed HTE report process if and only if \(\sup_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\mathfrak{F}_n(P)\to0\), with necessity interpreted in the minimax/adversarial first-order experiment of Assumption \ref{ass:curvature}.
\end{theorem}

The theorem says that exact identification by the AIPW signal is not the
limiting step.  Under consistency, conditional unconfoundedness, and fixed
overlap, \(\Psi_P(\ell)=\theta_0(\ell)\) is computable from the observed law.
Compared with the standard DML product-rate statement, the new content is the
second channel
\(\mathfrak{S}_n(P)=\{\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P)\}\mathcal{E}_n\), which
records nuisance empirical-process leakage for a growing
\(\mathcal{L}_n\)-indexed report process.

\begin{theorem}[Conjecture 2: Holder-rate frontier]\label{thm:holder-frontier}
Conjecture~\ref{conj:holder} is confirmed: in the canonical Holder-rate specialization with \(r_g=\alpha_g/(2\alpha_g+d)\), \(r_p=\alpha_p/(2\alpha_p+d)\), and \(\mathcal{E}_n\asymp n^\zeta\), the two-channel frontier satisfies
\[
\mathfrak F_n(P)\asymp
\max\{n^{1/2-r_g-r_p},\,n^{\zeta-\min(r_g,r_p)}\}
\]
up to logarithmic factors, so \(\mathfrak F_n(P)\to0\) if and only if \(r_g+r_p>1/2\) and \(\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta\); above this boundary the oracle-centered first-order DML report process is uniformly Gaussian under Assumption~\ref{ass:crossfit}, while below it uniform Gaussianity fails in the active-channel experiment of Assumption~\ref{ass:curvature}.
\end{theorem}

The theorem says that the abstract frontier has two independent rate
requirements in the canonical Holder specialization.  The deterministic
orthogonal-score drift disappears exactly when \(r_g+r_p>1/2\), while the
growing HTE report class creates a separate nuisance empirical-process channel
that disappears exactly when \(\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta\).  This is the Holder-rate
form of the two-channel frontier in Theorem \ref{thm:frontier}.

\section{Interpretation}
\paragraph{Conjecture 1.}
The theorem says that exact identification by the AIPW signal is not the
limiting step.  Under consistency, conditional unconfoundedness, and fixed
overlap, \(\Psi_P(\ell)=\theta_0(\ell)\) is computable from the observed law.
The frontier is the extra first-order inference requirement for a whole
\(\mathcal{L}_n\)-indexed report process.  The term
\(\mathfrak{D}_n(P)=\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P)\) is the usual
orthogonal second-order drift, uniformly over report weights.  The term
\(\mathfrak{S}_n(P)=\{\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P)\}\mathcal{E}_n\) is the
price of estimating nuisances inside a growing empirical process.  Both must
vanish for oracle-centered first-order Gaussian studentization to be uniformly
valid.  If either active channel remains visible in the minimax first-order
experiment of Assumption \ref{ass:curvature}, the failure is structural rather
than a proof artifact.

\paragraph{Conjecture 2.}
The theorem says that the abstract frontier has two independent rate
requirements in the canonical Holder specialization.  The deterministic
orthogonal-score drift is controlled by the product of the outcome-regression
and propensity rates, so it disappears at first order exactly when
\(\sqrt n\,n^{-r_g}n^{-r_p}\to0\), or \(r_g+r_p>1/2\).  The growing HTE report
class creates a separate nuisance empirical-process channel.  Since
\(\delta_{g,n}+\delta_{p,n}\) is governed by the slower of the two nuisance
rates, that channel disappears exactly when
\(n^{-\min(r_g,r_p)}n^\zeta\to0\), or \(\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta\).

This connects the motivating frontier to the usual DML product-rate message.
When \(\mathcal L_n\) is effectively fixed, \(\zeta=0\), the entropy channel
does not bind for positive Holder smoothness and the product-rate condition is
the familiar one.  As the report class grows, the entropy inequality becomes a
second and genuinely distinct requirement: even if the product remainder is
small, first-order Gaussian inference can fail because nuisance error leaks
through the indexed empirical process.

\paragraph{Synthesis.}
Together these interpretations connect the motivating conjectures back to the
same two channels named in the Introduction: the usual orthogonal
second-order drift and the price of estimating nuisances inside a growing
empirical process.  Exact identification by the AIPW signal is not the
limiting step; the limiting step is whether both channels vanish for the
\(\mathcal{L}_n\)-indexed report process.

\section{Step-by-step proof}
\subsection*{Supporting lemmas}
\begin{theorem}[Theorem 1: exact-ID score and exact plug-in remainder]\label{thm:score-remainder}
Under Assumptions \ref{ass:unconfounded}--\ref{ass:denominator}, for every
\(\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n\),
\(\theta_0(\ell)=\E[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_0)]\), and the cross-fit plug-in
expansion satisfies
\[
\hat\theta_n(\ell)-\theta_0(\ell)
=(\mathbb{P}_n-P)\ell(X)\{\Gamma(W;\eta_0)-\theta_0(\ell)\}
+\widetilde R_P(\ell;\hat g-g_0,\hat p-p_0,\hat p)
+\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)+o_P(n^{-1/2})
\]
uniformly in \(\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n\).  Assumption \ref{ass:crossfit} bounds
\(\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}\sqrt n|\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)|\) at the
scale \(\mathfrak{S}_n(P)\), so the displayed empirical-process summand can be
dropped only under the explicit condition \(\mathfrak{S}_n(P)\to0\).  Moreover,
under Assumption \ref{ass:denominator},
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}
\left|
\sqrt n\{\widetilde R_P(\ell;\hat g-g_0,\hat p-p_0,\hat p)
-R_P(\ell;\hat g-g_0,\hat p-p_0)\}
\right|=o_P(1),
\]
so the ideal-denominator bilinear map \(R_P\) is the valid frontier object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First, by consistency and conditional unconfoundedness in Assumption \ref{ass:unconfounded},
\(g_{d,0}(X)=\E[Y(d)\mid X]\) for \(d=0,1\).  The residual terms in the AIPW signal have conditional mean zero:
\[
\E\left[\frac{D}{p_0(X)}\{Y-g_{1,0}(X)\}\mid X\right]=0,
\qquad
\E\left[\frac{1-D}{1-p_0(X)}\{Y-g_{0,0}(X)\}\mid X\right]=0,
\]
where fixed overlap justifies the divisions.  Therefore
\(\E[\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\mid X]=g_{1,0}(X)-g_{0,0}(X)=\E[Y(1)-Y(0)\mid X]\), and multiplying by the measurable bounded weight \(\ell(X)\) gives
\(\theta_0(\ell)=\E[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_0)]\).

Let \(u_d=\hat g_d-g_{d,0}\), \(v=\hat p-p_0\), and \(\bar p=\hat p\).  Conditional on the training folds, cross-fitting makes \(\hat\eta_{-k(i)}\) fixed for the empirical average over the held-out fold.  Add and subtract \(\mathbb{P}_n\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\) and then add and subtract the population mean of the plug-in difference:
\[
\hat\theta_n(\ell)-\theta_0(\ell)
=(\mathbb{P}_n-P)\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_0)
+P\ell(X)\{\Gamma(W;\hat\eta)-\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\}
+\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)+o_P(n^{-1/2}),
\]
where the final uniform \(o_P(n^{-1/2})\) is the fold-stitching term covered by Assumption \ref{ass:crossfit}.  The centered-oracle convention in Assumption \ref{ass:entropy} replaces the first empirical term by
\((\mathbb{P}_n-P)\ell(X)\{\Gamma(W;\eta_0)-\theta_0(\ell)\}\) uniformly up to \(o_P(n^{-1/2})\).

It remains to compute the population plug-in drift.  Conditional on \(X\),
\[
\E[\Gamma(W;\hat\eta)-\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\mid X]
=u_1(X)\left\{1-\frac{p_0(X)}{\bar p(X)}\right\}
+u_0(X)\left\{-1+\frac{1-p_0(X)}{1-\bar p(X)}\right\}.
\]
Since \(v=\bar p-p_0\), the two braces equal \(v/\bar p\) and \(v/(1-\bar p)\), respectively.  Thus
\[
P\ell(X)\{\Gamma(W;\hat\eta)-\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\}
=\widetilde R_P(\ell;u,v,\bar p).
\]
This proves the displayed expansion.  Assumption \ref{ass:crossfit} gives
\(\sqrt n\sup_\ell|\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)|=O_P(\mathfrak{S}_n(P))\), hence that term is negligible only if \(\mathfrak{S}_n(P)\to0\).  Finally,
\(\widetilde R_P-R_P=Q_P\), so Assumption \ref{ass:denominator} gives
\(\sqrt n\sup_\ell|\widetilde R_P(\ell;u,v,\hat p)-R_P(\ell;u,v)|=o_P(1)\).  Therefore the ideal-denominator bilinear map \(R_P\), not the estimated-denominator residual, is the frontier object.
\end{proof}

\subsubsection*{Proof of Conjecture 1}
\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:frontier}]
All statements below are uniform over \(\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n\) and
\(P\in\mathcal{P}_n\).  Let \(u=\hat g-g_0\) denote the pair
\((\hat g_1-g_{1,0},\hat g_0-g_{0,0})\) and \(v=\hat p-p_0\).  The exact-ID
identity and plug-in expansion are supplied by Theorem
\ref{thm:score-remainder}.  After multiplying that expansion by \(\sqrt n\)
and using the denominator replacement in Assumption \ref{ass:denominator}, we
obtain
\[
\sqrt n\{\hat\theta_n(\ell)-\theta_0(\ell)\}-\mathbb{G}_n(\ell)
=\sqrt n\,R_P(\ell;u,v)
+\sqrt n\,\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)+r_n(\ell),
\]
where \(\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}|r_n(\ell)|=o_P(1)\).  The divisions in
\(R_P\) and in \(\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\) are justified by the fixed overlap part of
Assumption \ref{ass:unconfounded} and the estimated-denominator event in
Assumption \ref{ass:denominator}.

First prove sufficiency.  The bilinear functional defined in Section 7 obeys,
by Cauchy--Schwarz, bounded reports, and fixed overlap,
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}|R_P(\ell;u,v)|
\le L_\ell\underline e^{-1}
\{\|u_1\|_{2,P}+\|u_0\|_{2,P}\}\|v\|_{2,P}
\le C\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P),
\]
for a constant \(C\) independent of \(n\), \(P\), and \(\ell\).  Therefore
\[
\sqrt n\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}|R_P(\ell;u,v)|
\le C\,\mathfrak{D}_n(P).
\]
Assumption \ref{ass:crossfit} gives
\[
\sqrt n\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}
|\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_n(\ell)|=O_P(\mathfrak{S}_n(P)).
\]
If \(\sup_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\mathfrak{F}_n(P)\to0\), then both
\(\sup_P\mathfrak{D}_n(P)\to0\) and \(\sup_P\mathfrak{S}_n(P)\to0\).  The last
three displays imply
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_n}
\left|\sqrt n\{\hat\theta_n(\ell)-\theta_0(\ell)\}
-\mathbb{G}_n(\ell)\right|=o_P(1)
\]
uniformly in \(P\).  This is the uniform asymptotic linear representation.  By
the oracle Gaussian approximation clause of Assumption \ref{ass:crossfit},
\(\mathbb{G}_n\) has a tight Gaussian multiplier approximation on
\(\mathcal{L}_n\) whenever \(\mathfrak{F}_n(P)\to0\).  The preceding
\(\ell^\infty(\mathcal{L}_n)\) remainder bound and Slutsky's theorem for
bounded stochastic processes transfer that Gaussian approximation from the
oracle process to the cross-fitted DML report process.  This proves the
upper-bound half.

Now prove the minimax/adversarial necessity statement in the precise sense
specified by Assumption \ref{ass:curvature}.  Suppose
\[
\limsup_{n\to\infty}\sup_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\mathfrak{F}_n(P)>0.
\]
Then for some \(\varepsilon>0\), a subsequence \(n_j\), and laws
\(P_j\in\mathcal{P}_{n_j}\), either
\(\mathfrak{D}_{n_j}(P_j)\ge\varepsilon\) along a further subsequence or
\(\mathfrak{S}_{n_j}(P_j)\ge\varepsilon\) along a further subsequence.

If the active channel is \(\mathfrak{D}_n\), Assumption
\ref{ass:curvature} supplies tangent errors \(u_j\in\mathcal{T}_{g,n_j}\) and
\(v_j\in\mathcal{T}_{p,n_j}\), with norms bounded by the locked learner radii,
and report weights \(\ell_j\in\mathcal{L}_{n_j}\) such that
\[
|R_{P_j}(\ell_j;u_j,v_j)|
\ge c_R(P_j)\delta_{g,n_j}(P_j)\delta_{p,n_j}(P_j).
\]
On the active subsequence \(c_R(P_j)\ge c_0>0\), hence
\[
\sqrt{n_j}|R_{P_j}(\ell_j;u_j,v_j)|
\ge c_0\,\mathfrak{D}_{n_j}(P_j)
\ge c_0\varepsilon .
\]
Choosing cross-fit learners in the open Holder neighborhood that attain these
tangent directions gives, by Theorem \ref{thm:score-remainder}, an
oracle-centered process with a deterministic first-order translation of size at
least \(c_0\varepsilon+o(1)\) in the active direction.  Such a translated
process cannot be uniformly centered Gaussian under the usual
oracle-centered studentization.

If the active channel is \(\mathfrak{S}_n\), Assumption
\ref{ass:curvature} instead supplies cross-fit nuisance estimates in the
active tangent class for which
\[
\sqrt{n_j}\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_{n_j}}
|\Delta^{\mathrm{ep}}_{n_j}(\ell)|
\ge c_{\mathrm{ep}}(P_j)
\{\delta_{g,n_j}(P_j)+\delta_{p,n_j}(P_j)\}\mathcal{E}_{n_j}
\]
with probability bounded away from zero.  On the active subsequence
\(c_{\mathrm{ep}}(P_j)\ge c_0>0\), so the right side is at least
\(c_0\varepsilon\) with probability bounded away from zero.  The expansion from
Theorem \ref{thm:score-remainder} then leaves a non-negligible nuisance
empirical-process component in
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal{L}_{n_j}}
\left|\sqrt{n_j}\{\hat\theta_{n_j}(\ell)-\theta_{0,j}(\ell)\}
-\mathbb{G}_{n_j}(\ell)\right|.
\]
Consequently the oracle-centered first-order Gaussian approximation is not
uniformly valid in the minimax first-order experiment.  The theorem makes no
claim about faster oracle learners outside this active error class, so this is
exactly the promised necessity statement.
\end{proof}

\subsubsection*{Proof of Conjecture 2}
\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:holder-frontier}]
Set
\[
r_g=\frac{\alpha_g}{2\alpha_g+d},
\qquad
r_p=\frac{\alpha_p}{2\alpha_p+d}.
\]
In the canonical Holder-rate specialization of Assumption \ref{ass:holder},
the uniform nuisance envelopes satisfy
\[
\delta_{g,n}(P)\asymp n^{-r_g},
\qquad
\delta_{p,n}(P)\asymp n^{-r_p},
\]
up to the logarithmic factors suppressed in the statement.  Assumption
\ref{ass:entropy} and the conjecture's specialization give
\(\mathcal{E}_n\asymp n^\zeta\), again up to logarithmic factors.  Therefore
the deterministic drift channel is
\[
\mathfrak D_n(P)
=\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P)
\asymp
n^{1/2-r_g-r_p}.
\]
Similarly,
\[
\mathfrak S_n(P)
=\{\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P)\}\mathcal{E}_n
\asymp
\{n^{-r_g}+n^{-r_p}\}n^\zeta
\asymp
n^{\zeta-\min(r_g,r_p)}.
\]
The last equivalence is the elementary identity that the larger of
\(n^{-r_g}\) and \(n^{-r_p}\) is \(n^{-\min(r_g,r_p)}\).  Since
\(\mathfrak F_n(P)=\max\{\mathfrak D_n(P),\mathfrak S_n(P)\}\), this proves
\[
\mathfrak F_n(P)\asymp
\max\{n^{1/2-r_g-r_p},\,n^{\zeta-\min(r_g,r_p)}\}
\]
with the same logarithmic convention.

A power \(n^a\) tends to zero if and only if \(a<0\).  Hence
\(\mathfrak D_n(P)\to0\) if and only if
\[
1/2-r_g-r_p<0,
\quad\hbox{that is}\quad
r_g+r_p>1/2,
\]
and \(\mathfrak S_n(P)\to0\) if and only if
\[
\zeta-\min(r_g,r_p)<0,
\quad\hbox{that is}\quad
\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta.
\]
Because the maximum of two nonnegative sequences tends to zero exactly when
both sequences tend to zero, the two displayed inequalities are equivalent to
\(\mathfrak F_n(P)\to0\).  Substituting the definitions of \(r_g\) and \(r_p\)
gives the two inequalities displayed in the motivating claim.

Now suppose the two inequalities hold uniformly over the locked
Holder/entropy class.  Theorem \ref{thm:score-remainder} gives the uniform
expansion
\[
\sqrt n\{\hat\theta_n(\ell)-\theta_0(\ell)\}
=\mathbb G_n(\ell)
+\sqrt n\,\widetilde R_P(\ell;\hat g-g_0,\hat p-p_0,\hat p)
+\sqrt n\,\Delta_n^{\mathrm{ep}}(\ell)+o_P(1)
\]
uniformly in \(\ell\in\mathcal L_n\).  Assumption \ref{ass:denominator} lets
us replace \(\widetilde R_P\) by \(R_P\) at \(o_P(1)\) first-order scale.  The
boundedness and fixed-overlap calculation in the setup gives
\[
\sqrt n\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal L_n}|R_P(\ell;\hat g-g_0,\hat p-p_0)|
\lesssim
\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P)
=\mathfrak D_n(P)=o(1).
\]
Assumption \ref{ass:crossfit} gives
\[
\sqrt n\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal L_n}|\Delta_n^{\mathrm{ep}}(\ell)|
=O_P(\mathfrak S_n(P))=o_P(1).
\]
Thus
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal L_n}
\left|
\sqrt n\{\hat\theta_n(\ell)-\theta_0(\ell)\}-\mathbb G_n(\ell)
\right|
\to_P0.
\]
The oracle process \(\mathbb G_n\) admits the tight Gaussian multiplier
approximation by Assumption \ref{ass:crossfit} whenever
\(\mathfrak F_n(P)\to0\).  Combining this approximation with the displayed
uniform \(o_P(1)\) difference proves the upper-bound claim: first-order DML is
uniformly Gaussian above the Holder boundary.

Conversely, suppose the product inequality fails, so \(r_g+r_p\le1/2\).  Then
\(\mathfrak D_n(P)\) does not tend to zero.  In the active drift channel,
Assumption \ref{ass:curvature} supplies a nondegenerate Holder tangent cone
and a constant bounded away from zero along an admissible sequence such that
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal L_n}|R_P(\ell;u_n,v_n)|
\ge c_R(P)\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P).
\]
Multiplying by \(\sqrt n\) gives a first-order deterministic translation whose
liminf is bounded away from zero along the active sequence.  The oracle-centered
process therefore cannot be uniformly approximated by \(\mathbb G_n\) over the
locked first-order experiment.

If instead the entropy inequality fails, so \(\min(r_g,r_p)\le\zeta\), then
\(\mathfrak S_n(P)\) does not tend to zero.  In the active empirical-process
channel, Assumption \ref{ass:curvature} supplies cross-fit nuisance errors for
which
\[
\sqrt n\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal L_n}|\Delta_n^{\mathrm{ep}}(\ell)|
\ge
c_{\mathrm{ep}}(P)\{\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P)\}\mathcal{E}_n
\]
with probability bounded away from zero.  Thus the nuisance empirical-process
leakage remains visible at first order, again preventing a uniform
oracle-centered Gaussian approximation.  These two cases cover the complement
of the strict Holder boundary and prove the claimed failure below it in the
active-channel experiment of Assumption \ref{ass:curvature}.
\end{proof}

\section{Sanity checks}
\paragraph{Conjecture 1.}
Take the randomized special case \(X=\emptyset\), \(\mathcal{L}_n=\{1\}\), and
\(p_0=\Pr(D=1)\in[\underline e,1-\underline e]\).  The target is the ATE
\(\theta_0= \E[Y(1)-Y(0)]\), and the AIPW functional collapses to
\[
\Psi_P(1)=g_{1,0}-g_{0,0}
+\E\!\left[\frac{D}{p_0}(Y-g_{1,0})\right]
-\E\!\left[\frac{1-D}{1-p_0}(Y-g_{0,0})\right]
=g_{1,0}-g_{0,0}.
\]
Because treatment is randomized, \(g_{d,0}=\E[Y(d)]\), so
\(\Psi_P(1)=\theta_0\).  The report entropy is constant, so
\(\mathcal{E}_n\asymp1\), and the theorem reduces to the familiar scalar DML
condition
\(\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}\delta_{p,n}\to0\) together with
\(\delta_{g,n}+\delta_{p,n}\to0\) for the nuisance empirical-process term.
With oracle nuisances both channels are zero and the estimator is the usual
centered empirical mean of the efficient ATE signal.

\paragraph{Conjecture 2.}
Take the fixed-report corner \(\mathcal{E}_n\asymp1\), so \(\zeta=0\).  Because
\(\alpha_g,\alpha_p>0\), both \(r_g\) and \(r_p\) are positive, and the entropy
condition
\[
\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta
\]
is automatic.  The theorem reduces to the familiar first-order DML
product-rate condition
\[
r_g+r_p>1/2.
\]
For a symmetric nuisance class with \(\alpha_g=\alpha_p=\alpha\), this becomes
\[
2\frac{\alpha}{2\alpha+d}>\frac12,
\]
or equivalently \(\alpha>d/2\).  Thus the fixed-report special case recovers
the usual product-rate elbow: each nuisance may be slower than \(n^{-1/4}\)
only if the other is faster enough that the product remains \(o(n^{-1/2})\),
and in the symmetric case both must beat the \(n^{-1/4}\) rate.

\section{Counterexample or minimality check}
\paragraph{Conjecture 1.}
The required ExactID refutation primitive was the smallest finite SWIG/DAG
witness \(X\to D\to Y\) and \(X\to Y\).  I searched the two-point case
\(X\in\{0,1\}\) with \(0<\Pr(X=1)<1\), overlap
\(\underline e\le p_0(x)\le1-\underline e\), bounded potential outcomes
\(Y(d)\), and report classes containing either \(\ell\equiv1\) or the two cell
indicators.  This search cannot falsify the causal exact-ID part: by
Assumption \ref{ass:unconfounded}, for every admissible finite law,
\[
\E[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\mid X]
=\ell(X)\{g_{1,0}(X)-g_{0,0}(X)\}
=\ell(X)\E[Y(1)-Y(0)\mid X],
\]
so the observable AIPW functional equals the target report cell by cell.  I
also checked the one-cell randomized subcase \(X=\emptyset\), \(p_0\in
[\underline e,1-\underline e]\).  With oracle nuisances, \(\delta_{g,n}=
\delta_{p,n}=0\), hence \(\mathfrak{F}_n(P)=0\) and Theorem
\ref{thm:score-remainder} reduces the estimator to the oracle empirical
process, exactly as the conjecture predicts.  With adversarial nonzero nuisance
errors \(u=\hat g-g_0\) and \(v=\hat p-p_0\), the same one-cell law gives the
deterministic drift
\[
R_P(1;u,v)
=v\left\{\frac{u_1}{p_0}+\frac{u_0}{1-p_0}\right\},
\]
so if \(\sqrt n|uv|\) is bounded away from zero, the oracle-centered estimator
has a first-order translation rather than a counterexample to the theorem.  The
finite-support overlap-violation candidate with \(p_0(x)=0\) in one stratum is
outside Assumption \ref{ass:unconfounded}.  Thus the primitive search found no
admissible witness against the conjecture; it instead reproduces the two
channels named in the statement.

Fixed overlap is a genuine support condition.  In the two-stratum finite law
with \(X\in\{0,1\}\), \(\Pr(X=x)>0\), and \(p_0(1)=0\), the target contribution
\(\Pr(X=1)\ell(1)\E[Y(1)-Y(0)\mid X=1]\) is part of
\(\theta_0(\ell)\), but the treated conditional mean \(g_{1,0}(1)=\E[Y\mid
D=1,X=1]\) is not identified from the observed law.  The AIPW signal also
contains the undefined factor \(D/p_0(1)\).  Thus dropping positivity on a
stratum used by \(\ell\) breaks the computable identifying functional before
any DML frontier question arises.  Separately, keeping overlap but dropping
the active-curvature lower bound removes the minimax necessity conclusion:
there may be no admissible nuisance tangent direction that realizes a
first-order \(R_P\) translation or empirical-process leakage even when a
high-level error envelope is numerically large.

\paragraph{Conjecture 2.}
The required refutation-first check was run inside the exact-identification
regime locked by the setup: \(X\to D\to Y\), \(X\to Y\), binary \(D\),
consistency, conditional unconfoundedness, fixed overlap, Holder nuisance
classes, and the two-channel first-order experiment in Assumptions
\ref{ass:unconfounded}--\ref{ass:curvature}.

First, consider the three-node SWIG/back-door primitive with a finite law on
\((X,D,Y)\).  For any candidate satisfying
\((Y(1),Y(0))\perp D\mid X\), consistency, and
\(\underline e\le p_0(X)\le1-\underline e\), Theorem
\ref{thm:score-remainder} gives the exact observable identity
\[
\theta_0(\ell)=\E[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_0)]
\]
for every report weight \(\ell\).  Thus no three-node exact-ID witness can
make the target report differ from the AIPW functional.  Such a witness would
have to break the rate frontier, not the identifying formula.

Second, try the finite-support overlap-violation primitive.  On a two-point
support, setting \(p_0(x_0)=0\) or \(p_0(x_0)=1\) would indeed obstruct the
terms \(D\{Y-g_{1,0}(X)\}/p_0(X)\) or
\((1-D)\{Y-g_{0,0}(X)\}/\{1-p_0(X)\}\).  This is not an admissible
counterexample, because Assumption \ref{ass:unconfounded} imposes the fixed
overlap bound on every stratum actually used by \(\ell\).  Keeping fixed
overlap leaves the AIPW map well defined and does not change the two exponents
\(\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}\delta_{p,n}\) and
\((\delta_{g,n}+\delta_{p,n})\mathcal{E}_n\).

Third, trace the do-calculus/adjustment step.  The only conditional
independence required for the exact-ID part is the back-door independence
\((Y(1),Y(0))\perp D\mid X\), already named in Assumption
\ref{ass:unconfounded}.  Once that adjustment is in place, the conjecture
reduces to the deterministic exponent comparison
\[
\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}\delta_{p,n}
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
(\delta_{g,n}+\delta_{p,n})\mathcal{E}_n.
\]
The boundary cases do not refute the conjecture: if \(r_g+r_p=1/2\), the
drift channel is order one, and if \(\min(r_g,r_p)=\zeta\), the
empirical-process leakage channel is order one.  Assumption
\ref{ass:curvature} supplies active tangent directions for those channels, so
these boundary candidates support the strict inequalities rather than
contradicting them.  The bounded search therefore finds no admissible witness,
and the proof verifies the claim.

The strict inequalities are minimal.  At the deterministic-drift boundary,
choose an active Holder sequence from Assumption \ref{ass:curvature} with
\(r_g+r_p=1/2\) and \(\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta\).  Then
\[
\mathfrak D_n(P)\asymp n^{1/2-r_g-r_p}\asymp1,
\qquad
\mathfrak S_n(P)=o(1).
\]
Theorem \ref{thm:score-remainder} and the active \(R_P\) lower bound imply
that
\[
\sup_{\ell\in\mathcal L_n}
\left|
\sqrt n\{\hat\theta_n(\ell)-\theta_0(\ell)\}-\mathbb G_n(\ell)
\right|
\]
has a deterministic translation bounded away from zero along the active
sequence.  Replacing \(r_g+r_p>1/2\) by \(r_g+r_p\ge1/2\) would therefore be
false.

At the entropy boundary, choose an active report-class sequence from
Assumption \ref{ass:curvature} with \(\min(r_g,r_p)=\zeta\) and
\(r_g+r_p>1/2\).  Then
\[
\mathfrak D_n(P)=o(1),
\qquad
\mathfrak S_n(P)\asymp1.
\]
The empirical-process lower bound in Assumption \ref{ass:curvature} gives
non-negligible \(\sqrt n\,\Delta_n^{\mathrm{ep}}\) with probability bounded
away from zero.  Replacing \(\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta\) by
\(\min(r_g,r_p)\ge\zeta\) would therefore also be false.  Fixed overlap in
Assumption \ref{ass:unconfounded}, the denominator condition in Assumption
\ref{ass:denominator}, and the active-channel lower bounds in Assumption
\ref{ass:curvature} are the support and rank conditions that make these
minimality witnesses valid.

\paragraph{Substrate audit record.}
\texttt{bypass-justified}: the theorem is a semiparametric
Holder/entropy DML frontier over high-level stochastic-process assumptions.
Declaration-name searches for \texttt{Identifiable}, \texttt{Backdoor},
\texttt{Frontdoor}, and \texttt{ATE} did not surface a reusable
project-level declaration matching the two-channel rate boundary or active
first-order experiment; the formalization plan should therefore start from
the mathematical objects listed below.

\begin{description}[leftmargin=2.8cm,style=nextline]
\item[Observed law.]
Typed signature: a probability law \(P\) on \(W=(Y,D,X)\), with
\(D\in\{0,1\}\), \(X\in[0,1]^d\), and \(|Y|\le M\).
Role: source of all observable conditional means, propensities, expectations,
and \(L^2(P)\) norms.
Dependencies: measurability, bounded outcomes, binary treatment, covariate
support.

\item[Potential outcomes.]
Typed signature: random variables \(Y(1)\) and \(Y(0)\) on the same probability
space as \(W\).
Role: define the causal target
\(\theta_0(\ell)=\E[\ell(X)\{Y(1)-Y(0)\}]\).
Dependencies: consistency and conditional unconfoundedness.

\item[Unconfoundedness and overlap.]
Typed signature: assumptions \(Y=DY(1)+(1-D)Y(0)\),
\((Y(1),Y(0))\perp D\mid X\), and
\(\underline e\le p_0(X)\le 1-\underline e\) almost surely.
Role: identify the target by the observable AIPW functional and justify all
divisions by \(p_0\) and \(1-p_0\).
Dependencies: observed law and potential outcomes.

\item[Outcome regressions and propensity.]
Typed signature:
\[
g_{d,0}:x\mapsto \E[Y\mid D=d,X=x],\qquad
p_0:x\mapsto \Pr(D=1\mid X=x).
\]
Role: define \(\tau_0(x)=g_{1,0}(x)-g_{0,0}(x)\), the AIPW signal, and the
drift maps.
Dependencies: observed law and overlap.

\item[Report class.]
Typed signature: deterministic bounded measurable class
\(\mathcal L_n\subset\{[0,1]^d\to\mathbb R\}\) with envelope \(L_\ell\) and
entropy integral \(\mathcal E_n\).
Role: index the HTE report process and scale the nuisance empirical-process
channel.
Dependencies: measurability, boundedness, entropy condition.

\item[AIPW signal.]
Typed signature:
\[
\Gamma(W;\eta)=g_1(X)-g_0(X)
+\frac{D}{p(X)}\{Y-g_1(X)\}
-\frac{1-D}{1-p(X)}\{Y-g_0(X)\}.
\]
Role: computable exact-ID signal satisfying
\(\E[\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\mid X]=\E[Y(1)-Y(0)\mid X]\).
Dependencies: outcome regressions, propensity, overlap.

\item[Identifying functional.]
Typed signature:
\[
\Psi_P:\mathcal L_n\to\mathbb R,\qquad
\Psi_P(\ell)=\E_P[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\eta_P)].
\]
Role: observed-population map equal to \(\theta_0(\ell)\) under the
identifying assumptions.
Dependencies: AIPW signal, report class, unconfoundedness, overlap.

\item[Cross-fitted estimator.]
Typed signature:
\[
\hat\theta_n(\ell)=\mathbb P_n[\ell(X)\Gamma(W;\hat\eta_{-k(i)})].
\]
Role: estimator whose oracle-centered first-order expansion is analyzed.
Dependencies: fold split, nuisance estimates, AIPW signal.

\item[Nuisance error radii.]
Typed signature:
\[
\delta_{g,n}(P)=\|\hat g_1-g_{1,0}\|_{2,P}+\|\hat g_0-g_{0,0}\|_{2,P},
\qquad
\delta_{p,n}(P)=\|\hat p-p_0\|_{2,P}.
\]
Role: determine the deterministic drift and empirical-process channel sizes.
Dependencies: \(L^2(P)\) norm and cross-fitted nuisance estimates.

\item[Drift maps.]
Typed signature:
\[
\widetilde R_P(\ell;u,v,\bar p)=
\E_P\!\left[\ell(X)v(X)
\left\{\frac{u_1(X)}{\bar p(X)}+\frac{u_0(X)}{1-\bar p(X)}\right\}\right],
\]
\[
R_P(\ell;u,v)=
\E_P\!\left[\ell(X)v(X)
\left\{\frac{u_1(X)}{p_0(X)}+\frac{u_0(X)}{1-p_0(X)}\right\}\right].
\]
Role: exact plug-in drift and ideal-denominator frontier object.
Dependencies: nuisance errors, report weight, overlap, denominator condition.

\item[Bilinear bound.]
Typed signature:
\[
|R_P(\ell;u,v)|
\le L_\ell \underline e^{-1}
\{\|u_1\|_{2,P}+\|u_0\|_{2,P}\}\|v\|_{2,P}.
\]
Role: sufficiency step controlling deterministic drift by
\(\mathfrak D_n(P)\).
Dependencies: Cauchy--Schwarz, bounded reports, fixed overlap.

\item[Oracle empirical process.]
Typed signature:
\[
\mathbb G_n(\ell)=
\sqrt n(\mathbb P_n-P)[\ell(X)\{\Gamma(W;\eta_0)-\theta_0(\ell)\}].
\]
Role: Gaussian reference process for first-order asymptotic linearity.
Dependencies: oracle signal, report class, cross-fitting regularity.

\item[Nuisance empirical-process remainder.]
Typed signature:
\[
\Delta_n^{\mathrm{ep}}(\ell)=
(\mathbb P_n-P)[\ell(X)\{\Gamma(W;\hat\eta_{-k(i)})-\Gamma(W;\eta_0)\}].
\]
Role: second channel in the frontier, bounded at scale
\(\mathfrak S_n(P)\).
Dependencies: cross-fitting, report entropy, nuisance errors.

\item[Frontier channels.]
Typed signature:
\[
\mathfrak D_n(P)=\sqrt n\,\delta_{g,n}(P)\delta_{p,n}(P),\quad
\mathfrak S_n(P)=\{\delta_{g,n}(P)+\delta_{p,n}(P)\}\mathcal E_n,\quad
\mathfrak F_n(P)=\max\{\mathfrak D_n(P),\mathfrak S_n(P)\}.
\]
Role: necessary-and-sufficient boundary for first-order Gaussianity in the
active experiment.
Dependencies: nuisance radii, entropy integral, sample size.

\item[Holder-rate specialization.]
Typed signature:
\[
r_g=\frac{\alpha_g}{2\alpha_g+d},\qquad
r_p=\frac{\alpha_p}{2\alpha_p+d},\qquad
\delta_{g,n}\asymp n^{-r_g},\quad \delta_{p,n}\asymp n^{-r_p},\quad
\mathcal E_n\asymp n^\zeta.
\]
Role: converts the abstract frontier into the exponent boundary
\(r_g+r_p>1/2\) and \(\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta\).
Dependencies: Holder nuisance assumption and entropy specialization.

\item[Active curvature experiment.]
Typed signature: tangent cones
\(\mathcal T_{g,n}\times\mathcal T_{p,n}\) and lower-bound constants
\(c_R(P)\), \(c_{\mathrm{ep}}(P)\) realizing either the drift or leakage
channel.
Role: gives the minimax/adversarial necessity half and the boundary
minimality witnesses.
Dependencies: Assumption \ref{ass:curvature}, nuisance radii, report class.
\end{description}

\section{Conclusion and references}
Conjecture 1 (frontier) is confirmed; Equivalently, under Assumptions \ref{ass:unconfounded}--\ref{ass:curvature}, first-order orthogonal DML is uniformly asymptotically linear and Gaussian for the \(\mathcal{L}_n\)-indexed HTE report process if and only if \(\sup_{P\in\mathcal{P}_n}\mathfrak{F}_n(P)\to0\), with necessity interpreted in the minimax/adversarial first-order experiment of Assumption \ref{ass:curvature}.

Conjecture 2 (holder) is confirmed; Conjecture~\ref{conj:holder} is confirmed: in the canonical Holder-rate specialization with \(r_g=\alpha_g/(2\alpha_g+d)\), \(r_p=\alpha_p/(2\alpha_p+d)\), and \(\mathcal{E}_n\asymp n^\zeta\), the two-channel frontier satisfies
\[
\mathfrak F_n(P)\asymp
\max\{n^{1/2-r_g-r_p},\,n^{\zeta-\min(r_g,r_p)}\}
\]
up to logarithmic factors, so \(\mathfrak F_n(P)\to0\) if and only if \(r_g+r_p>1/2\) and \(\min(r_g,r_p)>\zeta\); above this boundary the oracle-centered first-order DML report process is uniformly Gaussian under Assumption~\ref{ass:crossfit}, while below it uniform Gaussianity fails in the active-channel experiment of Assumption~\ref{ass:curvature}.
