\section*{pid_cascade_escalation_rd (postselect_honest)}

\paragraph{TL;DR.} The proposal treats the selected LLM-cascade cutoff as a population-selected boundary problem whose primary object is the M-indexed interval [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)] generated by the local extension class H_*(P,M), not a uniquely observed-law point target in the atomic isotonic-score regime. The main theorem is an attainment result inside that pre-imposed class: once tau_*(P) and H_*(P,M) are fixed, the full set {mu(tau_*(P)) : mu in H_*(P,M)} is exactly [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)]. The companion proposition repackages this nested interval family into the critical-curvature summary M_*(P) and notes that K_P, Q_P, and tau_*(P) can remain fixed while admissible local representatives still disagree on the boundary value whenever the endpoint set is non-singleton. The dense-regime inference rung remains open and asks for a post-selection-honest generated-score correction only in the narrower setting where V_cont(P) = g_P(tau_*(P)) is point-identified.

\section{Project justification}

\paragraph{Gap.} Current cascade papers optimize or analyze threshold frontiers but treat the chosen cutoff as a descriptive operating point, not as a selected local causal target with an honest identified set. Honest RD papers derive fixed-cutoff endpoint intervals, and post-selection CI papers define selected targets in parametric families, but no cited work derives the sharp selected-target bound [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)] for a population routing cutoff chosen by the same cost-quality frontier that the paper studies.

\paragraph{Niche.} The open niche is a selected-target sensitivity theory for deterministic LLM routing that is local, comparator-aware, and compatible with isotonic score atoms.

\paragraph{Fill.} The project fills that niche by defining tau_*(P) at the population level, deriving the M-indexed interval generated by the selected local extension class at that cutoff, reducing to the fixed-cutoff honest endpoint problem when the cutoff is exogenous, recording through a same-law representative ambiguity statement that full cost and quality frontiers still need not identify the selected local value when the endpoint set is non-singleton, translating that nested interval family into the critical-curvature summary M_*(P), and isolating a narrower dense-regime generated-score correction problem for point-identified inference on V_cont(P).

\section{Related work}

Kotte2026UCCI is the application anchor because Section 4.3 selects the escalation threshold by constrained empirical cost minimization after isotonic calibration of token-margin uncertainty. The present proposal is informed by that selection problem rather than an extension of its optimality theorem: it asks what sensitivity interval is generated at the population-selected cutoff once the local extension class is fixed. Bouchard2026EscalationWorthIt is the closest deterministic-cascade comparator because it characterizes the cost-quality frontier through piecewise concavity and pairwise-envelope geometry. The present note uses that frontier literature mainly as a contrast case: even with the same observed law P, the same K_P, the same Q_P, and the same selected cutoff tau_*(P), admissible local representatives can still imply different selected boundary values when the endpoint interval is non-singleton. ArmstrongKolesar2018OptimalInference supplies the fixed-target honest endpoint logic for linear functionals over convex smoothness classes, and Theorem 1 is essentially that compactness-and-attainment argument evaluated at the selected cutoff. KolesarRothe2018DiscreteRD is the closest discrete-support fixed-cutoff analogue because its guaranteed-coverage endpoint logic under running-variable mass points matches the exogenous-cutoff special case of the selected-class problem. ZhangBenMichaelImai2022SafePolicyRD is downgraded to an informed-by policy-learning comparator: it decomposes utility into identifiable and unidentifiable parts and learns improved cutoffs under smooth cross-cutoff extrapolation, whereas the present proposal fixes one selected threshold and studies the sensitivity interval at that frontier point. ArmstrongKolesar2018BandwidthSnooping and BachocPreinerstorferSteinberger2016PostSelection are likewise informed-by templates for the open inference rung rather than theorem-level reductions, because they study selection-adjusted inference for bandwidths or parametric model-specific targets rather than smooth selected RD endpoints. DongKolesar2021RunningVariableMeasurementError and Pernot2023IsotonicStratification guide the generated-score side because they explain when an observed score proxy can carry causal meaning and why isotonic calibration creates score plateaus and mass points. FrauenEtAl2026CausalLLM motivates routing as a causal design problem and leaves open the formal local estimand under deterministic thresholding. KhanSaveskiUgander2023OPEBeyondOverlap supplies the partial-identification template when global overlap fails. CalonicoCattaneoTitiunik2014RDRobustCI and CalonicoCattaneoFarrell2018OptimalBandwidthRBC provide the fixed-cutoff local-linear machinery that the open inference handle adapts only in the locally dense score regime.

\section{Assumptions}

\begin{assumption}[ass:iid-audit]\label{ass:iid-audit}
O_1, ..., O_n are i.i.d. draws from P.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (i.i.d. sampling, \cite{KolesarRothe2018DiscreteRD}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:bounded-quality]\label{ass:bounded-quality}
Y_s in [0,1] and Y_l in [0,1] P-a.s.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (bounded outcomes, \cite{KhanSaveskiUgander2023OPEBeyondOverlap}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:population-feasible-threshold]\label{ass:population-feasible-threshold}
{tau in [0,1] : Q_P(tau) >= qbar} is nonempty.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Novel.} A calibrated cascade can only have a population selected cutoff if the deployment quality target is attainable by at least one threshold rule. This is realistic in audit settings that tune qbar to a service-level target below the always-escalate quality level and above the never-escalate quality level, which guarantees a nonempty feasible threshold set.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:local-two-sided-atom-support]\label{ass:local-two-sided-atom-support}
There exist s_minus, s_plus in A_P such that tau_*(P) - h_0 <= s_minus < tau_*(P) < s_plus <= tau_*(P) + h_0.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Novel.} Isotonic calibration commonly produces score plateaus with positive mass on both sides of an operational cutoff because nearby uncertainty bins are pooled into step levels. The assumption isolates the empirically relevant case where the selected threshold is bracketed by adjacent calibrated score atoms, so local interpolation between observed score cells is the right object.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:score-cef-extension]\label{ass:score-cef-extension}
There exists mu_P in C^2([tau_*(P) - h_0, tau_*(P) + h_0]) such that mu_P(s) = m_P(s) for every s in A_P intersect [tau_*(P) - h_0, tau_*(P) + h_0].
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Novel.} Calibrated cascade performance should vary smoothly across latent difficulty even when isotonic fitting coarsens the observed score into atoms. The assumption encodes that local smooth latent relation directly by requiring a twice-differentiable representative through the observed atomic conditional means near the selected cutoff.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:curvature-budget]\label{ass:curvature-budget}
sup_{c in [tau_*(P) - h_0, tau_*(P) + h_0]} |mu_P''(c)| <= M.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (second-derivative bound, \cite{ArmstrongKolesar2020SimpleHonest}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:quadratic-selection-growth]\label{ass:quadratic-selection-growth}
K_P(tau) - K_P(tau_*(P)) >= lambda_sel min{(tau - tau_*(P))^2, r_sel^2} for every tau in [0,1] with Q_P(tau) >= qbar.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Novel.} When the constrained deployment frontier is smooth and the selected cutoff is an isolated optimum with positive second derivative along the feasible frontier, nearby feasible threshold perturbations raise cost only quadratically while thresholds outside the local neighborhood remain uniformly worse.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:continuous-local-score-density]\label{ass:continuous-local-score-density}
C admits a density f_{C,P} on [tau_*(P) - h_0, tau_*(P) + h_0], f_{C,P} is continuous at tau_*(P), and f_{C,P}(tau_*(P)) > 0.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (positive local score density, \cite{CalonicoCattaneoTitiunik2014RDRobustCI}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:continuous-local-regression]\label{ass:continuous-local-regression}
The conditional mean g_P(c) = E_P[Delta | C = c] exists for c in [tau_*(P) - h_0, tau_*(P) + h_0] and equals mu_P(c) on that interval.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (observed-score conditional mean representation, \cite{DongKolesar2021RunningVariableMeasurementError}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:local-variance-continuity]\label{ass:local-variance-continuity}
v_P(c) = Var_P(Delta | C = c) is continuous at tau_*(P) and 0 < v_P(tau_*(P)) < infinity.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (continuous positive conditional variance, \cite{CalonicoCattaneoTitiunik2014RDRobustCI}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:bandwidth-shrinking]\label{ass:bandwidth-shrinking}
h_n -> 0.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (shrinking RD bandwidth, \cite{CalonicoCattaneoFarrell2018OptimalBandwidthRBC}).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[ass:local-effective-sample]\label{ass:local-effective-sample}
min{N_{P,-}(h_n), N_{P,+}(h_n)} -> infinity.
\par\smallskip\noindent\emph{Standard} (diverging local effective sample size, \cite{CalonicoCattaneoFarrell2018OptimalBandwidthRBC}).
\end{assumption}

\section{Definitions}

\begin{definition}[def:law-class-atomic]\label{def:law-class-atomic}
$P_atom(M) := {P : ass:bounded-quality, ass:population-feasible-threshold, ass:local-two-sided-atom-support, ass:score-cef-extension, ass:curvature-budget hold under P}$ --- the class of all objects satisfying: ass:bounded-quality; ass:population-feasible-threshold; ass:local-two-sided-atom-support; ass:score-cef-extension; ass:curvature-budget.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:law-class-continuous]\label{def:law-class-continuous}
$P_cont(M) := {P : ass:bounded-quality, ass:population-feasible-threshold, ass:continuous-local-score-density, ass:continuous-local-regression, ass:curvature-budget, ass:local-variance-continuity hold under P}$ --- the class of all objects satisfying: ass:bounded-quality; ass:population-feasible-threshold; ass:continuous-local-score-density; ass:continuous-local-regression; ass:curvature-budget; ass:local-variance-continuity.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:population-frontiers]\label{def:population-frontiers}
$K_P and Q_P := K_P(tau) = E_P[kappa * 1{C >= tau}] and Q_P(tau) = E_P[Y_s + 1{C >= tau}(Y_l - Y_s)]$
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:selected-threshold-set]\label{def:selected-threshold-set}
$T_*(P) := T_*(P) = argmin_{tau in [0,1]} K_P(tau) subject to Q_P(tau) >= qbar$ \quad (\text{inputs: } P, K_P, Q_P, qbar)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:selected-threshold]\label{def:selected-threshold}
$tau_*(P) := tau_*(P) = inf T_*(P)$ \quad (\text{inputs: } T_*(P))
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:local-extension-class]\label{def:local-extension-class}
$H_*(P,M) := H_*(P,M) = {mu in W^{2,\infty}([tau_*(P)-h_0, tau_*(P)+h_0]) : mu(s) = m_P(s) for each s in A_P intersect [tau_*(P)-h_0, tau_*(P)+h_0], ess sup_{c in [tau_*(P)-h_0, tau_*(P)+h_0]} |mu''(c)| <= M, and mu(c) in [-2,1] for every c in [tau_*(P)-h_0, tau_*(P)+h_0]}$ \quad (\text{inputs: } P, M, tau_*(P), h_0, m_P, A_P)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:selected-endpoints]\label{def:selected-endpoints}
$L_*(P,M) and U_*(P,M) := L_*(P,M) = inf_{mu in H_*(P,M)} mu(tau_*(P)) and U_*(P,M) = sup_{mu in H_*(P,M)} mu(tau_*(P))$ \quad (\text{inputs: } H_*(P,M), tau_*(P))
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:critical-curvature]\label{def:critical-curvature}
$M_*(P) := M_*(P) = inf{M >= 0 : 0 in [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)]}$ \quad (\text{inputs: } L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M))
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:dense-selected-value]\label{def:dense-selected-value}
$V_cont(P) := V_cont(P) = g_P(tau_*(P))$ \quad (\text{inputs: } g_P, tau_*(P))
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:generated-score-construction]\label{def:generated-score-construction}
$hat C_i := hat C_i is any out-of-fold monotone calibration score obtained by fitting a calibration map on a subsample that excludes observation i and evaluating that map at U_i$ \quad (\text{inputs: } O_1, ..., O_n, U, c_P)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:selected-ll-estimator]\label{def:selected-ll-estimator}
$hat V_ll(M) := hat V_ll(M) is the intercept from the local-linear weighted least-squares regression of Y_{l,i} - Y_{s,i} - kappa on 1 and hat C_i - hat tau using kernel weights K((hat C_i - hat tau)/h_n) over observations with |hat C_i - hat tau| <= h_n, where K is a bounded symmetric kernel with compact support, integral 1, positive mass on a neighborhood of 0, and nondegenerate second moment 0 < integral u^2 K(u) du < infinity$ \quad (\text{inputs: } hat C_i, hat tau, h_n, K, kappa)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:generated-score-drift-handle]\label{def:generated-score-drift-handle}
$hat G_n := Construct hat G_n additively as hat G_n = hat g_{gen,n} + hat g_{shift,n} + hat g_{rem,n}, where hat g_{gen,n} is a data-only envelope for the orthogonalized first-order generated-score perturbation created by replacing the population score C with the generated score hat C in the local-linear score, hat g_{shift,n} is a data-only envelope for the selected-threshold target shift |g_P(hat tau)-g_P(tau_*(P))| induced by evaluating at the random selected cutoff hat tau rather than the population cutoff tau_*(P), and hat g_{rem,n} is a data-only envelope for the residual second-order generated-score and score-discretization remainder.$ \quad (\text{inputs: } hat C_i, hat tau, h_n, K, c_P)
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[def:selected-honest-ci]\label{def:selected-honest-ci}
$C_n(M) := C_n(M) = [hat V_ll(M) - cv_{1-alpha}^{sel} hat sigma_n - hat B_n(M) - hat G_n, hat V_ll(M) + cv_{1-alpha}^{sel} hat sigma_n + hat B_n(M) + hat G_n]$ \quad (\text{inputs: } hat V_ll(M), hat sigma_n, hat B_n(M), hat G_n, cv_{1-alpha}^{sel})
\end{definition}

\section{Main results}

\begin{theorem}[thm:selected-sharp-set]\label{thm:selected-sharp-set}
Fix an observed-data law P in P_atom(M) and suppose H_*(P,M) is nonempty. Then the attainable evaluation set generated by the local extension class is exactly {mu(tau_*(P)) : mu in H_*(P,M)} = [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)]. Moreover each point of [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)] is attained by some mu in H_*(P,M).
\end{theorem}
Let \tau_*:=\tau_*(P), I:=[\tau_*-h_0,\tau_*+h_0], and H:=H_*(P,M). The theorem assumes H is nonempty. By def:local-extension-class, every \mu in H belongs to W^{2,\infty}(I), satisfies \mu(s)=m_P(s) for every local score atom s in A_P \cap I, obeys ess sup_I |\mu''| <= M, and lies in [-2,1] pointwise on I. First the interpolation values themselves are range-compatible. By ass:bounded-quality and \kappa in [0,1], one has -2 <= \Delta = Y_l - Y_s - \kappa <= 1 almost surely. Hence for every s in A_P \cap I, m_P(s)=E_P[\Delta \mid C=s] also lies in [-2,1]. Next H is convex: if \mu_1,\mu_2 in H and \lambda in [0,1], then \mu_\lambda:=(1-\lambda)\mu_1+\lambda\mu_2 preserves the interpolation equalities, stays in [-2,1] pointwise because that interval is convex, and satisfies ess sup_I |\mu_\lambda''| <= (1-\lambda) ess sup_I |\mu_1''| + \lambda ess sup_I |\mu_2''| <= M. Now prove compactness in C^1(I). Write I=[a,b]. For any \mu in H, the range restriction gives sup_I |\mu| <= 2. By the mean-value theorem there exists \xi_\mu in (a,b) with \mu'(\xi_\mu) = (\mu(b)-\mu(a))/(b-a). Since \mu(a),\mu(b) in [-2,1] and b-a=2h_0, |\mu'(\xi_\mu)| <= 3/(2h_0). Because ess sup_I |\mu''| <= M, the derivative \mu' is M-Lipschitz on I; therefore for every c in I, |\mu'(c)| <= |\mu'(\xi_\mu)| + M|c-\xi_\mu| <= 3/(2h_0) + 2Mh_0 =: B. Thus every \mu in H satisfies sup_I |\mu| <= 2, sup_I |\mu'| <= B, and \mu' is M-Lipschitz. By lem:arzela-ascoli-c1-compactness, H is relatively compact in C^1(I). It is also closed in that topology: if \mu_n in H and \mu_n -> \bar\mu, \mu_n' -> \bar\mu' uniformly, then the interpolation equalities pass to the limit pointwise, the range restriction passes to the limit because [-2,1] is closed, and the uniform limit of M-Lipschitz derivatives is again M-Lipschitz, so \bar\mu belongs to W^{2,\infty}(I) with ess sup_I |\bar\mu''| <= M. Hence \bar\mu in H. Therefore H is compact in C^1(I). Consider the evaluation functional E:H -> R, E(\mu):=\mu(\tau_*). It is continuous on C^1(I), so on compact H it attains its minimum and maximum. Therefore there exist \mu_L,\mu_U in H with E(\mu_L)=L_*(P,M) and E(\mu_U)=U_*(P,M). Since H is convex and E is linear, E(H) is a convex subset of R, hence an interval; because it contains its minimum and maximum, E(H)=[L_*(P,M),U_*(P,M)]. Finally, every point of that interval is attained. If L_*(P,M)=U_*(P,M), there is nothing to prove. Otherwise, for any v in [L_*(P,M),U_*(P,M)] define \lambda := (v-L_*(P,M))/(U_*(P,M)-L_*(P,M)) and \mu_v := (1-\lambda)\mu_L + \lambda \mu_U. By convexity \mu_v in H, and by linearity E(\mu_v)=v. Thus the attainable evaluation set generated by H_*(P,M) is exactly the closed interval [L_*(P,M),U_*(P,M)], and each point of that interval is sharp within the class.
\par\smallskip\noindent \textit{Justification.} This is the core attainment result for the pre-imposed extension class: once H_*(P,M) is fixed, the lower and upper endpoint definitions are not merely bookkeeping, because the full interval is attained by admissible representatives. \textit{Gap.} ArmstrongKolesar2018OptimalInference studies linear functionals such as a regression value at a point over convex smoothness classes, and KolesarRothe2018DiscreteRD applies honest endpoint logic to fixed-cutoff discrete running variables. When tau_*(P) is externally fixed, this is the same fixed-target compactness-and-attainment argument. The only extra bookkeeping here is that the evaluation point tau_*(P) comes from the population cost-quality selection rule, not that the observed law alone identifies a stronger target outside H_*(P,M). \textit{Consumer.} An offline cascade audit can report the full sensitivity interval generated by the chosen local extension class at the deployed cutoff rather than only the formal infimum and supremum definitions.

\begin{proposition}[prop:critical-curvature]\label{prop:critical-curvature}
Under the narrowed assumptions of thm:selected-sharp-set for every curvature budget and, in particular, assuming H_*(P,0) is nonempty, M -> L_*(P,M) is weakly decreasing and M -> U_*(P,M) is weakly increasing, so M_*(P) is well defined. If 0 notin [L_*(P,0), U_*(P,0)], then M_*(P) is the sign-certification frontier: for every M < M_*(P), the interval [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)] has the same strict sign as [L_*(P,0), U_*(P,0)], while 0 in [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)] for every M > M_*(P). If in addition L_*(P,M) < U_*(P,M) for some M, then there exist mu_L, mu_U in H_*(P,M) such that mu_L(s) = mu_U(s) = m_P(s) for every s in A_P intersect [tau_*(P)-h_0, tau_*(P)+h_0], mu_L(tau_*(P)) = L_*(P,M), and mu_U(tau_*(P)) = U_*(P,M); hence the same observed law P, the same frontier objects K_P and Q_P, and the same selected cutoff tau_*(P) are compatible with distinct selected local boundary values.
\end{proposition}
Write \tau_*:=\tau_*(P), H(M):=H_*(P,M), L(M):=L_*(P,M), and U(M):=U_*(P,M). The proposition assumes that H(M) is nonempty for every curvature budget under discussion and, in particular, that H(0) is nonempty, so the zero-budget interval [L(0),U(0)] is defined. By lem:endpoint-shape-in-budget, on every compact budget interval on which H(M) is nonempty one has: L is weakly decreasing and continuous, and U is weakly increasing and continuous. Hence the set S := {M >= 0 : 0 in [L(M),U(M)]} is an upper set. Indeed, if M in S and M' >= M, then L(M') <= L(M) <= 0 <= U(M) <= U(M'), so 0 in [L(M'),U(M')]. Therefore M_*(P)=inf S is well defined in [0,\infty]. Suppose now that 0 notin [L(0),U(0)]. Because [L(0),U(0)] is an interval, either L(0)>0 or U(0)<0. If L(0)>0 and M < M_*(P), then M notin S, so 0 notin [L(M),U(M)]; but U(M) >= U(0) >= L(0) > 0, and an interval with positive upper endpoint that does not contain 0 must have positive lower endpoint, hence L(M)>0. Thus [L(M),U(M)] stays strictly positive for every M < M_*(P). If instead U(0)<0 and M < M_*(P), then again M notin S; moreover L(M) <= L(0) <= U(0) < 0, and an interval with negative lower endpoint that does not contain 0 must have negative upper endpoint, hence U(M)<0. Thus [L(M),U(M)] stays strictly negative for every M < M_*(P). This proves the sign-preservation claim below the frontier. For M > M_*(P), if S is empty then M_*(P)=\infty and the statement is vacuous. Otherwise choose M' in S with M_*(P) <= M' < M; the upper-set property of S then gives M in S, that is 0 in [L(M),U(M)]. This proves the sign-certification frontier claim. Next assume L_*(P,M) < U_*(P,M) for some fixed M. By thm:selected-sharp-set there exist endpoint-attaining \mu_L,\mu_U in H_*(P,M) such that \mu_L(\tau_*)=L_*(P,M) and \mu_U(\tau_*)=U_*(P,M). Because both functions belong to the same extension class, they satisfy the same interpolation equalities \mu_L(s)=m_P(s)=\mu_U(s) for every s in A_P \cap [\tau_*-h_0,\tau_*+h_0]. The frontier objects K_P and Q_P are functions of the same observed law P alone by def:population-frontiers, and \tau_*(P) is determined from those same frontier objects by def:selected-threshold-set and def:selected-threshold. Hence the same observed law P, the same frontier objects K_P and Q_P, and the same selected cutoff \tau_*(P) are compatible with two distinct selected local boundary values whenever L_*(P,M) < U_*(P,M).
\par\smallskip\noindent \textit{Justification.} This is a decision-oriented reparameterization of the nested endpoint family: M_*(P) summarizes when the sign of the selected-boundary interval ceases to be certified, and the non-singletonity clause records the same-law ambiguity that remains even after the frontier objects are fixed. \textit{Gap.} Kotte2026UCCI selects a calibrated threshold by constrained cost minimization, and Bouchard2026EscalationWorthIt characterizes deterministic threshold-cascade frontiers through piecewise concavity and pairwise-envelope geometry. The present proposition is mainly bookkeeping on top of the nested M-indexed interval: it shows how to summarize that family by a critical-curvature frontier and reminds the frontier literature that fixing K_P, Q_P, and tau_*(P) still need not fix the selected local boundary value once H_*(P,M) allows multiple admissible representatives. ZhangBenMichaelImai2022SafePolicyRD remains an informed-by policy-learning comparator rather than a reduction target because it studies robust cutoff choice under multiple-cutoff extrapolation, not selected-cutoff local identification at a fixed frontier point. \textit{Consumer.} A deployment team can summarize sensitivity with one curvature threshold and see directly that cost-optimal frontier choice does not by itself settle the local sign of escalation worthiness when the endpoint interval is non-singleton.

\begin{conjecture}[conj:active-set-geometry]\label{conj:active-set-geometry}
Suppose the window [tau_*(P)-h_0, tau_*(P)+h_0] contains finitely many score atoms, H_*(P,M) is nonempty for every M in [0,M_*(P)], 0 notin [L_*(P,0), U_*(P,0)], and M_*(P) in (0,infinity). Then M -> L_*(P,M) is continuous, convex, and weakly decreasing on [0,M_*(P)], M -> U_*(P,M) is continuous, concave, and weakly increasing on [0,M_*(P)], the interval [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)] has the same strict sign as [L_*(P,0), U_*(P,0)] for every M < M_*(P), and at least one endpoint touches zero at the frontier: L_*(P,M_*(P)) = 0 or U_*(P,M_*(P)) = 0.
\end{conjecture}
Write \(L(M):=L_*(P,M)\), \(U(M):=U_*(P,M)\), and \(\bar M:=M_*(P)\). By assumption, \(H_*(P,M)\) is nonempty for every \(M\in[0,\bar M]\). Applying `lem:endpoint-shape-in-budget` on the compact budget interval \([0,\bar M]\) yields that \(M\mapsto L(M)\) is continuous, convex, and weakly decreasing on \([0,\bar M]\), while \(M\mapsto U(M)\) is continuous, concave, and weakly increasing on \([0,\bar M]\). This proves the regularity claim.

Next apply `prop:critical-curvature`. Because \(0\notin[L(0),U(0)]\) and \(\bar M=M_*(P)\in(0,\infty)\), that proposition implies that for every \(M<\bar M\), the interval \([L(M),U(M)]\) has the same strict sign as \([L(0),U(0)]\). This proves the pre-frontier sign-preservation claim.

It remains to show endpoint contact at \(\bar M\). If \([L(0),U(0)]\) is strictly positive, then by the previous paragraph \(L(M)>0\) for every \(M<\bar M\). Continuity of \(L\) on \([0,\bar M]\) rules out \(L(\bar M)<0\), because that would force \(L\) to cross zero at some \(M<\bar M\). It also rules out \(L(\bar M)>0\), because then positivity would persist on some left neighborhood of \(\bar M\), contradicting the definition \(\bar M=\inf\{M\ge 0:0\in[L(M),U(M)]\}\). Hence \(L(\bar M)=0\). If instead \([L(0),U(0)]\) is strictly negative, the symmetric argument using continuity of \(U\) shows that \(U(\bar M)=0\). Therefore at least one endpoint touches zero at the frontier: \(L_*(P,M_*(P))=0\) or \(U_*(P,M_*(P))=0\).
\par\smallskip\noindent \textit{Justification.} The honest geometric content at the sign-certification frontier is the continuity/convexity bookkeeping forced by the nested endpoint family together with endpoint contact at the first admissible zero budget. \textit{Gap.} ArmstrongKolesar2020SimpleHonest gives smoothness-indexed honest intervals, but not selected-cutoff bookkeeping at a curvature frontier. The stronger finite-type active-set picture suggested by optimal-recovery heuristics is not discharged here and remains open. \textit{Consumer.} Even without a full spline-combinatorics theorem, an audit can still report the guaranteed frontier geometry: monotone widening, persistence of the initial sign below M_*(P), and endpoint contact exactly at the certification boundary.

\begin{lemma}[lem:arzela-ascoli-c1-compactness]\label{lem:arzela-ascoli-c1-compactness}
Let I be a compact interval. If a family F \subset C^1(I) is uniformly bounded in sup norm, its derivative family F' is uniformly bounded and equicontinuous, and F is closed under joint uniform convergence of functions and derivatives, then F is compact in the C^1(I) topology.
\end{lemma}
Source: the Arzela--Ascoli theorem, for example Rudin, Principles of Mathematical Analysis, Theorem 7.25, states that a uniformly bounded equicontinuous family in C(I) on a compact interval I is relatively compact in the sup norm. Applying that theorem separately to a family {f_n} and to its derivative family {f_n'} shows: if sup_n ||f_n||_infty < infinity, sup_n ||f_n'||_infty < infinity, and {f_n'} is equicontinuous, then every sequence has a subsequence converging uniformly together with its derivative. If the class is closed under this joint uniform convergence, it is compact in the C^1(I) topology. This is exactly the classical compactness fact used below.

\begin{lemma}[lem:endpoint-shape-in-budget]\label{lem:endpoint-shape-in-budget}
Fix P and \bar M >= 0. Suppose H_*(P,M) is nonempty for every M in [0,\bar M]. Then M \mapsto L_*(P,M) is finite, continuous, convex, and weakly decreasing on [0,\bar M], while M \mapsto U_*(P,M) is finite, continuous, concave, and weakly increasing on [0,\bar M].
\end{lemma}
Fix P and a compact budget interval J=[0,\bar M] such that H_*(P,M) is nonempty for every M in J. Write H(M):=H_*(P,M), L(M):=L_*(P,M), and U(M):=U_*(P,M). Because every \mu in H(M) satisfies \mu(c) in [-2,1] pointwise by def:local-extension-class, one has -2 <= L(M) <= U(M) <= 1 for every M, so both endpoint maps are finite. If 0 <= M_1 <= M_2, then H(M_1) \subseteq H(M_2): the interpolation equalities and range restriction are identical, and ess sup |\mu''| <= M_1 implies ess sup |\mu''| <= M_2. Therefore L(M_2) <= L(M_1) and U(M_2) >= U(M_1), so L is weakly decreasing and U is weakly increasing. Now fix M_1,M_2 in J and \lambda in [0,1], and put M_\lambda := (1-\lambda)M_1 + \lambda M_2. If \mu_i in H(M_i) for i=1,2, then \mu_\lambda := (1-\lambda)\mu_1 + \lambda \mu_2 satisfies the same interpolation equalities, still takes values in [-2,1] because that interval is convex, and obeys ess sup |\mu_\lambda''| <= (1-\lambda)M_1 + \lambda M_2 = M_\lambda. Hence \mu_\lambda in H(M_\lambda). Taking \varepsilon-optimal \mu_1,\mu_2 for the upper endpoint and evaluating at \tau_*(P) gives U(M_\lambda) >= (1-\lambda)(U(M_1)-\varepsilon) + \lambda(U(M_2)-\varepsilon); letting \varepsilon downarrow 0 yields U(M_\lambda) >= (1-\lambda)U(M_1)+\lambda U(M_2), so U is concave. The same argument with \varepsilon-optimal lower-endpoint candidates gives L(M_\lambda) <= (1-\lambda)L(M_1)+\lambda L(M_2), so L is convex. Finite convex and concave functions are continuous on the interior of a compact interval, and the proved monotonicity gives one-sided continuity at the endpoints of J. Therefore L is continuous, convex, and weakly decreasing on J, while U is continuous, concave, and weakly increasing on J.

\begin{lemma}[lem:generated-score-expansion]\label{lem:generated-score-expansion}
Consider the cross-fitted orthogonalized local-linear selected-score estimator obtained by replacing the population score \(C\) with the generated score \(\hat C\). Under second-stage smoothness, stochastic equicontinuity of the localized score process, and first-stage rate conditions \(r_n=o(h_n)\) and \(\sqrt{n h_n}\,r_n^2\to 0\), the feasible estimator admits an oracle-plus-linear-perturbation expansion, and after orthogonalization the remaining generated-score effect is second order at the local-linear scale.
\end{lemma}
Source. Mammen, Rothe, and Schienle (2012, Annals of Statistics, Theorem 1) prove that a second-stage nonparametric estimator with a generated regressor admits an oracle-plus-linear-perturbation expansion, uniformly over the evaluation region, under smoothness of the second-stage map and stochastic equicontinuity of the score process. Hahn and Ridder (2013, Econometrica, Theorem 5) give the corresponding influence-function correction for generated regressors. In the present cross-fitted and Neyman-orthogonal construction, Chernozhukov et al. (2018) imply that the own-observation first-stage term is removed, so under the stated rate conditions \(r_n=o(h_n)\) and \(\sqrt{n h_n}\,r_n^2\to 0\) the remaining generated-score contribution is second order at the local-linear scale. This published package is exactly the classical fact used below.

\begin{lemma}[lem:oracle-snooping-coverage]\label{lem:oracle-snooping-coverage}
Assume `ass:iid-audit`, `ass:continuous-local-score-density`, `ass:continuous-local-regression`, `ass:local-variance-continuity`, `ass:curvature-budget`, `ass:bandwidth-shrinking`, the kernel regularity built into `def:selected-ll-estimator`, and `ass:local-effective-sample`. Let \(\tilde V_n(\tau)\) be the oracle local-linear intercept obtained from `def:selected-ll-estimator` by replacing \(\hat C_i\) with \(C_i\) and evaluating at deterministic cutoff \(\tau\), and let \(\rho_n\downarrow 0\). Then there exists a finite critical value \(cv_{1-\alpha}^{sel}\) such that
\[
\liminf_{n\to\infty}\inf_P P\Bigl(\sup_{|\tau-\tau_*(P)|\le \rho_n h_n}|\tilde V_n(\tau)-g_P(\tau)|\le cv_{1-\alpha}^{sel}\hat\sigma_n+\hat B_n(M)\Bigr)\ge 1-\alpha,
\]
uniformly over \(P\in P_{\mathrm{cont}}(M)\). Moreover \(cv_{1-\alpha}^{sel}=O(1)\), \(\hat\sigma_n=O_P((n h_n)^{-1/2})\), and \(\hat B_n(M)=O(M h_n^2)\).
\end{lemma}
Source. Armstrong and Kolesar (2018, Review of Economic Studies, Theorem 3.1 and Corollary 3.1) establish simultaneous honest coverage for a continuum-indexed studentized local-polynomial process via a strong Gaussian approximation and a supremum critical value, under i.i.d. sampling, standard local-polynomial kernel regularity, local density bounded away from zero on the index neighborhood, continuous conditional variance, and shrinking bandwidth with diverging local effective sample size. In the present oracle problem, the deterministic-cutoff process \(\tau \mapsto \tilde V_n(\tau)\) is the same local-linear intercept functional after re-indexing the running variable by \(c-\tau\). `ass:iid-audit` supplies the sampling premise. Because \(\rho_n \downarrow 0\), \(h_n \to 0\), and `ass:continuous-local-score-density` gives continuity of \(f_{C,P}\) with \(f_{C,P}(\tau_*(P))>0\), there is eventually a neighborhood \(|\tau-\tau_*(P)|\le \rho_n h_n\) on which the score density is bounded away from zero uniformly in the sense needed for the oracle RD approximation. The kernel regularity is built into `def:selected-ll-estimator`, while `ass:continuous-local-regression` and `ass:curvature-budget` are the local smoothness inputs that justify the standard local-linear bias bound \(\hat B_n(M)=O(M h_n^2)\). `ass:local-variance-continuity`, `ass:bandwidth-shrinking`, and `ass:local-effective-sample` supply the remaining classical premises. Therefore the Armstrong-Kolesar strong approximation and supremum critical-value construction apply to the oracle selected-neighborhood process. The standard local-linear orders \(\hat\sigma_n=O_P((n h_n)^{-1/2})\) and \(\hat B_n(M)=O(M h_n^2)\) are the classical RD orders of Calonico, Cattaneo, and Titiunik (2014) and Calonico, Cattaneo, and Farrell (2018).

\begin{lemma}[lem:selected-local-slope-envelope]\label{lem:selected-local-slope-envelope}
Assume `ass:bounded-quality`, `ass:continuous-local-regression`, and `ass:curvature-budget`. Then on the window \([\tau_*(P)-h_0,\tau_*(P)+h_0]\),
\[
|g_P(c)-g_P(c')|\le B_{\mathrm{slope}}(M,h_0)|c-c'|\qquad\text{for all }c,c',
\]
where \(B_{\mathrm{slope}}(M,h_0):=3/(2h_0)+2Mh_0\). In particular,
\[
|g_P(c)-V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)|\le B_{\mathrm{slope}}(M,h_0)|c-\tau_*(P)|.
\]
\end{lemma}
Let \(I=[a,b]=[\tau_*(P)-h_0,\tau_*(P)+h_0]\). By `ass:bounded-quality` and \(\Delta=Y_l-Y_s-\kappa\) with \(\kappa\in[0,1]\), one has \(-2\le \Delta\le 1\) almost surely. Therefore \(g_P(c)=E_P[\Delta\mid C=c]\in[-2,1]\) for every \(c\in I\). By `ass:continuous-local-regression`, \(g_P=\mu_P\) on \(I\); by `ass:curvature-budget`, \(\sup_{c\in I}|\mu_P''(c)|\le M\), so \(\mu_P'\) is \(M\)-Lipschitz on \(I\). Since \(|\mu_P(b)-\mu_P(a)|\le 3\), the mean-value theorem yields some \(\xi\in(a,b)\) such that \(|\mu_P'(\xi)|=|\mu_P(b)-\mu_P(a)|/(b-a)\le 3/(2h_0)\). For any \(c\in I\), Lipschitz continuity gives \(|\mu_P'(c)|\le |\mu_P'(\xi)|+M|c-\xi|\le 3/(2h_0)+2Mh_0=:B_{\mathrm{slope}}(M,h_0)\). Another mean-value-theorem step then gives \(|g_P(c)-g_P(c')|=|\mu_P(c)-\mu_P(c')|\le B_{\mathrm{slope}}(M,h_0)|c-c'|\) for all \(c,c'\in I\). Taking \(c'=\tau_*(P)\) yields \(|g_P(c)-V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)|\le B_{\mathrm{slope}}(M,h_0)|c-\tau_*(P)|\).

\begin{lemma}[lem:perfect-spline-extremal]\label{lem:perfect-spline-extremal}
Let \(I\) be a compact interval, let \(S=\{s_1,\dots,s_N\}\subset I\) be finite, let \(c_0\in I\), let \(M\ge 0\), and let \(y_1,\dots,y_N\in \mathbb R\). Define \(\mathcal F_M:=\{\mu\in W^{2,\infty}(I): \mu(s_j)=y_j\ \forall j,\ \operatorname*{ess\,sup}_{I}|\mu''|\le M\}\). If \(\mathcal F_M\) is nonempty, any maximizer or minimizer of the point-evaluation functional \(\mu\mapsto \mu(c_0)\) over \(\mathcal F_M\) may be chosen to be a quadratic perfect spline: \(\mu''=\pm M\) almost everywhere between finitely many knots, the sign of \(\mu''\) changes at at most \(N-1\) interior knots, and the spline is \(C^1\) across knots.
\end{lemma}
This is the classical perfect-spline extremal theorem from optimal recovery. Micchelli and Rivlin, "Optimal recovery of smooth functions" (Numerische Mathematik 26, 1976/77; originally circulated 1974), together with Micchelli and Rivlin, "A Survey of Optimal Recovery" in *Optimal Estimation in Approximation Theory* (1977), proves that extrema of a linear functional over a bounded-derivative ball \(W^{r,\infty}\) subject to finitely many linear information constraints are attained by perfect splines of order \(r\), with saturated derivative \(\pm M\) between finitely many knots and with the number of sign changes controlled by the number of interpolation constraints. Specializing those results to \(r=2\), the evaluation functional \(\mu\mapsto\mu(c_0)\), and the interpolation constraints \(\mu(s_j)=y_j\), yields exactly the fact used here: an extremizer can be chosen as a quadratic perfect spline with \(\mu''=\pm M\) almost everywhere, at most \(N-1\) interior sign-change knots, and \(C^1\) matching across knots. No claim about an additional global box constraint is used in this lemma.

\begin{lemma}[lem:finite-perfect-spline-candidate-types]\label{lem:finite-perfect-spline-candidate-types}
Let \(I\) be a compact interval, let \(S=\{s_1<\cdots<s_N\}\subset I\) be finite, let \(c_0\in I\), let \(y_1,\dots,y_N\in \mathbb R\), and fix \(\bar M\ge 0\). For each \(M\in[0,\bar M]\), define the unconstrained interpolation-curvature class \(\mathcal F_M:=\{\mu\in W^{2,\infty}(I): \mu(s_j)=y_j\ \forall j,\ \operatorname*{ess\,sup}_{I}|\mu''|\le M\}\). If \(\mathcal F_M\) is nonempty for every \(M\in[0,\bar M]\), then there exists a finite family of candidate perfect-spline combinatorial types on \([0,\bar M]\) such that for every \(M\in[0,\bar M]\), some maximizer and some minimizer of \(\mu\mapsto\mu(c_0)\) over \(\mathcal F_M\) belong to that family.
\end{lemma}
Fix \(M\in[0,\bar M]\). By `lem:perfect-spline-extremal`, some maximizer and some minimizer of \(\mu\mapsto\mu(c_0)\) over \(\mathcal F_M\) may be chosen to be quadratic perfect splines. For any such extremizer, record the discrete data consisting of: (i) whether it is a maximizer or a minimizer; (ii) the initial sign of \(\mu''\) on the first spline segment; (iii) the number \(r\in\{0,\dots,N-1\}\) of interior sign-change knots; and (iv) the relative order, along \(I\), of the fixed interpolation sites \(s_1,\dots,s_N\) and the \(r\) sign-change knots. Because there are only two endpoint directions, two initial curvature signs, at most \(N-1\) sign changes, and finitely many ways to interleave at most \(N-1\) free knots with the fixed ordered sites, only finitely many such records are possible. Let \(\mathcal T\) be the set of all records arising from some \(M\in[0,\bar M]\). Then \(\mathcal T\) is finite, and by construction for every \(M\in[0,\bar M]\), some maximizer and some minimizer belong to one of the types in \(\mathcal T\). This proves the finite-type claim for the unconstrained interpolation-curvature problem.

\begin{lemma}[lem:ghat-envelope-valid]\label{lem:ghat-envelope-valid}
Work under the corrected `def:generated-score-drift-handle`, so \(\hat G_n=\hat g_{\mathrm{gen},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{shift},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{rem},n}\). Fix \(M\). Let \(\tilde V_n(\tau)\) denote the oracle local-linear intercept obtained from `def:selected-ll-estimator` by replacing the generated score \(\hat C_i\) with the population score \(C_i\) and evaluating at deterministic cutoff \(\tau\). If \(P\in P_{\mathrm{cont}}(M)\) satisfies `ass:quadratic-selection-growth`, gate (i) with a sequence \(\rho_n\downarrow 0\), and gate (ii) with \(r_n=o(h_n)\) and \(\sqrt{n h_n}\,r_n^2\to 0\), then
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty}\sup_P P\Bigl(|[\hat V_{ll}(M)-V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)]-[\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)-g_P(\hat\tau)]|>\hat G_n\Bigr)=0.
\]
Thus the corrected \(\hat G_n\) dominates the full feasible-to-oracle selected-score gap uniformly with probability tending to one.
\end{lemma}
Work under the corrected `def:generated-score-drift-handle`, so \(\hat G_n=\hat g_{\mathrm{gen},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{shift},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{rem},n}\). Fix \(P\in P_{\mathrm{cont}}(M)\) satisfying `ass:quadratic-selection-growth`, write \(\tau_*:=\tau_*(P)\), \(V_*:=V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)=g_P(\tau_*)\), and let \(\tilde V_n(\tau)\) be the oracle intercept computed with the population score \(C\) at deterministic cutoff \(\tau\). Define \(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}:=\{|\hat\tau-\tau_*|\le \rho_n h_n\}\). By the localization gate, \(\sup_P P(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}^c)\to 0\). Because \(\rho_n\downarrow 0\) and `ass:bandwidth-shrinking` gives \(h_n\to 0\), we have \(\rho_n h_n<h_0\) eventually, so on \(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}\) the selected cutoff lies inside the fixed window of `lem:selected-local-slope-envelope`.

Decompose the feasible-to-oracle gap into primitive terms:
\[
[\hat V_{ll}(M)-V_*]-[\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)-g_P(\hat\tau)]
=
[\hat V_{ll}(M)-\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)] + [g_P(\hat\tau)-g_P(\tau_*)].
\]
The first bracket is the generated-score perturbation. By gate (ii) and the classical source `lem:generated-score-expansion`,
\[
\hat V_{ll}(M)-\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)=A_n+R_n,
\]
where \(A_n\) is the orthogonal first-order generated-score correction and \(R_n\) is second order under \(r_n=o(h_n)\) and \(\sqrt{n h_n}\,r_n^2\to 0\). By the corrected definition, \(\hat g_{\mathrm{gen},n}\) is a data-only upper envelope for \(|A_n|\) and \(\hat g_{\mathrm{rem},n}\) is a data-only upper envelope for \(|R_n|\). Hence
\[
|\hat V_{ll}(M)-\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)|\le \hat g_{\mathrm{gen},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{rem},n}
\]
with probability tending to one uniformly over \(P\).

The second bracket is the selected-target shift. On \(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}\), `lem:selected-local-slope-envelope` gives
\[
|g_P(\hat\tau)-g_P(\tau_*)|\le B_{\mathrm{slope}}(M,h_0)|\hat\tau-\tau_*|\le B_{\mathrm{slope}}(M,h_0)\rho_n h_n.
\]
By the corrected definition, \(\hat g_{\mathrm{shift},n}\) is constructed to upper-bound this localization term, so on \(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}\),
\[
|g_P(\hat\tau)-g_P(\tau_*)|\le \hat g_{\mathrm{shift},n}.
\]
Combining the last three displays, on the event that \(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}\) holds and the first-stage envelopes succeed,
\[
|[\hat V_{ll}(M)-V_*]-[\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)-g_P(\hat\tau)]|
\le \hat g_{\mathrm{gen},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{shift},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{rem},n}
=\hat G_n.
\]
Therefore
\[
\sup_P P\Bigl(|[\hat V_{ll}(M)-V_*]-[\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)-g_P(\hat\tau)]|>\hat G_n\Bigr)
\le \sup_P P(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}^c)+o(1)\to 0.
\]
This proves that the corrected \(\hat G_n\) dominates the full feasible-to-oracle gap, and the domination is derived term by term rather than assumed wholesale.

\begin{lemma}[lem:postselect-honest-ci-under-gates]\label{lem:postselect-honest-ci-under-gates}
Fix \(M\). Let \(\tilde V_n(\tau)\) denote the oracle local-linear intercept obtained from `def:selected-ll-estimator` by replacing the generated score \(\hat C_i\) with the population score \(C_i\) and evaluating at deterministic cutoff \(\tau\). If \(P\in P_{\mathrm{cont}}(M)\) satisfies `ass:quadratic-selection-growth`, gate (i) with \(\rho_n\downarrow 0\), gate (ii) with \(r_n=o(h_n)\) and \(\sqrt{n h_n}\,r_n^2\to 0\), and the oracle simultaneous-coverage gate from `lem:oracle-snooping-coverage`, then
\[
\liminf_{n\to\infty}\inf_P P\bigl(V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)\in C_n(M)\bigr)\ge 1-\alpha.
\]
Moreover the half-width of \(C_n(M)\) is
\[
O_P\bigl((n h_n)^{-1/2}+M h_n^2+\rho_n h_n\bigr),
\]
up to the second-order generated-score remainder already absorbed by \(\hat g_{\mathrm{rem},n}\); if \(\rho_n h_n\) dominates, this is the post-selection honesty cost.
\end{lemma}
Fix \(P\in P_{\mathrm{cont}}(M)\) satisfying `ass:quadratic-selection-growth`, and write \(\tau_*:=\tau_*(P)\), \(V_*:=V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)=g_P(\tau_*)\). Define the events
\[
E_{\mathrm{or},n}:=\Bigl\{\sup_{|\tau-\tau_*|\le \rho_n h_n}|\tilde V_n(\tau)-g_P(\tau)|\le cv_{1-\alpha}^{sel}\hat\sigma_n+\hat B_n(M)\Bigr\},
\]
\[
E_{\mathrm{loc},n}:=\{|\hat\tau-\tau_*|\le \rho_n h_n\},\qquad
E_{\mathrm{gap},n}:=\Bigl\{|[\hat V_{ll}(M)-V_*]-[\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)-g_P(\hat\tau)]|\le \hat G_n\Bigr\}.
\]
By `lem:oracle-snooping-coverage`, \(\liminf_{n\to\infty}\inf_P P(E_{\mathrm{or},n})\ge 1-\alpha\). By gate (i), \(\sup_P P(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}^c)\to 0\). By `lem:ghat-envelope-valid`, \(\sup_P P(E_{\mathrm{gap},n}^c)\to 0\).

Consider \(E_n:=E_{\mathrm{or},n}\cap E_{\mathrm{loc},n}\cap E_{\mathrm{gap},n}\). On \(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}\), the random cutoff \(\hat\tau\) belongs to the oracle neighborhood used in \(E_{\mathrm{or},n}\), hence on \(E_n\),
\[
|\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)-g_P(\hat\tau)|\le cv_{1-\alpha}^{sel}\hat\sigma_n+\hat B_n(M).
\]
On \(E_{\mathrm{gap},n}\),
\[
|[\hat V_{ll}(M)-V_*]-[\tilde V_n(\hat\tau)-g_P(\hat\tau)]|\le \hat G_n.
\]
Adding the two inequalities gives
\[
|\hat V_{ll}(M)-V_*|\le cv_{1-\alpha}^{sel}\hat\sigma_n+\hat B_n(M)+\hat G_n.
\]
By `def:selected-honest-ci`, the last display is exactly the event \(V_*=V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)\in C_n(M)\). Therefore \(E_n\subseteq\{V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)\in C_n(M)\}\), so
\[
P(V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)\in C_n(M))\ge P(E_{\mathrm{or},n})-P(E_{\mathrm{loc},n}^c)-P(E_{\mathrm{gap},n}^c).
\]
Taking infima over \(P\) and then limits yields
\[
\liminf_{n\to\infty}\inf_P P(V_{\mathrm{cont}}(P)\in C_n(M))\ge 1-\alpha.
\]
This proves the uniform asymptotic coverage claim.

For the width, `lem:oracle-snooping-coverage` gives \(cv_{1-\alpha}^{sel}=O(1)\), \(\hat\sigma_n=O_P((n h_n)^{-1/2})\), and \(\hat B_n(M)=O(M h_n^2)\). By `def:generated-score-drift-handle`,
\[
\hat G_n=\hat g_{\mathrm{gen},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{shift},n}+\hat g_{\mathrm{rem},n}.
\]
Gate (ii) together with `lem:generated-score-expansion` implies that the generated-score perturbation is oracle-plus-first-order with second-order remainder, so the envelopes \(\hat g_{\mathrm{gen},n}\) and \(\hat g_{\mathrm{rem},n}\) are second order at the local-linear scale used in `lem:ghat-envelope-valid`. On the localization event from gate (i), `lem:selected-local-slope-envelope` gives \(|g_P(\hat\tau)-g_P(\tau_*)|=O_P(\rho_n h_n)\), so the shift envelope satisfies \(\hat g_{\mathrm{shift},n}=O_P(\rho_n h_n)\). Hence
\[
\hat G_n=O_P(\rho_n h_n)+o_P((n h_n)^{-1/2}),
\]
and therefore the half-width of \(C_n(M)\) is
\[
O_P\bigl((n h_n)^{-1/2}+M h_n^2+\rho_n h_n\bigr).
\]
When \(\rho_n h_n\) dominates the fixed-cutoff variance and curvature-bias terms, this is the explicit post-selection honesty cost.

\section{Honest scope}

The main kernel is the class-indexed interval [L_*(P,M), U_*(P,M)] together with the critical-curvature summary M_*(P). When tau_*(P) is externally fixed, the atomic theorem reduces to the fixed-cutoff honest endpoint problem studied by Armstrong-Kolesar and Kolesar-Rothe; the genuinely new bookkeeping is that the evaluation point itself is selected by the population cost-quality frontier. The same-law ambiguity result is a consequence of allowing multiple admissible representatives in H_*(P,M), not a stronger observed-law identification theorem. Beyond that, the endpoint geometry remains deliberately modest: the core proves continuity, monotonicity, convexity or concavity, and endpoint contact at the sign-certification frontier, while stronger finite-type spline combinatorics remain open. The post-selection honest-CI rung is a separate dense-regime handle: it asks for a generated-score drift correction and simultaneous selected-cutoff critical value for V_cont(P), and it does not claim honest post-selection inference for the genuinely atomic isotonic-score regime.
