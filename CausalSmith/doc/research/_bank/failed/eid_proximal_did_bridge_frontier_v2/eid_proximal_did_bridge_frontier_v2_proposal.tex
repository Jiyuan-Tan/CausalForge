\begin{abstract}
This proposal studies a two-group, three-period difference-in-differences design with a binary negative-control outcome and a binary negative-control exposure. The focal object is the finite regime signature \(\Sigma(P)\), which records the ordinary parallel-trends residual, the proxy-bridge residual, the bridge rank, and the two ATT formulas. The main conjecture is that \(\Sigma(P)\) sharply separates ordinary DiD identification, proximal negative-control DiD identification, and nonidentification on a common finite support. A rational witness computes the signature from raw observed cells and realizes a proximal-only law where ordinary parallel trends fails. The result would replace the usual checklist of placebo and bridge diagnostics by a finite non-equivalence theorem for negative-control DiD.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
A proximal DiD design can use preperiod proxies to identify the untreated treated trend even when ordinary parallel trends fails. The main claim is that the finite regime signature \(\Sigma(P)\) gives a sharp three-way separation: ordinary DiD identifies ATT, proximal negative-control DiD identifies ATT, or neither restriction identifies ATT.

The closest comparators are \citet[Sec. 3]{MiaoGengTchetgen2018}, where proxy completeness gives point identification, \citet[Sec. 2]{MiaoShiLiTchetgen2024}, where double negative-control bridge functions identify effects, and \citet[Sec. 3]{ZhangZhangWangEtAl2025}, where negative-control outcomes calibrate DiD bias. The gap is a finite observed-cell object that proves ordinary parallel trends and double-proxy bridge identification are neither nested nor equivalent. The theorem lives in exact identification for negative-control panel designs.

The downstream consumer is negative-control DiD in electronic-health-record studies such as \citet{ZhangZhangWangEtAl2025}, where negative-control outcomes can diagnose bias but cannot by themselves tell whether a negative-control exposure restores identification. A reader would compute \(\Sigma(P)\) before reporting an ATT contrast.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
\item Theorem 1: \(\Sigma(P)\) is computable from raw observed cells and assigns every positive finite law to the PT-only, bridge-only, both, or neither regime. Gap: \citet{MiaoGengTchetgen2018} and \citet{MiaoShiLiTchetgen2024} supply bridge identification conditions but not a DiD regime signature. Consumer: \citet{ZhangZhangWangEtAl2025}.
\item Conjecture 1: The four regimes of \(\Sigma(P)\) are all nonempty open relative to a common finite latent-type panel model, so ordinary DiD and proximal bridge DiD are neither nested nor equivalent. Gap: \citet{SoferRichardsonColicinoSchwartzTchetgen2016}, \citet{RichardsonYeTchetgen2023}, and \citet{TchetgenParkRichardson2024} change the DiD identifying restriction, while the conjecture separates it from a double-proxy bridge restriction. Consumer: negative-control DiD design audits.
\item Theorem 2: A rational \(2\times3\) binary-proxy witness has \(\tau_{PT}(P)=1/4\), \(\rho_{br}(P)=0\), \(\kappa_{br}(P)=1/2\), \(\Delta_{DiD}(P)=1/5\), and \(\Delta_{br}(P)=-1/20\). Gap: \citet{RambachanRoth2023} uses pretrend information for sensitivity, not a proxy-rank witness that disagrees with ordinary pretrends. Consumer: finite negative-control bridge diagnostics.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related work}
\citet{MiaoGengTchetgen2018} gives the proxy-rank ancestor for proximal causal inference; its point-identification result requires rank and completeness conditions rather than a finite DiD regime signature. \citet{MiaoShiLiTchetgen2024} develops double negative-control bridge functions with a negative-control outcome and exposure; the present object keeps the double-proxy structure but compares the bridge restriction directly with ordinary parallel trends. \citet{CuiPuShiMiaoTchetgen2024} gives semiparametric proximal inference once a bridge is available; it is relevant because the signature isolates the finite condition under which that target differs from DiD. \citet{ZhangLiMiaoTchetgen2023} studies proximal inference without bridge uniqueness, and \citet{GhassamiShpitserTchetgen2023} studies proxy-based partial identification; both concern nonunique proxy information, while \(\Sigma(P)\) is a common-support non-nesting object for DiD and proximal DiD. \citet{SoferRichardsonColicinoSchwartzTchetgen2016} frames DiD as a negative-outcome-control design and emphasizes scale-sensitive bias equivalence; it supplies the DiD/negative-control bridge to the older epidemiologic literature. \citet{RichardsonYeTchetgen2023} and \citet{TchetgenParkRichardson2024} replace additive parallel trends with generalized or universal equi-confounding restrictions; they do not introduce an exposure-indexed bridge matrix. \citet{ZhangZhangWangEtAl2025} is the closest applied NC-DiD comparator because it uses negative-control outcomes to detect and calibrate bias from parallel-trends violations, but it is not an NCO-plus-NCE bridge-rank theorem. \citet{RambachanRoth2023} studies robust inference with pretrend information under parallel-trends relaxations; the regime signature instead comes from a finite negative-control moment operator. \citet{CallawaySantAnna2021} and \citet{SunAbraham2021} define the modern multiple-period DiD comparison class, but their identification restrictions are not proximal proxy restrictions. In-catalogue prior art is the failed \texttt{eid\_proximal\_did\_bridge\_frontier\_v1}, whose reviewer rejected moment-row witnesses without a full finite law; the present angle changes the focal object from bridge intervals to a finite non-nesting signature computed from raw observed cells.

\section{Formal setup}
For each group \(g\in\{0,1\}\), let \(D_{g,t}=\one\{g=1,t=0\}\). Let \(Y_t(d)\in\{0,1\}\) be the potential outcome, and let \(W_t\in\{0,1\}\) and \(Z_t\in\{0,1\}\) be a negative-control outcome and exposure. The observed law \(P\) is the joint law of \((G,Y_{-2},Y_{-1},Y_0,W_{-2},W_{-1},W_0,Z_{-2},Z_{-1},Z_0)\) with positive group probabilities. A finite latent completion adds \(U\in\{0,1\}\) and potential outcomes satisfying the exclusions in Assumption \ref{ass:nc-exclusion}. The target is
\[
\theta_{ATT}(P)=\E[Y_0(1)-Y_0(0)\mid G=1].
\]
Define \(\Delta Y=Y_{-1}-Y_{-2}\), \(m_g(w)=\E[\Delta Y\mid W=w,G=g]\), and
\[
\Delta_{DiD}(P)=\{\E[Y_0-Y_{-1}\mid G=1]\}-\{\E[Y_0-Y_{-1}\mid G=0]\}.
\]
For a latent completion, define the ordinary parallel-trends residual
\[
\tau_{PT}(P)=\{ \E[Y_0(0)-Y_{-1}(0)\mid G=1]\}-\{\E[Y_0(0)-Y_{-1}(0)\mid G=0]\}.
\]
For binary \(W=W_{-1}\) and \(Z=Z_{-1}\), define
\[
B_P(w,z)=\Pr(Z=z\mid W=w,G=1),\qquad r_P(w)=m_1(w)-m_0(w).
\]
Let \(c_P(z)=\Pr(Z_0=z\mid G=1)\). A finite bridge is a vector \(h=(h_0,h_1)\in[-1,1]^2\) satisfying \(B_P h=r_P\). Define
\[
\rho_{br}(P)=\inf_{h\in[-1,1]^2}\|B_P h-r_P\|_\infty,\qquad \kappa_{br}(P)=|\det(B_P)|,
\]
and, when \(\rho_{br}(P)=0\) and \(\kappa_{br}(P)>0\),
\[
\Delta_{br}(P)=\Delta_{DiD}(P)-c_P^\transpose B_P^{-1}r_P.
\]
The finite regime signature is
\[
\Sigma(P)=\left(\tau_{PT}(P),\rho_{br}(P),\kappa_{br}(P),\Delta_{DiD}(P),\Delta_{br}(P)\right),
\]
with \(\Delta_{br}(P)=\bot\) when the bridge equation is unsolved or nonunique.

\section{Assumptions}
\begin{assumption}[Finite positive support]\label{ass:finite-positive}
All cells used to condition on \((G,W,Z)\) have positive probability, and \(Y_t,W_t,Z_t\) are binary for \(t=-2,-1,0\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Panel consistency and no anticipation]\label{ass:consistency}
The observed \(Y_t\) equals \(Y_t(D_t)\), and \(Y_{-2}=Y_{-2}(0)\), \(Y_{-1}=Y_{-1}(0)\) for both groups.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Negative-control exclusions]\label{ass:nc-exclusion}
The negative-control outcome is not affected by the post treatment, and the negative-control exposure has no direct effect on \(Y_0(d)\) after conditioning on the latent confounding type that generates the bridge moments.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Bounded finite bridge class]\label{ass:bounded-bridge}
Admissible bridge functions are exactly vectors \(h\in[-1,1]^2\) on the binary support of \(Z\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Bridge point-identification regime]\label{ass:bridge-point}
The equation \(B_P h=r_P\) has a solution in \([-1,1]^2\), and \(\kappa_{br}(P)>0\).
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Ordinary parallel trends]\label{ass:parallel-trends}
The untreated post trend is the same in the treated and comparison groups, so \(\tau_{PT}(P)=0\).
\end{assumption}

\section{Main results}
\begin{theorem}[Finite regime signature]
Under Assumptions \ref{ass:finite-positive}, \ref{ass:consistency}, \ref{ass:nc-exclusion}, and \ref{ass:bounded-bridge}, the observable components \((\rho_{br}(P),\kappa_{br}(P),\Delta_{DiD}(P),\Delta_{br}(P))\) of \(\Sigma(P)\) are computable from the raw observed law. If Assumption \ref{ass:parallel-trends} holds, then \(\theta_{ATT}(P)=\Delta_{DiD}(P)\). If Assumption \ref{ass:bridge-point} holds and the finite bridge moment restriction is valid, then \(\theta_{ATT}(P)=\Delta_{br}(P)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{conjecture}[Finite non-nesting of DiD and proximal bridge DiD (NOT YET PROVED)]
There is a common positive-cell finite latent-type model on the locked support for which all four regimes below are nonempty and open relative to the model:
\[
\begin{array}{ll}
\text{both:} & \tau_{PT}(P)=0,\ \rho_{br}(P)=0,\ \kappa_{br}(P)>0,\\
\text{PT-only:} & \tau_{PT}(P)=0,\ \rho_{br}(P)>0,\\
\text{bridge-only:} & \tau_{PT}(P)\ne0,\ \rho_{br}(P)=0,\ \kappa_{br}(P)>0,\\
\text{neither:} & \tau_{PT}(P)\ne0,\ \rho_{br}(P)>0.
\end{array}
\]
Moreover, in the bridge-only regime \(\Delta_{br}(P)=\theta_{ATT}(P)\) while \(\Delta_{DiD}(P)-\theta_{ATT}(P)=\tau_{PT}(P)\ne0\). Thus ordinary DiD and proximal negative-control DiD are neither nested nor equivalent.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{theorem}[Bridge-only rational witness]
There exists a positive-cell observed law and finite latent completion on the locked \(2\times3\) binary-proxy support such that
\[
B_P=\begin{pmatrix}3/4&1/4\\1/4&3/4\end{pmatrix},\quad
r_P=\begin{pmatrix}1/4\\0\end{pmatrix},\quad
c_P=\begin{pmatrix}3/4\\1/4\end{pmatrix},\quad
\Delta_{DiD}(P)=1/5.
\]
For this law, \(\rho_{br}(P)=0\), \(\kappa_{br}(P)=1/2\), \(h=B_P^{-1}r_P=(3/8,-1/8)^\transpose\), \(\Delta_{br}(P)=-1/20\), and \(\tau_{PT}(P)=1/4\). Hence the law is bridge-only: proximal bridge DiD identifies ATT and ordinary parallel-trends DiD does not.
\end{theorem}

\section{Sanity-check corollaries}
\begin{corollary}[Point bridge corner]
Under Assumptions \ref{ass:finite-positive}, \ref{ass:bounded-bridge}, \ref{ass:consistency}, and \ref{ass:bridge-point}, \(\Delta_{br}(P)=\Delta_{DiD}(P)-c_P^\transpose B_P^{-1}r_P\). This is the usual point-bridge correction because the feasible bridge set is a singleton.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Parallel-trends corner]
Under Assumption \ref{ass:parallel-trends}, \(\Delta_{DiD}(P)=\theta_{ATT}(P)\). This collapses to ordinary DiD because the untreated post trend gap is zero.
\end{corollary}

\section{Proof-sketch route}
For Conjecture 1, use the chain: headline \(\leftarrow\) unfold \(\Sigma(P)\), ordinary parallel trends, and the bridge moment equation \(\leftarrow\) apply consistency, exclusions, finite positivity, and bounded bridges \(\leftarrow\) Step 1 construct a four-parameter rational latent-type family whose observed cells control \(\tau_{PT}\), \(r_P\), \(B_P\), and \(c_P\) independently to first order \(\leftarrow\) Step 2 prove the Jacobian from those latent parameters to \((\tau_{PT},\rho_{br},\kappa_{br},\Delta_{DiD}-\Delta_{br})\) has full row rank at the Exhibit 9.1 law \(\leftarrow\) Step 3 use the implicit-function theorem on the open positive-cell simplex to obtain local laws in all four sign/rank regimes \(\leftarrow\) Step 4 verify the bridge-only branch attains \(\theta_{ATT}=\Delta_{br}\) by substituting the unique bridge into the negative-control moment restriction \(\leftarrow\) classical result: local transversality of smooth maps on finite probability simplices. Theorem 1 is obtained by direct matrix inversion and DiD algebra. Theorem 2 is obtained by substituting the Exhibit 9.1 cells and computing the determinant, residual, bridge solution, and two ATT contrasts.

\section{Honest scope flags}
Theorem 1 and Theorem 2 are finite-dimensional algebraic statements. Conjecture 1 is not yet proved because the local transversality argument must be checked for a full finite latent-type family, not only for the bridge-only witness. This kernel-replace withdraws the singular bridge interval and uses the regime signature \(\Sigma(P)\) as the focal object.
