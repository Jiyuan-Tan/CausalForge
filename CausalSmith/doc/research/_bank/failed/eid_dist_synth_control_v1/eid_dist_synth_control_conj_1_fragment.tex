\paragraph{9-fragment-statement.}
\paragraph{Motivating conjecture restated verbatim.}
Under Assumptions~\ref{ass:consistency}--\ref{ass:exactfit}, \(Q^0_{0s}\) is point identified by \(P\) if and only if Assumption~\ref{ass:postconstant} holds a.e. in \(u\).  In the donor-weight submodel of Definition~\ref{def:donorweight}, this is equivalent to \(z(u)\in\operatorname{rowspan}\bar X(u)\) a.e.; under Assumption~\ref{ass:convexfit}, in the convex donor-weight submodel, the sharp quantile identified set is exactly the monotone LP envelope generated by the rankwise programs \(\underline q_C(u)\) and \(\overline q_C(u)\), and the sharp CDF bounds are
\[
\underline F_{DW,C}(y)=\inf_{q\in\mathcal{Q}_{DW,C}(P)}\int_0^1\one\{q(u)\le y\}\,du,\qquad
\overline F_{DW,C}(y)=\sup_{q\in\mathcal{Q}_{DW,C}(P)}\int_0^1\one\{q(u)\le y\}\,du.
\]
If \(z(u)\notin\operatorname{rowspan}\bar X(u)\) on a positive-measure set and Assumptions~\ref{ass:rank} and \ref{ass:affineinterior} hold, then \(\mathcal{Q}_{DW}(P)\) contains at least two distinct monotone compatible quantile functions and \(\mathcal{F}_{DW}(P)\) is non-singleton.  The stable convex-loading class in Theorem~\ref{thm:convex-sufficient} is therefore a strict point-identified subclass of the rank-invariant finite-donor quantile-factor class whenever the global post-period functional is not invariant; in the donor-weight submodel, augmented row-span failure is the observable certificate for that noninvariance.

\paragraph{Refutation summary.}
Refuted: the three-rank finite-support panel in \(\S13\) satisfies Assumptions~\ref{ass:consistency}--\ref{ass:exactfit}, \ref{ass:convexfit}, \ref{ass:rank}, and \ref{ass:affineinterior}, has \(z(u)\notin\operatorname{rowspan}\bar X(u)\) on \((1/3,2/3]\), but \(\mathcal{Q}_{DW}(P)=\{0\}\) and \(\mathcal{F}_{DW}(P)\) is singleton.

\paragraph{13-refutation-attempt.}
\paragraph{Primitive: finite-support row-span witness.}
The relevant exact-identification refutation primitive is the finite-support witness: shrink the observable rank support to finitely many strata and check whether the conjectured observable certificate, \(z(u)\notin\operatorname{rowspan}\bar X(u)\), really forces two globally monotone donor-weight counterfactual quantile functions.
A first attempt with \(X(u)\) constant across ranks does not refute the claim, because then the convex rank-wise endpoints inherit monotonicity from the donor post-period quantiles.
The obstruction must therefore use the part of Definition~\ref{def:donorweight} that is global in rank: feasible pointwise perturbations can be squeezed by monotonicity at neighboring ranks.

Take \(J=3\), \(T_0=1\), and split the rank interval into
\[
I_L=(0,1/3],\qquad I_M=(1/3,2/3],\qquad I_H=(2/3,1).
\]
Set the treated pre-period quantile to \(x_0(u)=0\) on all three blocks, and define the donor pre-period vector \(X(u)\) and donor post-period vector \(z(u)\) by
\[
\begin{array}{c|ccc}
u & I_L & I_M & I_H\\ \hline
X(u) & (-2,-1,0)^\transpose & (-1,0,1)^\transpose & (0,1,2)^\transpose\\
z(u) & (-2,-1,0)^\transpose & (0,0,1)^\transpose & (0,1,2)^\transpose .
\end{array}
\]
Choose the endpoint values at \(1/3\) and \(2/3\) by left-continuous convention; all following statements are unchanged a.e.
For each donor \(j\), both \(Q^0_{j1}(u)=X_j(u)\) and \(Q^0_{js}(u)=z_j(u)\) are finite, left-continuous, and nondecreasing in \(u\), and \(x_0(u)=0\) is also a valid treated pre-period quantile.

The panel satisfies the stated factor-law assumptions.
Let \(R=3\), take donor loadings \(b_1=e_1\), \(b_2=e_2\), \(b_3=e_3\), take the treated loading \(b_0=0\), set \(m_1(u)=m_s(u)=0\), set \(\ell_1(u)=X(u)\), and set \(\ell_s(u)=z(u)\).
Then the rank-invariant quantile-factor equations match all donor cells and the treated pre-period cell, while the compatible untreated treated post-period quantile \(q(u)=0\) is finite, left-continuous, and nondecreasing.
The donor loading matrix has affine rank two, so Assumption~\ref{ass:rank} holds for \(J=3\).
Convex pre-period fit holds at every rank: \(w=(0,0,1)^\transpose\) fits \(I_L\), \(w=(0,1,0)^\transpose\) fits \(I_M\), and \(w=(1,0,0)^\transpose\) fits \(I_H\).
Thus Assumptions~\ref{ass:consistency}--\ref{ass:exactfit} can be imposed by the usual deterministic observation convention, and Assumption~\ref{ass:convexfit} also holds.

On the middle block,
\[
\mathcal{W}_A(P,u)=\{(a,1-2a,a)^\transpose:a\in\mathbb R\},
\qquad
\mathcal{W}_C(P,u)=\{(a,1-2a,a)^\transpose:0\le a\le 1/2\}.
\]
Hence \(\mathcal{W}_C(P,u)\) has nonempty relative interior in \(\mathcal{W}_A(P,u)\) for every \(u\in I_M\), a positive-measure set, so Assumption~\ref{ass:affineinterior} holds.
The augmented row-span condition fails on this same block: for \(X(u)=(-1,0,1)^\transpose\),
\[
\operatorname{rowspan}\bar X(u)
=\{(\alpha-\beta,\alpha,\alpha+\beta):\alpha,\beta\in\mathbb R\},
\]
but \(z(u)=(0,0,1)^\transpose\) is not of this form because \(z_1+z_3\ne 2z_2\).
Equivalently, the affine null vector \(h=(1,-2,1)^\transpose\) satisfies \(\bar X(u)h=0\) and \(z(u)^\transpose h=1\).

Despite this positive-measure row-span failure, the global monotone donor-weight identified set is a singleton.
On \(I_L\), \(z(u)=X(u)\), so every affine feasible weight satisfies
\[
z(u)^\transpose w=X(u)^\transpose w=x_0(u)=0.
\]
On \(I_H\), the same identity \(z(u)=X(u)\) again gives \(z(u)^\transpose w=0\) for every affine feasible weight.
On \(I_M\), the feasible affine weights above give \(z(u)^\transpose w=a\), so the pointwise donor-weight value can move.
However any \(q\in\mathcal{Q}_{DW}(P)\) must be nondecreasing and must equal \(0\) a.e. on both \(I_L\) and \(I_H\).
For \(v\in I_L\), \(u\in I_M\), and \(r\in I_H\), monotonicity gives
\[
0=q(v)\le q(u)\le q(r)=0,
\]
so \(q(u)=0\) for a.e. \(u\in I_M\).
Therefore
\[
\mathcal{Q}_{DW}(P)=\{q:q(u)=0\ \text{a.e.}\},
\qquad
\mathcal{F}_{DW}(P)=\{F_0\},
\]
where \(F_0(y)=\int_0^1\one\{0\le y\}\,du\).

This contradicts the conjecture's assertion that row-span failure on a positive-measure set, together with Assumptions~\ref{ass:rank} and \ref{ass:affineinterior}, forces \(\mathcal{Q}_{DW}(P)\) to contain at least two distinct monotone compatible quantile functions and forces \(\mathcal{F}_{DW}(P)\) to be non-singleton.
The failure is not a sampling or support artifact: the pointwise post-period functional is genuinely noninvariant on \(I_M\), but global monotonicity and the fixed values on \(I_L\cup I_H\) collapse all admissible monotone selections to the same quantile curve.
Thus augmented row-span failure is not, by itself, an observable certificate for global noninvariance in the donor-weight submodel.
