Each generator swaps members only within its own pair block. Therefore the product action \(S_2\times S_2\) has one two-member orbit for each block and arm:
\[
\mathcal O=\{O_{1,0},O_{1,1},O_{2,0},O_{2,1}\}.
\tag{1}
\]
If \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) concentrates on \(v_{1,0}\), its arm-\(1\) lift lies in \(O_{1,1}\), so the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate of \(b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) is one and the other arm-\(1\) orbit coordinate is zero.

The mate-control state \((v_{1,1},0)\) lies in \(O_{1,0}\). Its orbit vector has mass one on \(O_{1,0}\) and zero on \(O_{1,1}\), whereas the target arm-\(1\) vector has mass one on \(O_{1,1}\). Thus the relevant stacked equations cannot both hold. Moreover, every legal arm-\(1\) selector is supported on \(\mathcal U_1\) by \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\), while the mate-control state is in \(\mathcal U_0\).

If a legal selector has \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{1,1})=0\), then \(O_{1,1}\), the sole target-supported arm-\(1\) orbit, has zero calibration mass. The three rank regimes and their exact sets and coverages now follow directly from \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-disjoint-orbit-witness}\). Under \(\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_1=\alpha/2\), the ranks coincide by \(\texttt{lem:armwise-finite-rank-staircase}\), leaving exactly the two regimes in the frozen sanity check.
