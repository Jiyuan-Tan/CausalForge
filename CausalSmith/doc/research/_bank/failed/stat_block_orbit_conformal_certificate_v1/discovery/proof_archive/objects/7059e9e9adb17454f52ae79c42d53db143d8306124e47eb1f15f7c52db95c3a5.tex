Assume \(\mathcal V_b=\{v_{b,0},v_{b,1}\}\) and \(\langle s_b\rangle\le\mathcal G\). By \(\texttt{def:pair-action}\), the nonidentity element satisfies
\[
s_b(v_{b,r},a)=(v_{b,1-r},a),
\qquad r,a\in\{0,1\},
\tag{1}
\]
fixes every member outside block \(b\), and acts on complete records through \(\texttt{def:group-action}\).

Every element of \(\mathcal G\) preserves blocks, and the action preserves arm labels. Since block \(b\) contains exactly the two displayed members, the global orbit of either \((v_{b,r},a)\) is contained in the two-state cell \(O_{b,a}\). Equation (1) shows that this orbit contains both states of the cell. Therefore the sufficient local audit gives
\[
\operatorname{Orb}_{\mathcal G}(v_{b,r},a)
=
O_{b,a}
=
\{(v_{b,0},a),(v_{b,1},a)\},
\qquad a\in\{0,1\}.
\tag{2}
\]
No converse is used: a different subgroup, including one generated by coupled swaps, may also generate the same orbit.

Suppose the target arm-\(1\) lifted law is supported on \(O_{b,1}\). Its arm-one orbit vector assigns mass one to row \((1,O_{b,1})\). An exclusive mate-control analogue is supported on a control-labelled state in the distinct arm-zero orbit \(O_{b,0}\), because the group action preserves arm labels. Its mass in row \((1,O_{b,1})\) is zero. Thus its stacked orbit-mass vector differs from the target vector, so the equations in \(\texttt{def:joint-orbit-balance}\), equivalently the relevant equations in \(\texttt{def:common-selector-feasibility-lp}\), cannot hold. Independently, \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) requires an arm-one selector to put mass only on members satisfying \(z_v=1\); the realized mate's control-labelled state is not a legal arm-one selection.

Now let a legal implemented arm-one selector satisfy
\[
q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{b,1})=0.
\tag{3}
\]
Because the target arm-one lift is supported on the single orbit \(O_{b,1}\), every target-supported arm-one orbit has zero calibration mass. Thus \(\texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness}\) applies with \(a=1\) and gives zero conditional coverage when \(k\le M\). If \(k=M+1\), \(\texttt{prop:rank-boundary}\) instead gives
\[
\mathcal C_w=[-2K,2K],
\qquad
\Pr_P(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T)=1.
\]
This proves the claimed reduction in both finite-rank regimes.
