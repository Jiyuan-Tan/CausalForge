Each generator swaps members only within its own pair block. Therefore the product action \(S_2\times S_2\) has one two-member orbit for each block and each arm, namely
\[
\mathcal O=\{O_{1,0},O_{1,1},O_{2,0},O_{2,1}\}.
\]
If \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) concentrates on \(v_{1,0}\), then its arm-\(1\) lift is \(\iota_1(v_{1,0})=(v_{1,0},1)\in O_{1,1}\). Hence the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate of \(b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) is one and every other arm-\(1\) orbit coordinate is zero.

The proposed mate-control state \((v_{1,1},0)\) belongs to \(O_{1,0}\). Its orbit vector has mass one on \(O_{1,0}\) and zero on \(O_{1,1}\), whereas the target arm-\(1\) vector has mass one on \(O_{1,1}\). Thus the relevant stacked equations \(Aw=b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) in \(\texttt{def:common-selector-feasibility-lp}\) cannot both hold for this arm-changing analogue. Moreover, \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) makes every legal arm-\(1\) integrated selector law supported on \(\mathcal U_1\), while \((v_{1,1},0)\in\mathcal U_0\); hence the analogue is not a legal arm-\(1\) selector.

Suppose next that a legal implemented selector satisfies \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{1,1})=0\). Since \(O_{1,1}\) is the only target-supported arm-\(1\) orbit, the premise of \(\texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness}\) holds with \(a=1\). If \(k\le M\), that proposition gives a bounded invariant law with \(I_{0,w}=I_{1,w}=\{0\}\), \(\mathcal C_w=\{0\}\), \(\tau_T=K\), and conditional coverage zero. If \(k=M+1\), \(\texttt{prop:rank-boundary}\) gives
\[
r_{0,(k)}=r_{1,(k)}=+\infty,\qquad I_{0,w}=I_{1,w}=[-K,K],\qquad \mathcal C_w=[-2K,2K],
\]
and conditional coverage one. This proves both rank-regime conclusions in the stated four-member example.
