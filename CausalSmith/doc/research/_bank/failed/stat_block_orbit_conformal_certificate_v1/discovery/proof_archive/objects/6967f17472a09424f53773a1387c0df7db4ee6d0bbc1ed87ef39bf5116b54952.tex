Because the two generators swap members only within their own blocks, the product action \(S_2\times S_2\) has one two-member orbit in each block and arm. Hence the orbit partition is \[\{O_{1,0},O_{1,1},O_{2,0},O_{2,1}\}.\] If \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) is concentrated on \(v_{1,0}\), its arm \(1\) lift is \((v_{1,0},1)\in O_{1,1}\), so the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate of \(b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) equals one. The proposed mate-control state is \((v_{1,1},0)\in O_{1,0}\), and its mass in the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate is zero. Thus the arm-changing candidate cannot satisfy \(Aw=b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) and is not a legal arm \(1\) selector because an arm \(1\) calibration law is supported on \(\mathcal U_1\).

If a legal implemented selector instead has \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{1,1})=0\), the target-supported arm \(1\) orbit is absent from calibration. When \(k\le M\), the disjoint-orbit construction then has all calibration scores zero, both thresholds zero, \(\mathcal C_w=\{0\}\), and effect \(K\), so coverage is zero by \(\texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness}\). When \(k=M+1\), both thresholds are infinite, \(\mathcal C_w=\mathbb R\), and coverage is one by \(\texttt{prop:rank-boundary}\). This verifies both the orbit bookkeeping and the rank-boundary bookkeeping in the two-pair instance without treating the illegal mate-control state as an arm \(1\) observation.
