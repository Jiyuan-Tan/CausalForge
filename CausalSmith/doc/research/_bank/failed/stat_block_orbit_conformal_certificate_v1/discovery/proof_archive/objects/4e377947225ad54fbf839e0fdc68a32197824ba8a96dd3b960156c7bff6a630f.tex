Because the two generators swap members only within their own blocks, the product action \(S_2\times S_2\) has one two-member orbit in each block and arm. Thus its orbit partition is
\[\{O_{1,0},O_{1,1},O_{2,0},O_{2,1}\}.\]
If \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) concentrates on \(v_{1,0}\), its arm-\(1\) lift is \(\iota_1(v_{1,0})=(v_{1,0},1)\in O_{1,1}\). Hence the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate of \(b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) equals one, and every other arm-\(1\) orbit coordinate equals zero.

The proposed mate-control state is \((v_{1,1},0)\in O_{1,0}\). Its mass in the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate is zero while the target coordinate there is one. Therefore this arm-changing analogue cannot satisfy \(Aw=b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) in \(\texttt{def:common-selector-feasibility-lp}\). It is also not a legal arm-\(1\) selector: \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) makes every arm-\(1\) integrated calibration law supported on \(\mathcal U_1\), whereas \((v_{1,1},0)\in\mathcal U_0\).

Now suppose a legal implemented selector satisfies \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{1,1})=0\). Since \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) concentrates on \(v_{1,0}\), the orbit \(O_{1,1}\) is the only target-supported arm-\(1\) orbit. Thus the premise of \(\texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness}\) holds with \(a=1\). If \(k\le M\), that proposition supplies a bounded invariant law for which both thresholds are zero, clipping leaves both arm intervals equal to \(\{0\}\), \(\mathcal C_w=\{0\}\), and \(\tau_T=K\). Since \(K>0\), conditional coverage is zero. If \(k=M+1\), the corrected \(\texttt{prop:rank-boundary}\) gives
\[r_{0,(k)}=r_{1,(k)}=+\infty,\qquad I_{0,w}=I_{1,w}=[-K,K],\qquad \mathcal C_w=[-2K,2K],\]
and conditional coverage is one. This proves the proposed corrected boundary statement.
