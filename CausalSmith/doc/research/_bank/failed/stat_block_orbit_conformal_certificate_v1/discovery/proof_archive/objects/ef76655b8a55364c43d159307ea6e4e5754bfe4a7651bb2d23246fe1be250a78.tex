By \(\texttt{def:pair-action}\), the nonidentity element \(s_b\) interchanges the two members in block \(b\), preserves the arm label, and fixes all outside members. Hence
\[
s_b(v_{b,r},a)=(v_{b,1-r},a),
\]
and the two arm-specific block orbits are
\[
O_{b,0}=\{(v_{b,0},0),(v_{b,1},0)\},
\qquad
O_{b,1}=\{(v_{b,0},1),(v_{b,1},1)\}.
\tag{1}
\]
The complete-record action is exactly the relabelling in \(\texttt{def:group-action}\).

If the target arm-\(1\) lift is supported on \(O_{b,1}\), its arm-\(1\) orbit vector assigns mass one there. An exclusive mate-control analogue lies in the distinct arm-\(0\) orbit \(O_{b,0}\), so it assigns zero mass to \(O_{b,1}\). The orbit vectors differ, and the equalities in \(\texttt{def:joint-orbit-balance}\), equivalently the relevant rows of \(Aw=b_{\mathrm{LP}}\), cannot hold for that analogue. It is independently illegal as an arm-\(1\) selector: \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) requires such a selector to choose a member with realized arm one, whereas the proposed control state lies in \(\mathcal U_0\).

Now suppose a legal arm-\(1\) selector has \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{b,1})=0\). Since \(O_{b,1}\) is the only target-supported arm-\(1\) orbit in this reduction, the premise of \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-disjoint-orbit-witness}\) holds. That proposition gives zero coverage when both ranks are finite, the effect set \([-K,K]\) with coverage one when exactly one rank is at the boundary, and \([-2K,2K]\) with coverage one when both are at the boundary. The diagonal specialization makes the ranks equal and yields the frozen symmetric reduction.
