For any \(w\in\Delta(\mathcal H)\), linearity of the integrated selector law gives \[(Aw)_{(a,O)}=\sum_{h\in\mathcal H}w_hq^{\mathrm{obs}}_{h,a}(O)=q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,a}(O).\] By the definition of \(b_{\mathrm{LP}}\), the equation \(Aw=b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) is therefore exactly the collection of joint orbit-balance equations. Together with \(w_h\ge0\) and \(\sum_hw_h=1\), this proves equivalence with feasibility of the stacked linear program. The same simplex constraints show \[\{Aw:w\in\Delta(\mathcal H)\}=\operatorname{conv}\{A_{\cdot h}:h\in\mathcal H\},\] proving the convex-hull characterization.

For completeness, infeasibility has an explicit separating certificate. Let \(C=\operatorname{conv}\{A_{\cdot h}:h\in\mathcal H\}\). This is compact. If \(b_{\mathrm{LP}}\notin C\), let \(x_*\in C\) minimize \(\|b_{\mathrm{LP}}-x\|_2\) and put \(y=b_{\mathrm{LP}}-x_*\). For every \(x\in C\), differentiating \(\|b_{\mathrm{LP}}-(x_*+t(x-x_*))\|_2^2\) at \(t=0+\) yields \(y^\top(x-x_*)\le0\). Since \(y\ne0\), \[\sup_{x\in C}y^\top x\le y^\top x_*<y^\top b_{\mathrm{LP}}.\] Thus \(y\) is the asserted finite Farkas separating hyperplane.

Now let \(w\) be feasible and set \(N=M+1\) and \(\varepsilon=\alpha/2\). By \(\texttt{thm:orbit-balance-iff}\), conditional on \(\mathcal T\), the \(N\) scores \(S_{1,a},\ldots,S_{M,a},S_{0,a}\) are iid for each fixed arm. Append independent continuous uniforms and order lexicographically, as permitted by \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\). Exchangeability makes the target's lexicographic rank uniform on \(\{1,\ldots,N\}\). If \(S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\), then at least \(k\) calibration scores are strictly smaller than \(S_{0,a}\), so the target's lexicographic rank is larger than \(k\). Hence \[\Pr(S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\mid\mathcal T)\le\frac{N-k}{N}.\] Since \(k=\lceil N(1-\varepsilon)\rceil\), \((N-k)/N\le\varepsilon=\alpha/2\). On the event \(S_{0,a}\le r_{a,(k)}\), the potential outcome \(Y_{0T}(a)\) belongs to \(I_{a,w}\). If this holds in both arms, then \(Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)\in I_{1,w}-I_{0,w}=\mathcal C_w\). A union bound over the two armwise failures, requiring no independence between arms, proves \[\Pr(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T)\ge1-\alpha.\]
