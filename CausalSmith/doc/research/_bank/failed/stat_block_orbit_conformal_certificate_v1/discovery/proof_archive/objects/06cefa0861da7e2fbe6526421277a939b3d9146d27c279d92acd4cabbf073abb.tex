By the hypothesis \(\langle s_b\rangle\le\mathcal G\) and \(\texttt{def:pair-action}\), the nonidentity element \(s_b\) interchanges the two members of block \(b\), preserves the arm label, and fixes every outside member. Thus
\[
s_b(v_{b,r},a)=(v_{b,1-r},a),
\]
and the two arm-specific block orbits are
\[
O_{b,0}=\{(v_{b,0},0),(v_{b,1},0)\},\qquad O_{b,1}=\{(v_{b,0},1),(v_{b,1},1)\}.
\tag{1}
\]
The action on complete records is the relabelling in \(\texttt{def:group-action}\).

If the target arm \(1\) lift is supported on \(O_{b,1}\), its arm \(1\) orbit vector places mass one there. An exclusive mate-control analogue lies instead in the distinct arm \(0\) orbit \(O_{b,0}\), and hence places zero mass on \(O_{b,1}\). The orbit vectors differ, so the equalities in \(\texttt{def:joint-orbit-balance}\), equivalently the corresponding rows of the system in \(\texttt{def:common-selector-feasibility-lp}\), cannot hold for that analogue. It is also not a legal arm \(1\) selector: \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) requires an arm \(1\) selector to choose a member whose realized arm is one, whereas a mate-control state belongs to \(\mathcal U_0\). These conclusions do not involve the numerical error allocation.

Now suppose a legal implemented arm \(1\) selector has \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{b,1})=0\). Because \(O_{b,1}\) is the target-supported arm \(1\) orbit in this reduction, the premise of \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-disjoint-orbit-witness}\) holds. That proposition gives zero coverage when both ranks are finite, the effect set \([-K,K]\) with coverage one when exactly one rank equals \(M+1\), and \([-2K,2K]\) with coverage one when both ranks equal \(M+1\). On the diagonal, \(\texttt{lem:armwise-finite-rank-staircase}\) makes the ranks equal, leaving precisely the two regimes and conclusions of the symmetric pair reduction.
