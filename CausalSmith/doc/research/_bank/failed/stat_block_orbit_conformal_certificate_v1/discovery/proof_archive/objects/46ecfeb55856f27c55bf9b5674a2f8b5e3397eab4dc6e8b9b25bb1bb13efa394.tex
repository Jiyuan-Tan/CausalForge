Put \(N=M+1\). The overlap condition and the common kernels imply \(\mathsf P\ll\mathsf Q\) with
\[
\frac{d\mathsf P}{d\mathsf Q}(O,r)=\lambda_O,
\qquad \int\lambda_O\,d\mathsf Q(O,r)=1.
\tag{1}
\]
For a deterministic collection \(e_j=(O_j,R_j)\), \(0\le j<N\), write \(W_j=\lambda_{O_j}\) and \(W=\sum_jW_j\). For each index \(j\), let \(q_j\) be the left-continuous \((1-\epsilon)\)-quantile of
\[
W^{-1}\left(\sum_{\ell\ne j}W_\ell\delta_{R_\ell}+W_j\delta_{+\infty}\right),
\tag{2}
\]
when \(W>0\); define it arbitrarily when \(W=0\). If \(R_j>q_j\), then \(q_j\) is finite and the mass in (2) at scores strictly below \(R_j\) is at least \(1-\epsilon\). Thus, with
\[
L_j=\sum_{\ell:R_\ell<R_j}W_\ell,
\]
one has \(L_j\ge(1-\epsilon)W\). Let \(A=\{j:R_j>q_j\}\). If \(A\ne\varnothing\), choose \(j_*\in A\) whose score is minimal over \(A\). Then
\[
\sum_{j\in A}W_j\le\sum_{j:R_j\ge R_{j_*}}W_j=W-L_{j_*}\le\epsilon W.
\tag{3}
\]
The same inequality is immediate when \(A=\varnothing\); it also holds when \(W=0\). Hence, pointwise,
\[
\sum_{j=0}^{N-1}W_j\mathbf 1\{R_j>q_j\}\le\epsilon\sum_{j=0}^{N-1}W_j.
\tag{4}
\]
Now draw \(e_0,\ldots,e_{N-1}\) iid from \(\mathsf Q\). By (1), change of measure in coordinate zero gives
\[
\Pr_{\mathsf P\otimes\mathsf Q^M}(R_0>q_0)=\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\!\left[W_0\mathbf 1\{R_0>q_0\}\right].
\tag{5}
\]
The iid reference law is permutation invariant, and the rule defining \(q_j\) is the same after relabelling indices. Therefore the last expectation equals
\[
\frac1N\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\!\left[\sum_{j=0}^{N-1}W_j\mathbf 1\{R_j>q_j\}\right].
\tag{6}
\]
Using (4), then (1), bounds (6) by
\[
\frac{\epsilon}{N}\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\!\left[\sum_{j=0}^{N-1}W_j\right]=\epsilon.
\tag{7}
\]
Under \(\mathsf P\otimes\mathsf Q^M\), one has \(W_0>0\) almost surely, and \(q_0=r^{\mathrm W}\) by construction. Equation (7) is the claimed inequality. The strict comparison in the failure event and the atom at \(+\infty\) make the argument valid without assumptions on score ties or on zero calibration weights.
