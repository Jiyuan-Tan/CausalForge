Because the two generators swap members only within their own blocks, the product action \(S_2\times S_2\) has one two-member orbit in each block and arm. Hence the orbit partition is
\[\{O_{1,0},O_{1,1},O_{2,0},O_{2,1}\}.\]
If \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) is concentrated on \(v_{1,0}\), its arm-\(1\) lift is \(\iota_1(v_{1,0})=(v_{1,0},1)\in O_{1,1}\). Therefore the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate of \(b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) equals one, while its other arm-\(1\) orbit coordinates equal zero.

The proposed mate-control state is \((v_{1,1},0)\in O_{1,0}\). Consequently its mass in the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate is zero, whereas the target coordinate there is one. Thus this arm-changing analogue cannot satisfy \(Aw=b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) in \texttt{def:common-selector-feasibility-lp}. Moreover, it is not a legal arm-\(1\) selector: by \texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}, every arm-\(1\) integrated calibration law is supported on \(\mathcal U_1\), but \((v_{1,1},0)\in\mathcal U_0\).

Now consider a legal implemented selector satisfying \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{1,1})=0\). Since \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) concentrates on \(v_{1,0}\), \(O_{1,1}\) is the only target-supported arm-\(1\) orbit. The premise of \texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness} therefore holds with \(a=1\). If \(k\le M\), that proposition supplies a bounded invariant law for which every calibration score in both arms is zero, both thresholds are zero, clipping leaves both arm intervals equal to \(\{0\}\), \(\mathcal C_w=\{0\}\), and \(\tau_T=K\). Since \(K>0\), conditional coverage is zero. If instead \(k=M+1\), the corrected \texttt{prop:rank-boundary} gives \(r_{0,(k)}=r_{1,(k)}=+\infty\), \(I_{0,w}=I_{1,w}=[-K,K]\), \(\mathcal C_w=[-2K,2K]\), and conditional coverage one.
