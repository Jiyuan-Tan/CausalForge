By \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\), when \(k=M+1\) the order-statistic convention gives \(r_{a,(k)}=+\infty\) in each arm. Since each fitted center is real-valued, \[I_{a,w}=[\widehat m_a(X_{0T})-\infty,\widehat m_a(X_{0T})+\infty]=\mathbb R.\] Therefore \(\mathcal C_w=\mathbb R-\mathbb R=\mathbb R\). The bounded potential outcomes make \(\tau_T\) real, so it belongs to \(\mathcal C_w\) surely, under every law and every fitted predictor map. Conditioning on \(\mathcal T\) preserves this probability-one statement.
