Let \(N=M+1\). Since \(k=\lceil N(1-\alpha/2)\rceil\),
\[k\le M\iff \left\lceil N(1-\alpha/2)\right\rceil\le N-1\iff N(1-\alpha/2)\le N-1\iff \alpha\ge\frac{2}{M+1}.\]
The middle equivalence uses the elementary fact that \(\lceil x\rceil\le n\) if and only if \(x\le n\) for integral \(n\). Fix the adverse arm \(a\). Under \(\texttt{def:disjoint-orbit-law}\), the potential-outcome array is constant on every \(\mathcal G\)-orbit: its arm-\(a\) value is \(K\) on target-supported arm-\(a\) orbits and zero elsewhere, and every arm-\(1-a\) value is zero. Complete this outcome prototype with any fixed covariate array and let \(G\) be uniform on the finite group \(\mathcal G\); the construction draws the latent array as \(GL^*\). For every fixed \(h\in\mathcal G\), the product \(hG\) is again uniform on \(\mathcal G\), so \(h(GL^*)\) and \(GL^*\) have the same law. Thus the latent law satisfies \(\texttt{ass:finite-group-latent-invariance}\). All potential-outcome coordinates belong to \(\{0,K\}\subseteq[-K,K]\), so \(\texttt{ass:bounded-potential-outcomes}\) also holds. Hence the law belongs to \(\mathcal P_{\mathcal G,K}\) by \(\texttt{def:invariant-laws}\). Independent copies, followed by the known assignment law and the stipulated auxiliary selector draws, satisfy the replicated-experiment assumptions.

Let \(O\) be an orbit with \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,a}(O)>0\). By hypothesis no such orbit is target-supported, so the construction assigns arm-\(a\) outcome zero on \(O\). The fixed predictor is identically zero, and \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) forces the selector to choose a member observed in arm \(a\). Thus every adverse-arm calibration score is zero almost surely. Since \(k\le M\),
\[r_{a,(k)}=0.\]
Every potential outcome and predictor in arm \(1-a\) is also zero, so every calibration score in that arm is zero and \(r_{1-a,(k)}=0\). The raw residual-centered interval in either arm is \(\{0\}\), and clipping to \([-K,K]\) leaves it unchanged because \(K>0\). By the corrected \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\),
\[I_{0,w}=I_{1,w}=\{0\},\qquad \mathcal C_w=\{0\}-\{0\}=\{0\}.\]
By \(\texttt{ass:prespecified-target}\), the selected member lies almost surely in a target-supported orbit. Hence \(Y_{0T}(a)=K\) and \(Y_{0T}(1-a)=0\). If \(a=1\), then \(\tau_T=K\); if \(a=0\), then \(\tau_T=-K\). Because \(K>0\), neither value belongs to \(\{0\}\). Therefore
\[\Pr_P\{\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T\}=0.\]
