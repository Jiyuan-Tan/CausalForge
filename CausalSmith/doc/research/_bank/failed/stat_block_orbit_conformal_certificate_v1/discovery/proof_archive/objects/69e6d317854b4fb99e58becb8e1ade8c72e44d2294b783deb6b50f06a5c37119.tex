Write \(N=M+1\), \(w_j=\lambda(O_j)\), and \(W=\sum_{j=0}^{M}w_j\). Under the target law, \(p_{O_0}>0\) almost surely; overlap then gives \(q_{O_0}>0\) and \(w_0>0\), so \(W>0\) almost surely. For a deterministic configuration \((O_j,R_j)_{j=0}^{M}\) and each \(j\), define \(t_j\) as the left-continuous \((1-\varepsilon)\)-quantile of
\[\frac{w_j\delta_{+\infty}+\sum_{\ell\ne j}w_\ell\delta_{R_\ell}}{W}.\]
On configurations with \(W=0\), which can arise under the reference product law used below, set \(t_j=+\infty\). Let \(A=\{j:R_j>t_j\}\). We first prove the deterministic inequality
\[\sum_{j=0}^{M}w_j\mathbf 1\{j\in A\}\le\varepsilon W.\tag{1}\]
It is immediate when \(A\) is empty or \(W=0\). Otherwise choose \(j_*\in A\) with minimal \(R_{j_*}\). Since \(R_{j_*}>t_{j_*}\), the quantile is finite and the discrete cumulative mass strictly below \(R_{j_*}\) is at least \(1-\varepsilon\). The atom indexed by \(j_*\) was placed at \(+\infty\), so
\[\sum_{\ell:R_\ell<R_{j_*}}w_\ell\ge(1-\varepsilon)W.\]
Consequently \(\sum_{\ell:R_\ell\ge R_{j_*}}w_\ell\le\varepsilon W\). By minimality of \(R_{j_*}\) on \(A\), every member of \(A\) belongs to this upper set, proving \((1)\). This argument also shows why ties cause no difficulty: miscoverage uses the strict event above the weakly included threshold.

Because the conditional score law \(F_o\) is the same under \(\mathsf P\) and \(\mathsf Q\),
\[\frac{d\mathsf P}{d\mathsf Q}(o,r)=\lambda(o)\quad\mathsf Q\text{-almost surely}.\]
Let \(\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\) denote expectation when all \(N\) pairs are independent from \(\mathsf Q\). Change of measure in coordinate zero gives
\[\Pr_{\mathsf P\otimes\mathsf Q^M}\{R_0>t_0\}=\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\!\left[w_0\mathbf 1\{R_0>t_0\}\right].\]
The reference product law is exchangeable, and the rule producing \(t_j\) is permutation equivariant. Averaging the last display over the \(N\) possible distinguished coordinates and invoking \((1)\) therefore yields
\[\begin{aligned}\Pr_{\mathsf P\otimes\mathsf Q^M}\{R_0>t_0\}&=\frac1N\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\!\left[\sum_{j=0}^{M}w_j\mathbf 1\{R_j>t_j\}\right]\\&\le\frac{\varepsilon}{N}\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}[W]=\varepsilon\,\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q}[\lambda(O)]=\varepsilon,\end{aligned}\]
since \(\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q}[\lambda(O)]=\sum_o q_o(p_o/q_o)=\sum_o p_o=1\), with zero-mass terms omitted. Under \(\mathsf P\otimes\mathsf Q^M\), \(t_0\) is precisely \(\widehat r\), completing the proof.
