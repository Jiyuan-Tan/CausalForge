Set \(N=M+1\). For every integer \(N\) and real \(x\), the elementary ceiling--floor identity
\[\lceil N-x\rceil=N-\lfloor x\rfloor\tag{1}\]
follows from \(\lfloor x\rfloor\le x<\lfloor x\rfloor+1\) after multiplication by \(-1\) and addition of \(N\). Taking \(x=N\alpha/2\) in (1) gives
\[N-k=N-\left\lceil N-\frac{N\alpha}{2}\right\rceil=\left\lfloor\frac{N\alpha}{2}\right\rfloor.\]
Division by \(N>0\) and \(\texttt{def:finite-rank-error}\) prove
\[\rho_{M,\alpha}=\frac{N-k}{N}=\frac{\lfloor N\alpha/2\rfloor}{N}.\]
The inequality \(\lfloor x\rfloor\le x\) gives \(\rho_{M,\alpha}\le\alpha/2\). Moreover, for every integer \(j\),
\[\left\lfloor\frac{N\alpha}{2}\right\rfloor=j\iff j\le\frac{N\alpha}{2}<j+1\iff\frac{2j}{N}\le\alpha<\frac{2(j+1)}{N}.\]
Restricting to those intervals that intersect \((0,1)\) gives the asserted staircase description. Finally, \(k=N\) if and only if \(N-k=0\), hence if and only if \(\lfloor N\alpha/2\rfloor=0\). Under \(0<\alpha<1\), this is exactly \(0<\alpha<2/N=2/(M+1)\), and it is also equivalent to \(\rho_{M,\alpha}=0\).
