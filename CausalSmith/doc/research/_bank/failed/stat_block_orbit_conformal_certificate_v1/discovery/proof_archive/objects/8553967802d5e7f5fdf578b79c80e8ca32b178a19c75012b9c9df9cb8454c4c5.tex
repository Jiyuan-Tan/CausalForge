Put \(N=M+1\). By \(\texttt{lem:finite-rank-staircase}\), \(k=M+1=N\) is equivalent to \(\rho_{M,\alpha}=0\), and, because \(0<\alpha<1\), this is equivalent to \(0<\alpha<2/(M+1)\). Thus this is precisely the \(j=0\) step of the finite-rank staircase. By \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\), the convention for \(k=M+1\) gives \[r_{0,(k)}=r_{1,(k)}=+\infty.\] Each fitted center \(\widehat m_a(X_{0T})\) is real-valued, so \[I_{a,w}=[\widehat m_a(X_{0T})-\infty,\widehat m_a(X_{0T})+\infty]=\mathbb R,\qquad a\in\{0,1\}.\] Hence \(\mathcal C_w=\mathbb R-\mathbb R=\mathbb R\). The bounded-potential-outcome setup makes \(\tau_T=Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)\) a finite real number. Therefore \(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\) surely under every law and every pair of trained predictor maps, and conditioning on \(\mathcal T\) preserves this probability-one assertion.
