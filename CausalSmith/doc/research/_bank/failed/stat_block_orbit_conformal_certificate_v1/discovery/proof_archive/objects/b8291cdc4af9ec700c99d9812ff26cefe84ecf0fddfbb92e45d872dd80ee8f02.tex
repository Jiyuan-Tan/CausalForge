Condition throughout on \(\mathcal T\), and put \(N=M+1\). Fix an arm \(a\). By \(\texttt{lem:orbit-score-factorization}\), the calibration-score law and target-score law are, respectively,
\[
Q_a(C)=\sum_{O\in\mathcal O_a}q_{a,O}F_{a,O}(C),
\qquad
P_a(C)=\sum_{O\in\mathcal O_a}p_{a,O}F_{a,O}(C)
\]
for every Borel set \(C\subseteq[0,\infty)\), where \(\mathcal O_a=\{O\in\mathcal O:O\subseteq\mathcal U_a\}\). Since both orbit-mass vectors sum to one, \(\sum_O(p_{a,O}-q_{a,O})=0\). Therefore, for every measurable \(f:[0,\infty)\to[0,1]\),
\[
\left|\int f\,dP_a-\int f\,dQ_a\right|
=\left|\sum_O(p_{a,O}-q_{a,O})\int f\,dF_{a,O}\right|
\le\frac12\sum_O|p_{a,O}-q_{a,O}|
=\Delta_a(w).
\tag{1}
\]
This is the finite-mixture total-variation contraction, derived here directly from the orbit kernels.

Suppose first that \(a\in\mathcal A_{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}\), equivalently \(k_a\le M\). Conditional on the \(M\) calibration scores, apply (1) to
\[
f_a(s)=\mathbf 1\{s>r_{a,(k_a)}\}.
\]
The factorization lemma also says that the calibration scores are iid with law \(Q_a\) and independent of the target score. Hence, if \(\widetilde S_{0,a}\sim Q_a\) is independent of the calibration scores,
\[
\Pr\{S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k_a)}\mid\mathcal T\}
\le\Pr\{\widetilde S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k_a)}\mid\mathcal T\}+\Delta_a(w).
\tag{2}
\]
The \(N\) scores \(S_{1,a},\ldots,S_{M,a},\widetilde S_{0,a}\) are iid. Append iid continuous uniforms and rank the resulting pairs lexicographically, as prescribed in \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\). The target pair has a uniform rank on \(\{1,\ldots,N\}\). The strict exceedance event in (2) implies that its augmented rank exceeds \(k_a\), and consequently
\[
\Pr\{\widetilde S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k_a)}\mid\mathcal T\}
\le\frac{N-k_a}{N}=\rho_{M,\varepsilon_a}.
\tag{3}
\]
Combining (2) and (3) yields
\[
\Pr\{S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k_a)}\mid\mathcal T\}
\le\rho_{M,\varepsilon_a}+\Delta_a(w),
\qquad a\in\mathcal A_{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}.
\tag{4}
\]
By boundedness, \(Y_{0T}(a)\in[-K,K]\). Thus \(S_{0,a}\le r_{a,(k_a)}\) implies \(Y_{0T}(a)\in I^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_{a,w}\). If instead \(a\notin\mathcal A_{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}\), then \(k_a=M+1\), and \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\) gives \(I^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_{a,w}=[-K,K]\); this arm contains its potential outcome surely and contributes no failure probability. If both armwise containments hold, then
\[
\tau_T=Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)\in I^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_{1,w}-I^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_{0,w}=\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w.
\]
The conditional union bound applied only to the active arms and (4) therefore give
\[
\Pr_P\!\left\{\tau_T\in\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w\mid\mathcal T\right\}
\ge\max\!\left\{0,1-\sum_{a\in\mathcal A_{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}}\bigl(\rho_{M,\varepsilon_a}+\Delta_a(w)\bigr)\right\}.
\tag{5}
\]
In particular, with one boundary arm only the other arm appears in (5); with two boundary arms the sum is empty, both intervals are \([-K,K]\), and coverage is one.

The identity in \(\texttt{lem:armwise-finite-rank-staircase}\) gives \(\rho_{M,\varepsilon_a}=j/(M+1)\) exactly on \(\varepsilon_a\in[j/(M+1),(j+1)/(M+1))\), so the coordinate elbows are as stated. Moreover, if \(d_O=p_{a,O}-q_{a,O}=-\delta_{a,O}(w)\), then \(\sum_Od_O=0\) and
\[
\Delta_a(w)=\frac12\sum_O|d_O|=\sum_{d_O>0}d_O=\sum_{d_O<0}(-d_O).
\]
Every positive or negative coordinate is bounded in absolute value by the corresponding sum, proving \(|\delta_{a,O}(w)|\le\Delta_a(w)\). Finally, on the diagonal \(\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_1=\alpha/2\), either \(k_0=k_1=k\le M\), in which case both arms are active and (5) is exactly the symmetric formula, or \(k=M+1\), in which case coverage is one and the symmetric lower bound still holds.
