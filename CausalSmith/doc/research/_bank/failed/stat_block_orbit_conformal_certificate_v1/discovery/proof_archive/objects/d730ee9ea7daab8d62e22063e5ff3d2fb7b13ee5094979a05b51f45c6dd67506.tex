The asymmetric allocation changes only \(k_0\) and \(k_1\), not the group action or selector support. Thus \(\texttt{prop:s2-reduction}\) still gives \(\operatorname{Orb}_{\mathcal G}(v_{b,r},a)=O_{b,a}\) under the audited local swap subgroup. A mate-control state lies in \(O_{b,0}\), whereas the target arm-one lift lies in \(O_{b,1}\). Their orbit-mass vectors differ, so the joint-balance and stacked-LP equations cannot both hold; selector observability also forbids a control-labelled state from an arm-one selector. If a legal arm-one selector has \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{b,1})=0\), then the sole target-supported arm-one orbit has zero calibration mass. Applying \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-disjoint-orbit-witness}\) gives \(\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w=\{0\}\) and zero coverage when both ranks are finite, \([-K,K]\) and coverage one when exactly one rank is a boundary rank, and \([-2K,2K]\) and coverage one when both are boundary ranks. On the symmetric diagonal \(k_0=k_1=k\), so the exactly-one-boundary case disappears and the result reduces to \(\texttt{prop:s2-reduction}\).
