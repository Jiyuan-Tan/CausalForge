Put \(N=M+1\). For either arm \(c\), integrality gives
\[
\begin{aligned}
k_c\le M
&\Longleftrightarrow
\lceil N(1-\varepsilon_c)\rceil\le N-1\\
&\Longleftrightarrow
N(1-\varepsilon_c)\le N-1\\
&\Longleftrightarrow
\varepsilon_c\ge\frac1{M+1}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{1}
\]
The orbit-constant construction, invariance, boundedness, and experimental-law verification are exactly those proved in \(\texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness}\); they concern actual \(\mathcal G\)-orbits and require no pair specialization. In particular,
\[
P\in\mathcal P_{\mathcal G,K},
\qquad
S_{i,0}=S_{i,1}=0\quad(1\le i\le M),
\tag{2}
\]
and
\[
\tau_T=
\begin{cases}
K,&a=1,\\
-K,&a=0.
\end{cases}
\tag{3}
\]
By \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\), a zero calibration sample gives
\[
r_{c,(k_c)}
=
\begin{cases}
0,&k_c\le M,\\
+\infty,&k_c=M+1.
\end{cases}
\tag{4}
\]

If both ranks are finite, (4) gives
\[
I^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_{0,w}
=
I^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_{1,w}
=
\{0\},
\qquad
\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w=\{0\}.
\tag{5}
\]
Since \(K>0\), (3) and (5) give zero conditional coverage. If exactly one rank equals \(M+1\), \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-rank-boundary}\) and (4) give one arm interval \([-K,K]\) and the other interval \(\{0\}\). In either order their Minkowski difference is
\[
[-K,K]-\{0\}=\{0\}-[-K,K]=[-K,K].
\tag{6}
\]
Both possible values in (3) belong to (6), so this witness has conditional coverage one. If both ranks equal \(M+1\), the same boundary proposition gives
\[
\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w=[-2K,2K],
\tag{7}
\]
which again contains (3) almost surely. Equation (1) proves the rank-regime equivalence. On the diagonal \(\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_1=\alpha/2\), the ranks coincide by \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\), so the two possible regimes reduce to \(\texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness}\) and its boundary case.
