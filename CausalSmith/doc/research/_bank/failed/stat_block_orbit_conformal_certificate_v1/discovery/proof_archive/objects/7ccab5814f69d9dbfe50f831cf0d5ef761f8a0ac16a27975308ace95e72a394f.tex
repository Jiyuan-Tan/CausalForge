Condition on \(\mathcal T\) and fix an arm \(a\). By \(\texttt{lem:orbit-score-factorization}\), define laws on orbit--score pairs by \[Q_a(\{O\}\times C)=q_{a,O}F_{a,O}(C),\qquad P_a(\{O\}\times C)=p_{a,O}F_{a,O}(C).\tag{1}\] Orbit overlap gives \(P_a\ll Q_a\), and their likelihood ratio is \[\frac{dP_a}{dQ_a}(O,s)=\lambda_{a,O}\quad Q_a\text{-almost surely}.\tag{2}\] Thus the quotient likelihood ratio depends only on the orbit even if the target and selector are singular at the member level. The factorization lemma further gives iid calibration pairs from \(Q_a\), independent of the target pair from \(P_a\). Apply \(\texttt{lem:finite-support-weighted-rank-validity}\) with \(X=(O,S)\), score map \(R(O,S)=S\), and likelihood ratio (2). Its weighted empirical law is exactly the law in \(\texttt{def:weighted-rank-inversion}\): calibration point \(i\) receives mass \(\lambda_{a,O_{i,a}}\), while the target receives its weight at \(+\infty\). Therefore \[\Pr\{S_{0,a}>r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}\mid\mathcal T\}\le\epsilon_a.\tag{3}\]
Boundedness again makes \(Y_{0T}(a)\in I^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}\) equivalent to \(S_{0,a}\le r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}\). If both arm events occur, then \(\tau_T\in I^{\mathrm W}_{1,w}-I^{\mathrm W}_{0,w}=\mathcal C^{\mathrm W}_w\). Bonferroni applied to (3) gives \[\Pr_P(\tau_T\in\mathcal C^{\mathrm W}_w\mid\mathcal T)\ge1-\epsilon_0-\epsilon_1\ge1-\alpha.\tag{4}\] No arm independence is used.
Under joint balance, \(p_{a,O}=q_{a,O}\), so \(\lambda_{a,O}=1\) on every orbit that can be sampled; zero--zero orbits are irrelevant. The augmented empirical measure then has \(M\) unit atoms at the calibration scores and one unit atom at \(+\infty\). Its left-continuous \((1-\epsilon_a)\)-quantile is the \(k_a\)-th calibration order statistic for \(k_a=\lceil(M+1)(1-\epsilon_a)\rceil\) when \(k_a\le M\), and is \(+\infty\) when \(k_a=M+1\). Hence weighted inversion equals ordinary arm-specific inversion. Setting \(\epsilon_0=\epsilon_1=\alpha/2\) yields the asserted symmetric formulas.
