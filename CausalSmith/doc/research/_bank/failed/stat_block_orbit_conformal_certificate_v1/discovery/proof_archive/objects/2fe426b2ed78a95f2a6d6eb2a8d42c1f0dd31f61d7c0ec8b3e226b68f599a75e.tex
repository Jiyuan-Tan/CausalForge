Assume \(k=M+1\). By the convention in \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\),
\[
r_{0,(k)}=r_{1,(k)}=+\infty.
\]
Thus the un-clipped residual-centered interval is all of \(\mathbb R\) in each arm. Intersecting with the known outcome range as required by \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\) gives, for \(a\in\{0,1\}\),
\[
I_{a,w}=\mathbb R\cap[-K,K]=[-K,K].
\]
Taking the Minkowski difference yields
\[
\mathcal C_w=[-K,K]-[-K,K]=[-2K,2K].
\]
By \(\texttt{ass:bounded-potential-outcomes}\), both \(Y_{0T}(1)\) and \(Y_{0T}(0)\) lie in \([-K,K]\) almost surely. Therefore
\[
-2K\le Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)=\tau_T\le2K
\]
almost surely, so \(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\) with probability one. This statement is independent of the trained predictor maps, and conditioning the probability-one assertion on \(\mathcal T\) proves the claimed conditional coverage. The identification of this case with the zeroth staircase regime also follows from \(\texttt{lem:finite-rank-staircase}\).
