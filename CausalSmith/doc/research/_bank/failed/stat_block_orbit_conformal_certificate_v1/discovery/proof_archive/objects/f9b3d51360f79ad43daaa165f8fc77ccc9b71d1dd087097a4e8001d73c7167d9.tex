By \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\), when \(k=M+1\) the reported order statistic is defined to be \(+\infty\) in each arm. Hence\n\[r_{0,(k)}=r_{1,(k)}=+\infty.\]\nFor either arm \(a\), the untrimmed interval with this radius is all of \(\mathbb R\), and clipping therefore gives\n\[I_{a,w}=\mathbb R\cap[-K,K]=[-K,K].\]\nTaking the Minkowski difference yields\n\[\mathcal C_w=[-K,K]-[-K,K]=[-2K,2K].\]\nBy \(\texttt{ass:bounded-potential-outcomes}\), both \(Y_{0T}(1)\) and \(Y_{0T}(0)\) belong to \([-K,K]\). Consequently\n\[-2K\le Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)=\tau_T\le2K\]\nalmost surely, for every latent law and every choice of the trained predictor maps. Thus \(\Pr_P(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T)=1\).
