Write \(N=M+1\), \(w_j=\lambda(O_j)\), and \(W=\sum_{j=0}^{M}w_j\). Under the target law, \(p_{O_0}>0\) almost surely; overlap gives \(q_{O_0}>0\), hence \(w_0>0\) and \(W>0\) almost surely.

For a deterministic configuration \((O_j,R_j)_{j=0}^{M}\), define \(t_j\) as the left-continuous \((1-\varepsilon)\)-quantile of the normalized measure
\[
\frac{w_j\delta_{+\infty}+\sum_{\ell\ne j}w_\ell\delta_{R_\ell}}{W}.\tag{1}
\]
On configurations with \(W=0\), which can occur only under the all-reference product law introduced below, set \(t_j=+\infty\). Let \(A=\{j:R_j>t_j\}\). We claim the deterministic inequality
\[
\sum_{j=0}^{M}w_j\mathbf 1\{j\in A\}\le\varepsilon W.\tag{2}
\]
It is immediate if \(A=\varnothing\) or \(W=0\). Otherwise choose \(j_*\in A\) with minimal \(R_{j_*}\). Since \(R_{j_*}>t_{j_*}\), the quantile in \((1)\) is finite and its cumulative mass strictly below \(R_{j_*}\) is at least \(1-\varepsilon\). The \(j_*\)-th atom is at \(+\infty\), so
\[
\sum_{\ell:R_\ell<R_{j_*}}w_\ell\ge(1-\varepsilon)W.
\]
Consequently \(\sum_{\ell:R_\ell\ge R_{j_*}}w_\ell\le\varepsilon W\). Minimality of \(R_{j_*}\) over \(A\) implies \(A\subseteq\{\ell:R_\ell\ge R_{j_*}\}\), which proves \((2)\). This also handles ties: failure is the strict event above a weakly included threshold.

Because \(F_o\) is the same under \(\mathsf P\) and \(\mathsf Q\),
\[
\frac{d\mathsf P}{d\mathsf Q}(o,r)=\lambda(o)\qquad\mathsf Q\text{-almost surely}.\tag{3}
\]
Let \(\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\) denote expectation when all \(N\) pairs are independent from \(\mathsf Q\). Changing measure only in coordinate zero gives
\[
\Pr_{\mathsf P\otimes\mathsf Q^M}\{R_0>t_0\}=\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\!\left[w_0\mathbf 1\{R_0>t_0\}\right].\tag{4}
\]
The reference product law is exchangeable, and the construction of \(t_j\) is permutation equivariant. Averaging \((4)\) over the \(N\) possible distinguished coordinates and applying \((2)\) yields
\[
\begin{aligned}
\Pr_{\mathsf P\otimes\mathsf Q^M}\{R_0>t_0\}&=\frac1N\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}\!\left[\sum_{j=0}^{M}w_j\mathbf 1\{R_j>t_j\}\right]\\
&\le\frac{\varepsilon}{N}\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q^N}[W]
=\varepsilon\,\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q}[\lambda(O)]
=\varepsilon.
\end{aligned}\tag{5}
\]
The last equality follows from
\[
\mathbb E_{\mathsf Q}[\lambda(O)]=\sum_{o:q_o>0}q_o\frac{p_o}{q_o}=\sum_op_o=1,
\]
where overlap makes every positive \(p_o\) appear in the sum. Under \(\mathsf P\otimes\mathsf Q^M\), the threshold \(t_0\) is exactly \(\widehat r\), so \((5)\) proves the claim. This is the finite-support likelihood-ratio weighted-exchangeability argument: the target atom at \(+\infty\), overlap, left-continuous quantile, zero reference weights, and weak tie inclusion are all explicit in \((1)\)--\((5)\).
