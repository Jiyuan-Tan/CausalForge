Condition throughout on \(\mathcal T\). Fix an arm \(a\in\{0,1\}\). By \(\texttt{lem:orbit-score-factorization}\), the calibration-score and target-score laws are respectively\n\[\mathsf Q_a=\sum_{O\in\mathcal O}q_{a,O}F_{a,O},\qquad \mathsf P_a=\sum_{O\in\mathcal O}p_{a,O}F_{a,O}.\tag{1}\]\nFor every measurable \(f:[0,\infty)\to[0,1]\), put \(h_O=\int f\,dF_{a,O}\in[0,1]\). Since both orbit-mass vectors are probability vectors, \(\sum_O(p_{a,O}-q_{a,O})=0\). Consequently, the positive and negative parts of the signed vector \((p_{a,O}-q_{a,O})_O\) have the same total mass, and hence\n\[\left|\int f\,d(\mathsf P_a-\mathsf Q_a)\right|=\left|\sum_O(p_{a,O}-q_{a,O})h_O\right|\le \frac12\sum_O|p_{a,O}-q_{a,O}|=\Delta_a(w).\tag{2}\]\nThus passage from orbit laws to score laws contracts total variation.\n\nThe factorization lemma, together with \(\texttt{ass:independent-replicated-arrays}\) and \(\texttt{ass:selector-auxiliary-randomization}\), says that the \(M\) calibration scores are independent and identically distributed from \(\mathsf Q_a\). Introduce, only for comparison, an independent score \(S'_{0,a}\sim\mathsf Q_a\). Append independent continuous tie-breakers as prescribed in \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\). The rank of the augmented \(S'_{0,a}\) among the \(M+1\) independent and identically distributed augmented scores is uniform on \(\{1,\ldots,M+1\}\). Weak inclusion for the reported, unaugmented scores can only decrease the failure probability. Therefore, including the case \(k=M+1\), in which the threshold is \(+\infty\),\n\[\Pr_{\mathsf Q_a^{M+1}}\{S'_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\mid\mathcal T\}\le\frac{M+1-k}{M+1}=\rho_{M,\alpha}.\tag{3}\]\nFor every fixed realization of the calibration scores, \(f(s)=\mathbf 1\{s>r_{a,(k)}\}\) is a measurable \([0,1]\)-valued function. The target pair is independent of the calibration pairs by \(\texttt{lem:orbit-score-factorization}\), a consequence in particular of prespecified independent target selection. Applying (2) conditionally on the calibration scores, then averaging and using (3), gives\n\[\Pr_P\{S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\mid\mathcal T\}\le\rho_{M,\alpha}+\Delta_a(w).\tag{4}\]\n\nIt remains to check that clipping the reported interval does not alter the implication needed for coverage. Write the un-clipped residual-centered interval as\n\[\widetilde I_{a,w}=[\widehat m_a(X_{0T})-r_{a,(k)},\widehat m_a(X_{0T})+r_{a,(k)}].\]\nOn \(\{S_{0,a}\le r_{a,(k)}\}\), the definition of \(S_{0,a}\) gives \(Y_{0T}(a)\in\widetilde I_{a,w}\). Moreover, \(P\in\mathcal P_{\mathcal G,K}\) and \(\texttt{ass:bounded-potential-outcomes}\) give \(Y_{0T}(a)\in[-K,K]\). Under the clipped construction in \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\),\n\[I_{a,w}=\widetilde I_{a,w}\cap[-K,K],\]\nso\n\[\{Y_{0T}(a)\notin I_{a,w}\}\subseteq\{S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\}.\tag{5}\]\nIf neither arm fails, then \(Y_{0T}(a)\in I_{a,w}\) for \(a=0,1\), and therefore\n\[\tau_T=Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)\in I_{1,w}-I_{0,w}=\mathcal C_w.\tag{6}\]\nCombining (4)--(6) for the two arms and applying the union bound, without assuming independence across arms, yields\n\[\Pr_P\{\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T\}\ge 1-2\rho_{M,\alpha}-\Delta_0(w)-\Delta_1(w).\]\nTaking the maximum with zero is valid because probabilities are nonnegative. The formula \(\rho_{M,\alpha}=j/(M+1)\) on \(\alpha\in[2j/(M+1),2(j+1)/(M+1))\), and therefore the locations of the elbows, follow from \(\texttt{lem:finite-rank-staircase}\).\n\nFinally, \(\sum_O\delta_{a,O}(w)=0\). Hence the sum of the positive coordinates of \((\delta_{a,O}(w))_O\), and also the absolute sum of its negative coordinates, equals\n\[\frac12\sum_O|\delta_{a,O}(w)|=\Delta_a(w).\]\nEvery coordinate is bounded in absolute value by this common sum, proving \(|\delta_{a,O}(w)|\le\Delta_a(w)\) for every \(a\) and \(O\).
