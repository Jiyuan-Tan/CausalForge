The stipulated product group contains both local swap subgroups, so the four sets used below are global \(\mathcal G\)-orbits by \(\texttt{def:pair-action}\).

Each generator in \(S_2\times S_2\) swaps members only within its own pair block. Therefore the product action has one two-member orbit for each block and arm:
\[
\mathcal O=\{O_{1,0},O_{1,1},O_{2,0},O_{2,1}\}.
\tag{1}
\]
If \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) concentrates on \(v_{1,0}\), its arm \(1\) lift lies in \(O_{1,1}\). Thus the \((1,O_{1,1})\) coordinate of \(b_{\mathrm{LP}}\) is one and the other arm \(1\) orbit coordinate is zero.

The mate-control state \((v_{1,1},0)\) lies in \(O_{1,0}\). Its orbit vector places mass one on \(O_{1,0}\) and zero on \(O_{1,1}\), whereas the target arm \(1\) vector places mass one on \(O_{1,1}\). Consequently the relevant stacked equations in \(\texttt{def:common-selector-feasibility-lp}\) cannot both hold. Independently, \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) forces every legal arm \(1\) selector to be supported on \(\mathcal U_1\), while the mate-control state is in \(\mathcal U_0\). Neither conclusion depends on the armwise rank allocation.

If a legal selector has \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{1,1})=0\), then the sole target-supported arm \(1\) orbit has zero calibration mass. Applying \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-disjoint-orbit-witness}\) gives \(\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w=\{0\}\) and coverage zero when both ranks are finite, \(\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w=[-K,K]\) and coverage one when exactly one rank is at the boundary, and \(\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w=[-2K,2K]\) and coverage one when both ranks are at the boundary. The diagonal identity from \(\texttt{lem:armwise-finite-rank-staircase}\) makes the two ranks equal and recovers the two-regime symmetric calculation.
