For \(a\in\{0,1\}\), aggregate the implemented selector over an orbit by
\[
\kappa_{w,a}(O\mid z)=\sum_{v:(v,a)\in O}\kappa_{w,a}(v\mid z).
\tag{1}
\]
The joint law of one calibration orbit pair is
\[
R_w(O_0,O_1)=\sum_z\nu(z)\kappa_{w,0}(O_0\mid z)\kappa_{w,1}(O_1\mid z).
\tag{2}
\]
Indeed, \(\texttt{ass:selector-auxiliary-randomization}\) makes the two arm selectors conditionally independent given their common assignment, and \(\texttt{ass:known-matched-randomization}\) gives that assignment law as \(\nu\). The same two assumptions imply that the \(M\) calibration orbit pairs are independent and identically distributed according to (2). Notice that (2), rather than the product of its marginals, retains every dependence induced by the shared assignment.

The two target lifts are generated by the same \(T\). Hence their joint orbit law is
\[
P_T(O_0,O_1)=\sum_{v\in\mathcal V}q_{\mathrm{tar}}(v)\mathbf 1\{O_0(v)=O_0,\ O_1(v)=O_1\}.
\tag{3}
\]
It is a probability mass function because every \(v\) has exactly one orbit in each arm and \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) sums to one. A single scan through \(\mathcal V\) forms (3) in \(O(|\mathcal V|)\) operations. Its support is the image of the support of \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) under \(v\mapsto(O_0(v),O_1(v))\), so
\[
L=|\operatorname{supp}(P_T)|\le|\mathcal V|.
\tag{4}
\]
It is also a subset of \(\mathcal O_0\times\mathcal O_1\), proving \(L\le m_0m_1\). Together these inequalities prove the asserted bound on \(L\). Independence in \(\texttt{ass:prespecified-target}\) makes (3) independent of all calibration pairs.

Fix a pair of total preorders. Write their ordered nonempty tie classes as
\[
C_{a,1}\prec_a C_{a,2}\prec_a\cdots\prec_a C_{a,d_a},\qquad d_a\le m_a,
\tag{5}
\]
and let \(c_a(O)\) be the unique class index of \(O\). Aggregate (2) to the class-pair table
\[
W_{r,s}=\sum_{\substack{O_0:c_0(O_0)=r\\O_1:c_1(O_1)=s}}R_w(O_0,O_1),\qquad 1\le r\le d_0,\quad1\le s\le d_1.
\tag{6}
\]
One scan of the \(m_0m_1\) entries of \(R_w\) forms (6). Form the strict two-dimensional prefix sums
\[
H(j,\ell)=\sum_{r<j}\sum_{s<\ell}W_{r,s},\qquad 1\le j\le d_0+1,\quad1\le\ell\le d_1+1.
\tag{7}
\]
The usual recurrence
\[
H(j,\ell)=H(j-1,\ell)+H(j,\ell-1)-H(j-1,\ell-1)+W_{j-1,\ell-1}
\tag{8}
\]
for \(j,\ell\ge2\), with zero boundary row and column, forms the entire table in \(O(d_0d_1)\subseteq O(m_0m_1)\) operations.

Fix a supported target pair \((O_0^t,O_1^t)\), and put \(j=c_0(O_0^t)\) and \(\ell=c_1(O_1^t)\). For a calibration orbit pair define
\[
b_0(O_0)=\mathbf 1\{c_0(O_0)<j\},\qquad b_1(O_1)=\mathbf 1\{c_1(O_1)<\ell\}.
\tag{9}
\]
The four probabilities required by the two-count dynamic program are recovered from (7) by
\[
\begin{aligned}
\pi_{11}&=H(j,\ell),\\
\pi_{10}&=H(j,d_1+1)-H(j,\ell),\\
\pi_{01}&=H(d_0+1,\ell)-H(j,\ell),\\
\pi_{00}&=1-\pi_{11}-\pi_{10}-\pi_{01}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{10}
\]
Thus each supported target pair requires only constant-time prefix lookups and arithmetic to obtain its four-cell law. By the order-statistic identity, conditional on that target pair the joint rank event is
\[
\sum_{i=1}^M b_0(O_{i,0})<k_0\quad\text{and}\quad\sum_{i=1}^M b_1(O_{i,1})<k_1.
\tag{11}
\]
Applying \(\texttt{lem:four-cell-rank-event-dynamic-program}\) with the probabilities in (10) evaluates (11) in \(O(Mk_0k_1)\) operations and \(O(k_0k_1)\) memory. If its value is denoted by \(D(O_0^t,O_1^t)\), independence of the target and calibration pairs and the coherent law (3) give the unconditional event probability
\[
\sum_{(O_0^t,O_1^t)\in\operatorname{supp}(P_T)}P_T(O_0^t,O_1^t)D(O_0^t,O_1^t).
\tag{12}
\]
Equation (12) averages over one joint target pair, not two independent target marginals, while every \(D\) uses the full law (2). This proves the two advertised coherence claims.

For a fixed preorder pair, (6)--(8) cost \(O(m_0m_1)\), and the \(L\) supported pairs cost \(O(L\{1+Mk_0k_1\})\) by (10)--(12). There are \(F_{m_0}F_{m_1}\) preorder pairs. Adding the one-time cost for (3) gives
\[
O\!\left(|\mathcal V|+F_{m_0}F_{m_1}\left[m_0m_1+L\{1+Mk_0k_1\}\right]\right).
\tag{13}
\]
A sparse table for (3) stores \(O(L)\) entries, the class table and its prefix table require \(O(m_0m_1)\) memory, and two rolling dynamic-program arrays require \(O(k_0k_1)\). The class-index vectors use \(O(m_0+m_1)\), which is absorbed by \(O(m_0m_1)\) because both orbit sets are nonempty. Reusing these arrays across target pairs and preorder pairs proves the stated working-memory bound.
