The declared product group \(\mathcal G=S_2\times S_2\) contains the pure local swap subgroup for each pair block. Hence the sufficient audit in \(\texttt{def:pair-action}\) applies separately to both blocks. The action preserves blocks and arms, and each block has exactly two members, so the four global arm-specific orbits are
\[
O_{1,0},\quad O_{1,1},\quad O_{2,0},\quad O_{2,1}.
\tag{1}
\]
If \(q_{\mathrm{tar}}\) concentrates on \(v_{1,0}\), then \(T^1=(v_{1,0},1)\in O_{1,1}\) almost surely. The target arm-one orbit vector therefore assigns mass one to \(O_{1,1}\). The exclusive mate-control state \((v_{1,1},0)\) belongs to the distinct arm-zero orbit \(O_{1,0}\), so its analogue vector assigns mass one to \(O_{1,0}\) and zero to the target row \((1,O_{1,1})\). The corresponding equations in \(\texttt{def:common-selector-feasibility-lp}\) cannot both hold. Moreover, \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) excludes this control-labelled mate from a legal arm-one selector.

If a legal implemented selector instead has
\[
q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{1,1})=0,
\tag{2}
\]
then the sole target-supported arm-one orbit has zero calibration mass. For \(k\le M\), \(\texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness}\) gives
\[
\mathcal C_w=\{0\},
\qquad
\Pr_P(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T)=0.
\tag{3}
\]
For \(k=M+1\), \(\texttt{prop:rank-boundary}\) gives
\[
I_{0,w}=I_{1,w}=[-K,K],\qquad
\mathcal C_w=[-2K,2K],\qquad
\Pr_P(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T)=1.
\tag{4}
\]
Equations (1)--(4) prove every assertion.
