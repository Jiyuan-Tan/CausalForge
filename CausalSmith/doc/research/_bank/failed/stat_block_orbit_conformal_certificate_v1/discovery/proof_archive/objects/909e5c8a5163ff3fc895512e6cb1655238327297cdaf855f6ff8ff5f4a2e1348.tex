The pair-swap, orbit, balance, and selector-legality conclusions in \(\texttt{prop:s2-reduction}\) use the sufficient local hypotheses \(\mathcal V_b=\{v_{b,0},v_{b,1}\}\) and \(\langle s_b\rangle\le\mathcal G\), the arm-preserving action, and \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\). They do not involve the allocation \(\boldsymbol\varepsilon\), so they remain unchanged. This invocation asserts sufficiency of the audited local subgroup and no necessity.

Assume that the target arm-one lift is supported on \(O_{b,1}\) and that the legal implemented arm-one selector has
\[
q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{b,1})=0.
\tag{1}
\]
Then the unique target-supported arm-one orbit has zero calibration mass, so \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-disjoint-orbit-witness}\) applies with \(a=1\). It gives
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c}
\text{rank regime}
&
\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w
&
\text{conditional coverage}
\\ \hline
k_0,k_1\le M&\{0\}&0\\
\text{exactly one }k_c=M+1&[-K,K]&1\\
k_0=k_1=M+1&[-2K,2K]&1.

\tag{2}
\]
On the diagonal \(\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_1=\alpha/2\), \(\texttt{lem:armwise-finite-rank-staircase}\) gives \(k_0=k_1=k\). Hence the middle row of (2) cannot occur, while the first and third rows are precisely the two regimes of \(\texttt{prop:s2-reduction}\).
