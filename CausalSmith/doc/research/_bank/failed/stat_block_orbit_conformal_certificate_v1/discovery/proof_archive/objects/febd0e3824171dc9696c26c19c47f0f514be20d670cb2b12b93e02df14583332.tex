Condition throughout on \(\mathcal T\), and fix \(a\in\{0,1\}\). Write \(\mathcal O_a=\{O\in\mathcal O:O\subseteq\mathcal U_a\}\). By \(\texttt{lem:orbit-score-factorization}\), the conditional laws of a calibration score and the target score are, respectively,
\[
\mathsf Q_a=\sum_{O\in\mathcal O_a}q_{a,O}F_{a,O},\qquad \mathsf P_a=\sum_{O\in\mathcal O_a}p_{a,O}F_{a,O}.
\tag{1}
\]
For a measurable \(f:[0,\infty)\to[0,1]\), put \(h_O=\int f\,dF_{a,O}\in[0,1]\) and \(d_O=p_{a,O}-q_{a,O}\). Both orbit-mass vectors are probability vectors, so \(\sum_Od_O=0\). The positive and negative parts of \((d_O)_O\) consequently have the same total mass, equal to
\[
\frac12\sum_{O\in\mathcal O_a}|d_O|=\Delta_a(w).
\]
It follows that
\[
\left|\int f\,d(\mathsf P_a-\mathsf Q_a)\right|=\left|\sum_{O\in\mathcal O_a}d_Oh_O\right|\le\Delta_a(w).
\tag{2}
\]
Thus mixing through common orbit-specific score laws contracts the total variation distance between the orbit-mass vectors.

The factorization lemma also gives conditionally iid scores \(S_{1,a},\ldots,S_{M,a}\) with law \(\mathsf Q_a\). On an auxiliary product extension, introduce an independent comparison score \(S'_{0,a}\sim\mathsf Q_a\). Append independent continuous tie-breakers as prescribed by \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\). The rank of the augmented \(S'_{0,a}\) among the \(M+1\) iid augmented scores is uniform on \(\{1,\ldots,M+1\}\). Whenever the unaugmented comparison score strictly exceeds the reported \(k\)-th calibration order statistic, its augmented rank is greater than \(k\), regardless of tie-breaking. Hence
\[
\Pr_{\mathsf Q_a^{M+1}}\{S'_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\mid\mathcal T\}\le\frac{M+1-k}{M+1}=\rho_{M,\alpha}.
\tag{3}
\]
This includes \(k=M+1\), when the event is empty.

The actual target score is independent of the calibration scores and has conditional law \(\mathsf P_a\). For each fixed calibration realization, apply \((2)\) to \(f(s)=\mathbf 1\{s>r_{a,(k)}\}\). Comparing the actual target with the independent \(\mathsf Q_a\)-distributed target and then averaging over calibration data gives, by \((3)\),
\[
\Pr_P\{S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\mid\mathcal T\}\le\rho_{M,\alpha}+\Delta_a(w).
\tag{4}
\]
Define the un-clipped residual interval
\[
\widetilde I_{a,w}=\{y\in\mathbb R:|y-\widehat m_a(X_{0T})|\le r_{a,(k)}\}.
\]
On \(\{S_{0,a}\le r_{a,(k)}\}\), the target outcome lies in \(\widetilde I_{a,w}\). Because \(P\in\mathcal P_{\mathcal G,K}\), \(\texttt{ass:bounded-potential-outcomes}\) also gives \(Y_{0T}(a)\in[-K,K]\). Since \(I_{a,w}=\widetilde I_{a,w}\cap[-K,K]\),
\[
\{Y_{0T}(a)\notin I_{a,w}\}\subseteq\{S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\}.
\tag{5}
\]
If neither arm fails in \((5)\), then
\[
\tau_T=Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)\in I_{1,w}-I_{0,w}=\mathcal C_w.
\tag{6}
\]
Applying the union bound to \((4)\)--\((6)\), without requiring independence between arms, yields
\[
\Pr_P\{\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T\}\ge1-2\rho_{M,\alpha}-\Delta_0(w)-\Delta_1(w).
\]
A probability is nonnegative, so taking the maximum of the right-hand side with zero proves the displayed bound. The staircase and its elbows follow directly from \(\texttt{lem:finite-rank-staircase}\).

Finally, for each arm, \(\sum_{O\in\mathcal O}\delta_{a,O}(w)=0\), because both orbit-mass vectors have total mass one. The sum of the positive coordinates and the absolute sum of the negative coordinates therefore both equal \(\frac12\sum_O|\delta_{a,O}(w)|=\Delta_a(w)\). Each coordinate's absolute value is bounded by the corresponding sum, proving \(|\delta_{a,O}(w)|\le\Delta_a(w)\) for every \(a\) and \(O\).
