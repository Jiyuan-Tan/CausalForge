Set \(\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_1=\alpha/2\) in \(\texttt{lem:armwise-finite-rank-staircase}\). Then \(k_0=k_1=k\) and \(\rho_{M,\varepsilon_0}=\rho_{M,\varepsilon_1}=\rho_{M,\alpha}\). Its identity and staircase intervals become
\[
\rho_{M,\alpha}=\frac{N-k}{N}=\frac{\lfloor N\alpha/2\rfloor}{N}\le\frac\alpha2,
\qquad
\alpha\in\left[\frac{2j}{N},\frac{2(j+1)}{N}\right).
\]
The boundary condition \(0<\varepsilon_a<1/N\) becomes \(0<\alpha<2/N\), which is equivalent to \(k=M+1\). These are exactly the asserted formulas.
