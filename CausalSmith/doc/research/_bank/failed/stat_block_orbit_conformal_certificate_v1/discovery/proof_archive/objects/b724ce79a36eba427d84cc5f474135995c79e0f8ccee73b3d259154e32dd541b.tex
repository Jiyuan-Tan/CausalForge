Fix an arm \(a\), condition on \(\mathcal T\), and write \(\varepsilon=\alpha/2\). The orbit-kernel calculation in the proof of \(\texttt{thm:orbit-balance-iff}\) supplies probability kernels \(F_{a,O}\) such that the calibration orbit-score pair has law \[Q_a(O,ds)=q_{a,O}F_{a,O}(ds),\] whereas the target orbit-score pair has law \[R_a(O,ds)=p_{a,O}F_{a,O}(ds).\] Orbit overlap implies \(R_a\ll Q_a\), with Radon--Nikodym derivative \(\lambda_a(O)=p_{a,O}/q_{a,O}\) on target-supported orbits. Set this derivative to zero on orbits with \(p_{a,O}=0<q_{a,O}\); its value on \(p_{a,O}=q_{a,O}=0\) is immaterial. In particular, \(\int\lambda_a\,dQ_a=1\). The independence assumptions make the \(M\) calibration pairs iid from \(Q_a\), independent of the target pair from \(R_a\).

We first prove the required deterministic weighted-rank inequality. Take \(N=M+1\) labelled pairs \(x_j=(O_j,s_j)\), weights \(\ell_j=\lambda_a(O_j)\ge0\), and total weight \(W=\sum_j\ell_j>0\). When label \(j\) is designated as target, let \(R_j\) be the left-continuous \((1-\varepsilon)\)-quantile after putting weight \(\ell_j\) at \(+\infty\) and weight \(\ell_l\) at \(s_l\) for \(l\ne j\), normalized by \(W\). If \(s_j>R_j\), then \(R_j<+\infty\) and \[\sum_{l:s_l<s_j}\ell_l\ge\sum_{l\ne j:s_l\le R_j}\ell_l\ge(1-\varepsilon)W.\tag{3}\] Let \(F=\{j:s_j>R_j\}\). If \(F\ne\varnothing\), choose a member of \(F\) having the smallest score among \(F\). Equation (3) shows that the total weight at scores at least this smallest failing score is at most \(\varepsilon W\). Since every member of \(F\) lies in that upper set, \[\sum_{j=0}^M\ell_j\mathbf 1\{s_j>R_j\}\le\varepsilon\sum_{j=0}^M\ell_j.\tag{4}\]

Let \(X_0\sim R_a\) and \(X_1,\ldots,X_M\overset{\mathrm{iid}}{\sim}Q_a\). Relative to \(Q_a^N\), their joint law has density \(\lambda_a(O_0)\). Symmetry of \(Q_a^N\), followed by (4), gives \[\begin{aligned}\Pr(S_{0,a}>r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}\mid\mathcal T)&=\frac1N\int\sum_{j=0}^M\lambda_a(O_j)\mathbf 1\{s_j>R_j\}\,dQ_a^N\\&\le\frac{\varepsilon}{N}\int\sum_{j=0}^M\lambda_a(O_j)\,dQ_a^N=\varepsilon.\end{aligned}\] This is exactly the infinity-augmented rule in \(\texttt{def:weighted-rank-inversion}\). Thus each potential outcome lies in its weighted arm interval with conditional probability at least \(1-\alpha/2\). Simultaneous armwise inclusion implies \(\tau_T\in I^{\mathrm W}_{1,w}-I^{\mathrm W}_{0,w}\), and Bonferroni proves \[\Pr_P(\tau_T\in\mathcal C^{\mathrm W}_w\mid\mathcal T)\ge1-\alpha.\]

If joint balance holds, then \(p_{a,O}=q_{a,O}\) on every orbit that can occur and all relevant weights equal one. The normalized weighted empirical measure then has \(N\) equal atoms, one at \(+\infty\); its left-continuous \((1-\alpha/2)\)-quantile is the \(k\)-th calibration order statistic when \(k\le M\), and \(+\infty\) when \(k=M+1\). Hence the weighted construction reduces to ordinary rank inversion.
