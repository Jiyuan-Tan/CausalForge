Condition on \(\mathcal T\), and define \(R_{iv,a}=|Y_{iv}(a)-\widehat m_a(X_{iv})|\). If \((v,a)\) and \((v',a)\) are in the same orbit, then \(v'=\pi_g(v)\) for some \(g\in\mathcal G\). Latent-array invariance and \(\texttt{def:group-action}\) imply \(R_{iv',a}\overset d=R_{iv,a}\). Denote this common orbit-specific law by \(F_{a,O}\).

The selector-support condition gives \(Z_{iJ_{i,a}}=a\) almost surely, hence \(S_{i,a}=R_{iJ_{i,a},a}\). Randomization is conditionally independent of the latent array and the auxiliary selector uses only \(Z_i\). Thus, for every Borel \(C\), \[\begin{aligned}\Pr(O_{i,a}=O,S_{i,a}\in C\mid\mathcal T)&=\sum_z\nu(z)\sum_{v:(v,a)\in O}\kappa_{w,a}(v\mid z)\Pr(R_{iv,a}\in C\mid\mathcal T)\\&=F_{a,O}(C)\sum_z\nu(z)\sum_{v:(v,a)\in O}\kappa_{w,a}(v\mid z)\\&=q_{a,O}F_{a,O}(C).\end{aligned}\] Target independence similarly gives \[\Pr(O_{T,a}=O,S_{0,a}\in C\mid\mathcal T)=\sum_{v:(v,a)\in O}q_{\mathrm{tar}}(v)\Pr(R_{0v,a}\in C\mid\mathcal T)=p_{a,O}F_{a,O}(C).\] Finally, iid latent arrays, product randomization, the auxiliary-product draw, and independent target selection imply that the calibration pairs are iid and independent of the target pair.
