Set \(\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_1=\alpha/2\). By \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\), \(k_0=k_1=k\) and \(\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w=\mathcal C_w\), while \(\texttt{lem:finite-rank-staircase}\) gives the common identity \(\rho_{M,\varepsilon_0}=\rho_{M,\varepsilon_1}=\rho_{M,\alpha}\). If \(k\le M\), both arms belong to \(\mathcal A_{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}\), so \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-finite-rank-orbit-gap-bound}\) gives
\[
\Pr_P(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T)\ge\max\{0,1-2\rho_{M,\alpha}-\Delta_0(w)-\Delta_1(w)\}.
\]
If \(k=M+1\), neither arm is active and the same proposition gives coverage one. Since \(\rho_{M,\alpha}=0\) in this case and \(\Delta_0(w),\Delta_1(w)\ge0\), coverage one is at least the displayed lower bound. Thus the bound holds in every rank regime.

The staircase identity in \(\texttt{lem:finite-rank-staircase}\) states that, with \(N=M+1\),
\[
\rho_{M,\alpha}=\frac{j}{N}
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\alpha\in\left[\frac{2j}{N},\frac{2(j+1)}{N}\right),
\]
for every integer \(j\) for which the interval meets \((0,1)\). Hence the elbows are \(2j/(M+1)\). Finally, the coordinate inequality \(|\delta_{a,O}(w)|\le\Delta_a(w)\) is part of \(\texttt{prop:asymmetric-finite-rank-orbit-gap-bound}\), and is unchanged by the diagonal specialization.
