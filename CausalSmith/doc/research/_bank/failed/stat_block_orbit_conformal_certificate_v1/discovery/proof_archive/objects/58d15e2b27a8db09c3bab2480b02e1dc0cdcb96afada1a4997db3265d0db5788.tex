Assume \(\mathcal V_b=\{v_{b,0},v_{b,1}\}\) and \(\langle s_b\rangle\le\mathcal G\). By \(\texttt{def:pair-action}\),
\[
s_b(v_{b,r},a)=(v_{b,1-r},a),\qquad r,a\in\{0,1\},
\tag{1}
\]
with every outside member fixed, and complete records are relabelled through \(\texttt{def:group-action}\). Every element of \(\mathcal G\) preserves blocks, and the action preserves arms. Because block \(b\) has exactly the two displayed members, the global orbit of either \((v_{b,r},a)\) is contained in \(O_{b,a}\). Equation (1) puts both states in that orbit. Therefore
\[
\operatorname{Orb}_{\mathcal G}(v_{b,r},a)=O_{b,a}=\{(v_{b,0},a),(v_{b,1},a)\},\qquad a\in\{0,1\}.
\tag{2}
\]
This is only a sufficient audit; no necessity claim about the pure local swap is used.

If the target arm-one lift is supported on \(O_{b,1}\), its orbit vector has mass one in row \((1,O_{b,1})\). An exclusive mate-control analogue is supported on a control-labelled state in the distinct orbit \(O_{b,0}\), because the action preserves arms, and hence has mass zero in that row. Thus its orbit-mass vector differs from the target vector, so the equations in \(\texttt{def:joint-orbit-balance}\), equivalently the relevant equations of \(\texttt{def:common-selector-feasibility-lp}\), cannot hold. Independently, \(\texttt{ass:observable-selector-kernel}\) requires an arm-one selector to put mass only on a member with \(z_v=1\); the realized mate's control state is therefore not a legal arm-one selection.

Now suppose a legal implemented arm-one selector satisfies
\[
q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,1}(O_{b,1})=0.
\tag{3}
\]
Since \(O_{b,1}\) is the only target-supported arm-one orbit, (3) is exactly the disjoint-orbit premise of \(\texttt{prop:disjoint-orbit-witness}\). That proposition gives zero conditional coverage when \(k\le M\). When \(k=M+1\), \(\texttt{prop:rank-boundary}\) instead gives \(\mathcal C_w=[-2K,2K]\) and conditional coverage one.
