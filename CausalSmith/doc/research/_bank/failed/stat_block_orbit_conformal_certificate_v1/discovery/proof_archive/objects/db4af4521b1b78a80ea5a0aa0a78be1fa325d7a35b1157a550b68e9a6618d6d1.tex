The full two-arm attainment, primal and dual identities, Farkas margin, prespecification, exact-balance characterization, fixed-\(w_*\) coverage certificate, dominance statement, and equal-split formula are all proved in \(\texttt{thm:active-arm-minimum-imbalance-selector-certificate}\). In particular, that theorem retains \(D_*\), \(\mathcal W_*\), and \(w_*\) unchanged and proves the exact identity
\[
D_*=\max_{\lVert y\rVert_\infty\le1/2}
\left\{\min_{h\in\mathcal H}y^\top A_{\cdot h}-y^\top b_{\mathrm{LP}}\right\}.
\]
Its active-arm certificate is stronger: it omits every boundary arm and optimizes only the remaining rows. The frozen theorem follows by retaining both arms in its numerical certificate, or equivalently by using the inequalities
\[
\sum_{a\in\mathcal A_{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}}\rho_{M,\varepsilon_a}\le \rho_{M,\varepsilon_0}+\rho_{M,\varepsilon_1},
\qquad
\frac12\sum_{a\in\mathcal A_{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}}\sum_O|\delta_{a,O}(w_*)|\le D_*.
\]
On the equal-split finite-rank regime, \(\mathcal A_{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}=\{0,1\}\), so the active and frozen formulas coincide. At the two-boundary elbow, the stronger theorem gives coverage one.
