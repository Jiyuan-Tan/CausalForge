Let \(\mathcal O_{T,a}=\{O:p_{a,O}>0\}\). The premise says \(q_{a,O}=0\) for every \(O\in\mathcal O_{T,a}\), so a state drawn from the calibration selector in arm \(a\) lies outside \(\bigcup\mathcal O_{T,a}\) almost surely. In \(\texttt{def:disjoint-orbit-law}\), the outcome in arm \(a\) is \(K\) on this union and zero on its complement, the other-arm outcome is zero everywhere, and both fitted maps are zero. This outcome prototype is constant on every \(\mathcal G\)-orbit. Averaging the full latent prototype over a uniform group element therefore gives an invariant latent law; all outcome coordinates lie in \([-K,K]\). Thus the law belongs to \(\mathcal P_{\mathcal G,K}\).

Every selected adverse-arm calibration state has outcome and score zero. Every other-arm outcome and score is also zero. When \(k\le M\), the \(k\)-th order statistic of each all-zero calibration vector is zero, and the zero fitted centers give \[I_{0,w}=I_{1,w}=\{0\},\qquad\mathcal C_w=\{0\}.\] On the other hand, \(T^a\) belongs to a target-supported orbit almost surely. Hence \(Y_{0T}(a)=K\) and \(Y_{0T}(1-a)=0\). It follows that \(\tau_T=K\) when \(a=1\) and \(\tau_T=-K\) when \(a=0\). Since \(K>0\), the effect is not in \(\{0\}\), and conditional coverage is zero.

It remains only to verify the stated rank regime. With \(N=M+1\), \(k\le M=N-1\) is equivalent to \(\lceil N(1-\alpha/2)\rceil\le N-1\), which is equivalent to \(N(1-\alpha/2)\le N-1\), or \(\alpha\ge2/N=2/(M+1)\).
