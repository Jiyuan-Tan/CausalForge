Assume that \(\mathcal H\) is finite and nonempty, fix or prespecify \(w\in\Delta(\mathcal H)\), and condition on \(\mathcal T\). Fix an arm \(a\). By \(\texttt{lem:orbit-score-factorization}\), the calibration orbit--score pairs are independent and identically distributed with law
\[
\mathsf Q_a(\{O\}\times C)=q_{a,O}F_{a,O}(C),
\]
whereas the independent target pair has law
\[
\mathsf P_a(\{O\}\times C)=p_{a,O}F_{a,O}(C).
\]
Orbit overlap implies \(\mathsf P_a\ll\mathsf Q_a\). Since the conditional score law \(F_{a,O}\) is identical under the two measures on each orbit fibre,
\[
\frac{d\mathsf P_a}{d\mathsf Q_a}(O,s)=\frac{p_{a,O}}{q_{a,O}}=\lambda_{a,O}\qquad\mathsf Q_a\text{-almost surely}.\tag{1}
\]
Thus the likelihood ratio is measurable with respect to the finite orbit quotient. Equation \((1)\) also proves the member-singularity claim: selectors may put different or even mutually singular mass on individual members within one orbit, because the common conditional score kernel cancels before the quotient likelihood ratio is formed.

Because \(0<\epsilon_a<1\), we may apply \(\texttt{lem:finite-support-weighted-rank-validity}\) to the finite state set \(\mathcal O_a=\{O\in\mathcal O:O\subseteq\mathcal U_a\}\), with \(q_O=q_{a,O}\), \(p_O=p_{a,O}\), and \(R_i=S_{i,a}\). The measure appearing in that lemma is exactly the infinity-augmented measure in \(\texttt{def:weighted-rank-inversion}\); its target atom has positive weight almost surely because \(p_{a,O_{T,a}}>0\) and orbit overlap gives \(q_{a,O_{T,a}}>0\). Therefore
\[
\Pr_P\!\left(S_{0,a}>r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}\mid\mathcal T\right)\le\epsilon_a.\tag{2}
\]
Let \(E_a=\{S_{0,a}\le r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}\}\). On \(E_a\), the definition of the residual gives
\[
Y_{0T}(a)\in[\widehat m_a(X_{0T})-r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w},\widehat m_a(X_{0T})+r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}].
\]
The bounded-outcome assumption also gives \(Y_{0T}(a)\in[-K,K]\), so \(Y_{0T}(a)\in I^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}\) on \(E_a\), including the case \(r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}=+\infty\). Applying \((2)\) to both arms and using the union bound, without arm independence, yields
\[
\Pr_P(E_0\cap E_1\mid\mathcal T)\ge1-\epsilon_0-\epsilon_1\ge1-\alpha.\tag{3}
\]
On \(E_0\cap E_1\), set subtraction gives
\[
\tau_T=Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)\in I^{\mathrm W}_{1,w}-I^{\mathrm W}_{0,w}=\mathcal C^{\mathrm W}_w.\tag{4}
\]
Equations \((3)\)--\((4)\) prove the asserted conditional coverage uniformly over the declared laws and trained maps.

Under joint orbit balance, \(p_{a,O}=q_{a,O}\) for every arm and orbit. Hence every orbit that can be drawn has \(\lambda_{a,O}=1\); zero-mass orbits are irrelevant. The normalized weighted measure then has \(M+1\) equal-mass atoms, one at each calibration score and one at \(+\infty\). Its left-continuous \((1-\epsilon_a)\)-quantile is the \(k_a\)-th atom, where
\[
k_a=\left\lceil(M+1)(1-\epsilon_a)\right\rceil.
\]
If \(k_a\le M\), this is the \(k_a\)-th calibration order statistic; if \(k_a=M+1\), it is \(+\infty\). Thus
\[
r^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}=r_{a,(k_a)},\qquad I^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}=I^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_{a,w},\qquad \mathcal C^{\mathrm W}_w=\mathcal C^{\boldsymbol\varepsilon}_w,\tag{5}
\]
where \(\varepsilon_a=\epsilon_a\). On taking \(\epsilon_0=\epsilon_1=\alpha/2\), one has \(k_0=k_1=\lceil(M+1)(1-\alpha/2)\rceil\), and \((5)\) reduces to \(I^{\mathrm W}_{a,w}=I_{a,w}\) and \(\mathcal C^{\mathrm W}_w=\mathcal C_w\).

The one-arm step above is precisely the finite-support likelihood-ratio weighted-conformal mechanism formalized in \(\texttt{lem:finite-support-weighted-rank-validity}\), including the infinity augmentation, overlap, ties, and the left-continuous quantile convention. The theorem-specific deduction is \((1)\): invariant matched-block laws collapse the likelihood ratio to the orbit quotient, after which the same one common target, common two-arm selector, clipping, and Bonferroni produce \((3)\)--\((4)\).
