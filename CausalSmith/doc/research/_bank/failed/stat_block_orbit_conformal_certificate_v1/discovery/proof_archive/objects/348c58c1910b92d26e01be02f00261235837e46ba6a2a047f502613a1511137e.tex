Fix the adverse arm \(a\), and let \(\mathcal A_a=\{O\in\mathcal O:q_{\mathrm{tar},a}(O)>0\}\). The premise gives \(q^{\mathrm{obs}}_{w,a}(O)=0\) for every \(O\in\mathcal A_a\). Hence the prescriptions in \(\texttt{def:disjoint-orbit-law}\) do not conflict: arm-\(a\) outcomes equal \(K\) on the union of target-supported orbits and zero on every calibration-supported orbit, while all arm-\(1-a\) outcomes equal zero. All outcomes lie in \([-K,K]\). The prescriptions are orbit-constant, and the uniform group symmetrization in the definition makes the latent-array law invariant. Taking constant covariates completes a bounded invariant latent array, so the constructed law belongs to \(\mathcal P_{\mathcal G,K}\). Independent copies and the stated invariant randomization then satisfy the replicated-experiment assumptions.

The construction fixes \(\widehat m_0=\widehat m_1=0\). A selected arm-\(a\) calibration state lies in a calibration-supported orbit almost surely and thus has outcome and score zero. Every arm-\(1-a\) outcome and score is also zero. Since \(k\le M\), the \(k\)-th order statistic in both arms is zero, whence \[I_{0,w}=I_{1,w}=\{0\},\qquad\mathcal C_w=\{0\}.\] On the other hand, \(T^a\) belongs to \(\bigcup_{O\in\mathcal A_a}O\) almost surely. Therefore \(Y_{0T}(a)=K\), \(Y_{0T}(1-a)=0\), and \[\tau_T=\begin{cases}K,&a=1,\\-K,&a=0.\end{cases}\] Since \(K>0\), the target effect is never in \(\mathcal C_w\), so the conditional coverage is zero. Finally, \[k\le M\iff\left\lceil(M+1)(1-\alpha/2)\right\rceil\le M\iff(M+1)(1-\alpha/2)\le M\iff\alpha\ge\frac{2}{M+1}.\]
