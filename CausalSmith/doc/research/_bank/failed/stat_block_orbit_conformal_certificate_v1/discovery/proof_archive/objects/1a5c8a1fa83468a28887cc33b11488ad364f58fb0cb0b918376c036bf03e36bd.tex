Specialize \(\texttt{lem:armwise-finite-rank-staircase}\) to \(\varepsilon_0=\varepsilon_1=\alpha/2\) and put \(N=M+1\). The diagonal identities in that lemma give \(k_0=k_1=k\) and \(\rho_{M,\varepsilon_0}=\rho_{M,\varepsilon_1}=\rho_{M,\alpha}\). Its armwise formula therefore becomes\n\[\rho_{M,\alpha}=\frac{N-k}{N}=\frac{\lfloor N\alpha/2\rfloor}{N}\le\frac{\alpha}{2}.\]\nLikewise, \(\rho_{M,\varepsilon_a}=j/N\) exactly for \(\varepsilon_a\in[j/N,(j+1)/N)\); substituting \(\varepsilon_a=\alpha/2\) gives\n\[\rho_{M,\alpha}=\frac jN\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad\alpha\in\left[\frac{2j}{N},\frac{2(j+1)}{N}\right),\]\nfor every indicated integer \(j\). Finally, the armwise boundary condition \(k_a=M+1\) exactly when \(0<\varepsilon_a<1/N\) becomes\n\[k=M+1\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad0<\alpha<\frac2N.\]\nOn the same interval \(\lfloor N\alpha/2\rfloor=0\), and outside it that floor is at least one. Thus the boundary condition is also equivalent to \(\rho_{M,\alpha}=0\), proving all claims.
