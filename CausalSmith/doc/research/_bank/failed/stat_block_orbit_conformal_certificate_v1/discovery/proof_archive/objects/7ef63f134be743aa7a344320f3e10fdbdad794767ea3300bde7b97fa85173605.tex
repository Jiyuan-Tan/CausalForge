Condition throughout on \(\mathcal T\). By \(\texttt{lem:orbit-score-factorization}\), the calibration and target score laws in arm \(a\) are respectively
\[Q_a=\sum_{O\in\mathcal O}q_{a,O}F_{a,O},\qquad P_a=\sum_{O\in\mathcal O}p_{a,O}F_{a,O}.\]
For a measurable function \(f:[0,\infty)\to[0,1]\), put \(h_O=\int f\,dF_{a,O}\in[0,1]\). Since \(\sum_O(p_{a,O}-q_{a,O})=0\), the positive and negative parts of the signed vector \((p_{a,O}-q_{a,O})_O\) have the same total mass. Therefore
\[\left|\int f\,d(P_a-Q_a)\right|=\left|\sum_O(p_{a,O}-q_{a,O})h_O\right|\le\frac12\sum_O|p_{a,O}-q_{a,O}|=\Delta_a(w).\tag{1}\]
This is the total-variation contraction from orbit laws to score laws.

The \(M\) calibration scores are iid from \(Q_a\), by the factorization lemma together with the replicated-array and auxiliary-randomization assumptions. Introduce an independent comparison score \(S'_{0,a}\sim Q_a\). Append independent continuous tie-breakers as prescribed in \(\texttt{def:rank-inversion}\). The rank of the augmented \(S'_{0,a}\) among the \(M+1\) iid augmented scores is uniform on \(\{1,\ldots,M+1\}\). Weak inclusion of tied unaugmented scores can only reduce failure. Hence, with \(r_{a,(k)}\) computed from the \(M\) calibration scores,
\[\Pr_{Q_a^{M+1}}(S'_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\mid\mathcal T)\le\frac{M+1-k}{M+1}=\rho_{M,\alpha}.\tag{2}\]
For every fixed realization of the calibration scores, the indicator of \(\{S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\}\) is a measurable \([0,1]\)-valued function of the target score. Replacing its law from \(Q_a\) by \(P_a\) changes its conditional expectation by at most \(\Delta_a(w)\) by (1). Averaging over the calibration scores and using (2) yields
\[\Pr(S_{0,a}>r_{a,(k)}\mid\mathcal T)\le\rho_{M,\alpha}+\Delta_a(w).\tag{3}\]

If neither arm fails, then \(Y_{0T}(a)\in I_{a,w}\) for both \(a=0,1\), and therefore
\[\tau_T=Y_{0T}(1)-Y_{0T}(0)\in I_{1,w}-I_{0,w}=\mathcal C_w.\]
The union bound applied to (3), without any cross-arm independence, gives
\[\Pr_P(\tau_T\in\mathcal C_w\mid\mathcal T)\ge1-2\rho_{M,\alpha}-\Delta_0(w)-\Delta_1(w).\]
A probability is nonnegative, which gives the displayed maximum with zero. The staircase formula and its elbows follow directly from \(\texttt{lem:finite-rank-staircase}\).

Finally, \(\sum_O\delta_{a,O}(w)=0\). Thus the sum of the positive coordinates and the absolute sum of the negative coordinates both equal
\[\frac12\sum_O|\delta_{a,O}(w)|=\Delta_a(w).\]
Each individual positive or negative coordinate is bounded in absolute value by that common sum, proving \(|\delta_{a,O}(w)|\le\Delta_a(w)\).
