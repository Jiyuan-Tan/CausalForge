Condition throughout on \(\mathcal T\), so the maps \(\widehat m_a\) are fixed. By \(\texttt{lem:orbit-score-factorization}\), for each arm \(a\) there are orbit-specific probability laws \(F_{a,O}\) such that the calibration orbit-score pairs are iid with law \[\mathsf Q_a(O,ds)=q_{a,O}F_{a,O}(ds),\] while the target pair, independent of them, has law \[\mathsf P_a(O,ds)=p_{a,O}F_{a,O}(ds).\] If joint orbit balance holds, then \(q_{a,O}=p_{a,O}\) for every \(a,O\), hence \(\mathsf Q_a=\mathsf P_a\). It follows that, armwise, \(S_{1,a},\ldots,S_{M,a},S_{0,a}\) are iid conditional on \(\mathcal T\). In particular, every calibration score has the same law as the target score. This conclusion is armwise: the common target \(T\) and the shared within-replicate assignments may couple the two arms, and no cross-arm independence or joint exchangeability has been used.

Conversely, suppose that for some arm \(a\) and orbit \(O\), \(q_{a,O}\ne p_{a,O}\). Use \(\texttt{def:orbit-separating-law}\), take constant covariates, and fix \(\widehat m_0=\widehat m_1=0\). The arm-\(a\) score is then exactly \(K\mathbf 1\{U\in O\}\). The construction is bounded because its outcomes lie in \(\{0,K\}\), and it is invariant because \(O\) is a \(\mathcal G\)-orbit; equivalently, the uniform group symmetrization in the definition makes its law invariant. Thus it is a member of \(\mathcal P_{\mathcal G,K}\). Under this law, \[\Pr(S_{i,a}=K\mid\mathcal T)=q_{a,O},\qquad \Pr(S_{0,a}=K\mid\mathcal T)=p_{a,O}.\] These score laws differ. Hence equality of the armwise calibration and target score laws for every law in \(\mathcal P_{\mathcal G,K}\) and every fixed measurable predictor implies every orbit-mass equality. Applying the argument to both arms proves the claimed equivalence and the stated witness assertion.
